## Definition

An "aerial dogfight" in the UAP context refers to an encounter in which a military pilot engages with an unidentified object that performs responsive, evasive, or aggressive maneuvers — turning into the pilot, making head-on passes, circling, outclimbing, and otherwise behaving as if guided by intelligent intent. The term is most closely associated with the **Gorman Dogfight** of 1 October 1948 but describes a class of military UAP encounters where the object actively interacts with the pursuing aircraft.

## Characteristics

- Object performs maneuvers that are tactically responsive (turns into pursuer, makes counter-passes, evades interdiction)
- Object demonstrates performance capabilities beyond the pursuing aircraft (outclimbing, outspeeding)
- Duration typically minutes to tens of minutes (not seconds)
- Multiple witnesses often corroborate from ground (tower personnel, radar if available)
- Pilot typically reports the object appeared to have "thought" or "intent" behind its movements

## Documented Examples

- **Gorman Dogfight (1 October 1948)** — [[entities/gorman-dogfight]], [[summaries/dow-uap-d088-us-airforce-analysis-of-flying-objects-in-the-us-101-172]] Incident #172: 27-minute engagement over Fargo, ND between F-51 pilot George F. Gorman and a 6-8 inch ball of white light. Object made head-on passes, 180° turns, outclimbed the F-51 to 14,000+ ft, and finally departed straight up. Gorman stated "there was 'thought' behind the maneuvers." Three tower personnel corroborated. Geiger counter check performed on aircraft.
