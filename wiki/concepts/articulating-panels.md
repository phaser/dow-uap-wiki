## Definition

**Articulating panels** describes a surface morphology observed on the Cheyenne Mountain UAP (February 2022) in which the object's exterior was composed of non-symmetrical, non-overlapping, irregular-shaped panels that appeared to shift independently in slow waves. Although the object as a whole was perfectly still, individual panels moved in coordinated patterns starting from different points of origin simultaneously.

## Characteristics

- **Panel shape**: Non-symmetrical, irregular, non-overlapping — described as resembling "fish scales"
- **Motion**: Each panel shifted in slow waves, starting at different points of origin but at the same time (coordinated, not random)
- **Object stationary**: The object itself was perfectly motionless while panels moved across its surface
- **Material appearance**: Creamy/whitish opalescent color, somewhat translucent with a slight shimmer
- **Witness**: Former U.S. Army intelligence officer; described the pattern in detail during an FBI forensic sketch interview

## Documented Examples

- **Cheyenne Mountain UAP (February 2022)** — Former Army intelligence officer and four unit members observe a "potato-shaped" object with independently shifting panel surfaces over Cheyenne Mountain, Colorado Springs. See [[entities/cheyenne-mountain-uap-2022]] and [[summaries/fbi-uap-d002-colorado-springs-2022]].
- **Possible related**: The same object was described by another witness (D001) as having "intersecting lines or ridges forming an abstract polygon pattern" — possibly a different vocabulary for the same surface feature. See [[summaries/fbi-uap-d001-colorado-springs-2022]].

## Notes

- The 1960 Tacoma "waffle-like" surface description in [[summaries/fbi-uap-d013-wa-ufo-investigation-1952-1960]] and the "intersecting polygon ridges" in D001 suggest that complex surface textures on otherwise smooth/ovoid UAPs may be a recurring observation across decades. The Cheyenne Mountain account is unique in describing the surface as **actively articulating** rather than static.
