## Definition

A pattern observed across the early UFO record (and continuing into the modern UAP era) in which unidentified objects appear disproportionately over **U.S. atomic-energy installations** — Los Alamos, Sandia / Kirtland, Hanford, Savannah River, Oak Ridge — relative to chance. The pattern is most clearly documented in the **green fireball wave** of 1948–1949 (see [[concepts/green-fireball]]) and continued into the **1960s–1970s ICBM-base UFO incursion** literature (less represented in this corpus). The pattern is a non-trivial argument for non-random behavior, since the physical-area fraction of CONUS occupied by atomic-energy installations is small while their UFO-report density is disproportionately high.

## Characteristics

- Geographic concentration over fissile-material handling, storage, and weapons assembly facilities.
- Mixed object descriptions: green fireballs (1948-49), discs (multiple eras), orbs (modern), unidentified lights at ICBM silos (1960s-70s).
- The pattern is preserved in FBI 62-HQ-83894 sections 4 and 6, with internal correspondence treating the clustering as an investigative priority.

- Hanford, WA cluster (cross-referenced) — also Section 4
