## Definition

A descriptor used in modern AARO Mission Reports for UAPs whose visual signature is consistent with a free-floating balloon — slow drift, near-spherical, no apparent powered propulsion — but whose other characteristics (sustained station-keeping in defined airspace, station-keeping at altitudes/locations difficult for cooperative weather balloons, anomalous IR signature) prevent positive ID as a known weather / research / commercial balloon.

## Characteristics

- Slow apparent groundspeed, drift consistent with prevailing winds within a tolerance window.
- Near-spherical or pendant-shape morphology.
- Lack of expected RF / IFF / Mode-S signatures that would identify a tracked balloon.
- May be the modern descendant of the [[concepts/skyhook-balloon]] line of conventional explanations from the 1948-1955 era — but inverted in framing: instead of "this UAP is a balloon," the modern usage is "this UAP looks balloon-like but cannot be positively ID'd as one."
- A genuinely difficult discriminator since (a) the global high-altitude balloon population is large and growing, and (b) balloon-like UAPs are precisely the cases most likely to have mundane explanations.
