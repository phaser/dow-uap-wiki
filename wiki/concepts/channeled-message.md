## Definition

**Channeled messages** are UFO-adjacent communications received by a human contactee from a claimed non-human intelligence via telepathy, automatic writing, mediumistic trance, or radio-style mental reception. The phenomenon overlaps the [[concepts/contactee]] tradition and frequently borrows vocabulary, narrative structures, and ethical frames from earlier 19th-century Theosophical and spiritualist movements. The Bob Renaud / Korendor correspondence (May 1966 onward, **Serial 449 + Section 10** of [[entities/62-hq-83894]]) is the most extensively-preserved example in the FBI HQ file.

## Characteristics

- Recurrent named "alien" interlocutors (Korendor, Orthon, Indrid Cold, etc.).
- Messages typically combine cosmological claims, ethical prescriptions, and political commentary.
- Source claims often include specific star systems (Venus, Mars, Korendor — a fictional planet), specific orbital craft or "scout ships."
- The FBI's interest is rarely in the truth of the channeling but in (a) potential domestic-political messaging activity and (b) repeat-reporter / mental-health context.
