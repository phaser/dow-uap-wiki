## Definition

A **cold thermal signature** is a UAP imaged in mid- or long-wave IR that registers as *cooler* than its background — the inverse of what one expects from a vehicle with active propulsion or heated surfaces. In the DoW UAP release set the cold-IR class appears across multiple AORs (D23 UAE, D44 Arabian Sea, D57 Gulf of Aden) and is treated as a distinct phenomenological sub-class from the more common white-hot signature.

## Characteristics

- IR-cold against ambient — the object appears as a *dark* shape against atmospheric / land-sea / sky background.
- Distinct from radar-only or passive RF-only contacts.
- Counter-intuitive for any propulsion-bearing vehicle: a cold-IR object is in apparent thermal equilibrium (or sub-equilibrium) with ambient, which is hard to reconcile with sustained powered flight.
- Possible mundane explanations include: pre-imaged objects with very low emissivity surfaces, atmospheric optical effects, sensor calibration artifacts. None of these are zero-cost in the case files.
