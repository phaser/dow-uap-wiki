## Definition

A **contactee** is an individual claiming direct, often repeated, communication with extraterrestrial beings — typically through face-to-face encounters, telepathy, channeling, or automatic writing. The term is associated primarily with the **1950s–1960s contactee movement** (George Adamski, Truman Bethurum, George Hunt Williamson, Daniel Fry, Howard Menger, Orfeo Angelucci, George Van Tassel, Reinhold Schmidt, Sid Padrick, Mel Noel) and overlaps with the broader *channeled-message* tradition (see [[concepts/channeled-message]]). Contactees are documented in the FBI HQ file [[entities/62-hq-83894]] both as subjects of correspondence the Bureau received and as objects of investigative interest. The distinction between contactee and **abductee** (which became dominant in the 1970s onward, post-Hill abduction) is roughly: contactees describe willing, often benevolent contact and explicit messages; abductees describe involuntary contact and frequently traumatic experiences.

## Characteristics

- Claims include face-to-face meetings, ride-alongs, philosophical / spiritual / political messages "for humanity," and recurring contact across years.
- Frequently overlap with esoteric / Theosophical traditions (Williamson, Van Tassel, King).
- The Bob Renaud / Korendor case (May 1966 onward, in **Serial 449 + Section 10** of the FBI file) is a representative late-period channeling-style contactee record preserved in primary U.S. government archives.

## Documented Examples

- **Bob Renaud / Korendor** channeled messages, May 1966 — see [[summaries/62-hq-83894-serial-449]] and [[summaries/62-hq-83894-section-10]]
- **George Adamski**, **Truman Bethurum**, **George Hunt Williamson**, **George Van Tassel**, **Daniel Fry**, **Howard Menger** — named correspondents in the FBI HQ file
- **Mrs. Frances Swan** and **Adm. Herbert B. Knowles** — channeled-message correspondents named in the file
