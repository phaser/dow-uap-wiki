## Definition

A discrete reporting register found in U.S. embassy cables in which UAP / UFO subject matter is treated with deliberate rhetorical hedging — sometimes literal ("rhetorical 'UFO'" framings, e.g. Ambassador Vershbow's 2001 Moscow-to-Washington cable on Russian and Georgian overflights), sometimes ironical, sometimes anthropological (foreign-press treatment, NGO activity). Diplomatic UAP rhetoric is the institutional opposite of the operational AAARO Misrep template: it foregrounds the foreign actor's framing rather than committing the cable author to a phenomenological claim.

## Characteristics

- Quoted use of "UFO" as a foreign-actor's term rather than the cable author's.
- Anthropological observation of foreign UFOlogist NGOs (Turkmenistan 2004), foreign-government hearings (Mexico 2023), foreign media (Russia 2001), foreign commercial-aviation reports (Tajik Air 1994, Air Niugini 1985).
- Reluctance to make positive identification — cables typically *report that someone reported something* rather than *report that something happened*.
- Even where the underlying event has high-credibility witnesses (commercial pilots, embassy personnel), the cable register tends to filter through the foreign-actor's framing.
