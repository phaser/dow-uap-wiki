## Definition

An **electro-optical (EO) sensor** is a camera operating in the visible (and often near-IR) band whose output is digitised for tracking, recording, or live operator viewing. In the UAP record, EO sensors appear as airborne FLIR/EO balls (e.g., on F/A-18s, MQ-9s, U-2s, RC-135s) and as ground-mounted EO trackers / cinetheodolites at FBI / DoD installations. EO sensors are distinct from radar, MAWS (missile-approach warning), SIGINT, or SAR — and they carry their own characteristic artifacts: rolling-shutter distortion, optical-defocus shape change, glare/lens flares, and reticle/HUD overlays.

## Characteristics

- Typically grayscale at output (color is usually post-processed or omitted in operational feeds).
- Two configurations common: **wide-field-of-view (WFOV)** for search and **narrow-field-of-view (NFOV) / zoom** for ID. The FBI sequence (see [[entities/fbi-tracking-camera-sequence-19991231]]) shows both.
- Subject to atmospheric obscuration (cloud, dust, haze) — the [[summaries/dow-uap-d3-mission-report-arabian-gulf-2020]] case explicitly notes cloud cover preventing visual track.
- EO imagery is the most common single source of public-released UAP "video" — Nimitz / GoFast / Gimbal style.
