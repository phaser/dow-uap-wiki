## Definition

The **Extraterrestrial Hypothesis (ETH)** is the proposition that some unidentified flying objects / UAPs are craft of non-terrestrial origin. The phrase entered popular usage later, but the *substance* of the hypothesis appears in U.S. military files at least as early as **1948**, in the Markham letter preserved in the [[entities/project-sign]] incident-summary corpus — making this one of the earliest documented instances of the ETH being seriously raised inside a U.S. Army intelligence file.

## Characteristics

- Markham (Norman G. Markham, U.S. Army, 1948) explicitly catalogues a series of 1947–1948 incidents (Boise, Ticonderoga, Phantom Reef, Cape Mendocino, Oakland, Mantell, Maury Island) and proposes that the pattern is consistent with reconnaissance by non-terrestrial intelligence.
- The hypothesis is held in tension with the **meteorite hypothesis** (advanced by Dr. Lincoln La Paz, [[entities/lapaz-lincoln]], for fireball cases like the Norcatur, KS event of 18 February 1948) and the **conventional misidentification hypothesis** (Project Grudge's later operational stance).
- ETH is **not** an institutional position of [[entities/project-sign]] / [[entities/project-grudge]] / Project Blue Book — those programs leaned toward conventional / atmospheric / psychological explanations. ETH appears in the files as an idea entertained by individual officers and witnesses, then logged.

## Documented Examples

- Markham letter, 1948 — references Boise object (24 June 1947), Ticonderoga discs (12 Nov 1947), Phantom Reef (24 Nov 1947), Cape Mendocino blimp (1 July 1947), Oakland "Saturn-like" object (13 Oct 1947), [[entities/mantell-incident]] (7 Jan 1948), and the Maury Island / Davidson-Brown B-25 crash narrative (June 1947) — see [[summaries/army-incident-summaries-1-100]]
