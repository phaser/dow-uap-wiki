## Definition

"Flying disc" was the dominant descriptor used in U.S. military, FBI, and press accounts during the 1947 wave and through the [[entities/project-sign]] / [[entities/project-grudge]] era. It encompassed silvery, aluminum, white, or grey objects with a circular, oval, or saucer profile observed performing aerial maneuvers — usually high altitude, high speed, no audible sound, no exhaust trail — that could not be identified as conventional aircraft.

## Characteristics

From [[summaries/army-incident-summaries-1-100]] sample:
- Estimated diameter 5–25 ft (a few cases up to 50 ft)
- Color: silver / aluminum / "milk-white" / shiny
- Speed estimates 200–600 mph; many cases noted as **faster than known aircraft**
- Altitude 4 000–30 000 ft typical
- No exhaust trail, no audible sound
- Frequent reports of tight circles, instantaneous 90° heading changes, and oscillating "forward whirling" motion
- Often paired or in groups (2–15 objects)

## Documented Examples

- **Muroc AAF cluster, 7-8 July 1947** — Strapp, McHenry, Wise, Shoop sightings of silver discs / "unconventional shape" objects (see [[summaries/army-incident-summaries-1-100]])
- **Portland Police Wave, 4 July 1947** — McDowell, Patterson, Lissy, Cross — multiple disc-shaped objects performing 90° turns (see [[summaries/army-incident-summaries-1-100]])
