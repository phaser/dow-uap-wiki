## Definition

"Flying disc" was the dominant descriptor used in U.S. military, FBI, and press accounts during the 1947 wave and through the [[entities/project-sign]] / [[entities/project-grudge]] era. It encompassed silvery, aluminum, white, or grey objects with a circular, oval, or saucer profile observed performing aerial maneuvers — usually high altitude, high speed, no audible sound, no exhaust trail — that could not be identified as conventional aircraft.

## Characteristics

From [[summaries/army-incident-summaries-1-100]] sample:
- Estimated diameter 5–25 ft (a few cases up to 50 ft)
- Color: silver / aluminum / "milk-white" / shiny
- Speed estimates 200–600 mph; many cases noted as **faster than known aircraft**
- Altitude 4 000–30 000 ft typical
- No exhaust trail, no audible sound
- Frequent reports of tight circles, instantaneous 90° heading changes, and oscillating "forward whirling" motion
- Often paired or in groups (2–15 objects)

## Documented Examples

- **Muroc AAF cluster, 7-8 July 1947** — Strapp, McHenry, Wise, Shoop sightings of silver discs / "unconventional shape" objects (see [[summaries/army-incident-summaries-1-100]])
- **Portland Police Wave, 4 July 1947** — McDowell, Patterson, Lissy, Cross — multiple disc-shaped objects performing 90° turns (see [[summaries/army-incident-summaries-1-100]])

## U-2 / OXCART as Source of "Flying Disc" Reports (1956–1968)

The U-2 program generated a large volume of "flying disc" type reports that match the classic 1947 descriptions — **silver, high-altitude, no sound, slow apparent motion** — but for a completely different reason. The CIA's 1992 internal history documents this directly ([[summaries/cia-uap-003-cia-overhead-reconnaissance-u2-oxcart]]):

- U-2s flew above 60,000 ft when commercial airliners were at 10,000–20,000 ft and military aircraft below 40,000 ft.
- **At twilight**, a U-2 at 60,000 ft still caught direct sunlight while the airliner below was in darkness; the U-2's silver wings reflected sunlight "and appear to the airliner pilot, 40,000 feet below, to be fiery objects."
- **In daylight**, the silver fuselage produced glints visible from the ground at 60,000 ft against an empty sky — appearing as stationary or slowly-moving silver discs.
- Since "no one believed manned flight was possible above 60,000 feet" in the mid-1950s, these sightings were reported as genuine unknowns. BLUE BOOK investigators could check U-2 flight logs and identify the source, but could not tell the public. U-2 and OXCART flights "accounted for more than one-half of all UFO reports during the late 1950s and most of the 1960s."

This does not explain the 1947 wave (which predates the U-2), but it means a significant fraction of "flying disc" reports filed after mid-1956 that match the silver-disc pattern are attributable to U-2/OXCART. This must be kept in mind when assessing the continuity or evolution of "flying disc" characteristics across the 1947–1969 period — the 1956–1968 segment is heavily contaminated by a known classified program.
