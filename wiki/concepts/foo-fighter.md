## Definition

"Foo fighter" was the WW2-era Allied aircrew term for unidentified luminous balls or spheres reported pacing aircraft over Europe and the Pacific in 1944–1945. The term predates [[concepts/flying-disc]] and the modern [[concepts/uap]] vocabulary by roughly two years. Reports typically described glowing orange, red, or white spheres (sometimes pairs or formations) that paralleled bombers and fighters at high altitude, occasionally appearing to respond to evasive maneuvers. Period investigators considered candidates including German secret weapons (Wasserfall, Henschel SD-1400, V-weapons), St. Elmo's fire / electrical phenomena, and natural ball lightning.

## Characteristics

- Spherical / orb morphology (rarely disc-shaped — the disc descriptor came in 1947)
- Glowing / luminous, often orange or red
- Paced military aircraft for minutes at a time
- No hostile action recorded
- Sometimes seen in pairs or small formations
- Altitude band roughly 8 000–25 000 ft
