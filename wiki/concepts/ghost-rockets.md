## Definition

"**Ghost rockets**" is the Allied / Swedish term for a wave of unidentified rocket-like objects observed primarily over **Sweden** in the summer of 1946 — slightly before the [[syntheses/1947-flying-disc-wave]]. Witnesses reported elongated, fast-moving objects sometimes leaving a trail; the events prompted Swedish Defense Staff investigations and U.S./UK intelligence coordination. The contemporary explanation favored by Allied intelligence was that the objects were Soviet test launches of captured German V-2 / V-1 hardware — a thesis preserved (with some scepticism) in the [[entities/cometa-report]] historical appendix. Some events involved reported water impacts in Swedish lakes; physical recovery efforts produced no confirmed material.

## Characteristics

- Elongated / rocket-like, occasionally with visible exhaust
- Reported speeds higher than known late-WW2 V-weapons in level flight
- Seen primarily over Sweden, Finland, Norway in summer 1946
- Some reports include water impacts and crater-forming events

## Documented Examples

- The **1948 Swedish lake-crash + uncharted crater** (USAFE TT-1524 Item 14 to Gen. [[entities/cabell-charles-p]]) — see [[summaries/army-intelligence-collection-1948-1955]]. Possibly a late ghost-rocket event or an early flying-disc-era physical-trace case.
