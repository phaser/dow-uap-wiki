## Definition

The U.S. government gravity research programs were a series of classified and semi-classified research initiatives from the 1950s through 1960s aimed at understanding and controlling gravity for propulsion purposes. According to the Australian Department of Defence's 1971 assessment, these programs were directly motivated by intelligence collection on UFO performance characteristics and represented "an intensive onslaught on the gravity enigma" that was "entirely irrational from the standpoint of conventional science" — rational only "within the context of a firm belief that UFOs were real and that the intelligences behind them know how to control gravity."

## Characteristics

- **Motivation**: Intelligence on UFO "performance and propulsion methods" collected during 1948-1952 by "the government agency (almost certainly CIA)" presumably influenced U.S. government funding of advanced propulsion projects. The drive to "harness this power before the USSR could do so would be a strong incentive."
- **Scale**: By 1966, 46 separate projects receiving financial support, 33 under USAF supervision. Participating institutions included:
  - **Institute for Advanced Study** (Princeton, NJ) — J. Robert Oppenheimer, Freeman Dyson
  - **Princeton University** — John A. Wheeler
  - **University of Indiana** — Vaclev Hlavaty (worked with Einstein in Prague)
  - **Purdue University Research Foundation**
  - **University of North Carolina**
  - **MIT / Babson Gravity Research Institute** (Roger Babson, founded 1949) — George M. Rideout, President; 485 essays written on the subject by 1955, $10,800 in awards for original contributions
- **Key scientists involved**: Edward Teller (UC), J. Robert Oppenheimer (IAS), Freeman Dyson (IAS), John A. Wheeler (Princeton), Vaclev Hlavaty (Indiana), Stanley Deser
- **Industry involvement**:
  - **Glenn L. Martin Aviation Co.** — contracted Dr. B. Heim (Göttingen) and Dr. P. Jordan (Hamburg)
  - **Convair** (San Diego)
  - **Bell Aircraft** (Buffalo)
  - **Sikorsky Division**
  - **Lear Inc.** (Santa Monica) — William P. Lear, chairman, publicly stated in February 1955 that "because of flying saucers, serious efforts are being made to prove the existence of anti-gravitational forces"
  - **Clarke Electronics** (Palm Springs, CA)
  - **Sperry Gyroscope Division** (Great Neck, NY)
- **Theoretical basis**: Einstein's Unified Field Theory (incomplete at time of his death). "Current theories on UFO propulsion were a mixture of gravity control and electromagnetic propulsion."
- **Gravitational waves**: Work by J. Weber and associates under USAF Cambridge Research Laboratory jurisdiction was reported fairly extensively since 1966.
- **Avro Canada saucer project**: Directly linked to the gravity/UFO propulsion research. Drawing released October 1955 showed "a typical flying disc as described in many UFO reports." U.S. assumed funding when Canada withdrew in 1955, even preventing Canadian officials from inspecting the premises.

## Documented Examples

- **Avro Canada VZ-9 Avrocar**: Circular VTOL aircraft funded by U.S. Army and USAF, publicly presented as a utility vehicle but whose design specifications (hover, 1,500 mph, rapid direction change) matched UFO performance characteristics
- **Babson Gravity Research Institute**: Private foundation offering prizes for gravity research since 1949
- **1955 British recruitment**: "Because insufficient staff could be recruited for the project, recourse was made to an urgent appeal for theoretical physicists and mathematicians from Atomic Harwell, UK"
- **1954 statements**: General Nathan Twining, USAF Chief of Staff, stated "the best brains in the Air Force are working on this problem [UFOs]" — but the Australian assessment notes he was NOT referring to Project Blue Book
