## Definition

**Green fireballs** are a distinct subclass of luminous aerial events observed primarily over **New Mexico** in late 1948 through 1949 — bright green, slow-moving (slower than meteors), often horizontal-trajectory, soundless, occasionally observed by hundreds of witnesses simultaneously. The cluster centered on atomic-energy installations (Los Alamos, Sandia / Kirtland, Hanford WA via secondary cluster) and prompted the dedicated [[entities/project-twinkle]] investigation. Dr. Lincoln La Paz ([[entities/lapaz-lincoln]]) argued the events did *not* fit the natural-meteor profile; the case is one of the strongest historical examples of [[concepts/meteorite-vs-craft-ambiguity]].

## Characteristics

- **Color**: vivid green (distinct from typical bolide oranges/yellows)
- **Speed**: slower than typical meteors, sometimes reported as horizontal flight rather than ballistic
- **Sound**: silent
- **Site clustering**: concentrated over nuclear-weapons installations
- **Duration**: seconds to a few seconds — long enough for multi-witness corroboration but too short for cinetheodolite cueing
- Distinct from the modern AARO **"orb"** descriptor used in the DoW UAP set
