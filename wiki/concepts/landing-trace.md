## Definition

A **landing trace** is a set of physical disturbances — depressed earth, scorched vegetation, fused soil, residual radiation, geometric burn / crush patterns — left at a location where a UFO/UAP is reported to have made contact with the ground. Landing traces are among the strongest "[[concepts/physical-trace-case]]" categories of evidence in the UFO record because they admit instrumental measurement (photography, soil chemistry, geometry) independent of witness testimony.

## Characteristics

- Typical features: depressed earth indentations in geometric patterns (often four equally-spaced supports), scorched / withered vegetation, occasional residual ionising radiation, sometimes fused-sand "slag" residue at the contact points.
- Investigators measure the geometry to constrain object weight (from depression depth and ground compaction).
- Most landing-trace cases are isolated single-witness events, but Socorro / Zamora is a multi-investigator documented case.
