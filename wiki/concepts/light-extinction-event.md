## Definition

A **light-extinction event** is a UAP observation in which a beam of visible or NVG-visible light terminates abruptly in mid-air — i.e., the beam appears to be *truncated* at a point in space rather than continuing to natural diffuse-out distance. The descriptor enters the modern UAP record via the 2025 Western U.S. helicopter event ([[entities/western-us-helicopter-orb-2025]]) where a "Transparent Kite" object exhibited NVG-partial transparency and apparent light-beam truncation.

## Characteristics

- Beam terminates at a discrete spatial point rather than fading.
- May indicate (a) a partially-transparent absorbing/reflecting object, (b) a sensor/optical artifact specific to NVG bandpass, or (c) something more exotic.
- A high-information observation because it admits geometric reconstruction — the truncation point can be triangulated from multiple witness positions.
