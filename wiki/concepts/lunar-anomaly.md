## Definition

A **lunar anomaly** is an unexplained luminous, kinematic, or structural feature observed at, on, or near the Moon — typically by Apollo or Skylab astronauts. The category covers transient lunar phenomena (TLPs — flashes on the lunar surface), structures or shadows resembling artifacts in lunar imagery, and bright optical sources in the cislunar volume (such as the "possible laser" Apollo 11 reported from lunar orbit, later self-revised to a sun-glint reflection from a terrestrial lake).

## Characteristics

- **Surface flash events** — Schmitt's report from [[entities/apollo-17]] of a flash near the crater Grimaldi, observed in real time from lunar orbit (consistent with later TLP literature and confirmed independent of his post-flight technical debrief).
- **Cislunar bright sources** — Aldrin's Apollo 11 "possible laser" from lunar orbit, where Earth came up above the lunar horizon. Crew revised this post-flight to a likely Sun-glint reflection from a smooth terrestrial lake; *"I still think it's an unusual phenomenon, at that distance, to see so bright a source of light."*
- **Optical illusions at the lunar limb** — Armstrong's "image of Mars" from a dark portion of the lunar limb during Apollo 11, attributed by him to refraction at the horizon.
- Distinct from [[concepts/light-flashes-cosmic-rays]] (which are intra-cabin retinal events) and [[concepts/orbital-uap]] (which covers free-space objects observed from orbit).
