## Definition

The practice — and effective primary disposition criterion — used by [[entities/project-sign]] of treating a case as "Confirmed" when it was supported by two or more independent witnesses (or by independent radar / instrument tracks).

## Characteristics

- Project SIGN dispositions explicitly include "Confirmed by other sources" as a category alongside "Insufficient data" and "No further action."
- Sign multi-witness clusters were assigned a parent case number plus letter suffixes (e.g., Incident 48 + 48a, 48b, 48c, 48d) to keep the corroborating accounts grouped.
- The sub-letter scheme **does not always indicate same-event witnesses** — in some cases it groups separate but topically related reports filed near-simultaneously by the same investigator.
- The criterion descends through Project Grudge, Project Blue Book, and into modern UAP triage — though modern AARO Mission Reports (see [[concepts/incident-checklist-template]]) lean more on sensor cross-cueing (radar + EO + RF) than on visual multi-witness alone.

## Documented Examples

- Incidents 48 / 48a / 48b / 48c / 48d at [[entities/clinton-county-aaf]], 7 January 1948 (the Mantell-evening cluster) — see [[summaries/army-incident-summaries-1-100]]
- Cross-references like *"See also 48, 48a, 48b, 48c, 48d & 33, 33a–33f and Incidents 30 and 32"* in the case files
