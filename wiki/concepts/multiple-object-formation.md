## Definition

In the modern AARO Mission Report (Misrep) framework, **multiple-object formation** is a distinct sub-class of UAP event in which two or more unidentified objects appear in apparent geometric or temporal coordination within the host sensor's field of view. The Misrep template carries explicit fields for object count and inter-object geometry. The category overlaps with [[concepts/formation-flight]] (a behavioral descriptor of any era) but is named with this specific phrase in modern release documents.

## Characteristics

- Logged with timestamps per object (e.g., the D3 Arabian Gulf 2020 incident: 1 UAP at 17:36:22Z, 2 UAPs side-by-side at 17:36:30Z, 1 UAP at 17:36:49Z).
- Counted into the mission summary even when individual objects cannot be positively tracked beyond their initial detection (e.g., due to cloud cover).
- Reduces but does not eliminate the probability of birds, drones, balloons — multiple coincident detections at the same sensor are usually explainable, but coordinated motion is not.

## Documented Examples

- **Arabian Gulf, 2020-xx-xx, ~1736Z** — 4 UAPs across a 27-second window, including a side-by-side pair — see [[summaries/dow-uap-d3-mission-report-arabian-gulf-2020]]
