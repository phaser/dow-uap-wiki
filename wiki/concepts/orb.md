## Definition

**Orb** (sometimes "sphere," "round object," or "bouncy ball" in DoW UAP filings) is the dominant single-shape descriptor in the modern AARO release set, particularly for the Arabian Gulf, Persian Gulf, Gulf of Aden, and Strait of Hormuz clusters. The term denotes a **spherical, generally featureless** target observed by airborne EO/IR or radar — distinct from disc, triangle, cigar, or sphere-with-tail. Orbs are the modern descendant of the WW2 [[concepts/foo-fighter]] tradition, descriptively if not necessarily phenomenologically.

## Characteristics

- Roughly spherical / circular cross-section in EO/IR imagery
- Often featureless surface (no observable structure, fins, lights)
- Variable thermal signature — most commonly white-hot, but a non-trivial sub-class is [[concepts/cold-thermal-signature]] (IR-cold)
- Sizes estimated from object-to-platform ranging vary: small (1–2 m) to medium (~5 m); larger sizes reported but rare
- Frequently observed singly, but multi-orb formations occur (see [[concepts/multiple-object-formation]])

## Documented Examples

- **Gulf of Aden, September 2020** — 172 ATKS MQ-9 round IR-cold orbs with abrupt directional changes; see [[summaries/dow-uap-d57-mission-report-gulf-of-aden-september-2020]]
- **Arabian Sea, October 2020** — range-fouler IR-cold orb; see [[summaries/dow-uap-d44-range-fouler-arabian-sea-october-2020]]
