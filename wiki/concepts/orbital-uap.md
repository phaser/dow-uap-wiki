## Definition

**Orbital UAP** are unidentified objects observed from a crewed spacecraft against the background of space — distinct from atmospheric UAP and from intra-cabin [[concepts/light-flashes-cosmic-rays]]. The category covers translunar/transearth objects (Apollo), free-space objects pacing or near a station (Skylab), and reentry-region anomalies. NASA released the relevant Apollo / Skylab debriefings as part of its UAP-disclosure document set despite many of the events being plausibly identifiable as spacecraft debris, S-IVB / SLA panel residue, or sensor / optical artefacts — capturing the crews' contemporaneous uncertainty.

## Characteristics

- Typical witness chain: trained astronaut(s) using sextant, monocular, or naked eye against the dark cosmic background.
- The "limit-of-resolution" problem dominates: without independent range information, size and distance are unconstrained. Apollo 11 crew explicitly: *"there was no way to tell the size without knowing the range or the range without knowing the size."*
- Optical-defocus artifacts complicate identification — the Apollo 11 "hollow cylinder / open suitcase" object switched morphologies depending on sextant focus.
- Many events are now plausibly attributed to known spacecraft debris (S-IVB, SLA panels, jettisoned LM gear, water dumps, urine dumps) — but the contemporaneous record preserves the crews' uncertainty.

## Documented Examples

- **Apollo 11 "hollow cylinder / two rings / open suitcase / L-shape" object** observed ~1 day before lunar arrival (1969). See [[summaries/nasa-uap-d4-apollo-11-technical-crew-debriefing-1969]]
- **Apollo 17 post-S-IVB-sep tumbling debris field** and a **"two-flash-per-rev" rotating object visible 10–12 Earth diameters out** — Cernan requested NOUN-20 hack for tracking. See [[summaries/nasa-uap-d2-apollo-17-transcript-1972]]
- **Skylab 3 "red satellite"** — bright reddish rotating object pacing the station for ~10 minutes, ~30–50 nmi away, brighter than Jupiter, 10-second rotation period; never identified despite explicit crew ID request. See [[summaries/nasa-uap-d7-skylab-technical-crew-debriefing-1973]]
- **Skylab 4 tumbling flashing lights** — "two or three" objects moving relative to the station, also unidentified. Same source.
