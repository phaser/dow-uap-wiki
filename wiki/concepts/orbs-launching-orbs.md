## Definition

"**Orbs launching orbs**" is a descriptor coined by federal LE witnesses (USPER1–USPER6) during the 2025 [[entities/western-us-helicopter-orb-2025]] event for sequential flare-up formations in which a primary [[concepts/orb]] appears to release or generate a series of smaller orbs at high speed. The descriptor was repeated **5+ times** by independent witnesses in the slide deck and supporting USPER statement, suggesting either a real distinct phenomenon or — at minimum — a shared visual percept across multiple trained observers.

## Characteristics

- Multi-orb sequential appearance in close formation, sometimes described as a "string" or "row" of bright points.
- Witnessed at NVG-relevant illumination conditions; some observations describe partial transparency of host orbs.
- Distinct from the simpler [[concepts/multiple-object-formation]] descriptor: orbs-launching-orbs implies a parent-child generative relationship, not just spatial co-presence.
- Possible mundane analogies: aerial-decoy-launch profiles (e.g., MJU flare dispenser), drone-swarm release from a mothership platform, atmospheric optical effects (mirage / lens flare). None comfortably fits the witness chain.
