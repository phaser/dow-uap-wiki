## Definition

A **paired object** is a UAP target consisting of two close-spaced sub-features that may or may not be physically separate. In the FBI 1999-12-31 sequence the zoom (NFOV) frames resolve the target as twin dark blobs that sometimes appear as separate points and sometimes fuse into one elongated smear — characteristic of two co-orbiting / co-flying objects, or of a single bilobed object, or (less likely) of a single point with a sensor / atmospheric-distortion artifact.

## Characteristics

- Persistence across many frames argues against sensor noise or bad-pixel artifact.
- Variable separation (separated → fused → separated) suggests changing relative aspect angle of two real objects with a shared trajectory rather than a fixed structure.
- May indicate close formation flight (see [[concepts/formation-flight]]) or a parent-child release (deployment).

- FBI tracking-camera sequence, 1999-12-31, NFOV/zoom frames (b13–b18, b22–b24) — see [[summaries/fbi-photo-b13]]
