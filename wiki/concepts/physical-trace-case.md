## Definition

A **physical-trace case** is any UFO/UAP report supported by tangible physical evidence — landing impressions, scorched vegetation, recovered slag/material, lubricant or chemical residues, vehicle interference, photographic plates with anomalous exposure, or radar-paint anomalies recorded on tape. Physical-trace cases are the strongest evidence category in the post-witness-testimony hierarchy because they admit independent instrumental analysis.

## Characteristics

- Sub-types include [[concepts/landing-trace]], [[concepts/slag-residue]], [[concepts/disc-photograph]], [[concepts/vehicle-interference-case]], and [[concepts/radar-paint-anomaly]].
- Inherent complication: the chain of custody for physical evidence is fragile (the [[entities/maury-island-affair]] is the foundational example — the Maury Island slag samples were lost when the CIC officers' B-25 crashed at Kelso, WA on 1 August 1947).
- Many physical-trace cases reach institutional disposition via mainstream chemical analysis showing the residue is consistent with industrial slag, fused soil from natural fire, etc. — but a small subset resist conventional analysis.
