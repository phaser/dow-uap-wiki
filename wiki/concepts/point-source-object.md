## Definition

A **point-source object** is an aerial target whose angular extent is at or near the diffraction/resolution limit of the observing sensor — i.e., it is "a dot." Point-source objects are intrinsically hard to identify because shape, attitude, and structural features cannot be discerned from a single observation. Identification depends on motion, persistence, and cross-cued data (radar return, ranging, multi-frame trajectory).

## Characteristics

- Single-pixel or sub-pixel detection in EO/IR imagery.
- Distinguishable from genuine sensor noise only across multiple frames or with independent corroborating data.
- A point source can resolve into structure under zoom — the FBI 1999-12-31 sequence shows this transition: WFOV frames show a single dark point, NFOV/zoom frames show the same target as a [[concepts/paired-object]] (twin dark blobs).
- In the modern UAP record, "point source" descriptors usually accompany IR / radar-only contacts.
