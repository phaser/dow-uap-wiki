## Definition

The cluster of bureaucratic, scientific, legal, and diplomatic considerations attendant on a hypothetical authoritative confirmation of contact with non-human intelligence. Policy-on-first-contact is conceptually distinct from the [[concepts/extraterrestrial-hypothesis]] (a question of fact) and from operational UAP investigation (a question of evidence). It is a *governance* question: who decides, who is informed, in what order, and with what public and international communication.

## Characteristics

- Typical components: scientific verification, classification & need-to-know, presidential/executive notification, congressional notification, allied-government coordination, public communication, international (UN-level) coordination, commercial-market continuity considerations.
- The earliest serious U.S.-government memo on first-contact policy in this corpus is the **Maxwell W. Hunter II 1963 NASC memo** to State; see [[entities/hunter-policy-memo-1963]].
- The 1999 [[entities/cometa-report]] (France) makes parallel recommendations from a non-U.S. perspective, including specific recommendations for French and EU institutional posture.
- The modern [[entities/aaro]] charter implicitly addresses first-contact components without naming them explicitly — rules for evidence handling, congressional reporting, and de-classification.
