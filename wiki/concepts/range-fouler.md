## Definition

**Range fouler** is U.S. military aviation jargon for an unidentified or uncleared aircraft, vessel, or object that intrudes on an active U.S. military training/test range, forcing a halt or deconfliction of the planned exercise. Within the modern UAP record (DoW/AARO disclosure stream) the term is used for incursions where the intruder cannot be identified as a known civilian aircraft, vessel, or drone — making it a UAP under standard definitions. The term predates the UAP-disclosure era and originally covered any non-cooperative range intrusion (errant boats in a naval gunnery box, civilian aircraft penetrating a MOA, etc.).

## Characteristics

- Incident occurs in a defined range/operating area (e.g., a U.S. Navy carrier strike group operating area in the Arabian Sea, an Air Force MOA, a JOPES exercise box).
- Intrusion is detected by participating aircraft sensors (radar, FLIR/EOIR, MAWS) or by surface-platform radars; visual confirmation may follow.
- Object behavior is often inconsistent with cooperative, regulated traffic: no IFF / Mode-S squawk, no flight plan, unannounced presence.
- The post-event "**range-fouler debrief**" is a formal aircrew/command write-up — kinematic data, sensor screen-grabs, witness statements — distinct from a Misrep / mission report.
- In the AARO release set, range-fouler debriefs cluster around the **Arabian Sea / Persian Gulf / Strait of Hormuz / Middle East** and **Western Pacific (Japan)** in 2020-2023.
