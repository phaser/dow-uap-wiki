## Definition

**Redaction** is the act of excising specific text or imagery prior to public release of an originally classified document, leaving black bars, white-outs, or equivalent markers in place of the redacted content. Across the UAP record, redactions principally cover (1) sources & methods (sensor type, callsigns, units, tail numbers, operator identity), (2) location / geometry (coordinates, basing, takeoff/landing points), (3) personally identifying information of witnesses, and (4) classified narrative content under FOIA exemption (b)(1) ("national defense").

## Characteristics

- FBI imagery: black rectangles over fixed positions in the frame chyron — site, sensor, az-el, range, operator.
- DoW Misreps / Mission Reports: paragraph-level redactions with marginal exemption codes (e.g., `(b)(1)1.4a` = national defense classified info).
- Project SIGN / 1948 records: stamped CONFIDENTIAL / RESTRICTED markings, occasionally hand-blacked; many original records were *originally* unclassified and required no redaction at release.
- Redaction patterns are themselves diagnostically useful — consistent overlay positions across the FBI photo set tell the analyst that the same template was applied to every frame, suggesting bulk release of one captured-event sequence.

## Documented Examples

- FBI tracking-camera sequence, 1999-12-31 — 7 redaction rectangles per frame consistently positioned; see [[summaries/fbi-photo-b1]]
- DoW UAP D3 Arabian Gulf, 2020 — full Narrative block redacted under (b)(1)1.4a; see [[summaries/dow-uap-d3-mission-report-arabian-gulf-2020]]
