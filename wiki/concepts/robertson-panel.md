## Definition

The Robertson Panel was a CIA-convened scientific advisory panel that met in Washington, D.C. from January 14-18, 1953, to evaluate the national security threat posed by Unidentified Flying Objects. The panel was chaired by physicist Dr. H.P. Robertson and included four other prominent scientists. The panel concluded that UFOs posed no direct physical threat to national security but identified "indirect threats" from the public reaction to UFO reports — communications overload, false alarms, and mass hysteria susceptibility. Its primary recommendation was a multi-agency "training and debunking" educational program to reduce public interest in UFOs. The panel's report and its implementation are widely viewed as the pivotal moment when U.S. government policy shifted from investigation to suppression of the UFO subject.

## Characteristics

- **Convened**: At the direction of DCI General Walter B. Smith, following recommendation of the Intelligence Advisory Committee (IAC) on December 4, 1952.
- **Membership** (5 voting scientists):
  - **Dr. H.P. Robertson** (Chairman) — California Institute of Technology, theoretical physicist
  - **Dr. Luis W. Alvarez** — University of California, Berkeley (later Nobel Prize in Physics, 1968)
  - **Dr. Lloyd V. Berkner** — Associated Universities, Inc., geophysicist
  - **Dr. Samuel A. Goudsmit** — Brookhaven National Laboratory, atomic physicist
  - **Dr. Thornton Page** — Johns Hopkins University, astronomer/astrophysicist
- **Associate members**: Dr. J. Allen Hynek (Ohio State, astronomy) and Mr. Frederick C. Durant (Arthur D. Little, rockets/guided missiles)
- **Evidence reviewed**: 75 best-documented sighting cases, ATIC/Blue Book status reports, Project STORK (Battelle) statistical data, Project TWINKLE results, Tremonton and Great Falls motion picture films, radar data, Soviet intelligence
- **Key conclusions**: (1) No direct physical threat to national security; (2) No evidence of foreign artifacts or extraterrestrial visitations; (3) Indirect dangers from communications clogging, false alarms, and mass psychology; (4) No "residuum of cases" indicating phenomena attributable to foreign technology; (5) No evidence requiring revision of current scientific concepts
- **Key recommendations**: (1) "Strip UFOs of special status and aura of mystery"; (2) Institute training and debunking educational program (1.5-2 year minimum); (3) Expand ATIC effort to support the program; (4) Use mass media to reduce public gullibility; (5) Monitor civilian UFO groups for potential subversive use; (6) Discontinue government-sponsored sky patrol
- **Implementation**: The report was transmitted to the Secretary of Defense, FCDA, National Security Resources Board, White House, and Psychological Strategy Board. ATIC personnel were reduced from ~10 to 1 airman by September 1953. JANAP 146 was issued prohibiting service personnel from discussing UFOs.

## Documented Examples

- **Tremonton, Utah film (July 2, 1952)**: Panel rejected Navy Photo Interpretation Lab's "self-luminous objects" conclusion, favored birds or balloons
- **Great Falls, Montana film (August 15, 1950)**: Panel believed bright lights were aircraft reflections
- **Washington D.C. radar-visuals (July 19-26, 1952)**: Impetus for panel's convening; recognized radar mutual interference as probable cause
- **Bellefontaine, Ohio (August 1, 1952)**: One of the detailed case studies
- **Port Huron, Michigan (July 29, 1952)**: Discussed case
- **Haneda AFB, Japan (August 5, 1952)**: Foreign theater case
- **Presque Isle, Maine (October 10, 1952)**: Northern border case
