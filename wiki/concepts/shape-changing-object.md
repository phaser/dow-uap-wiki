## Definition

A shape-changing object refers to UAP reports where the observed object cyclically changes its apparent geometry during a single sighting — typically from oblong/parallelogram to circular and back — without apparent change in direction, speed, or behavior. This phenomenon is distinct from objects reported in different shapes by different witnesses; here the same witness(es) report dynamic form changes in real time.

## Characteristics

- Shapes typically cycle between two or more apparent geometries (e.g., oblong → circular → oblong)
- Changes are smooth and gradual, unfolding over seconds to minutes
- The object's path, speed, and behavior do not change during shape transitions
- Witnesses often report the object "changing position" or "presenting different appearances" while maintaining its course
- In at least one documented case, the effect is described as an object "rolling vertically on its edge" — suggesting 3D orientation change rather than physical deformation

## Documented Examples

- **Columbus, Ohio (2 August 1948)** — [[summaries/dow-uap-d088-us-airforce-analysis-of-flying-objects-in-the-us-101-172]], Incident #154: Charles Saunders observed a 20-30 ft round object with grey-black perimeter and transparent center cycling through three shape phases (parallelogram → circular → parallelogram) over 10-15 minutes. Four sketches drawn. Same evening, Mrs. Saunders and neighbors independently observed the same shape changes.
- **Uniontown, Pennsylvania (29 June 1948)** — [[summaries/dow-uap-d088-us-airforce-analysis-of-flying-objects-in-the-us-101-172]], Incident #164: Three luminous oval objects observed "rolling vertically on their edges" like a "meat platter rolling along on its edge," appearing to spin. One object was transparent — lightning could be seen through it. Witnessed by a police sergeant, multiple civilians.
