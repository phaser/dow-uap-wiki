## Definition

**Skyhook balloons** were classified U.S. Navy / Office of Naval Research high-altitude polyethylene research balloons (1947 onward) used for cosmic-ray and atmospheric experiments. Reaching altitudes >100 000 ft and silvery-white at sun-glare angles, they are the conventional explanation invoked for several prominent early-wave UFO sightings — most famously the [[entities/mantell-incident]] of 7 January 1948. **Dr. Urner Liddel** of ONR briefed the FBI on this hypothesis; the briefing material is preserved in **Section 6 of the FBI HQ file [[entities/62-hq-83894]]**.

## Characteristics

- Polyethylene envelope, naturally translucent — appears as a bright stationary or slow-drifting point at extreme altitude
- Originally classified — a witness on the ground in 1947–1949 had no public way to identify a Skyhook as the source of an observation
- Drift rate dependent on jet-stream winds — can give the false impression of a controlled, slowly-traversing object
- Provides a real, mundane mechanism for the "very high, very bright, very slow" subset of early-wave reports — but does not account for the multi-witness 90°-turn / oscillation cases like Muroc Incidents 1–4
