## Definition

**Slag residue** is glassy / metallic material claimed to have been ejected, dropped, or left behind by a UFO. Slag analysis is a recurring chain-of-custody / chain-of-analysis challenge in early UFO cases — most famously in the [[entities/maury-island-affair]] of June 1947, where Harold Dahl reported one of the donut-shaped objects raining "molten material" onto his boat. The samples were collected by CIC officers Brown and Davidson, but their B-25 crashed near Kelso, WA on 1 August 1947 with the samples aboard — and the surviving residue was eventually classified as ordinary industrial smelter slag.

## Characteristics

- Typical analysis: chemical (XRF / mass-spec), microstructural (electron microscopy), isotopic.
- Most analysed slag samples in the published record are conventional metallurgical waste — high-Fe, high-Si, mixed-metal compositions consistent with ferrous-metals smelting.
- Atypical analyses (e.g., unusual isotope ratios, anomalous trace elements) make headlines but rarely survive independent replication.
- Modern AARO holds and reviews physical-trace specimens; results from that effort are not in this corpus.
