## Definition

The methodological challenge — pervasive in [[concepts/orbital-uap]] reports — of distinguishing genuine unidentified objects from spent rocket stages, jettisoned hardware, water/urine dump particulates, paint/Mylar fragments, or LM-jettison residue trailing the host spacecraft.

## Characteristics

- Cislunar geometry favors confusion: at long ranges, debris can have ambiguous shapes that switch under focus changes (Apollo 11's "hollow cylinder ↔ open book" through the sextant).
- Apollo crews explicitly considered S-IVB stages, SLA adapter panels, high-gain antenna fragments, Mylar, and water dumps as candidate identifications.
- The "limit of optical resolution" plus passive-thermal-control "barbecue roll" (PTC) conspire to make repeated independent observation difficult.
- Skylab crews experienced a milder form: known Earth-orbit debris populations interleave with the unidentified targets.

## Documented Examples

- **Mercury MA-6 / MA-7 "Glenn effect" luminous particles** — Yellow-green particles ("snowflakes") observed by John Glenn and Scott Carpenter traveling with the spacecraft; Carpenter proved capsule origin by hitting the wall and liberating more particles. Scientifically resolved as condensation/insulation debris. See [[summaries/nasa-uap-d015-astronaut-scientific-debriefings-1962-1963]]

- **Gemini 4 "fire flies"** — Small particles continuously around the spacecraft, visible even in daylight occasionally. Probability: same Glenn-effect condensation phenomenon. See [[summaries/nasa-uap-d017-preliminary-gemini-4-crew-debriefing-part2-1965]]

- **Gemini 5 "silver balls"** — Liquid oxygen and hydrogen vented from the spacecraft, seen as "old silver balls" floating by; vented at low sun angles created visible trails. "We were continually floating around in these old silver balls of either hydrogen or oxygen." See [[summaries/nasa-uap-d020-gemini-5-technical-debriefing-part2-1965]]

- Apollo 11 hollow-cylinder object, ~1 day from the Moon — crew speculated on S-IVB, SLA panel, LM Mylar debris, high-gain antenna. None definitively confirmed in debrief. See [[summaries/nasa-uap-d4-apollo-11-technical-crew-debriefing-1969]]
- Apollo 17 post-S-IVB-sep tumbling debris field — see [[summaries/nasa-uap-d2-apollo-17-transcript-1972]]
- Apollo 12 SLA panels and "moon pigeons" — debris from docking ring, electrical harness, and pyrotechnic events mistaken for UFOs; Lockheed "Unidentified Visual Phenomena" report (NAS 9-5191) confirmed routine sources. See [[summaries/usg-uap-d001-congress-whitehouse-ufo-correspondence-1998]]
- Gemini-11 Proton-3 satellite — NORAD tracking errors created apparent anomaly; orbit-decay extrapolation error produced "maneuvering" appearance. See [[summaries/usg-uap-d001-congress-whitehouse-ufo-correspondence-1998]]
- Gemini-12 discarded bags/ELSS — four objects "linked in a row" were life support equipment jettisoned during EVA. See [[summaries/usg-uap-d001-congress-whitehouse-ufo-correspondence-1998]]
- X-15 ice flakes — "fleets of UFOs at 30 feet" were ice shedding from LOX tanks (a "fireflies" phenomenon also seen on Mercury/Vostok). See [[summaries/usg-uap-d001-congress-whitehouse-ufo-correspondence-1998]]
- Skylab Brazilian airstrip — a long cylindrical object with paddle ends, initially investigated as a Soviet satellite, identified as a jungle airstrip at altitude zero. See [[summaries/usg-uap-d001-congress-whitehouse-ufo-correspondence-1998]]
