## Definition

**Tracking-camera imagery** in the UAP record refers to single frames or sequences captured by a slewed/cued optical or electro-optical sensor (e.g., FLIR, EO/IR ball, fixed cinetheodolite) tasked to follow a discrete target. Such imagery is distinct from incidental photography (snapshots) and from FMV/full-motion video sensor product. Tracking cameras typically encode metadata (date, time, site, sensor mode, azimuth, elevation, range, operator) as a chyron / overlay on each frame.

## Characteristics

- Centered crosshair reticle with angular tick marks (e.g., 5°, 10°, 15° off-axis); reticle scale changes between wide-field and zoom optical configurations.
- Burned-in timestamp in the frame corner.
- Edge overlays for site / sensor / az-el / range / operator — these are the most commonly redacted fields when imagery is released to the public.
- Sensor may be ground-mounted (tripod, vehicle) or airborne (gimballed pod). The presence of a stationary terrestrial horizon across frames is a tell for ground-mount.
