## Definition

**Transmedium** describes UAP that move between media — typically air ↔ water, or air ↔ space — without apparent loss of speed, control, or signature. The term entered formal U.S. government UAP discourse around 2021–2022 as part of the All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office's ([[entities/aaro]]) brief, and it is the principal reason "Aerial" was replaced with "Anomalous" in UAP. Transmedium events (objects observed entering or exiting water from the air, or transiting between atmospheric and orbital regimes) are flagged in modern Mission Reports as a high-priority signature.

## Characteristics

- Apparent traversal of the air-water interface without observed splash, deceleration, or hydrodynamic effects expected of conventional vehicles.
- Sometimes paired with absence of expected RF / IR / acoustic signatures.
- Frequently observed in U.S. Navy / Marine littoral environments — Arabian Sea, Persian Gulf, Strait of Hormuz, Eastern Mediterranean, Western Pacific — i.e., regions also dominant in the [[concepts/range-fouler]] record.
