## Definition

**UAP** stands for *Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena* (formerly *Unidentified Aerial Phenomena*; historically *UFO*, "Unidentified Flying Object"). The term is the U.S. government's preferred bureaucratic label for objects, lights, or signals detected by trained observers or sensors that cannot be immediately identified as known aircraft, weather phenomena, balloons, satellites, debris, or natural events. The 2022 expansion from "Aerial" to "Anomalous" was an explicit broadening to cover transmedium (water/space) objects and non-visual signatures (sensor-only contacts).

## Characteristics

- An observation, not a thing — a UAP is by definition an *unidentified* report; once explained (e.g., as a balloon or a Starlink train) it ceases to be a UAP.
- Recorded in standardized formats — the modern DoW/AARO Misrep template (see [[concepts/incident-checklist-template]]) descends from the [[entities/project-sign]] 26-field "CHECK-LIST – UNIDENTIFIED FLYING OBJECTS" form of 1948.
- Modern UAP reports decompose phenomena into: object count, shape, kinematics (speed, altitude, acceleration), signatures (RF, IR, visible), apparent intelligent control, propulsion, and witness platform.
- Witness chain: the observer is almost always a trained operator (pilot, sensor specialist, police officer, astronaut) — see [[concepts/witness-reliability-evaluation]].
