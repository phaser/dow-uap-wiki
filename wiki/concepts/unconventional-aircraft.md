## Definition

"**Unconventional aircraft**" is the deliberately-neutral designation introduced by **AFOIN Memorandum No. 4** of **15 February 1949**, signed by Maj. Gen. Charles P. Cabell ([[entities/cabell-charles-p]]) as USAF Director of Intelligence. The memorandum was the institutional Air Force response to the 1947–1948 wave: it directed continued reporting and analysis but reframed "flying disc / saucer" reports as observations of *possibly* foreign or *possibly* novel domestic aircraft, deliberately avoiding the [[concepts/extraterrestrial-hypothesis]] framing. The memo is preserved in full text in **Serial 164 of [[entities/62-hq-83894]]**.

## Characteristics

- The memo's vocabulary — "unconventional aircraft" — sits between the press's "flying saucer" and the eventual neutral acronym **UFO**.
- Functional purpose: keep the reporting pipeline open without making institutional commitments to any explanatory hypothesis.
- Lists candidate explanations: foreign (Soviet) advanced aircraft, domestic black-program aircraft, captured German technology (per the [[summaries/german-armament-equipment-1944-1945]] file), atmospheric / natural phenomena, hoax.
