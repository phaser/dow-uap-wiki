## Overview

The **415th Night Fighter Squadron** of the U.S. Army Air Forces, operating P-61 Black Widow night fighters over Western Europe in late 1944 and early 1945, is the unit most associated with the original [[concepts/foo-fighter]] reports. The squadron's intelligence summaries and sortie reports — collected by the **Supreme Headquarters Allied Expeditionary Force (SHAEF)** and routed to the British Air Ministry — form the core of the WW2 foo-fighter dossier, preserved in this corpus as part of the German Armament / Equipment file (`raw/331_120752_numeric_files_1944-1945_37153_german_armament_equipment_documents.pdf`). Howard P. Robertson — later of the 1953 [[entities/robertson-panel]] — reviewed the same material on the U.S. side.

## Role / Activity

- ~12 named foo-fighter encounters Dec 1944 – Jan 1945, including pacing-orange-balls cases at altitude over the German front.
- Air Ministry response treated the encounters as candidate Me-262 jet sightings or German anti-aircraft "flak rocket" projectiles — a deliberate institutional explanation that tracked official intelligence concerns about German jet/rocket programs without invoking unconventional aircraft.
- The squadron's reporting is the source documentation for the term "foo fighter" and the prototype [[concepts/incident-checklist-template]] later formalised in 1948.
