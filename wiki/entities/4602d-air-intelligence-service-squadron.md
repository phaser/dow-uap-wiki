## Overview

The 4602d Air Intelligence Service Squadron (AISS) was an Air Defense Command unit tasked in August 1954 with conducting field investigations of UFO reports. Under AF Reg. 200-2 (dated 12 August 1954, establishing the UFOB reporting program), the 4602d AISS was deployed to arrive "on the spot" within a very short time after a sighting report was received.

## Role / Activity

- **Field investigation**: Replaced the previous decentralized reporting system. The squadron had "sufficient units" deployed to respond rapidly to sightings anywhere in the U.S.
- **Information pipeline**: After investigation, all information was forwarded to ATIC at Wright-Patterson AFB for final evaluation.
- **Statistical impact**: According to Blue Book Special Report No. 14, the introduction of 4602d AISS field investigations coincided with a dramatic reduction in unidentified cases:
  - Pre-1953: ~20% unknown
  - 1953-1954: 9% unknown
  - Jan-May 1955 (after 4602d AISS deployment): 3% unknown
  - Insufficient information cases also dropped to 7%
- Whether improved investigation genuinely resolved unknowns, or merely reclassified them, is a matter of debate. The rapid on-the-spot presence may have allowed investigators to find conventional explanations that would have been lost to time, or it may have enabled more aggressive debunking.
