## Overview

The 4602nd Air Intelligence Service Squadron (AISS) was a USAF unit that assumed the primary UFO investigation function from Project Blue Book in September 1953. Unlike Blue Book, which was a research/analysis organization, the 4602nd was a field intelligence unit "trained both for rapid access to remote areas and to interrogate enemy personnel." The transfer of investigation authority from Blue Book to the 4602nd was a key element of the Robertson Panel's policy implementation — moving the investigative function from a public-facing analytical team to a covert intelligence-gathering unit.

## Role / Activity

- **Investigation role (September 1953 onward)**: Under AFR 200-2, the 4602nd AISS was authorized to conduct UFO field investigations, replacing the ATIC/Blue Book investigation function. The unit was "dispersed over Area Commands and trained both for rapid access to remote areas and to interrogate enemy personnel."
- **Reporting chain**: Unlike Blue Book reports, which went to ATIC for analysis, 4602nd AISS reports went to Air Defense Command and USAF Intelligence in Washington — bypassing Blue Book entirely. "Evaluating the threat is the first concern, and as a result reports are sent to USAF Headquarters in Washington, original reports first going to Air Defense Command and forwarded immediately to interested investigative agencies as well as addressed to both these headquarters and to ATIC."
- **Public information policy**: The public was only to be informed of the "results of individual cases in that the sighting is positively identified." Headquarters USAF was to release "summaries of evaluated data" — but only identified cases.
- **Transfer context**: This transfer followed the Robertson Panel's recommendation to reduce Blue Book to a public relations function while intensifying covert intelligence collection. Blue Book staff was reduced from ~10 qualified personnel to one airman (first-class) by September 1953 — the same month the 4602nd took over investigations.
