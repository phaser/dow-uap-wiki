## Overview

**62-HQ-83894** is the FBI Headquarters file number for the Bureau's flying-disc / UFO general investigation. It is one of the most-cited single FBI files in the UAP literature, containing the famous **Guy Hottel memo** (the so-called "Roswell-area" report mentioning recovered saucers and bodies) along with thousands of pages of correspondence with [[entities/j-edgar-hoover]] and field-office reports from across the United States. The file is released in 10 sections plus six numbered serials (130, 164, 220, 403, 438, 449) and a "Sub A" packet — totaling ~1.5 GB of scanned PDFs in this corpus.

## Role / Activity

- HQ-level coordination point for FBI flying-disc investigations during the 1947 wave and through the late 1960s (the file extends well past the formal Project Blue Book era).
- Contains witness statements, field-office reports, press clippings, and correspondence with cooperating agencies (Air Force AMC / Project SIGN at Wright-Patterson, etc.).
- Heavy redaction throughout — confidential informants, witnesses' addresses, FBI sources & methods.

### Confirmed contents from the released sections / serials (sampled)

- **[[entities/maury-island-affair]] (21 June 1947)** — full CIC investigation report by Lt. Col. Donald L. Springer (Section 3), including the **Kelso, WA B-25 #1316 crash** of 1 August 1947 in which CIC investigators Brown and Davidson died with Maury Island samples aboard.
- **William Rhodes Phoenix photographs (7 July 1947)** — Section 2, with negative-handover documentation. See [[entities/rhodes-phoenix-photographs]].
- **Cabell AFOIN Memo No. 4, "Unconventional Aircraft" (15 February 1949)** — full text in Serial 164. See [[entities/cabell-charles-p]] and [[concepts/unconventional-aircraft]].
- **[[entities/mantell-incident]] (7 Jan 1948)** — referenced via Dr. Urner Liddel's Skyhook-balloon explanation in Section 6. See also [[concepts/skyhook-balloon]].
- **Chiles-Whitted Eastern Airlines sighting (July 1948 AL)** — Section 4.
- **[[concepts/green-fireball]] wave** at Los Alamos (Dec 1948 – Jan 1949) — Sections 4 and 6. See also [[entities/project-twinkle]].
- **[[entities/socorro-zamora-1964]] / Lonnie Zamora landing (24 April 1964)** — full FBI on-scene LHM in Serial 438.
- **Nash-Fortenberry Pan American sighting (14 July 1952 VA)** — Sub A.
- **Capt. Peter Killian American Airlines sighting (24 Feb 1959)** — Sub A.
- **Carl Jung's APRO statement (29 July 1958)** — Sub A.
- **Bob Renaud / Korendor channeled-message correspondence (May 1966)** — Serial 449 + Section 10. See [[concepts/contactee]] and [[concepts/channeled-message]].
- **Gray Barker's *They Knew Too Much* dust jacket (1956)** — Serial 403; foundational Men-in-Black literature. See [[concepts/men-in-black]].

### Notable absence in sampled spans

- The famous **Guy Hottel memo (22 March 1950)** was *not* found in the sampled portions of Sections 5 or 6. It likely sits in unread middle pages — a targeted full-section OCR pass on Sections 5–6 would be needed to confirm.
