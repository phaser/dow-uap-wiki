## Overview

The **All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office (AARO)** is the U.S. Department of Defense's UAP-focused investigative office, established in 2022. AARO succeeded prior offices including UAPTF (UAP Task Force) and AOIMSG, and it consolidates UAP-relevant reporting from the services, COCOMs, and IC agencies. Within the document corpus catalogued here, AARO is the receiving / aggregating authority for the **DoW UAP** disclosure release set (`dow-uap-d<N>`), which includes Mission Reports (Misreps), range-fouler debriefs, and email correspondence.

## Role / Activity

- Receives UAP Misreps from MAJCOMs (e.g., AFCENT) and COCOMs (CENTCOM, INDOPACOM, EUCOM).
- Adjudicates UAP fields: physical state, propulsion signature, intelligent control, advanced capabilities/materials.
- Publishes annual reports to Congress and curates the public-facing disclosure document set indexed by D-numbers (D1–D75 etc.) in the source filenames.
- The 2022 expansion of "UAP" from "Unidentified Aerial Phenomena" to "Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena" — covering [[concepts/transmedium]] and orbital domains — was driven by AARO's all-domain remit.
