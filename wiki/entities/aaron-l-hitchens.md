## Overview

Aaron L. Hitchens was a Chemical Engineer in charge of the Chemical and Physical Laboratories at Winchester Repeating Arms Company, New Haven, Connecticut. He is known for a credible flying-saucer sighting reported to the FBI in November 1950.

## Characteristics

- Chemical Engineer, 15 years of experience in research and development
- Not a pilot but had done considerable flying and reading on technical data concerning flying saucers
- Described as "very reliable and sincere individual" with "considerable technical knowledge and experience"
- Requested no public announcement or comment be given to his report

## Role / Activity

On October 20, 1950, at approximately 6:15 PM, Hitchens and his wife and daughter observed a sphere-like object with a steady golden-orange glow directly overhead from their backyard at 495 Edgewood Avenue, New Haven. The object was estimated at 10–20,000 ft altitude, approximately 10× Venus diameter, traveling west toward New York City then veering south (possibly over Long Island). No sound, no attachments or projections, "a complete circular ball." Hitchens calculated speed at 400–700 mph. Wife and daughter corroborated. Neighbors arrived too late. The object left no trail and did not diminish in apparent size as it traveled away.
