## Overview

Admiral Roscoe Henry Hillenkoetter (May 8, 1897 – June 18, 1982) served as the Director of Central Intelligence and the first Director of the CIA from its establishment in 1947 until October 1950. After his retirement, he publicly stated that the U.S. Government knew UFOs were extraterrestrial but was withholding this fact from the public. His post-retirement position, as a former DCI, made his statements among the most authoritative and troubling for the official debunking policy.

## Role / Activity

- **First CIA Director (1947-1950)**: Headed the CIA during the critical early period of the UFO phenomenon — from Kenneth Arnold's June 1947 sighting through the establishment of Project SIGN, the Mantell incident, and Project GRUDGE. The National Security Act of 1947 creating the CIA was signed on July 26, 1947 — during the first "flying disc" wave.
- **CIA-Directorate period**: While Hillenkoetter was DCI, the CIA was described by the Australian DoD assessment as the "almost certainly" covert agency that "had been collecting data on UFO performance and propulsion methods" and "had rocket, nuclear, and intelligence experts" studying UFO reports for "design data on interplanetary spaceships." This would have occurred under his tenure and/or his immediate successors.
- **Post-retirement advocacy**: According to the Australian assessment, after retiring, Hillenkoetter "publicly stated that the U.S. Government knew UFOs were extraterrestrial but was withholding this fact from the public." He served on the Board of Directors of NICAP (National Investigations Committee on Aerial Phenomena) alongside other retired military and intelligence officials.
- **NICAP significance**: Along with Hillenkoetter, other NICAP directors included Admiral D.S. Fahrney (directed Navy guided missile program), Major Donald Keyhoe, Major Dewey Fournet (Blue Book Project Officer), Rear Admiral H.B. Knowles, General A. Wedemeyer, and Col. J.J. Bryan (special assistant to Secretary of the Air Force) — constituting a remarkable concentration of retired defense/intelligence leaders publicly contradicting official policy.
