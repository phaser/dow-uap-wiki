## Overview

Al (Alfred) Hixenbourg was a staff photographer for the *Louisville Times* who claimed to have photographed a flying saucer on film in June 1950. The film was later debunked by fellow photographers.

## Characteristics

- Staff photographer for the *Louisville Times*
- Used a 16mm magazine-loading movie camera
- Previous history of faking a "flying disk" photograph (approximately two years prior to the 1950 incident)

## Role / Activity

On June 27, 1950, at approximately 4:15 PM near his home at 2205 Longest Avenue, Louisville, Kentucky, Hixenbourg claimed to have photographed a large disk while on his way to shoot birds. He stated the object appeared "motionless for about ten seconds" then began to get smaller and vanished into the west. The film was carried on a national television network and received national comment from Walter Winchell.

Courier-Journal photographers Steinau and Davis debunked the film: a friend reported Hixenbourg had previously faked a flying disk photo using matches across the camera lens. Steinau noted the "saucer" appeared in sharp detail while the DC-3 plane in the background was out of focus, consistent with a projection or optical trick rather than a distant object.
