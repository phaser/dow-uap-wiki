## Overview

Albert K. Bender was a Bridgeport, Connecticut individual who founded the International Flying Saucer Bureau (IFSB) and served as its President and Editor. He published the IFSB house organ *Space Review*.

## Role / Activity

Bender appointed Robert D. Wolf (Director of Civil Defense for Johnson County IN) as a Franklin IN representative in October 1952. Wolf later wrote to J. Edgar Hoover asking whether the IFSB had been "cleared" or was "subversive." Hoover's reply (January 27, 1953) noted the FBI was "strictly a fact-finding agency" and could not evaluate organizations; internal FBI files contained "No references" on the IFSB, *Space Review*, or Bender.

Of historical note: Bender will dissolve the IFSB in 1953 after his "three men in black" experience, the foundational moment for the Men-in-Black mythos.
