## Overview

**Apollo 11** (16–24 July 1969) was the first crewed lunar landing mission. Crew: Neil A. Armstrong (CDR), Edwin "Buzz" Aldrin Jr. (LMP), Michael Collins (CMP). The mission's UAP-relevant content is concentrated in the post-flight Technical Crew Debriefing (31 July 1969), originally classified CONFIDENTIAL with a 12-year automatic-declassification rider.

## Role / Activity

Three discrete UAP-relevant observations were captured in the Apollo 11 debriefing:

1. **The "hollow cylinder / two rings / open suitcase / L-shape" object**, observed ~1 day before lunar arrival during PTC ("barbecue roll"). Aldrin: *"You could see this thing tumbling and, when it came around end-on, you could look right down in its guts. It was a hollow cylinder. But then you could change the focus on the sextant and it would be replaced by this open-book shape. It was really weird."* Crew speculated on S-IVB, SLA panel, LM Mylar debris, high-gain antenna fragments — none confirmed in the debrief.
2. **Aldrin's intra-cabin light flashes** — single point flashes and double flashes "separated by maybe a foot," interpreted as particle penetrations; Armstrong counted ~90 events in ~1 hour of focused observation. This is the first crew description of what would become the [[concepts/light-flashes-cosmic-rays]] phenomenon, leading to the ALFMED experiment on Apollo 16/17.
3. **"Possible laser" / lake-reflection bright Earth-side light source** observed during lunar orbit; revised post-flight to a probable Sun-glint reflection from a smooth terrestrial lake.
