## Overview

**Apollo 12** (14–24 November 1969) was the second crewed lunar landing. Crew: Pete Conrad (CDR), Alan Bean (LMP), Richard Gordon (CMP). Apollo-12 is arguably the most UFO-lore-encrusted Apollo mission — subject to extensive claims of a "UFO escort" to and from the Moon, cemented by a 1970 Saga magazine article and promoted by [[entities/apro|APRO]] research director Dr. James Harder. The NASA UAP disclosure transcript (D1) is thin; the more consequential material is the 1976-1977 NASA analysis ([[summaries/usg-uap-d001-congress-whitehouse-ufo-correspondence-1998]]) which systematically dismantles the Apollo-12 UFO claims.

The key contested event: Conrad radioed to Earth "hey, gang, we're being followed" — a joke about tumbling SLA (Spacecraft Lunar Adapter) panels, amplified into a UFO story. The "object suddenly taking off at high velocity" was a misreading of Conrad referring to SLA panel motion during initial separation 33 hours earlier. The "bright red object flashing brilliantly" during return was identified by Rusty Schweickart as the Moon's reflection on the Indian Ocean. The "moon pigeons" — debris objects seen on TV during lunar operations — were analyzed by Lockheed (NASA Contract NAS 9-5191) and determined to be docking channel fragments, docking ring seals, and electrical harness pieces from routine pyrotechnic events. All three crewmen explicitly denied seeing UFOs: Conrad, Gordon, and Bean.

## Role / Activity

- Released as part of the NASA UAP disclosure document set (D1).
- No dramatic UAP event recorded in the transcript itself — its inclusion in disclosure appears precautionary / completionist.
- The more significant Apollo-12 UAP record is the post-hoc controversy: the 1970 Saga magazine article, APRO's transcript analysis (Sparks), Harder's 1975 UFO symposium claims, and the Lockheed "moon pigeons" report — all documented and critiqued in [[summaries/usg-uap-d001-congress-whitehouse-ufo-correspondence-1998]].
