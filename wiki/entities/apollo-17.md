## Overview

**Apollo 17** (7–19 December 1972) was the final crewed lunar landing. Crew: Eugene Cernan (CDR), Harrison "Jack" Schmitt (LMP, geologist), Ronald Evans (CMP). CapCom: Bob Parker. The Apollo 17 release comprises three documents in this corpus: a transcript (D2), a science debriefing (D5 — UV astronomy, no UAP content), and a technical crew debriefing (D6).

## Role / Activity

UAP-relevant content from Apollo 17:

- **Bright tumbling debris field** observed after S-IVB separation.
- **"Two-flash-per-rev" rotating object** visible at ~10–12 Earth diameters out — Cernan requested a NOUN-20 hack for tracking. (NOUN-20 was the AGC routine for sextant target acquisition; the request to repurpose it to track this unidentified object is itself notable.)
- **Cosmic-ray "headlight between my eyes"** flashes — Cernan's vivid description of the [[concepts/light-flashes-cosmic-rays]] phenomenon.
- **Schmitt's lunar-surface flash near Grimaldi** — a real-time report from lunar orbit of a flash on the lunar surface, confirmed in his post-flight technical debrief. This is one of the better-documented [[concepts/lunar-anomaly]] events in the Apollo record.
- **Evans's reentry "tunnel with bright spot"** — reentry-region optical event.
