## Overview

The Aerial Phenomena Research Organization (APRO) was a civilian UFO research group active during the 1960s and 1970s. Its research director was Dr. James Harder, a professor of engineering. APRO was one of the leading organizations promoting the idea that NASA astronauts had witnessed and photographed UFOs during space missions, particularly during Gemini-11 and Apollo-12.

## Role / Activity

- **Research Director Dr. James Harder** was the primary public face of APRO's astronaut-UFO advocacy. Harder claimed at a 1975 UFO symposium at UC Santa Cruz that a UFO "followed Apollo 12 on three orbits around the moon" and that "NASA suppressed the UFO incidents for fear of panic."
- **Gemini-11 Proton-3 case**: APRO and Harder considered this "the best Gemini UFO ever seen" — despite NORAD tracking corrections and astronaut Conrad's later confirmation that the object was the Proton-3 satellite.
- **Apollo-12 UFO claims**: APRO member Brad Sparks analyzed the Apollo-12 transcript and argued Conrad saw a maneuvering spacecraft, based on a misreading of the CAPCOM-astronaut exchange about SLA panel separation timing.
- **Saga magazine involvement**: APRO and its members contributed to the May 1970 Saga magazine article "Apollo 12's Mysterious Encounter with Flying Saucers" by T.G. Beckley and H. Salkin, which the NASA analysis describes as heavily distorted.
- **Methodology critiques**: The NASA analysis ([[summaries/usg-uap-d001-congress-whitehouse-ufo-correspondence-1998]]) accuses APRO of selective quotation, deliberate punctuation alteration of transcripts, and ignoring astronaut denials. APRO members are described as falling back on "secret reports" and "private information" when their published claims were refuted.
