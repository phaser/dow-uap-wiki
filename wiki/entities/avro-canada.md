## Overview

Avro Canada was the aircraft manufacturing company that developed what became known as the "Avrocar" — a circular VTOL aircraft whose design was directly influenced by UFO reports. The project began in 1951-1952, and when the Canadian government withdrew financial backing in 1954-1955, the U.S. government assumed funding and imposed high security restrictions, even preventing Canadian officials from inspecting the premises. The design released publicly in October 1955 "showed a typical flying disc as described in many UFO reports."

## Role / Activity

- **Project origins**: Started by Avro Canada in 1951-1952. The design was circular with jet engines, intended to take off and land vertically, hover, travel at up to 1,500 mph, and change direction rapidly.
- **U.S. takeover**: When Canada withdrew backing (1954-1955), the U.S. assumed funding "on grounds of security" and "even prevented Canadian officials from inspecting the premises." The Secretary of the Air Force, Donald Quarles, "appeared moderately confident that such a vehicle would be successfully developed by the U.S."
- **UFO connection**: The Australian DoD assessment explicitly states that the design specifications for the Avro saucer came from intelligence collection on UFO performance: "the government agency (almost certainly CIA) that had been collecting data on UFO performance and propulsion methods during 1948-52 presumably influenced U.S. governmental funding of certain advanced projects. One project was the Canadian Avro saucer." The design was "clearly directly related to reporting" — meaning the specifications were reverse-engineered from UFO sighting characteristics.
- **Press release (October 25, 1955)**: At the press release for Blue Book Special Report No. 14, photographs or a drawing of the proposed version showed "a typical flying disc."
- **Outcome**: The VZ-9 Avrocar was built and tested but never achieved its design specifications — it suffered from stability problems and could not fly faster than ~35 mph or higher than ~3-4 feet in ground effect. The project was cancelled in 1961.
