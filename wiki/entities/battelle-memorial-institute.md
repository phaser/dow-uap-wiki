## Overview

Battelle Memorial Institute was the research organization contracted by the USAF under Project STORK to perform statistical analysis of UFO sighting reports. Their work produced Project Blue Book Special Report No. 14 — the most comprehensive statistical analysis of UFO data ever conducted. Battelle's findings, however, contradicted the official USAF public position and were subsequently suppressed.

## Role / Activity

- **Project STORK contract**: Worked under contract to ATIC/Blue Book to transfer sighting data onto IBM punch cards and perform systematic statistical analysis. The analysis covered 3,200 reports out of approximately 4,000 received by the end of 1952.
- **Blue Book Special Report No. 14**: Produced the 116-page core report that was used to brief the Robertson Panel in January 1953. Key findings:
  - Unknowns INCREASED with sighting reliability: 16.6% for poor reliability, 33.3% for excellent
  - Excellent reliability cases had the LOWEST "insufficient information" rate (1.2%)
  - Statistical test showed probability that unknown and known object populations were the same was less than 1 in 10^28
  - After removing "arbitrarily" assigned astronomical identifications (green fireballs, short-duration night sightings), odds reduced to 1 trillion trillion to one
- **Suppression of findings**: The report's actual body, showing mathematically that the evidence favored a scientifically unknown explanation, was not released to the public. Only ~100 copies were distributed on a restricted basis. A misleading "summary" was released "concentrating on 1953-1955 reporting designed to reduce the residual unknowns to an insignificant number."
- **Analysts' "irrational" conclusion**: According to the Australian DoD assessment, Battelle's analysts "could not find a way to reduce these odds sufficiently further to warrant additional testing, and irrationally considered the results to be 'inconclusive'" — a remarkable characterization suggesting the analysts knew their results pointed toward something anomalous but chose not to pursue it.
