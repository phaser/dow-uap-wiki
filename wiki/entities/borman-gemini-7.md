## Overview

During the **Gemini 7** mission in December 1965, astronaut **Frank Borman** (with crewmate **Jim Lovell**) reported to Houston Mission Control: **"Bogey at 10 o'clock high."** The transcript exchange is preserved in the **T-763 series** transcripts (`255_t_763_r1b_transcripts.pdf`) and is one of the more famous astronaut UAP-style sightings of the early space program — distinct from the Apollo / Skylab events because Gemini 7 was an Earth-orbital long-duration test mission, not a lunar / station-keeping flight.

## Role / Activity

- The exchange was a real-time intra-flight call from Borman; ground response acknowledged but did not produce a definitive identification.
- Apocryphal popular-press accounts later inflated the event into a structured-craft observation; the actual transcript is a single-pass kinematic call without elaboration.
- The event is contextually a [[concepts/spacecraft-debris-confusion]] candidate: the Gemini 6A / Gemini 7 rendezvous (the first crewed-vehicle rendezvous) generated significant nearby debris, and a "bogey" call could plausibly have been Gemini 6A itself or jettisoned hardware.
