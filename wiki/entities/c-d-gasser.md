## Overview

Colonel C. D. Gasser was the Engineering Officer for the U.S. Air Force Material Command at the NEPA (North East Project Area) site at Oak Ridge, Tennessee. He played a central role in the March 1950 investigation of Stuart Adcock's radar detections over Oak Ridge.

## Role / Activity

Gasser investigated Adcock's claims by visiting his home and examining his radar equipment. He found Adcock to be "in a much inebriated condition" and his equipment "haphazard at best," yet stated that flight at 100,000 ft was "absolutely possible from a scientific and engineering standpoint." He arranged for Captain Robert Cross (radar expert) to observe with Adcock.

After Adcock failed to be found at his residence on March 5, Gasser concluded Adcock's reliability was "extremely dubious." Gasser then wrote critical letters to his commanding officer at Wright Field regarding the handling of the Adcock incident, noting that multiple agencies had been instructed to act only as "observers" with no authority to act, resulting in procedural gaps.
