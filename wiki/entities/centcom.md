## Overview

**U.S. Central Command (CENTCOM)** is the unified COCOM responsible for U.S. military operations across the Middle East, Central Asia, and parts of South Asia (AOR). The DoW UAP release set is geographically dominated by CENTCOM-AOR incidents — Arabian Gulf, Persian Gulf, Strait of Hormuz, Gulf of Aden, Iraq, Syria, Iran, Djibouti, UAE — and by the related air component, [[entities/afcent]].

## Role / Activity

- COCOM of record for the majority of post-2016 UAP Mission Reports in this corpus.
- 2020 was a particularly active year in the CENTCOM AOR: multiple range-fouler debriefs and Misreps cluster in the Arabian Gulf / Strait of Hormuz / Persian Gulf in the period July–November 2020.

## Documented in Sources

- Implied in [[summaries/dow-uap-d3-mission-report-arabian-gulf-2020]] (AFCENT MAJCOM) and most other dow-uap-d* mission reports tagged middle-east / arabian-gulf / strait-of-hormuz / iraq / syria.
