## Overview

A **February 2022 UAP incident** at Fort Carson, Colorado Springs, in which five U.S. Army soldiers — including one Army intelligence officer — observed a motionless, silent, white/creamy oval object hovering over a saddle of Cheyenne Mountain for 2–5 minutes before vanishing instantaneously. The case is notable for **multi-witness corroboration with independent drawings**, the unusual **articulating surface-panel morphology**, and the fact that AARO was aware of the incident but never conducted an in-person interview until the FBI did so in 2024–2025.

## Role / Activity

- Five soldiers from the same unit witnessed the object over Cheyenne Mountain while walking toward a building at Fort Carson
- Two separate FBI interviews (FD-302 in March 2025, FD-1057 forensic sketch in July 2024) from different witnesses provide corroborating accounts with different descriptive vocabularies
- Both witnesses describe: **white/creamy/opalescent** color, **translucent/shimmering** quality, **horizontal oval/potato/bean shape**, distinct surface pattern (polygon ridges / fish-scale panels), **motionless hover**, and **instantaneous disappearance** when not directly observed
- Object was not drifting in wind (maintained fixed position relative to mountain saddle)
- Witness D001: object covered in "intersecting lines or ridges forming an abstract polygon pattern"
- Witness D002: object made of "articulating fish scales or panels" that shifted in slow waves from different origin points simultaneously
- D002 witness explicitly used the term "**cloaked**" — disappeared in a head-turn with no shadow
- All five soldiers independently drew the object afterward; drawings were consistent
- Incident was reported to the U.S. Army
- AARO contacted one witness in early 2024 but conducted no interview
- FBI interviews occurred in 2024 (forensic sketch) and 2025 (FD-302); forensic sketch produced
- **AARO assessment (ICA-UAP-D001)**: Proposed [[concepts/backscattering|backscattering]] of sunlight off snow-covered ground illuminating low-level clouds as a possible natural explanation, but holds **low confidence** in this assessment. Cross-referenced AFWA and Weather Underground data, sun-angle calculation.
