## Overview

The **Chiles-Whitted Encounter** (Project SIGN Incident #144, 24 July 1948) is one of the most significant and thoroughly documented cases in the early USAF UAP record. Eastern Air Lines pilots **Clarence Shipe Chiles** (Captain) and **John B. Whitted** (Co-Pilot) observed a cigar-shaped object approximately 100 ft long at close range — including structural details — while flying DC-3 Flight 576 near Montgomery, Alabama. The case features multiple independent corroborations, including a passenger and two other pilot sightings the same night.

## Role / Activity

The encounter occurred at 0245 hours, 24 July 1948, 20 miles southwest of Montgomery, Alabama. Chiles and Whitted were cruising at 5,000 ft under a clear moonlit night. A jet/rocket-like object approached from their right and slightly above:

- **Shape/size**: Cigar-shaped, approximately 100 ft long, about twice the diameter of a B-29 fuselage (roughly 3:1 ratio)
- **Internal structure**: **Two rows of windows** indicating an upper and lower deck, with a "very bright light" glowing from inside. Windows appeared to be square, approximately 1-2 ft per side
- **Underside**: A **blue fluorescent glow** running beneath the fuselage
- **Propulsion**: A **red-orange flame approximately 50 ft long** shot from the rear — "some jet or other type of power shooting flames from the rear"
- **Speed**: Estimated 500-700 MPH ("much larger than any C-47 and about the speed of a jet")
- **Sound/exhaust**: No prop wash or rough air felt; no audible noise
- **Maneuver**: After passing, object "pulled up sharply into some light broken clouds" and was lost from view
- **Duration**: 5-10 seconds of observation

Chiles contacted the company operator at Columbus, GA to query whether any jet/rocket aircraft was reported in the area — confirmed negative by both Army and CAA.

### Corroborating Sightings Same Night

**Passenger C.L. McKelvie**: Seated in the 5th or 6th row from the front, observed a "sudden streak of light" moving in a straight line above the aircraft. "No physical shape — only a streak of flame." McKelvie noted that "the vibration of the plane was much greater than normal."

**Lewis Feldwary (Pilot, near Blackstone, VA) — Incident #144a**: At ~0230 hours, observed a jet or rocket trail on heading 230°, covering an 80°-90° arc laterally at "terrific speed." The ~15-minute time difference between this sighting (0230) and Chiles-Whitted (0245) matches a distance of approximately 400-475 miles at the reported speeds.

**Capt Perry R. Mansfield and C. Kingsley (between Blackstone, VA and Greensboro, NC) — Incident #144b**: At ~0230 hours observed an "unusual meteor" brighter than any ever seen before — traveling in a **horizontal** direction (not downward), slightly above the horizon, for 3 seconds before dying out. The horizontal trajectory was considered anomalous for a meteor.

### Independent Netherlands Sighting

Incident #168 (20 July 1948, The Hague, Netherlands): **A.D. Otter**, Chief Investigator of the Court of Damage, described an "aircraft with 2 decks and no wings" traveling at very high altitude at high speed comparable to V-2, seen 4 times intermittently through clouds. This description matches the Chiles-Whitted object — observed **4 days earlier** in Europe. Whether SIGN connected these two cases is unknown.
