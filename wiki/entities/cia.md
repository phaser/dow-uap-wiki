## Overview

The Central Intelligence Agency (CIA) was established by the National Security Act of 1947 — the same summer as Kenneth Arnold's sighting and the first "flying disc" wave. The CIA's involvement in the UFO problem operated on multiple levels: public oversight (OSI awareness of Blue Book), covert intelligence collection (gathering data on UFO performance and propulsion for reverse-engineering), policy direction (the Robertson Panel and subsequent debunking program), and foreign intelligence monitoring (Soviet UFO investigations, British committee activity, sightings in Soviet-bloc countries, and international incidents).

## Role / Activity

### Public/oversight role
- **Office of Scientific Intelligence (OSI)**: Assumed responsibility for the CIA's UFO project in May 1953, confined to "maintaining awareness" of other agencies' activities and file maintenance.
- **Physics and Electronics Division (P&E/SI)**: By December 1953, monitoring Blue Book/ATIC activities at a reduced level.
- **Intelligence Advisory Committee (IAC)**: The interagency body that recommended the convening of the Robertson Panel and received its final report.

### Covert intelligence collection
- According to the Australian DoD assessment, the CIA was the "almost certainly" secret agency that "had rocket, nuclear, and intelligence experts" studying UFO reports "in an effort to attain design data on interplanetary spaceships" (1948-1952).
- Collection of foreign intelligence on UFOs: Soviet scientific papers (Kardashev/Sakharov), Soviet-bloc sighting reports (Hungary 1955, Azerbaijan 1955, Sary Shagan 1973), British UFO committee activity (1952), Himalayan sightings (1968), Zimbabwe airport incident (2008).
- The CIA's intelligence on UFO "performance and propulsion methods" directly influenced U.S. funding of the Avro Canada saucer project and multiple gravity research programs.

### Policy direction
- **Robertson Panel (January 1953)**: Convened by OSI; produced the "training and debunking" policy that shaped U.S. government UFO posture for decades.
- **Post-Robertson implementation**: According to the Australian assessment, the CIA "in a report dated 16 February 1953 showed a preference to publicly abandon the investigation whilst intensifying the collection of data" — diverging from the Robertson Panel's recommendation to continue publicly. By September 1953, the CIA's position had been "largely achieved."
- **JANAP 146 (1953)**: Service personnel gag order, progressively tightened.

### Documented incidents in CIA UAP files
- Hungary 1955 wave (reported via civilian correspondence)
- Azerbaijan 1955 unconventional aircraft launch
- Zimbabwe 2008 Harare airport disc with beams
- British 1952 Yorkshire "perfect saucer" at RAF show
- Himalayan 1968 sightings with physical debris recovery
- Sary Shagan 1973 green circular UAP at Soviet missile range
- Leon Davidson Case 17708 — coordinated multi-agency response to persistent researcher
- German scientist flying disc article in Chilean magazine (1950)
- Soviet cosmic ray phosphene research (1976)
- Kardashev/Sakharov charged mass spacetime paper (1971)
