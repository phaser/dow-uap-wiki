## Overview

The **COMETA Report** — *"Les OVNI et la défense — à quoi devons-nous nous préparer?"* (UFOs and Defense: What Must We Be Prepared For?) — is a 1999 French study authored by **COMETA** (Comité d'Études Approfondies), a private association of retired senior French defense, military, and aerospace officials. The report is one of the most consequential foreign-government-adjacent UFO studies ever publicly released; it argues that some UAPs are best explained by the [[concepts/extraterrestrial-hypothesis]], catalogues a series of high-credibility French and international cases, and recommends a policy posture that anticipates the modern [[entities/aaro]] framework. The English-translation copy in this corpus is preceded by a 2001 cover letter from **Carol Rosin** to NASA.

## Role / Activity

- Published privately in 1999, distributed to French President Chirac and PM Jospin.
- Notable signers: André Lebeau (former CNES president), Denis Letty (Air Force general, COMETA chair), Jean-Jacques Vélasco (then-head of the SEPRA UFO investigation office at CNES).
- Documents the institutional French UAP infrastructure: **GEPAN** (1977–1988) → **SEPRA** (1988–2004) at CNES.
- Cites: Mirage IV intercept (Hervé Giraud, 7 March 1977), T-33 case (Claude Bosc, 3 March 1976), **Air France AF 3532** (Capt. Jean-Charles Duboc, 28 January 1994 — ~250 m craft), Lakenheath–Bentwaters (UK, 1956), RB-47 (USA, 1957), and Roswell (with explicit treatment of Project Mogul as cover).
