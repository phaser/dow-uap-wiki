## Overview

The **Condon Committee** was a USAF-funded UFO research project conducted at the University of Colorado from late 1966 through 1968, formally titled *Scientific Study of Unidentified Flying Objects* and directed by physicist **Dr. Edward U. Condon** (former Director of the National Bureau of Standards). The program was commissioned at an estimated **$300,000 over 18 months** under Air Force contract **F44620-76-C-0035** and was intended to provide an independent academic basis for the AF's eventual disposition of [[entities/project-blue-book|Project Blue Book]].

The final report — three volumes, 1,465 pages, 68 plates — was published in 1969 (NTIS AD 680:975 / 976 / 977) and reviewed by a panel of the **National Academy of Sciences** (NTIS AD 688:541, 6 pp.). The report's headline conclusion — that further scientific study of UFOs was not warranted — was the basis for **Project Blue Book's termination on 17 December 1969**.

## Role / Activity

- The commissioning of the project is documented in the **19 October 1966 Bureau memo** preserved in [[summaries/62-hq-83894-section-10]], which records Rep. **Gerald R. Ford**'s parallel push for a congressional UFO investigation and the AF's response of contracting the University of Colorado.
- **Project Co-ordinator Robert J. Low** was the operational manager. Low's January 1967 visit to Santa Ana CA to study the **Rex Heflin photographs** (reproduced in the same IGAP / "ufo contact" magazine enclosure as the [[entities/white-sands-march-1967|White Sands March 1967 sighting]]) is one example of the project's case-by-case re-evaluation work.
- Steckling's 20 March 1967 Pentagon visit (preserved in the same enclosure) recorded Lt. Col. George P. Freeman, Jr. directing him to "inform the UFO investigation committee in Colorado". That ad-hoc routing — Pentagon → University of Colorado — is illustrative of the project's intended one-stop role.
- Project termination corresponded with broader AF withdrawal from UFO investigation. The 1977 Air Force UFO Fact Sheet (preserved in [[summaries/62-hq-83894-section-10]]) treats the Condon report as the dispositive document.
- **Critique within the contactee community** is preserved in the IGAP magazine enclosure: contactee groups argued that the project's terms of reference were narrow and that follow-up film analyses (e.g., of Madeleine Rodeffer's footage) were insufficient. Whether the project actually examined the Steckling/Rodeffer films is not established in this corpus.
