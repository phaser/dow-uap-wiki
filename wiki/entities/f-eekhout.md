## Overview

F. Eekhout was a Dutch citizen from The Hague who visited the FBI office in Washington on May 5, 1952, to discuss "plans for a workable flying disc." He later wrote to Hoover from The Hague five months later.

## Role / Activity

Eekhout promised Hoover not to discuss the subject until he heard from the Bureau. Five months later (October 6, 1952), he wrote asking whether the silence agreement was still valid, noting the subject had "become active in Europe" and he wanted to "go ahead with it." Hoover replied (October 10, 1952) that the matter was not within FBI jurisdiction, was forwarded to the Air Force for evaluation, and that further contact would come from the interested government agency. Hoover noted no mention was made of Eekhout "keeping the facts secret pending a reply."
