## Overview

A 24-frame electro-optical tracking-camera sequence released by the [[entities/fbi]] under the file slugs `fbi-photo-b1` … `fbi-photo-b24`. The sequence's burned-in timestamps span **18:10:00 to 18:21:02 on 12/31/99** — though the date is suspect (almost certainly a Y2K-era default/reset on the camera firmware rather than the true capture date — flagged in every per-frame summary).

The sequence captures (a) at least one helicopter visible in frames b6 and b7 at 18:10:00–18:10:02, and (b) a small dark distant target tracked through both wide-field and zoom optical configurations, which under zoom resolves into a [[concepts/paired-object]] (twin dark blobs that periodically fuse and separate). All site / sensor / azimuth / elevation / range / operator metadata is redacted via consistent black-rectangle overlays at fixed positions per frame.

## Role / Activity

- The umbrella event for all 24 fbi-photo-bN summaries.
- Two distinct observation segments separated by ~6.5 minutes (18:12:20 → 18:18:53), suggesting either an operator stand-down or footage withheld from release.
- The sensor appears stationary — the same mountain ridgeline appears across many wide-field frames, suggesting a fixed tripod / vehicle-mounted camera rather than an airborne platform.
