## Overview

Frederick C. Durant III was a rocket and guided missile specialist with Arthur D. Little, Inc., and served as an associate member of the CIA's 1953 Robertson Panel. He was the author of the detailed meeting minutes ("Report of Meetings of Scientific Advisory Panel on Unidentified Flying Objects") that provide the most complete record of the panel's deliberations.

## Role / Activity

- **Associate panel member**: Not a voting member, but participated in all sessions and authored the comprehensive meeting minutes that form a substantial portion of the panel's released report.
- **Minutes author**: Durant's 19-page record of the four days of meetings (January 14-18, 1953) is the most detailed surviving account of the panel's discussions, including: (1) Specific cases reviewed; (2) Individual panel members' opinions and disagreements; (3) The exact reasoning behind each conclusion; (4) The detailed proposals for the educational program. His minutes show the panel was more nuanced than the final report suggests.
- **Expertise**: Rockets and guided missiles — relevant to the panel's assessment of whether UFOs could represent missile tests or advanced aircraft.
- **Background**: Served as a naval aviator during WWII. After the war, worked at Bell Aircraft and the Naval Air Rocket Test Station. At Arthur D. Little, he specialized in rockets and space flight. Later became a prominent advocate for space exploration and served as assistant director of the National Air and Space Museum (Smithsonian). He maintained a lifelong interest in UFOs while remaining skeptical.
