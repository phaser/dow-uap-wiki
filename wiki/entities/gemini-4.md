## Overview

Gemini 4 (GT-4) was the second manned Gemini mission, launched June 3, 1965 from Cape Kennedy. The crew was James A. McDivitt (Command Pilot) and Edward H. White II (Pilot). The 4-day, 62-revolution mission was the longest two-man spaceflight to date and included the first American extravehicular activity (EVA) by White. It also demonstrated the first U.S. orbital rendezvous attempt (station-keeping with the spent booster second stage).

## Role / Activity

- First American EVA — Ed White spent ~20 minutes outside the spacecraft using a hand-held maneuvering unit (ZIP gun), tethered by a 25-foot umbilical.
- Rendezvous station-keeping with the GLV second stage using flashing lights installed on the booster.
- Two crew debriefings were conducted: Part I (operational) aboard the USS Wasp on June 9, 1965, and Part II (systems, visual sightings, experiments) concluding at MSC Houston on June 12, 1965.
- The crew reported a notable visual phenomenon: an aurora-like "curtain" of parallel running lines of lights near Australia, observed twice during night passes.
