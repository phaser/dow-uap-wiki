## Overview

Gemini 9 (GT-9) was launched June 3, 1966, crewed by Thomas P. Stafford (Command Pilot) and Eugene A. Cernan (Pilot). The mission attempted rendezvous with an Agena Target Vehicle (failed — the Agena shroud did not separate) and later with the Augmented Target Docking Adapter (ATDA). An ambitious EVA with the Astronaut Maneuvering Unit (AMU) proved unsuccessful due to excessive workload in controlling body position without adequate handholds.

## Role / Activity

- Rendezvous with ATDA after Agena failure; docking not achieved due to shroud issue.
- Cernan's EVA was the most complex to date but was cut short — the AMU exercise was "too much work" under the spacecraft arrangements.
- Experiment debriefing (June 16, 1966) recorded by J.R. Gill documented: 40 usable airglow photographs (S001/S011), "the best pictures yet of the zodiacal light surpassing the results achieved on Gemini V," 300 color terrain/weather photos, micrometeorite impact analysis (9 impacts on a small portion analyzed), and a newly discovered glacial lake in the Andes.
- Hand-held photography during EVA deemed "out of the question" given current EVA body control difficulties.
- The Maurer camera was carried operationally for the first time alongside the Hasselblad.
