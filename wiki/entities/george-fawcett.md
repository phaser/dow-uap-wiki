## Overview

George Fawcett was a respected but controversial UFO researcher based in Maiden, North Carolina. He compiled what became known as the "Fawcett List" — a catalog of claimed astronaut UFO sightings from Mercury through Apollo — that was widely circulated in UFO literature. The NASA analysis (c. 1976–1977) devotes significant attention to critiquing Fawcett's claims.

## Role / Activity

- **The "Fawcett List"**: A compilation of astronaut sightings including Gemini-12 (four UFOs linked in a row), Mercury-7 ("classical saucer-shaped UFO with dome"), and Apollo-12 ("UFOs accompanied them all the way to within 132,000 miles of the moon"). The NASA analysis demonstrates that each entry was either a distortion of mundane events (discarded bags, tracking balloons, SLA panels) or outright fabrication.
- **Gemini-12 claim**: Fawcett claimed Lovell and Aldrin "saw four UFOs linked in a row." The actual GT-12 debriefing shows Lovell stating the objects "must have been these same things we tossed overboard" — the ELSS life support system and three bags.
- **Mercury-7 claim**: Fawcett claimed Carpenter photographed a "classical saucer-shaped UFO with dome that followed his capsule." In reality, the photo showed a limp tracking balloon — a scheduled exercise confirmed by flight schedule and voice transmissions.
- **Defense of claims**: Confronted with refutations (letter of May 10, 1976), Fawcett insisted "I sincerely believe some of the astronaut sightings are bonafide, based on some of my sources of information" — a pattern of retreating to "private information" when open claims collapsed.
