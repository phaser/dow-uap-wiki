## Overview

George Koehler was a Denver, Colorado individual who claimed to have observed flying saucers and to know others who had encountered them. He was active in early 1950 and gave lectures at Denver University.

## Characteristics

- Address: 315 Franklin St., Denver, CO; telephone Spruce 2563
- Claimed to have made "some previous claims" regarding flying saucer observations
- Denver OSI considered him a "probable mental case"
- Had an article published in *True Magazine* about flying saucers
- Visited by Don Shoemaker (author) in connection with his saucer claims

## Role / Activity

In January 1950, Koehler told an advertising agency employee (Armstrong) that he knew a prominent Denver oilman ("STLASAN WION," alias "Mysterious 'X'") and an official of Newton Oil Co. who had leased Mojave Desert land where a flying disc was found intact with three 3-foot-tall dead human-like occupants. Koehler claimed to have obtained a radio set as a "souvenir." He had been telling this story off-and-on for three months prior.

In March 1950, Koehler gave lectures at Denver University (via a known intermediary, GeorgexKoehler connected to radio station KMYR) claiming to have personally observed several saucers, one of which allegedly landed in New Mexico, with three-foot-tall human occupants. Denver OSI and press/wire services carried stories.
