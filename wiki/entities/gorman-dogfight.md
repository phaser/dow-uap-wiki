## Overview

The **Gorman Dogfight** (Project SIGN Incident #172, 1 October 1948) was a **27-minute aerial engagement** between an F-51 Mustang flown by Air National Guard pilot **George F. Gorman** and a small ball of clear white light over Fargo, North Dakota. It is one of the most intensively investigated cases in Project SIGN history, featuring four independent witnesses, a radiation (Geiger counter) check of the aircraft, and the pilot's assertion that "there was 'thought' behind the maneuvers."

## Role / Activity

At 2100 hours, 1 October 1948, Gorman was flying an F-51 in the vicinity of Fargo Air Field. After being advised of a Piper Cub in the area, Gorman sighted a **small round ball of clear white light, 6-8 inches in diameter**, with "no physical form or shape attached." He engaged the object for 27 minutes:

- **First contact**: Object traveling at ~250 MPH at 1,000 ft altitude, blinking on and off
- **Performance**: At higher speeds, the white light was continuous. Object traveled "much faster than F-51" (Gorman's speed varied from 300-400 MPH under full power)
- **Maneuvers**: Several left turns, one right turn, diving and climbing, head-on passes. Object performed both evasive and aggressive maneuvers
- **Closest pass**: Head-on pass at less than 500 ft — object appeared 6-8 inches in diameter, white with clear-cut edge, "apparently had depth" but seemed "flat"
- **180° turn and counter-pass**: Object initiated pass at Gorman
- **Vertical climb**: Gorman followed to 14,000 ft where he stalled; object was still 2,000 ft above him, circling
- **Final maneuver**: Object outclimbed Gorman, traveling **straight up** until lost from view
- **Pilot's conclusion**: "Gorman states positively that there was 'thought' behind the maneuvers"
- **Ruled-out explanations**: Other aircraft, meteorological balloons, and Canadian Vampire jets "have been discredited"
- **Novel investigation**: Geiger check (radiation survey) performed on the F-51 for comparison with unaffected aircraft — "technical studies have been initiated"

### Tower Corroboration

**L.D. Jensen (Incident #172A)**: Airport traffic controller at Fargo Airport tower. Observed object ~1,000 ft from tower in NW direction "passing very fast over the field." Used binoculars — "unable to distinguish any shape or form other than what appeared to be the taillight of a very fast-moving craft." Described as "round light; perfectly formed, with no fuzzy edges or rays leaving its body. The edges were clear cut."

**Manuel E. Johnson (Incident #172B)**: Assistant airport traffic controller. Saw object at ~2,000-2,500 ft, heading north then NW, "extremely fast." Saw both object and Piper Cub simultaneously. "No definite outline could be identified."

**Dr. A.D. Cannon (Incident #172C)**: Oculist and amateur pilot with 2 years flying experience. Observed from control tower after landing his Piper Cub. Object at 5,000-6,000 ft, "very fast," headings NNW, SE, and W. Round, clear white light, "tail light of a plane" size.
