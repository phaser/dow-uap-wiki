## Overview

Guy Hottel served as Special Agent in Charge (SAC) of the FBI Washington field office in early 1950. He is best known for composing the **Hottel memo** on March 22, 1950, which transmitted an informant's report of three recovered flying saucers with nine humanoid occupants in New Mexico.

## Role / Activity

Hottel received the saucer report from SA R. H. Kurtzman (who had received it from "Kartahowe," identified as a Special Investigator with the Metropolitan Police Department Sex Squad). Hottel forwarded the information to FBI HQ in Washington with the standard routing to the Air Force OSI. The memo was classified "Confidential" and forwarded to the Department of the Army Staff Communications Office.

The Hottel memo is the **most-viewed page in the FBI Vault** (FBI Vault.gov), frequently cited in UFO literature as the primary-source confirmation of the "three saucers, nine bodies" claim. The informant — described as an Air Force investigator — claimed the saucers were detected because government high-powered radar in the New Mexico area interfered with their control mechanisms.
