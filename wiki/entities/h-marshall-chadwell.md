## Overview

Dr. H. Marshall Chadwell was Assistant Director of the CIA's Office of Scientific Intelligence (OSI) during the early 1950s. He was the CIA official most directly responsible for the agency's UFO investigation program, including the convening of the Robertson Panel in January 1953.

## Role / Activity

- **CIA UFO lead**: As Assistant Director of OSI, Chadwell was the driving force behind CIA's engagement with the UFO problem. The Robertson Panel was convened "pursuant to the request of the Assistant Director for Scientific Intelligence."
- **Briefed the Robertson Panel**: Chadwell personally briefed the panel, alongside Brig. Gen. William M. Garland (Commanding General, ATIC) and Capt. Edward J. Ruppelt (Project Blue Book officer).
- **British liaison**: Author of the December 18, 1952 memo documenting British activity in the UFO field — reporting on his conversation with a messenger from Britain who had spoken with Dr. R.V. Jones. This memo shows Chadwell actively coordinating international intelligence on UFO investigations.
- **CIA organizational role**: The panel convened under his authority within OSI. The final report was transmitted to the IAC via the OSI chain of command (James Q. Reber, Secretary of IAC).
- **Position on UFOs**: Consistent with the panel's conclusions — viewed the primary danger as indirect (communications overload, mass hysteria, degradation of early warning) rather than the objects themselves representing a direct physical threat.
