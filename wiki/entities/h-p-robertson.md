## Overview

Dr. Howard Percy Robertson (January 27, 1903 – August 26, 1961) was a prominent American mathematician and theoretical physicist at the California Institute of Technology. In January 1953, he was selected by the CIA's Office of Scientific Intelligence to chair the Scientific Advisory Panel on Unidentified Flying Objects — now known as the Robertson Panel.

## Role / Activity

- **Panel Chairman**: Led the five-member panel of scientists convened to evaluate the national security threat posed by UFOs and recommend future action. The panel met January 14-18, 1953.
- **Key contributions to panel**: (1) Drew on his WWII experience investigating the German V-1 and V-2 programs (Operation CROSSBOW) — noting the key difference was the absence of "hardware" (crashed vehicles) in the UFO case; (2) Personally investigated "Foo Fighters" during WWII with Dr. Luis Alvarez; (3) Advocated that extraterrestrial artifacts, if they existed, would be of "immediate and great concern" — disagreeing with Goudsmit's view that they would merely be "natural phenomena subject to scientific study"; (4) Proposed the "training and debunking" policy that became the CIA's official posture; (5) Suggested that after the educational program took effect, a "very modest-sized ATIC section" should continue evaluating residual cases for possible scientific intelligence value.
- **Background**: Caltech physicist; contributed to general relativity and cosmology. During WWII, served as an intelligence officer analyzing German weapons programs. His experience in distinguishing between real threats and misidentified phenomena informed his approach to the UFO problem.
- **Position on UFOs after panel**: He believed that most sightings could be explained conventionally and that the real danger was public hysteria and communications overload, not the objects themselves. However, he maintained that a small monitoring capability should remain for intelligence purposes.
