## Overview

Retired Rear Admiral Herbert B. Knowles of South Berwick, Maine, was a neighbor of Frances Swan who became interested in her telepathic "outer space" messages and brought the matter to the attention of the U.S. government.

## Role / Activity

Admiral Knowles wrote to Adm. Espey, Director of Naval Intelligence, on June 1, 1954, enclosing messages received by Swan. When receiving no reply, he wrote again on June 7, 1954. Knowles became interested in flying saucers after receiving no Government response and began reading books on the subject. He hosted John Hutson (Bureau of Aeronautics Security Officer) and Canadian physicist Wilber B. Smith at his residence (July 24–26, 1954) where they observed Swan writing messages and had "contact with outer space" through her.

Knowles wrote to Senator Margaret Chase Smith (Maine), who forwarded the information to the Secretary of Defense with copies to Army, Navy, and Air Force. Knowles also wrote to the President of the United States. He wanted to know if physical contact could be had with the people in "outer space."
