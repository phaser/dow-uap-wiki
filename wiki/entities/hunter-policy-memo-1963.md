## Overview

The **Hunter NASC memo** of **18 July 1963** is one of the earliest known formal U.S. interagency policy memoranda addressing first-contact contingencies with extraterrestrial intelligence. Authored by **Maxwell W. Hunter II** of the National Aeronautics and Space Council (NASC) and addressed to **Robert F. Packard** at the U.S. Department of State (S/P–Policy Planning), titled "**Thoughts on the Space Alien Race Question.**"

## Role / Activity

- A *policy* document — not an investigative case file. Hunter discusses possible institutional postures, scientific framing (paralleling the parallel SETI debate of the era), and inter-agency handling if/when extraterrestrial intelligence is encountered.
- Predates by roughly six decades the modern [[entities/aaro]] / Congressional UAP-disclosure framework, but addresses a remarkably similar set of questions.
- Cross-references the **Brookings 1960** report (Wernher von Braun cited indirectly).
- Two byte-identical copies of this PDF exist in the raw corpus (filenames differ only in punctuation: `59_214434_sp_16_7.18.1963.pdf` and `59_214434_sp_16_[7.18.1963].pdf`); MD5 confirmed identical and folded to one summary.
