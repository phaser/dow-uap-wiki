## Overview

**James Collins**, 212 Sharon Drive, Chesapeake VA (telephone 547-4648), proprietor of Oak Grove TV at 6424 Great Bridge Road. At **04:10 a.m. on 18 January 1967** Collins telephoned the FBI to report observing a **large oblong-shaped object** which "alighted in the street" in front of him as he was walking home from his TV repair shop. He reported being **taken into the craft** — described as made of a "glass like substance and being transparent" — and meeting **several "undersized creatures similar to members of the human race, probably not more than 4 feet tall, … wearing regular trouser pants and T-Shirts"**. Collins reported being transported "for an undetermined distance and returned to his point of take-off approximately one hour later" and was unable to account for the time between **8:00 p.m. (the previous evening) and 4:00 a.m.**

The Bureau noted Collins "spoke in a coherent manner although he appeared to be under certain emotional strain. He claimed he had not been drinking any intoxicants but he was unable to account for the time…" He furnished the information to night supervisor SA **A. R. Ware** of the Special Investigative Division. Bureau indices contained no information identifiable with Collins. The report was forwarded "for your information" with no further FBI action.

## Role / Activity

- A **structurally complete abduction-narrative report** (sky-craft / small humanoid pilots / interior witnessed / missing time) lodged with the FBI in January 1967.
- Predates the **May 1968 *Look Magazine* publication of the Betty and Barney Hill case** by 18 months — i.e., before the cultural template of "alien abduction" had widely diffused. Each archetypal element appears in Collins's spontaneous account: small (4-ft) anthropomorphic crew, transparent vehicle, period of unaccounted-for time bounded by witness contact-points, return to point of origin.
- One of two unusually substantive direct-witness reports in [[summaries/62-hq-83894-section-10]] (the other being [[entities/paul-peyerl-1967|Peyerl]] — a wholly different framing: wartime photograph rather than personal contact).
- Does not appear to have surfaced in the popular abduction-canon literature; the FBI's policy of perfunctory forwarding to USAF OSI without independent corroboration would have buried it.
