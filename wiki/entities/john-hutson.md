## Overview

John Hutson was a Security Officer at the Bureau of Aeronautics, U.S. Navy Department. He "unofficially" became interested in the Frances Swan telepathic case and visited Admiral Knowles at South Berwick, Maine, where he observed Swan writing messages and had contact with "outer space" through her.

## Role / Activity

Hutson visited Knowles at South Berwick, Maine (July 24–26, 1954) at the invitation of Admiral Knowles. During his stay, he and Canadian physicist Wilber B. Smith had various interviews with Mrs. Swan and had "contact with the 'outer space' through Mrs. Swan." He observed Swan writing messages she claimed to be receiving from "outer space." He asked her to relay a question to "outer space" and she began writing the answer before she had time to relay it. Hutson indicated he could not make a commitment about contacting "outer space" as he "did not know how far the Navy would go." He stated he would be willing to assist the FBI in investigation of the matter.

Hutson later briefed the FBI WFO (July 29, 1954) along with Cdr. L. T. McQuiston.
