## Overview

Captain Joseph W. Ruppelt served at the Air Technical Intelligence Center (ATIC), Wright-Patterson Air Force Base, Dayton, Ohio. He was familiar with FBI policy regarding flying-saucer investigations and definitively denied that the FBI had ever been called upon to furnish reports on flying saucers.

## Role / Activity

Ruppelt was contacted by Colonel C. M. Young (Executive Officer to Maj. Gen. John A. Samford, Director of Air Intelligence) to verify whether Daniel Lang (New Yorker writer) had been in contact with ATIC. Ruppelt advised:
- The FBI has never been called upon to furnish reports on flying saucers
- ATIC had never indicated in any way to Lang that the FBI has an interest in flying saucers
- Lang likely "made the story up or picked it up from some magazine or newspaper article sometime back"
- Ruppelt was "thoroughly familiar with Bureau policy pertaining to flying saucer investigations"

Ruppelt is also the ATIC contact who studied the Riley/Stock Paterson NJ film and the Washington National flap radar data.
