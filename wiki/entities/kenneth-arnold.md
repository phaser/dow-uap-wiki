## Overview

**Kenneth Arnold**, a private pilot, made the **24 June 1947** sighting near Mount Rainier, Washington that catalysed the modern UFO era — nine objects "skipping like saucers across the water," paraphrased by the press into "**flying saucers**." Arnold's report converted what had been disparate WW2 [[concepts/foo-fighter]] reports and isolated 1946–early-1947 sightings into a sustained national wave (see [[syntheses/1947-flying-disc-wave]]). Arnold is referenced in the FBI HQ file [[entities/62-hq-83894]], with later cross-references including his investigative entanglement with the [[entities/maury-island-affair]] of June 1947.

## Role / Activity

- Originator of the press-coined term "flying saucer" (which he himself disputed — the *motion* was saucer-like, not the object shape).
- Investigator-of-record in the Maury Island affair, alongside Ray Palmer and CIC officers Lt. Frank M. Brown and Capt. William L. Davidson.
- **Arnold's complete first-person statement** — including his 24 June 1947 search-flight context (looking for a missing marine transport SW of Mt. Rainier), the eight-day-clock sweep-second-hand timing of the formation across the Mt. Rainier → Mt. Adams 50-mile span (1 minute 42 seconds, ~1,700 mph), the "geese flying in a diagonal chain-like line" analogy, and the figures showing the objects "longer than wide, their thickness about 1/20th of their width" colored "mirror bright" — is preserved in the FBI HQ file at [[summaries/62-hq-83894-section-2]] alongside SA **Frank M. Brown's CIC interview report dated 16 July 1947** (Brown died 15 days later in the Kelso B-25 crash). Brown's "Agent's Notes" verdict: *"It is the personal opinion of the interviewer that Mr. Arnold actually saw what he stated that he saw... a man of Mr. Arnold's character and apparent integrity would [not] state that he saw objects and write up a report to the extent that he did if he did not see them."*
- Arnold's biographical sketch in his own hand: born 29 March 1915, Subelas Minnesota; family homesteaded at Scobey Montana; Eagle Scout; **U.S. Olympic fancy-diving trials 1932**; University of Minnesota; **Red Comet Inc. of Littleton CO** (automatic fire-fighting apparatus, 1938); founded **Great Western Fire Control Supply** in 1940. Plane: a Callair (license 333487; airframe 33355) bought January 1947.
