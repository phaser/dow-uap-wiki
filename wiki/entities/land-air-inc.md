## Overview

Land-Air, Inc. was a company based in Alamogordo, New Mexico, contracted by the Geophysical Research Division of the Air Materiel Command (Cambridge, Massachusetts) to conduct a scientific study of green fireballs and other unexplained aerial phenomena in the New Mexico area.

## Characteristics

- Contracted for a "limited scientific study" of green fireballs
- Established observation posts near Vaughn, New Mexico, for photographing and determining speed, height, and nature of the phenomena
- On May 24, 1950, Land-Air personnel sighted 8–10 objects of aerial phenomena
- A 24-hour day-watch was established and designated **"Project Twinkle"**
- Dr. Anthony O. Mirarchi served as Project Engineer
- FBI Albuquerque Office briefed Mirarchi on the Bureau's espionage/sabotage jurisdiction
