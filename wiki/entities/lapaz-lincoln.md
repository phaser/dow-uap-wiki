## Overview

**Dr. Lincoln La Paz** was Director of the Institute of Meteoritics at the University of New Mexico, Albuquerque. He served as [[entities/project-sign]]'s consulting authority for the natural-meteor / fireball explanation of ambiguous luminous events. The 1947–1948 incident summaries route fireball cases to La Paz for scientific adjudication, and his disposition is recorded against the case file alongside any contrary witness account.

## Role / Activity

- Adjudicator for [[concepts/meteorite-vs-craft-ambiguity]] cases.
- Most prominent in the **Norcatur, KS fireball of 18 February 1948** (Incident 101 in [[summaries/army-incident-summaries-101-172]]) — La Paz's natural-meteor disposition was contested by [[entities/markham-norman-g]] and witness Leland Sammons, whose near-encounter testimony argued for a controlled craft. A 109 lb meteorite was recovered.
