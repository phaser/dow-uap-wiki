## Overview

Dr. Leon Davidson was an American researcher who corresponded with the CIA in the late 1950s regarding a "space message" and the Air Force's handling of UFO sightings. The CIA maintained a file on him (Case 17708) and coordinated multiple agencies' responses to his inquiries. He published articles on Air Force/Pentagon handling of UFO data and was persistent in seeking information from government agencies.

## Role / Activity

- **Investigation of "space message"**: Davidson contacted the CIA regarding "the spice message and its transmitter" (likely "space message" — OCR degraded). The CIA informed him that transmitter records had been destroyed by the originating agency.
- **Publication efforts**: Had a second article prepared for publication "concerning Air Force handling of space sightings in the hands of the Pentagon security review people," which he believed was "mild enough to pass them." 
- **Multiple agency involvement**: The CIA memo notes "there have been many cooks in the kitchen on this dish" — multiple agencies had been responding to Davidson, requiring coordinated but evasive answers. The Chicago CIA office expressed concern that the "noncommittal and evasive answer" was "hardly fair to Davidson" and "not likely to be fully accepted by him."
- **CIA identity concealed**: During telephone conversations, efforts were made to conceal CIA identity from Davidson. He may not have known he was dealing with the CIA — the memo references contacts with "Walker" and "Smikich" (names uncertain due to OCR).
- **Persistence**: Davidson was described as someone who would likely "take a crack at a later date" at the matter, and the memo predicted "more will be heard from Davidson."
