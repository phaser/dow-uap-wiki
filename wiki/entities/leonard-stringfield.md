## Overview

Leonard H. Stringfield (L. H. Stringfield) was a young UFO researcher based in Cincinnati, Ohio, who founded and directed Civilian Research, Interplanetary Flying Objects (CRIFO). He published the monthly CRIFO Newsletter with a claimed world-wide circulation of ~11,000 copies at $2/year.

## Role / Activity

Stringfield's Newsletter (Vol. I, No. 7, September 1954) contained a self-reported "exclusive" private interview with Lt. Col. John O'Mara, Deputy Commander, Intelligence, Wright-Patterson AFB (September 21, 1954). In the interview, O'Mara reportedly told Stringfield: "Flying saucers 'do exist'" and "past contradictions were unfortunate." O'Mara categorized saucers into three types: (1) controlled saucer from outer space, (2) secret American saucer-like device, (3) unexplained natural phenomena. O'Mara said "Definitely not!" when asked if all saucers seen by Americans were American devices, and that the Air Force "plans to cooperate with the public."

Stringfield was a host for the June 7, 1954 dinner at his home (7017 Britton Ave Cincinnati 27) with Bethurum, Williamson, Manspeaker, Zimmermann, and Eickhoff. He was a close friend of Jack Gunderman (layout artist, DuBois Company) who forwarded Stringfield's Newsletter to the FBI. Stringfield claims he communicates regularly with Frank Edwards (former AF of L radio news commentator) on the subject of saucers. Edwards reportedly warned Stringfield to be careful or the Air Force would stop his publishing activities.

Stringfield would later author *Situation Red: The UFO Siege!* and the "crash retrieval" Status Reports.
