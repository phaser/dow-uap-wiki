## Overview

Dr. Lincoln LaPaz was Director of the Institute of Meteoritics and Head of the Department of Mathematics and Astronomy at the University of New Mexico. He served as a voluntary consultant to the 17th District OSI (Air Force) on green fireball investigations starting in 1948.

## Characteristics

- Research Mathematician at the New Mexico Proving Grounds under OSRD appointment in 1943–1944
- Technical Director of the Operations Analysis Section, Headquarters, Second Air Force, 1944–45
- Voluntary consultant to the 17th District OSI on green fireball investigations since 1948
- Initially concluded approximately half of recorded phenomena were of meteoric origin; the other half (green fireballs + discs) he believed to be U.S. guided missiles being tested near the installations
- If not U.S. missiles, he calculated they could be Soviet missiles launched from the Urals (which could reach New Mexico in <15 minutes at observed speeds)
