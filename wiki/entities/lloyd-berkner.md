## Overview

Dr. Lloyd Viel Berkner (February 1, 1905 – June 4, 1967) was an American geophysicist and science administrator. He was president of Associated Universities, Inc. (which operated Brookhaven National Laboratory) and served on the CIA's 1953 Robertson Panel. His expertise in geophysics and radar made him particularly valuable in evaluating atmospheric and radar-related UFO reports.

## Role / Activity

- **Panel participation**: One of five voting members. Expertise in geophysics, radar systems, and ionospheric research.
- **Key contributions**: (1) Proposed the "Doppler filter" solution to radar mutual interference — adding a filter to the receiving circuit to distinguish real targets from station-to-station interference; (2) Contributed the "noise to signal ratio" analysis concept — characterizing the mass of low-quality UFO reports as overwhelming the communications "signal" with "noise," degrading the system's ability to detect genuine hostile threats; (3) Suggested the U.S. Navy (ONR) Special Devices Center at Sands Point, Long Island, as a potential organization to assist the educational program — citing its WWII aircraft identification training film production as a model; (4) Mentioned Arthur Godfrey as a potential valuable mass-communication channel.
- **Background**: Pioneer in ionospheric research; first to propose the International Geophysical Year (1957-1958). During WWII, led radar development at the Naval Research Laboratory. Later served on numerous government scientific advisory boards.
- **Position on UFOs**: Viewed the phenomenon primarily through the lens of communications and signal processing — the problem was not the objects themselves but the degradation of defense readiness caused by false reports.
