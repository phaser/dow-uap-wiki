## Overview

Norman G. Markham, U.S. Army, is the author of a 1948 letter preserved in the Project SIGN incident-summary corpus that catalogues a series of 1947–1948 sightings (Boise, Ticonderoga, Phantom Reef, Cape Mendocino, Oakland, Mantell, Maury Island) and explicitly proposes a non-terrestrial-intelligence interpretation. This is one of the **earliest documented instances** of the [[concepts/extraterrestrial-hypothesis]] being raised seriously inside a U.S. Army intelligence file — predating the more cautious institutional posture that followed under [[entities/project-grudge]].

## Role / Activity

- **The Markham letter** — primary artifact, contained in the Box 7 incident summaries 1-100 file. Cross-references the [[entities/mantell-incident]] (7 Jan 1948), Maury Island / Davidson-Brown B-25 crash (June 1947), Boise object (24 Jun 1947), Ticonderoga discs (12 Nov 1947), Phantom Reef (24 Nov 1947), Cape Mendocino blimp (1 Jul 1947), Oakland "Saturn-like" object (13 Oct 1947).
- **Norcatur, KS fireball, 18 Feb 1948** — Markham contested Dr. Lincoln La Paz's natural-meteor disposition, arguing for a controlled craft based on Leland Sammons's near-encounter testimony.
