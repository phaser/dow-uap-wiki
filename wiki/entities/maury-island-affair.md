## Overview

The **Maury Island affair** of **21 June 1947** is the earliest CIC-investigated UFO event in the FBI HQ flying-disc file. Witnesses Harold Dahl (and his son) and Fred Crisman reported six donut-shaped objects over Maury Island, Puget Sound, with one apparently in distress raining "slag" / molten material onto Dahl's boat. Their account, brought to *Amazing Stories* publisher Ray Palmer and then to [[entities/kenneth-arnold]] for investigation, drew U.S. Army Air Forces Counter-Intelligence Corps (CIC) officers **Lt. Frank M. Brown** and **Capt. William L. Davidson** to Tacoma. Brown and Davidson collected slag samples and departed for Hamilton Field; their B-25 (#1316) crashed near **Kelso, Washington on 1 August 1947**, killing both — an event extensively investigated and preserved in Section 3 of the file.

## Role / Activity

- The first formally-investigated UFO case to leave a paper trail across multiple federal agencies (FBI, Army Air Forces CIC) in the modern era.
- The Kelso B-25 crash and the loss of the Maury Island samples is one of the foundational "evidence destroyed" episodes cited throughout post-1947 UFO literature.
- Officially classified by the Air Force as a hoax — Crisman and Dahl having allegedly fabricated the slag from industrial residue. The FBI file preserves both the hoax disposition and the CIC investigation that preceded it.
