## Overview

**Muroc Army Air Field** in California (renamed **Edwards Air Force Base** in 1949–1950) was the U.S. Army Air Forces / Air Force flight-test center. In the early UFO record it occupies a privileged epistemic position: the witnesses to the **8 July 1947 Muroc cluster** were serving USAF officers and test pilots, removing a major degree of witness-reliability uncertainty (see [[concepts/witness-reliability-evaluation]]). The Muroc cluster comprises Incidents 1–4 in the Project SIGN Box 7 incident-summary file.

## Role / Activity

- Site of Incidents 1–4 of [[entities/project-sign]] — silver discs / "unconventional shape" / "yellowish-white sphere" objects observed at the airfield and over Rogers Dry Lake during planned test operations.
- Witness chain across the four incidents includes:
  - **Incident 1 (8 Jul 1947, 0930)** — 1st Lt. Joseph C. McHenry (Billeting Officer), S/Sgt Newman, T/Sgt Ruvolo, Miss Scotte
  - **Incident 2 (8 Jul 1947, ~noon)** — Major Richard R. Shoop (Tech Engineering)
  - **Incident 3 (7 Jul 1947, 1010)** — Major J.G. Wise (Test Pilot, XP-84)
  - **Incident 4 (8 Jul 1947, 1150)** — Captain John Paul Strapp (Flight Test) at Rogers Dry Lake during a planned ejection-seat experiment with two P-82s and an A-26 at 80 000 ft
