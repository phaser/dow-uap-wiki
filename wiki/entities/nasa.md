## Overview

The U.S. National Aeronautics and Space Administration. NASA's relevance to the UAP record is twofold: (1) Apollo and Skylab astronauts produced systematic, in-flight observations of unidentified objects and visual phenomena, and (2) NASA released crew debriefings and transcripts as part of a UAP-disclosure document set in the 2020s. The famous in-flight observations include the Apollo 11 "hollow cylinder / two rings / open suitcase" object, the Apollo 17 tumbling debris field with a "two-flash-per-rev" rotating object, the Skylab 3 "red satellite," and Skylab 4 tumbling flashing lights — none of which were definitively identified in the contemporaneous debriefings.

## Role / Activity

- **Mission Operations / Manned Spacecraft Center**, Houston, conducted post-mission technical crew debriefs that captured UAP observations as part of standard "Visual Sightings" sections (Section 21 in the Apollo 11 debriefing).
- **Disclosure release** — published a curated set of Apollo / Skylab transcripts and debriefings flagged with the prefix `nasa-uap-d<N>` for UAP-relevance, indexed via this wiki.
