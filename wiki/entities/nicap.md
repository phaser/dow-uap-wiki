## Overview

The National Investigations Committee on Aerial Phenomena (NICAP) was formed in 1955 to counter the publicly suppressed USAF investigation of UFOs. It became the most authoritative civilian UFO organization of the era, with a board of directors that included multiple retired admirals, generals, and intelligence officers — giving it unprecedented access and credibility. Its formation and membership directly challenged the official debunking policy.

## Role / Activity

- **Founding (1955)**: Established "to counter the publicly suppressed USAF investigation of UFOs." The first Chairman was Admiral D.S. Fahrney, who had previously directed the Navy's guided missile program from its inception.
- **Distinguished directors**: Board members included:
  - Admiral Roscoe Hillenkoetter — first CIA Director (1947-1950), who publicly stated the U.S. Government knew UFOs were extraterrestrial
  - Major Donald Keyhoe — Marine Corps aviator and author
  - Major Dewey Fournet — Blue Book Project Officer at the Pentagon until late 1952
  - Rear Admiral H.B. Knowles — former ONI intelligence officer
  - General A. Wedemeyer — WWII China-Burma-India theater commander
  - Colonel J.J. Bryan — special assistant to the Secretary of the Air Force
- **Significance**: The concentration of retired senior defense and intelligence officials on NICAP's board represented "similar defections from official policy after retirement." The fact that the first CIA Director, the Navy's missile program director, and a Blue Book project officer all endorsed the extraterrestrial hypothesis after leaving government service was a serious challenge to the official USAF position.
- **FBI relationship**: NICAP formally inquired into the FBI's UFO role in the late 1950s; the Bureau's file notes characterized Keyhoe's prior writings as "irresponsible" — reflecting the FBI's dismissive institutional posture toward the organization.
- **Effect of JANAP 146E (1960)**: The revised regulation making it an offense under the Espionage Act to reveal "data on UFOs" was explicitly intended "to reduce the effect of these and similar defections from official policy after retirement."
