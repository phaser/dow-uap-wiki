## Overview

The persistent target across the [[entities/fbi-tracking-camera-sequence-19991231]] sequence — observed across at least 1 minute 43 seconds of zoom (NFOV) coverage — appearing as twin close-spaced dark blobs that sometimes resolve as separate points and sometimes fuse into a single elongated smear. Persistence across this duration argues for a real target rather than a sensor / dust artifact. Under wide-field (WFOV) configuration the same target appears as a single dark point above the central crosshair (see [[concepts/point-source-object]]).

## Role / Activity

- Subject of the FBI sensor's track for the duration of the released sequence.
- Possible interpretations include: (1) two co-flying objects (close formation), (2) a single bilobed object whose aspect angle changes, (3) a single point with optical/atmospheric distortion. Without sensor metadata or ranging, none can be discriminated from the released frames alone.
