## Overview

**Paul L. Peyerl** (1430 Southwest 76th Street, Miami FL; born 3 May 1924, Austria; naturalized US citizen 1958; mechanic at Eastern Airlines, Miami). On **26 April 1967** Peyerl walked into the Miami FBI office and reported that, while a **Luftwaffe test pilot in November 1944**, assigned to a **"top secret project in the Black Forest of Austria"** after release from Russian Front duty, he had photographed a saucer-shaped craft in flight and at rest. He claimed to retain both the original negative (taken at 7,000 m / 20,000 ft, 30-second exposure) and a hangar-interior photograph "at the risk of my life"; Xerox copies of both were furnished and reproduced in the Miami SAC's June 1967 LHM (62-0-11328).

The craft as Peyerl described it:

- "saucer shaped, about twenty-one feet in diameter, radio controlled, and mounted several jet engines around the exterior portion of the craft. He further described the exterior portion as revolving around the dome in the center which remained stationary."
- Designed and engineered by **"(First Name Unknown) KUEHR, a German engineer"**. Peyerl believed KUEHR had been apprehended by the Gestapo in Vienna in late 1943 / early 1944, then taken into Allied custody at war's end with the project's secrets.
- **Fuel/engine specs** copied from a hangar board: "Fuel mixture of N₂H₄O in Methyl Alcohol (CH₃OH) rather than 'oxygen-holding' mixture of hydrogen peroxide H₂O₂ in water. 7m, 1.3m high two rocket motors; smooth flow, rotary drive over 2,000 meters per second."
- Combat use claim: "responsible for the downing of at least one American B-26 airplane."

## Role / Activity

- Peyerl had two earlier, unrelated walk-ins at the Miami FBI office: **24 July 1959** (volunteered to return to Austria as a US intelligence agent) and **4 July 1961** (incoherent story about being asked to kill his mother). The Miami SAC's 1967 memo notes "PEYERL seemed genuinely concerned about the existence of the object he allegedly photographed during November, 1944. He exhibited no emotional nor mental disorder and appeared to be rational."
- Wartime biography (verifiable in principle): graduated German Air Academy 1943, Luftwaffe Russian Front; reassigned end-1944 to the Black Forest project; reassigned to Western Front; **shot down by the British 14 March 1945**; held prisoner first in London then Brussels until 1946 release; departed Bremerhaven 26 December 1951; entered the US in New Jersey 7 January 1952; naturalized in Miami 1958.
- The Miami SAC submitted the case "to the Bureau for consideration of transmittal to the U. S. Air Force" in light of "recent publicity afforded UFO sightings, some apparently by responsible sources".
- **Engineering plausibility**: the cited "rotary drive over 2,000 m/s" is a tip-speed roughly 6× the speed of sound, mechanically infeasible at 1944 materials limits; the H₂O₂/CH₃OH bipropellant mention is interestingly close to the **Walter HWK 109-509** rocket-motor chemistry used in the Me 163 Komet (T-Stoff/C-Stoff = H₂O₂ / methanol-hydrazine), suggesting Peyerl's recollection conflated genuine wartime German rocket-propulsion knowledge with embellished or imagined platform claims.
