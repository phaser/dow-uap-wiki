## Overview

Peter Cameron Jones was a Los Angeles-area individual who wrote to Walter Winchell in August 1947 (the letter surfaced via Winchell's aide Ernest Cuneo in July 1949) claiming to have observed a flying saucer in the LA mountains.

## Characteristics

- Addressed as "16 W. 37th Street, Los Angeles 7, California" (per Winchell letter)
- Described as a hiker in the LA mountains in August 1947
- Letter was described by Cuneo as "very well written, obviously by a man of intelligence"

## Role / Activity

Jones claimed that while hiking in the LA mountains in August 1947 at approximately 10:00 AM, he observed about one-half block away a large silver/greenish metal object shaped like a child's top, about the size of county-fair balloons, with two windows and transparent-metal portions. He gained the impression of "life within the object." When he stood up and waved, the object took off in a second, knocking him to the ground. He questioned whether it was an "exploration landing on our planet" from occupants of another planet curious about the effects of the atomic bomb.

Los Angeles SAC investigated — negative. The apartment-court owner had no record of a Peter Cameron Jones. FBI Lab confirmed the letter's physical characteristics but could not verify the author.
