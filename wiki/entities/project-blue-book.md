## Overview

**Project Blue Book** was the United States Air Force's principal program for collecting, investigating, and adjudicating reports of unidentified aerial objects, running from **1952 (succeeding [[entities/project-grudge|Project Grudge]] / [[entities/project-sign|Project Sign]]) until its termination on 17 December 1969**. Headquartered at **[[entities/wright-patterson-afb|Wright-Patterson AFB]], Ohio**, the program ingested sightings funneled by Air Force base information offices, the FBI, civilian witnesses, NICAP, and police agencies, and adjudicated each as identified (e.g., aircraft, balloon, astronomical, weather, hoax, psychological) or "unidentified".

The program was terminated on the basis of the [[entities/condon-committee|University of Colorado / Condon Committee]] report (*Scientific Study of Unidentified Flying Objects*, 1969), the National Academy of Sciences' review of that report, and Air Force experience accumulated since 1948. The Secretary of the Air Force's announcement on 17 December 1969 formally rescinded the Air Force regulation that had established Blue Book.

## Role / Activity

- The **headline statistic** preserved in the [[summaries/62-hq-83894-section-10|FBI 62-HQ-83894 Section 10]] copy of the **Air Force UFO Fact Sheet (January 1977)** is **12,618 total sightings 1947–1969**, with a peak year of **1952 (1,501 sightings, 303 unidentified)**. Per-year totals from the Fact Sheet table: 122, 156, 186, 210, 169, 1,501, 509, 487, 545, 670, 1,006, 627, 390, 557, 591, 474, 399, 562, 887, 1,112, 937, 375, 146.
- Blue Book's three formal conclusions, restated in the 1977 Fact Sheet:
  1. **No UFO ever indicated a threat to national security.**
  2. **No evidence that "unidentified" sightings represented "technological developments or principles beyond the range of present-day scientific knowledge."**
  3. **No evidence that "unidentified" sightings were extraterrestrial vehicles.**
- All Blue Book documentation was transferred to the **Modern Military Branch, National Archives and Records Service**, 8th & Pennsylvania Avenue, Washington DC 20408 (now NARA) and is publicly accessible.
- **Persistent post-1969 institutional posture**: the Air Force retained no successor investigative function, and from 1969 onward both the FBI and AF responded to public UFO inquiries by referring them onward to OSI / Office of Information / Public Affairs with a stock formula. The June 1977 Carter White House inquiry recorded in [[summaries/62-hq-83894-section-10]] confirms the absence of any executive-branch coordination function for UFO information across the late-1970s.
