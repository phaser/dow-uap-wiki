## Overview

**Project GRUDGE** was the U.S. Air Force's successor to [[entities/project-sign]], running from 1949 onward at [[entities/wright-patterson-afb]]. GRUDGE inherited Sign's case files and intake practices but reportedly tilted the institutional disposition toward conventional / atmospheric / psychological explanations, downgrading the [[concepts/extraterrestrial-hypothesis]] consideration that some Sign-era officers had entertained. GRUDGE was eventually superseded by **Project Blue Book** (1952–1969).

## Role / Activity

- Custodian of the [[concepts/incident-checklist-template]] check-list practice inherited from SIGN.
- Operational tilt toward "explained" dispositions; the inferred disposition behavior is reflected in many cases documented in the Box 7 incident-summary volumes — explicitly identifying this institutional tilt would require reading volumes outside the sampled ranges.
- Cases that began as Sign incidents continue to be referenced under GRUDGE custody.
