## Overview

**Project SIGN** was the U.S. Air Force's first formal UFO investigation program, run out of [[entities/wright-patterson-afb]]'s Air Materiel Command (AMC) Technical Intelligence Division (T-2) from late 1947 / early 1948. Project SIGN authored the **CHECK-LIST – UNIDENTIFIED FLYING OBJECTS** intake form (see [[concepts/incident-checklist-template]]) and accumulated the case-by-case "Incident Summary" series (Box 7, 1–233+ at minimum) that constitutes the operational evidence base for the early UFO record. SIGN was succeeded by [[entities/project-grudge]] in 1949 and ultimately by Project Blue Book.

## Role / Activity

- **Standardised intake** — the 26-field check-list applied to every reported case, capturing Date, Time, Location, Observer, Object kinematics, and disposition.
- **Investigator practice** — explicit grading of [[concepts/witness-reliability-evaluation]] and use of [[concepts/multi-witness-corroboration]] as the primary disposition criterion.
- **Inter-agency routing** — fireball cases sent to Dr. Lincoln La Paz at the Institute of Meteoritics, UNM (see [[entities/lapaz-lincoln]] and [[concepts/meteorite-vs-craft-ambiguity]]).
- **Cross-referencing** — multi-witness clusters assigned suffix-letter case IDs (48a, 48b, 48c, 48d, etc.).
