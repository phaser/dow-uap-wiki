## Overview

**Project TWINKLE** was a U.S. Air Force surveillance program established to investigate the **green fireball** wave seen primarily over New Mexico (Los Alamos, Sandia / Kirtland, Vaughn, Socorro) and adjacent atomic-energy sites in late 1948 through 1949. The investigation involved Dr. Anthony O. Mirarchi and Dr. Lincoln La Paz ([[entities/lapaz-lincoln]]) and was preserved in correspondence within the FBI HQ flying-disc file [[entities/62-hq-83894]] (Sections 4 and 6).

## Role / Activity

- Surveillance program with cinetheodolite cameras at multiple New Mexico sites attempting to triangulate green-fireball trajectories.
- Inconclusive — the cameras failed to capture a definitive event during the program's tenure.
- Closely tied to the [[concepts/atomic-energy-site-overflight]] thread — the green fireballs concentrated over installations housing nuclear weapons material.
