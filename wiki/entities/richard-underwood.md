## Overview

Richard "Dick" Underwood was a senior photometric engineer at the NASA Johnson Space Center (formerly Manned Spacecraft Center) in Houston. He personally examined virtually every photograph returned from orbit during the Mercury, Gemini, Apollo, and Skylab programs. Underwood maintained an encyclopedic memory of mission photography and was a key internal NASA resource for identifying objects appearing in spaceflight imagery.

## Role / Activity

- **Chief photo analyst**: Examined "every single photograph brought back from orbit and beyond" for NASA. Described as being able to identify any frame from memory, describe the situation, and locate the citation in record books within minutes.
- **UFO-related work**: Underwood stated he "never found anything in these pictures that puzzled me for long" and expressed disappointment at not finding evidence of "someone else's spaceship." He debunked several UFO claims by examining original film sequences and identifying mundane explanations — window reflections, dust particles, debris, and glare.
- **Alan Sandler incident**: UFO researcher Alan Sandler visited Underwood's office and selected photographs for a 1974 book/movie ("UFOs: Past, Present and Future"). Underwood advised Sandler that two Gemini-12 photographs showing "a string of lights" were routine Agena ranging-light images. Sandler used them anyway, captioning them "Said to be ranging lights" — implying a NASA coverup. Underwood possessed the full 20-photo sequence proving the explanation.
- **Apollo-11 "UFO" photos**: Underwood quickly identified the "mysterious blue halo" around an astronaut as a solar glare development defect, locating "half a dozen similar halos" on other space photographs.
- **Critique of UFO methodology**: When UFO writers hint at a "NASA coverup" or "space censorship," Underwood noted, "they are essentially calling [Underwood] and his colleagues a pack of liars."
