## Overview

Dr. Samuel Abraham Goudsmit (July 11, 1902 – December 4, 1978) was a Dutch-American physicist at Brookhaven National Laboratory. He was famous for co-discovering electron spin (with George Uhlenbeck) and for serving as scientific director of the WWII Alsos Mission, which investigated Nazi Germany's nuclear weapons progress. He served on the CIA's 1953 Robertson Panel on UFOs.

## Role / Activity

- **Panel participation**: One of five voting members. Expertise in atomic physics and statistical analysis.
- **Key contributions**: (1) Expressed the view that extraterrestrial artifacts, if they existed, were "no cause for alarm" — they "are in the realm of natural phenomena subject to scientific study, just as cosmic rays were at the time of their discovery 20 to 30 years ago"; (2) His view was directly contradicted by Robertson, who felt such artifacts would be "of immediate and great concern not only to the U.S. but to all countries"; (3) Contributed to the panel's statistical reasoning about the reported cases.
- **Background**: Co-discovered electron spin (1925). Scientific head of the ALSOS Mission, which followed advancing Allied forces into Germany to capture Nazi nuclear scientists and determine how far Germany had progressed toward an atomic bomb. His discovery that the German program was far behind — famously learning it from Werner Heisenberg — made him a figure of historical significance.
