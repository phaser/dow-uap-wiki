## Overview

Sary Shagan was a Soviet anti-ballistic missile (ABM) weapons testing range located in Kazakhstan. It was operational from the late 1960s through the 1980s as part of the Soviet ABM-1 GALOSH system. Two CIA intelligence reports document a green circular unidentified aerial phenomenon observed at the range in late summer 1973 by a former Soviet employee. The range was also reportedly the site of rumored laser weapons research.

## Role / Activity

- **ABM test site**: Primary Soviet testing facility for the ABM-1 GALOSH (System-300/Aldan) anti-ballistic missile system, deployed from 1967-1989. Also tested System-75 (SA-2) surface-to-air missile warheads.
- **Warhead checkout**: Site 4 conducted warhead verification — ABM-1 warheads were ~2 meters long, 80-100 cm diameter, ~400 kg, containing "black cassettes" with metal balls (fragmentation warhead design). SA-2 warheads had ~100 grey cassettes with 200-300 metal balls (~1.5 cm diameter) each.
- **Rumored laser research**: Experiments involving laser weapons reportedly conducted at an unknown location at the range, involving "power-beam antennas."
- **UAP sighting (late summer 1973)**: A former Soviet employee observed a bright green circular object in the sky west of the site at ~70-degree angle. Within 10-15 seconds, green concentric circles formed around the mass. Colors disappeared within minutes. No sound. Source provided limited testimony — no diameter estimate, no further details. No resultant rumors at the facility.
- **Facility details**: Regional headquarters designated V/Ch 03080. Security fencing around the facility. Work described as "experimental" (opytnaya).
