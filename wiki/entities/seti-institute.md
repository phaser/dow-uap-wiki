## Overview

The Search for Extraterrestrial Intelligence (SETI) Institute is a nonprofit research corporation based in Mountain View, California. It was formed after Congress terminated NASA's High Resolution Microwave Survey (HRMS) / SETI program in October 1993, effectively replacing the government effort. The Institute borrowed NASA's signal processing system and continued the search for artificial radio signals from other civilizations through private and academic funding.

## Role / Activity

- **NASA transition**: When Congress directed NASA to end its SETI program in October 1993 (citing federal budget pressures), the SETI Institute assumed the research. NASA's 1997 Fact Sheet (FS-1997-01-017-HQ, reproduced in [[summaries/usg-uap-d001-congress-whitehouse-ufo-correspondence-1998]]) designates the Institute as the primary point of contact for extraterrestrial intelligence inquiries.
- **UFO inquiry routing**: By 1997-1998, NASA's standard response to UFO-related constituent letters was to enclose the Fact Sheet and direct inquiries to the SETI Institute, the National Archives (for Project Blue Book records), and law enforcement agencies (for sighting reports).
- **Field scope**: The Institute's research spans astronomy, planetary sciences, chemical evolution, the origin of life, biological evolution, and cultural evolution — not specifically UAP investigation.
