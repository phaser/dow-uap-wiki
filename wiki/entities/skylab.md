## Overview

**Skylab** was the first U.S. orbital space station, occupied by three crews in 1973–1974. Crews:

- **Skylab 2** (May–Jun 1973): Pete Conrad (CDR), Joseph Kerwin (SPT, MD), Paul Weitz (PLT)
- **Skylab 3** (Jul–Sep 1973): Alan Bean (CDR), Owen Garriott (SPT), Jack Lousma (PLT)
- **Skylab 4** (Nov 1973–Feb 1974): Gerald Carr (CDR), Edward Gibson (SPT), William Pogue (PLT)

The Skylab technical crew debriefings (D7 in the NASA UAP release set) contain unusually rich [[concepts/orbital-uap]] content.

## Role / Activity

Notable UAP content from the Skylab debriefings:

- **The "red satellite" (Skylab 3)** — a bright reddish rotating object pacing the station for ~10 minutes at ~30–50 nmi, brighter than Jupiter, with a 10-second rotation period. Crew explicitly requested ground identification; **never identified.** This is one of the most striking single events in the released NASA UAP corpus.
- **Skylab 4 "two or three" tumbling flashing lights** — moving relative to the station, also unidentified.
- **SAA-correlated cosmic-ray flashes** (Kerwin, Skylab 2) — the orbital-environment confirmation of the Apollo [[concepts/light-flashes-cosmic-rays]] phenomenon, with explicit correlation to passes through the [[entities/south-atlantic-anomaly]].
