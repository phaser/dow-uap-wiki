## Overview

On **24 April 1964** at Socorro, New Mexico, **Officer Lonnie Zamora** of the Socorro Police Department observed a landed egg-shaped object in a desert arroyo with two humanoid figures nearby; the object lifted off vertically and departed at high speed. Zamora's report is preserved in the FBI HQ file as a **full on-scene FBI Letterhead Memorandum (LHM)** — an unusually detailed primary record because the case was investigated jointly by the FBI (SA D. Arthur Byrnes Jr.), Army intelligence, the Air Force (Project Blue Book), and the Socorro PD (Sgt. M. S. Chavez, Officer Ted Jordan, Undersheriff Jim Luckie). Physical landing traces (depressed earth, scorched vegetation) were photographed and measured.

## Role / Activity

- One of the most-cited [[concepts/landing-trace]] cases in the UFO record.
- Project Blue Book ultimately classified Socorro as "unidentified" — the most uncommon disposition in the program's archive.
- The FBI Serial 438 LHM provides Bureau-level corroboration of the Air Force investigation rather than a separate Bureau finding.

## Documented in Sources

- [[summaries/62-hq-83894-serial-438]] — the primary on-scene LHM and Bureau record of the case.
- [[summaries/62-hq-83894-section-10]] — internal Bureau provenance note appended to a December 1966 reply to NICAP/AIAA member J.A. Hennessey (London): "Bufiles reflect that an unidentified flying object was sighted at Socorro, New Mexico, on April 24, 1964. Captain Holder, United States Air Force, along with SA D. Arthur Byrnes, Jr., interviewed Lonnie Zamora of the Socorro Police Department who had observed the phenomena. SA Byrnes typed such a statement from Zamora which was later included in a memo for dissemination. The Albuquerque Office did not conduct any investigation other than to keep the Bureau advised and maintain liaison with the Air Force." This is one of the cleanest internal Bureau acknowledgements that the **Albuquerque Office produced the original Zamora witness statement** that fed downstream Bureau and AF dissemination.
