## Overview

The **South Atlantic Anomaly (SAA)** is a region over the South Atlantic where the inner Van Allen radiation belt dips closest to Earth's surface. Spacecraft and satellites passing through the SAA experience a markedly elevated flux of high-energy charged particles, which manifests aboard crewed vehicles as increased rates of [[concepts/light-flashes-cosmic-rays]] and increased single-event upsets on electronics.

## Role / Activity

- Skylab 2 Science Pilot Joseph Kerwin (MD) explicitly correlated the cabin light-flash phenomenon with passes through the SAA in his post-mission debriefing — see [[summaries/nasa-uap-d7-skylab-technical-crew-debriefing-1973]].
- The SAA is the principal known terrestrial reason an orbital UAP-adjacent observation can have a strictly geophysical explanation.
