## Overview

Captain John Paul Strapp, Flight Test, Muroc Army Air Field — the named witness in **Incident 4** of [[entities/project-sign]] (8 July 1947, 1150 local, Rogers Dry Lake / Area #3, Muroc, CA). The OCRed name "Strapp" is almost certainly **John Paul Stapp**, the U.S. Air Force flight surgeon who became famous for the rocket-sled deceleration experiments at Muroc / Edwards in the 1950s. The Box 7 incident-summary file's transliteration as "Strapp" is an OCR / transcription artifact of the encrypted scanned PDF.

## Role / Activity

- Witnessed a silvery ovular object ~50 ft across, descending against the prevailing wind, during a planned ejection-seat experiment involving two P-82s and an A-26 at 80 000 ft.
- Strapp's report to investigators recorded "two projections on the upper surface" of the object, suggesting rotation or oscillation.
- A civilian witness, "Mr. Lens" of Wright Field / Dayton, OH, was also present.
