## Overview

Dr. Thornton Leigh Page (August 13, 1913 – January 2, 1996) was an American astronomer and astrophysicist. At the time of the Robertson Panel, he was at the Office of Research Operations, Johns Hopkins University. He served as one of the five voting members and was the panel's specialist in astronomy and astrophysics.

## Role / Activity

- **Panel participation**: One of five voting members. Provided astronomical expertise.
- **Key contributions**: (1) Noted that "present astronomical knowledge of the solar system makes the existence of intelligent beings (as we know the term) elsewhere than on the earth extremely unlikely, and the concentration of their attention by any controllable means confined to any one continent of the earth quite preposterous" — providing a strong scientific argument against the extraterrestrial hypothesis; (2) Advocated for more competent screening/filtering of reported sightings at or near the source, achievable by an educational program; (3) Proposed wide-angle cameras for sky patrol to record peculiar radar echoes simultaneously — which "would serve several purposes including the better understanding of radar interference as well as identification of UFOs"; (4) Co-signed (with Hynek) the point that a "considerable fraction of the sky has been under surveillance" by astronomers for many years, and "no case of any striking unidentified object" had been found — suggesting that if anomalous objects existed, astronomers would have seen them.
- **Background**: Astronomer at Yerkes Observatory, University of Chicago; later at Wesleyan University. Expert in galaxies and observational astronomy. Served as a naval officer during WWII.
