## Overview

Dr. Urner Liddel was Chief of the Nuclear Physics Branch of the Office of Naval Research (ONR). He directed the Skyhook balloon program — a $40 million/year basic and applied research program using 100-foot-diameter helium balloons to carry instruments to study atomic explosions in the stratosphere. He became known for asserting that all flying saucer reports were Skyhook balloons.

## Characteristics

- Charged with the Skyhook-Flying Saucer project
- Balloons traveled at speeds up to 200 mph at heights up to 19 miles (100,000 ft)
- Carried cloud chambers, beam chambers, ionization chambers, Geiger telescopes, photographic equipment
- At least 270 Skyhook flights made by the time of his LOOK magazine article (January 1951)
- "When this project first began, it was kept secret. Now, there is no longer any need for secrecy on a scientific basis."
- Explained Captain Mantell's death as pursuit of a Skyhook balloon, not a saucer
- Explained "squadrons" of saucers as clusters of 20–30 Skyhook 10–15 ft test balloons
- Claims saucers were "unheard of until the ONR's experiments in the stratosphere began"
