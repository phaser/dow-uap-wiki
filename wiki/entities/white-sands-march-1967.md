## Overview

On **Thursday, 2 March 1967**, between roughly **10:30 and 11:00 a.m.**, **at least 29 personnel at White Sands Missile Range (WSMR), New Mexico** witnessed one or more "silvery discs" overflying the range. The objects were **estimated at 20,000–25,000 ft altitude moving "approximately 2,000 miles per hour"**, were **reportedly tracked on radar**, and crossed from near Ruidoso NM south past Holloman AFB, then south toward Orogrande NM, then over the Sacramento Mountains. **No missile firings were active at WSMR at the time.** Cloud cover was minimal; sky observation conditions were good. Several Air Force planes airborne at the time circled the area but reported seeing nothing unusual. Estimates of object speed and altitude came from on-range eyewitnesses whose names were withheld under Air Force regulation.

The incident was reported regionally by the **El Paso Times (5 March 1967)** as *"29 sight UFO's over White Sands Range"* (byline Dorrance Guy), citing **Terry Clark, president and general manager of radio station KALG, Alamogordo NM**, as the originating local reporter. Per the El Paso Times: "News concerning the UFOs has been cut off by orders from the Pentagon. All service personnel and civilians working for the services have been forbidden to discuss the sightings." The investigation was directed away from local Holloman AFB control to the **Flight Safety Office at Kirtland AFB, Albuquerque**, with all information to come from the Pentagon. Capt. **Frank J. Morris**, Holloman Information Officer, confirmed Maj. **James Stephens** at Holloman was the assigned UFO officer; results would route Holloman → Wright-Patterson AFB → Pentagon Public Information Office.

The sighting reaches the FBI's HQ file only via a third-hand path: the El Paso Times article was reproduced inside an **IGAP / "ufo contact" magazine** (August 1967 issue, edited from Maaloev, Denmark) that **Theodore Gray Hullett of San Francisco mailed to Hoover**, where it was preserved as enclosure to the Hullett file. See [[summaries/62-hq-83894-section-10]] for the full enclosure context.

## Role / Activity

- One of the largest mass-witness UFO sightings of the late-1960s era preserved in primary AF-adjacent records, despite the Pentagon-ordered news suppression.
- The reported speed (~2,000 mph) and the radar-track claim — combined with the absence of any active missile firing — would by the standards of the [[entities/condon-committee|Condon Committee]] criteria have warranted a Blue Book residual-unidentified disposition; whether it received one is not clear in this file.
- Useful as a counter-example to the popular narrative that Project Blue Book's late-period investigations were uniformly perfunctory: the Pentagon-imposed information lockdown around this WSMR event suggests the contrary — that some events received heightened internal handling outside Blue Book's normal channels.
- The case is also a useful demonstration of how primary-source UFO history travels through unexpected channels: an Adamski-orbit contactee magazine preserved an El Paso Times story that the Pentagon had attempted to keep out of the national press.
