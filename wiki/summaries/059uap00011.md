## Key Points

- The cable's "UFO" reference is *figurative*, not a substantive UAP report. Subject line: "UFOS OVER GEORGIA: STRANGE ENCOUNTERS OF AN MFA KIND."
- Context: October 28-29, 2001, Georgia accused Russia of overflying and bombing Kodori Gorge (Abkhazia). Ambassador Vershbow raised the issue with Russian Deputy Foreign Minister Mamedov on October 30, warning the violations could be "disastrous" for U.S.-Russian relations and the upcoming Bush-Putin summit.
- Russian MFA Georgia Desk Chief Tereoken denied any Russian aircraft involvement, claiming the Abkhaz had only used helicopters and that "reports of planes in the area might as well have been about 'UFOs.'" He claimed Moscow lacks the technical capability to determine whether foreign planes were in the region.
- Tereoken added it was "possible that 'any side' had sent planes over Kodori."
- Vershbow's COMMENT (para 8): "It is hard to accept official Russian denials... To posit that they could be UFOs would be humorous if it were not for the seriousness of the violations. Most likely the Russians want to keep the pressure on the Georgians and the Chechens in the gorge in an unsubtle effort to prevent these groups' movement to Abkhazia or to Russia. Their official denials reflect a traditional Russian penchant to avoid an awkward admission with a bold lie."
- Cable also covers Gudauta base withdrawal (one trainload of equipment departed October 29; ~600 Russian personnel remain; Moscow wants to retain ~340 as "guards"); and Georgian parliamentary speaker Zurab Zvania's October 25-28 visit to Moscow.
