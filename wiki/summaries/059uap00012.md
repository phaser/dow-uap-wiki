## Key Points

- Despite the "UFOs" in the subject line, the cable is substantively about a Turkmen NGO named the **Union of UFOlogists (UOU) of Turkmenabat**, Lebap Welayet province — established 1992, originally to study extraterrestrial life.
- The UOU is the *first* NGO to have registered after Turkmen independence and the first independent NGO to re-register under the austere 2003 NGO law.
- DCM and USAID Director met the UOU board November 5, 2004. UOU was working on an $8,532 USAID grant to help other Turkmen NGOs navigate the registration process.
- UOU President **Ovezberdy Muradov** stated Turkmen military and government authorities had "consulted him about mysterious occurrences in Turkmen airspace, but he said there had been no confirmed sightings of UFOs in Turkmenistan." This is the only substantive UAP-related claim in the cable.
- The UOU's "practical" activities expanded over time: assisted 187 small/medium businesses register, ran computer/accounting/UFOlogy/massage courses, distributed humanitarian aid (Tajik civil-war refugees, Afghan refugees) under State Dept-funded CHAP via Counterpart International.
- USAID was considering a $15,000 capacity-building grant; the UOU also requested $30k for printing equipment to launch an independent newsletter (capped at 999 copies to avoid Turkmenistan's 1000-copy licensing rule).
- Cable concludes (Comment, para 12): "Crazy? Like a fox; and worthy of USG attention and support."
