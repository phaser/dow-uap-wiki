## Key Points

- Cable is Mission Mexico's "Weekly Political Blotter" covering Sep 11-15, 2023. UAP appears as item 11.
- **September 12, 2023**: Mexican Congress held hearings on Unidentified Aerial Phenomena (UAP).
- Witnesses included:
  - **Jaime Maussan** — Mexican journalist
  - **Ryan Graves** — former U.S. Navy pilot who had previously testified before U.S. Congress
- The hearing's purpose was to debate language on UAP in Mexico's "Aerial Space Protection Law" — which, if approved, "would make Mexico the first country to formally acknowledge the presence of alien life on Earth."
- Experts asked legislators to (1) recognize UAP, (2) guarantee airspace security, and (3) allow UAP to be studied.
- **Two alleged "alien corpses"** were presented to Congress, plus videos of Mexican pilots' encounters with fast-moving flying objects.
- Photo (Figure 1) credited to Reuters: alleged remains of "non-human beings" presented by Maussan.
- Graves "lamented the display took away from his and other pilots' experiences with UAP and expressed disappointment with Maussan's 'unsubstantiated stunt.'"
- Cable notes: "Scientists have discredited previous alleged alien corpses Maussan presented as evidence of alien life."
- Distribution: NSC, OVP, DNI, CIA, DIA, USNORTHCOM, DHS, DOJ, USSOUTHCOM.
