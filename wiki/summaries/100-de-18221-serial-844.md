## Key Points

- Single-page FBI office memorandum recording a citizen UFO report.
- Witness: **David Weaver**, 15801 Decosta, Detroit, Michigan; phone KEnwood 5-7546.
- Weaver telephoned the FBI Detroit office at 4:08 a.m. on April 17, 1958, reporting he had "just seen an object in the sky described as a circular object with a crystal-type dome that reflected lights."
- Object's path: traveling in a *northern* direction from southwest; crossed the City three blocks south of Six Mile at Lamphere Street.
- Weaver said he had attempted to contact Selfridge Field (Air Force) but his attempts were "unproductive" — then called the FBI to furnish the information.
- Weaver was 23 years old, son of a Detroit policeman, had previous experience with the Civil Air Patrol (CAP), but "was not too familiar with the identification of aircraft." He was on his way home from his employment in Detroit when he observed the object.
- **Recommendation** (handwritten): "Advise proper air force authorities."
- Handwritten annotations: "Lt. Col (Bonnie) E. Prater advised 4/17/58 to letter via mc[?]" — indicating phone-relay to an Air Force liaison and follow-up correspondence.
- Cross-reference: file number 100-26505 (the Krasuski / Detroit foo-fighter-related file) appears in handwriting on this serial; serial number 844 from FOI/PA stamp.

## Notable Incidents / Cases

- **Detroit "Crystal-Dome" Disc Sighting, April 17, 1958, ~04:00 hours** — David Weaver reports a circular object with a crystal-type dome reflecting lights, crossing Detroit three blocks south of Six Mile Road at Lamphere Street, traveling SW→N. Single-witness, brief; no confirming radar or independent corroboration documented in this serial. FBI relayed the report to USAF (Selfridge Field / Lt. Col. Prater).
