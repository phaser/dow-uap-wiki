## Key Points

- November 4, 1957: Krasuski wrote to **Robert Cutler**, Special Assistant to the President (Eisenhower), saying he "might have some information about the rocket in Texas. I heard about it on the Polish radio program. I would [not] know to whom I could speak to."
- The "rocket in Texas" reference is to the **Levelland, Texas UFO/EM-effect events of November 2-3, 1957** (multiple drivers' vehicles stalled by a luminous object).
- November 6, 1957: J. Edgar Hoover (FBI Director) teletyped SAC Detroit ordering immediate interview of Krasuski.
- November 7, 1957: SA Robert Ross Reynolds (signature on memo) interviewed Krasuski in Detroit. Krasuski:
  - Born in Poland; brought as a **Prisoner of War to Gut Alt Golssen, ~30 miles east of Berlin, Germany, in May 1942**; remained until a few weeks after end of WWII.
  - Post-war: DP camps at Kork, Strasburg, Offenburg, Mulheim, Freiburg; radio-technician school at Freiburg; ~1 year textile mill at Laurachbaden.
  - Arrived NY May 2, 1951 aboard "S.S. General Stewart" as Displaced Person; destined for Rev. Father Petro Bidalkowicz (or similar — OCR uncertain), Hamtramck, Michigan.
  - Since May 1951, employed at Gobel Brewery, Detroit.
  - News reports of the Texas engine-stall events prompted him to contact the U.S. government about a similar phenomenon he had observed in 1944 at Gut Alt Golssen.
- **The 1944 incident (Gut Alt Golssen, Germany)**:
  - During 1944 (month not recalled), while en route to work in a field "a short distance north of Gut Alt Golssen," the work crew's **tractor engine stalled** on a road through a swamp area.
  - **No machinery or other vehicle was visible**, but a **noise was heard described as a high-pitched whine similar to that produced by a large electric generator**.
  - Approximately 3 hours later, in the same area but a short distance from the road, Krasuski cautiously approached (because German "SS" guards were in the otherwise deserted enclosure).
  - In the enclosure, ~100-by-? m, screened from viewers by a tarpaulin, he observed a **vehicle**:
    - **Circular in shape, 75 to 100 yards in diameter**, approximately 14 feet high.
    - Consisting of **dark gray stationary top and bottom sections (5 to 6 feet thick)** with a **3-foot middle section** [moving / rotating — phrase OCR-uncertain].
    - A "component producing a continuous [whining sound]" (text damaged/OCR partial).
    - The vehicle was observed to **slowly rise vertically** to a height sufficient to clear a 50-foot wall and then "move slowly horizontally a short distance out of view obstructed by trees."
- Per the file's continuation, "no indication of irrational or otherwise [unusual] behavior by Krasuski was observed during the interview" — i.e., FBI did not assess him as deranged.
- The teletype summary (FBI HQ ↔ Detroit) restates the same: "OBSERVED A VEHICLE DESCRIBED AS CIRCULAR IN SHAPE, SEVENTY FIVE TO ONE HUNDRED YARDS IN DIAMETER, APPROXIMATELY FOURTEEN FEET HIGH. THE VEHICLE WAS OBSERVED TO SLOWLY RISE VERTICALLY TO HEIGHTS SUFFICIENT TO CLEAR FIFTY FOOT WALL AND TO MOVE SLOWLY HORIZONTALLY A SHORT DISTANCE OUT OF VIEW OBSTRUCTED BY TREES."

## Notable Incidents / Cases

- **Gut Alt Golssen "German Disc" Account, 1944** — Polish POW Wladyslaw Krasuski reports observing a circular vehicle 75-100 yards in diameter, 14 ft high, with stationary upper and lower disks and a rotating(?) middle section, guarded by SS, that rose vertically over a 50-ft wall and translated slowly. Tractor engine had stalled ~3 hrs earlier in the same area accompanied by a generator-like whine. Account given to FBI in 1957 after Krasuski connected it to the Levelland Texas EM-effect cases.
- **Levelland, Texas EM-Effect Events, November 2-3, 1957** — context: triggered Krasuski to come forward; not directly investigated in this file but explicitly referenced as the news report that prompted his letter.
