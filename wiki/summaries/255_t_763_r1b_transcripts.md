## Key Points

- Transcript of NASA PAO playback of an in-flight tape from **Gemini 7** (crew: Frank Borman, Jim Lovell), during the GT-7/GT-6 rendezvous mission (December 1965).
- Borman: "We have a bogey at ten o'clock high." Houston requested clarification: "Is that the booster or is that a natural sighting?"
- Borman: "We have debris up here — this is an actual sighting." Borman states: "We also have the booster in sight." So at least three categories of objects were referenced: (a) the booster, (b) the bogey at 10 o'clock high, (c) "hundreds of little particles going by to the left out about three or four miles."
- Borman estimated the particles "passed now... going into polar orbit" and the path looked like the vehicle's path at 90 degrees.
- Lovell separately reports the booster: "Its a brilliant body in the sun against a black background with trillions of particles on it... at my two o'clock position... ahead of us at two o'clock, slowly tumbling."
- PAO commentary clarifies: "the third object was a bogey... There were several references to the bogey. At 4 hrs 24 min into the flight."
- The handwritten page 2 explicitly captions the file as "UFO SIGHTING BY BORMAN (GT-7)."
- Time stamp: "05 min: 34 sec" handwritten at end (likely the runtime of the audio segment).

## Notable Incidents / Cases

- **Gemini 7 "bogey" sighting, December 1965** — Frank Borman calls out a bogey at 10 o'clock high, distinct from the booster (visible at the same time) and from the "hundreds of little particles" (typically interpreted as exhaust or insulation debris). Lovell separately observes the booster with "trillions of particles on it." This is the well-known Borman bogey incident; the transcript distinguishes carefully among the three classes of objects.
