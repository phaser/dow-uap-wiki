## Key Points

- **Folder cover (page 1)**: Manila folder with handwritten label **"5-2500 / 9-544"**, declassification stamp NND 857013, two crossed-out "SECRET" stamps. Otherwise blank.
- **Cover sheet (page 2)**: AF Form 112 Air Intelligence Information Report. Reporting agency: **US AIR ATTACHÉ** (likely Prague). Report Number: **IR 193-55**. Date of information: ~Oct 1955 (date partially redacted). Originating address: a Lt. Col. or full Col. (signature partially visible — the sender appears to be **Thomas R. Ryan, Lt. Col. USAF, Air Attaché**) at the U.S. Embassy in Czechoslovakia. The report addresses "Observations of an aircraft of the recent fast flight of an unconventional aircraft in the Trans Caucasus region of the USSR." The Air Attaché Prague is reporting **a sighting by three highly reliable United States observers who were traveling by rail at the time of the sighting**. The three observers were firmly convinced that they saw a peculiar flying saucer or flying disc. Two sightings occurred at 1521 hrs on 6 Oct 55 between **Aussig** and **Litavi(?)** — between modern Ústí nad Labem and another Czech town (place names OCR-degraded). The observers were located approximately at altitude ~6000 ft when they reported the sighting at speed; they increased speed sharply, headed north. The second sighting occurred a few minutes later. Two separate Czech aircraft were performing the same maneuvers on radar tracks taken later. The take-off area of about 5-7 miles south of the rail line where the observation [began] was discussed. (Exact wording uncertain due to OCR.)
- **Page 3**: First flying object was recognized as something drastically different — looking up to get Mr. Efron and Col. Hathaway to see it. Col. Hathaway stated that he got to a window of the train just in time to see the **first object** which had not yet reached the rail line. Col. Hathaway stated that he had only a glance at the first object as the rear coach, just shaped aircraft which passed under a little further. ([REDACTED] reports were located in the area.) — page is heavily damaged but indicates close-range visual confirmation by multiple witnesses on the train.
- **Pages 3-5 (witness statements)**:
  - Reports the sighting was made by **three observers**, all believed to be highly credible: Mr. **Efron** (probable U.S. Embassy / Foreign Service official), **Col. Hathaway** (military attaché), and a third whose name appears in OCR as "[redacted]" or "Russell."
  - Aircraft described as: "circular," "the aircraft was round" — i.e., **disc-shaped**.
  - Surface "appeared to be a smooth surface… completely smooth on top of the disc."
  - Estimated diameter and altitude given in the body of the report (specific figures partially OCR'd: ~mention of altitude ~6000 ft and a comparable rate of climb; dimensions on the order of small jet fighter).
  - The aircraft had no visible markings.
  - Light reflection: page references "diamond-bright reflection" and discusses the angle at which light reflected.
  - Pursuit/maneuver: aircraft "rose vertically" and accelerated; estimated speed "approximately 1900 km/h" (very high for 1955 jet aircraft).
  - The passengers of the train relayed the observation to the USAIRA Prague office.
- **Page 5**: References **Aussig** (Ústí nad Labem) → **Lovosice** route on Oct 5, '55 the day prior to the sighting; the train was returning between Lovosice and Russell or Bohemia toward another town. Reports identify a **military training airfield** about 25 miles from the commercial field of the sighting. The Air Attaché requested USAFE confirm whether Czech jet aircraft based at the field could have produced the sighting profile.
- **Pages 5-9 (analysis)**: USAIRA discusses Czech and Soviet aircraft profiles known to be operating from the airfield in the period (MiG-15, possibly MiG-17). Concludes that no known Czech jet aircraft type matches the observed performance: in particular, the rate of vertical climb (described as "rising vertically") and the apparent disc/circular planform are inconsistent with MiG-15 / MiG-17 / Yak series aircraft of the era.
- File is part of the "Numerical File 5-2500" sequence within RG 341 USAF intelligence collection records 1948-1955 — i.e., adjacent to [[summaries/army-intelligence-collection-1948-1955]].

## Notable Incidents / Cases

- **Czechoslovakia "Fast Aircraft" Sighting, October 6, 1955, ~1521 hrs** — observed from a train traveling between Aussig (Ústí nad Labem) and a town to the south by three U.S. witnesses (Mr. Efron, Col. Hathaway, and a third — name OCR-uncertain). Object described as a circular/disc-shaped aircraft, smooth surface, no markings, rising vertically, with apparent speed ~1900 km/h. Air Attaché Prague filed Air Intelligence Information Report IR 193-55. USAIRA's analysis ruled out known Czech / Soviet aircraft of the period.
