## Key Points

- Hunter writes that the question of policy toward extraterrestrial intelligence has "occasionally, though rarely, arisen" in BNSP Task I deliberations and offers "miscellaneous thoughts."
- Acknowledges the **scientific consensus** that the probability of running across an alien intelligent race in our solar system is "negligible" — primarily due to assumed unsuitability of conditions on other planets.
- Notes the shift in cosmology: under earlier (collision) theories of solar system formation, planetary systems were rare; under modern theory of stellar evolution, planetary systems are a "natural consequence" — "most stars would possess planetary systems, and the number of habitable planets in our galaxy would be tremendous."
- Notes biological sciences "have almost completely traced a series of natural occurrences which lead from inanimate molecules to elementary living viruses" — i.e., abiogenesis is plausible.
- Considers (somewhat tongue-in-cheek) the *flying saucer advocates'* claim that "the Martians have been mining our moon for natural resources." Calculates that a Mars-launched chemical rocket could reach Earth's moon at ~10% mass-payload efficiency at favorable conjunctions.
- Lists three "broad hints of intelligent life on the moon" (hypothetically): (1) hot gases from crater Alphonsus when the moon was thought dead, (2) infrared "hot spots" suggesting cities or mining camps, (3) failure of lunar/planetary probes "in spite of major efforts of two very successful earth orbitfaring nations" — interpreted as "someone was denying us deep space."
- Three categories of possible alien race technology:
  1. **Primitive/chemical** — could be handled with current national policy.
  2. **Nuclear-energy comparable** — at half-c possible "with what we now know about nuclear energy"; a population-pressured race expanding star-to-star with 20-year stops every 10 light-years could traverse the galaxy in ~200,000 years.
  3. **Beyond-Einstein (FTL)** — "scientifically abhorrent" but cannot be ruled out; "if we were to meet such a race, our policy had better be to negotiate fast."
- **Conclusions**: Although unlikely, the probability is "finite, and perhaps should not be completely ignored." Recommends rapid identification of any alien race's technology level, "since our policies would be affected in the most drastically possible way." A policy of "immediate burying of all Terrestrial hatchets would likely be in order."
- Tone: half-serious, half-self-deprecating ("the only body of writing on the subject available in an emergency is science fiction... no one of consequence is going to take this rubbish seriously unless it happens. At that point, our policy will be determined in the traditional manner of grand panic.")
