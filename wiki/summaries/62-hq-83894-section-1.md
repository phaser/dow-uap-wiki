## Key Points

Section 1 captures the first ~7 weeks of the FBI's engagement with the modern UFO phenomenon, beginning with the **24 June 1947 [[entities/kenneth-arnold|Kenneth Arnold]] sighting** and running through mid-August 1947. The volume documents the **formal opening of FBI / Army Air Corps cooperation on flying-disc investigations**, the **prank-and-hoax inundation problem** that nearly derailed that cooperation, multiple **canonical 1947-wave cases preserved as primary records** (Portland police 4 July, Rhodes Phoenix photographs 7 July, Turrentine Norfolk photograph 8 July, the Maury Island / Davidson-Brown / Kelso B-25 crash sequence), and the singular **"Memorandum of Importance" from San Diego** that preserved the earliest "etheric origin" theory of UFOs in the Bureau's primary file.

### The Schulgen-FBI cooperation negotiation (July 1947)

- **9 July 1947** — **Brig. Gen. George F. Schulgen, Chief, Requirements Intelligence Branch, Army Air Corps Intelligence**, formally asked **FBI Liaison Section SA S. W. Reynolds** for Bureau cooperation on flying-disc reports. Schulgen's framing was operationally serious:
  - "Air Corps has taken the attitude that every effort must be undertaken to run down and ascertain whether or not the flying disks are a fact and, if so, to learn all about them."
  - Air Corps Intelligence was "utilizing all of their scientists in order to ascertain whether or not such a phenomenon could in fact occur." Two hypotheses were on the table: "the flying objects might be a celestial phenomenon" *or* "they might be a foreign body mechanically devised and controlled."
  - Schulgen had personally interrogated, with scientists and a psychologist, an **Air Corps pilot who reported one of the objects** — pilot was "adamant in his claim that he saw a flying disk."
  - Schulgen entertained the alternative that "the first reported sightings of the so-called flying disks were fallacious and prompted by individuals seeking personal publicity, or were reported for political reasons" — including the possibility that "the first reported sightings might have been by individuals of Communist sympathies with the view to causing hysteria and fear of a secret Russian weapon."
  - Schulgen "**assured Mr. Reynolds that there are no War Department or Navy classified research projects presently being conducted which could in any way be tied up with the flying disks**." This denial — at the level of a senior AAF Intelligence officer — is preserved in the file at the exact moment when the Roswell-area events of early July 1947 were unfolding.
- **Hoover's marginal notation (24 July 1947)** on the Reynolds memo: "**I would do it but before agreeing to it we must insist upon full access to discs recovered. For instance in the Ia. case the Army grabbed it and would not let us have it for cursory examination.**" The "Ia. case" reference is striking — it implies an **Iowa flying-disc recovery the AAF retained without FBI access**, the identity of which is not further specified in this section.
- **Col. L. R. Forney (MID, War Department, Security Group)** seconded Schulgen, telling Reynolds it was his attitude "that inasmuch as it has been established that the flying disks are not the result of any Army or Navy experiments, the matter is of interest to the FBI."
- **Bureau Bulletin No. 42, Series 1947, dated 30 July 1947** formalized the cooperation: every field office was to investigate each sighting brought to its attention, distinguish bona fide reports from hoaxes / pranks / hysteria, teletype the Bureau immediately, and follow up with letter reports. *"The Army Air Forces have assured the Bureau complete cooperation in these matters and in any instances where they fail to make information available to you or make the recovered discs available for your examination, it should promptly be brought to the attention of the Bureau."*
- **Early-August 1947 internal Bureau ADDENDUM** to the cooperation memo: *"I would recommend that we advise the Army that the Bureau does not believe it should go into these investigations, it being noted that a great bulk of those alleged discs reported found have been pranks. It is not believed that the Bureau would accomplish anything by going into these investigations."* This is the institutional moment the FBI began stepping back. The hoax inundation (next section) is the immediate cause.

### The hoax inundation

A near-comic catalogue of confirmed pranks reaches the Bureau across July 1947 — each enumerated, investigated, and individually disposed:

- **Father Joseph Brasky, St. Joseph's Catholic Church, Grafton WI (6 July 1947)** — found 19-inch circular saw blade with attached wires and condensers wrapped in black tape, "approved Dunlap" stamped on it, on his church lawn after hearing a "bang." Father Brasky tried to call an FBI Agent he knew. Multiple newspapers (Milwaukee Sentinel, Milwaukee Journal, etc.) printed the story. The Milwaukee SAC's letter to the Director (8 July 1947) records that the AP reporter said the priest "had been drinking quite heavily" before granting the interview.
- **Alvin B. Parker, 200 6th Street, Laurel MD (10:45 PM, 11 July 1947)** — called WFO claiming a flying disc "had just landed in Parker's yard and the machinery is still buzzing." Sergeant Lonis of Laurel MD Police examined: "made from a Gulf Oil sign and the top of a garbage can ... painted with aluminum paint ... a dry cell battery, a flashlight bulb, some wires and a buzzer."
- **Sig Hanson, City Engineer, Black River Falls WI (10 July 1947)** — found 17" disc made of cardboard painted with silver airplane dope, fitted with a small motor and propeller; Lt Col Harry W. Schaefer, Civilian Air Patrol, said "this disc would not be able to fly by itself." Hanson initially wanted the disc returned to him for publicity.
- **East St. Louis IL (Sunday)** — J. T. Hartley, locomotive engineer, gathered up disks whirling over rooftops; railroad workers identified them as **pressed-paper locomotive packing washers** (11 inches diameter, 2-inch hole, 39-state coverage attributed to coordinated prank).
- **Birmingham AL radio station** — 400 calls in one hour about "fluorescent balls"; identified as a **carnival at Alabaster AL playing searchlights on cloud wisps**.
- **Lewiston ID** — Weatherman Louis Krezak: **"objects were moving eastward with the prevailing wind and probably were weed seeds."**
- **Lodi CA** — Mrs. F. C. Smith heard a noise "like a four-motored bomber" before a power outage. Newcomb of Pacific Gas & Electric: a low-flying crop dusting plane probably struck a power line.
- The **"Combined and Amalgamated Committee of Sky-Scanners, Disc Decipherers and New-Product Introducers"** mass-mailed satirical promotional letter (11 July 1947) forwarded to Hoover by Mrs. J. M. Clark, 41 Wright St., Westport CT, wife of Columbia University economist John Maurice Clark.

### Canonical 1947-wave cases preserved here

- **Portland Police 4 July 1947 sightings** — multi-officer 1505-hr radio-alert event. Witnesses include Patrolman Walter Lissy + Robert Ellis (Car #82, both WWII vets and civilian pilots; saw 3 disks in 30 seconds; "objects seemed erratic and changed direction"); Patrolman Karl Patterson (Car #15, former Air Corps pilot; saw aluminum-or-eggshell-white disk; "appeared radio-controlled because the disk could change direction at a 90-degree angle without difficulty"); Patrolman Kenneth A. McDowell at Precinct #1 (was feeding pigeons in the parking lot when "the pigeons became very excited over something and fluttered in the air all at once" — McDowell looked up and saw 5 disks); Harbor Patrol Capt. K. A. Prehn, Harbor Pilot A. T. Austed, Patrolman K. C. Hoff at NW Irving St; Sergeant Claude Cross, Oregon State Police at Milwaukie OR. Investigated by **SA Keith A. Sorensen, CIC, 6th Army**, with formal "Unexplained Flying Discs" memorandum dated 7 July 1947. Sorensen's note: "All informants mentioned herein are known to this agent as very dependable and trustworthy officers, not being afflicted with hallucinations." [[entities/kenneth-arnold|Kenneth Arnold]]'s (24 June 1947) and Capt. E. J. Smith / United Airlines (4 July 1947) sightings are referenced in adjacent newspaper clippings.
- **William Rhoads photographs, 7 July 1947, Phoenix AZ** — Mr. William Rhoads, 4333 N. 14th St., Phoenix, took two photographs at sunset on a 620 box camera. Object made three circles in the vicinity of Rhoads's home, height estimated 1,000 ft. Memorandum by **SA Lynn C. Aldrich, CIC-AAF, FDTRG** preserves both photographs as Exhibits 1 and 2, "enlarged approximately 20 times." See [[entities/rhodes-phoenix-photographs]].
- **Bill Turrentine photograph, 8 July 1947, Norfolk VA** — 15-year-old, 410 West 14th Street (third-floor porch), photographed an unidentified object around noon on a 1/100-second exposure. He described it as "lots bigger than an automobile", "more oval than disc-like" / "shaped like a football", "wobbled in flight", ~600 mph, ~5,000 ft, "gray, almost black, looked like a burned crisp", "trailing dust", followed by two smaller objects of the same character. Photograph published in the **Norfolk Ledger-Dispatch** and reproduced in the file. Negative furnished to Mr. Brown of Photo Craftsman Service who supplied **International News Service**. Photo experts at Photo Craftsman: "Bill did a fine job with the old camera he was using" — only flaw was the film type. Interviewed 8 August 1947 by **SA Thomas J. Connor**. **One of the earliest UAP photographs in the FBI's primary files.**
- **Frank Ryman Coast Guard photograph, Seattle** — Coast Guardsman Frank Ryman, 27, photographed "white saucer" over the north end of Lake Washington from his front porch. Acme News Pictures NY supplied a 7×9 print showing two dots, one of which was identified as a flaw in the print.
- **Maury Island / Crisman / Dahl + Kelso B-25 crash, July-August 1947** — section 1 documents the FBI's parallel-track involvement with what became the canonical **[[entities/maury-island-affair]]**:
  - **27 July 1947** — Captain William L. Davidson and Lt Frank M. Brown, 4th AAF HQ San Francisco, traveled to Portland and interviewed [[entities/kenneth-arnold|Kenneth Arnold]], Capt. E. J. Smith and co-pilot Ralph Stevens (United Airlines), Dave Johnson (Aviation Editor, Idaho Statesman), and Dick Rankin (experienced pilot, brother of the late Tex Rankin) — Rankin had reported a 10-disk formation over Bakersfield CA on 24 June 1947, sun-bathing on his lawn.
  - **1 August 1947** — Davidson + Brown's plane, returning to California, **crashed at Kelso, WA, killing both**. The Tacoma News Tribune and UP reported the plane was carrying parts of a disc that had struck a boat owned by [[entities/harold-dahl|Harold Dahl]] and Fred Crisman. AAF Intelligence at McChord Field "screened" the wreckage. Dahl subsequently denied saying the metal fragments came from a disc; AAF analysis identified them as material **"from a Tacoma slag mill."** Aviation Editor Leaverett G. Richards (Oregonian) gained the impression "AAF General intends to wash out flying disc reports since they are definitely not of AAF origin." See also FBI Seattle teletype 14 August 1947 noting Dahl did NOT admit to SAC Smith his story was a hoax — only that he intended to claim it as such if questioned.

### The Bureau and the AEC: Mrs. Gwynne M. Merchant (Santa Fe NM)

A repeat-visitor to the **Santa Fe Resident Agency** in July 1947 reporting flying objects, light-and-explosion events, and missile-like phenomena around Park View, Tierra Amarilla, and Canjilon NM (witness: Frank Burtram, Forest Ranger near Canjilon). Mrs. Merchant claimed to be "a student of radio waves and ray forms" and believed the objects "may be missiles similar to those appearing over Sweden some time ago" (a [[concepts/ghost-rockets|ghost-rocket]] reference). She had **discussed the matter with officials of the Atomic Energy Commission including Mr. Sidney Newburger, Chief of Security and Intelligence for the AEC**. Officials at Los Alamos rated Mrs. Merchant "possibly unreliable, not well balanced mentally" — she had been struck by lightning as a child. On 15 July 1947 she announced she had written to Walter Winchell promising a flying-disc story. **Significance**: Mrs. Merchant's case is the file's first explicit AEC / Los Alamos-security entanglement with the UFO topic — pre-dating any of the canonical [[concepts/atomic-energy-site-overflight|atomic-energy-site overflight]] cases.

### The "Memorandum of Importance" from 3615 Pine Place, San Diego CA (8 July 1947)

A **single-page anonymous treatise** received by the Bureau and preserved in the file. The author claims "several university degrees and was formerly a university department head." The text is striking — perhaps the **earliest preserved primary-source statement of the "etheric / interpenetrating-plane" theory of UFO origin**, in language that directly anticipates the Borderland Sciences Research Foundation / Meade Layne tradition (3615 Pine Place was Layne's San Diego address):

- "Part of the disks carry crews, others are under remote control."
- "Their mission is peaceful. The visitors contemplate settling on this planet."
- "These visitors are human-like but much larger in size."
- "They are NOT excarnate earth people, but come from their own world."
- "They do NOT come from any 'planet' as we use the word, but **from an etheric planet which interpenetrates with our own** and is not perceptible to us."
- "The bodies of the visitors, and the craft also, automatically 'materialize' on entering the vibratory rate of our dense matter. (Cp. 'apports.')"
- "The disks possess a type of radiant energy, or a ray, which will easily disintegrate any attacking ship."
- "The region from which they come is NOT the 'astral plane', but corresponds to the **Lokas or Talas**."
- "They probably cannot be reached by radio, but only by radar, if a signal system can be devised for that purpose."
- "Let the newcomers be treated with every kindness."

That this document — using Hindu cosmology vocabulary, "etheric plane" terminology, and the suggestion that the disks are an interdimensional / vibratory phenomenon rather than nuts-and-bolts spacecraft — reached the FBI's primary HQ flying-disc file *the same week* as the Schulgen-Reynolds cooperation negotiation, sets up the central tension that runs through the entire 62-HQ-83894 corpus: the Bureau as **funnel for both serious military-intelligence inquiry and pre-formed metaphysical-contactee theory**, sometimes in adjacent paragraphs.

### Other miscellaneous incidents preserved in the file

- **Charles Casella, age 20, 287 Euclid Avenue, Hackensack NJ + William Truex, US Army, Ft. Dix NJ** — late afternoon 3 August 1947, sighted disc 200 yards in air, revolving slowly, "neither a kite nor a balloon."
- **"Red" Hatfield, Navy pilot, airport manager, Myrtle Creek OR** — 6 August 1947 evening, mysterious object on two occasions while flying at 5,000 ft above Myrtle Creek.
- **Woodson C. Cope, Cliffside Hall, San Marcos TX** — telegram 7 July 1947 informing FBI he had submitted a "MULTI-SPRING CATAPULT" invention to the War Department in 1945 that "could conceivably catapult a disc and in silence for several miles" — possible information leakage. Hoover acknowledged and forwarded to War Department.
- **Edwin M. Bailey, Jr. (Stamford CT, American Cyanamid Research Labs / formerly MIT Radiation Laboratory / Manhattan Project)** — orbital atom-bomb / great-circle-line theory through Mexico City → Halifax → Paris → Yugoslavia.
- **Soviet "atom-powered planes resembling flying saucers"** — anonymous letter to LA Examiner, 6 July 1947, claiming a Russian tanker officer described 18-inch-thick kidney-shaped saucers, pilots prone, "highly polished outer surface", convex top and bottom "like a giant lens", lifting force "from an entirely different principle found about 10 years ago among unpublished papers of a Russian chemist", "energy required only for climbing... no energy needed for support when the airplane goes along the earth's gravitational contour lines"; with claimed radioactive-cloud experiments at Lake Baikal killing all life. A "top flight atomic scientist" the Examiner consulted said "not all nonsense" and recommended forwarding to FBI.
- **The Williams Field AZ "Powdered Soap Stone" sample** — registered Bureau evidence consigned to "Tilly Nixon, Route 1 Box 30, [REDACTED] South Carolina" — a soap-stone "flying-saucer" specimen referred to the FBI Lab.
- **Letter from a US Army veteran witness to a 1945-46 sighting in Germany near "Habberbishopshiem"** — descending object, oscillating descent then sudden spiral, observed from a jeep ride. The witness wrote in 1947 in retrospect after reading about the disc wave. Preserves an early-postwar [[concepts/foo-fighter|foo-fighter]]-adjacent recollection in the FBI file.

## Open Questions

- **What was Hoover's "Iowa case"?** His marginal note that "the Army grabbed it and would not let us have it for cursory examination" implies an early-July 1947 Iowa flying-disc recovery the AAF retained. Bureau index 62-HQ-83894 cross-references in surrounding sections may identify it; an external NARA RG-341 / 4th AAF cross-walk would be the natural next step.
- **The "Memorandum of Importance" attribution to Meade Layne / Borderland Sciences Research Foundation** is highly suggestive (San Diego address 3615 Pine Place, "etheric plane" / "Lokas or Talas" Hindu cosmology, "apports" parapsychology vocabulary, occult typography) but not directly confirmed by signature in the FBI file. A Meade Layne biographical archive cross-walk would close the loop.
- **The Davidson-Brown Kelso crash** has long had a coverup-allegation literature attached to it (the "AAF Intelligence screening of wreckage" line is preserved verbatim here). Whether the crash report and the Tacoma News Tribune wire-service contradictions were ever reconciled is not resolved in this section.
- **The Sidney Newburger / Los Alamos-security thread** through Mrs. Merchant connects the FBI's earliest-1947 UFO files to AEC site security at the moment when Roswell-area events would have been most operationally relevant. The volume preserves only the Merchant-side correspondence; AEC-side records on this Santa Fe-area channel would be a natural follow-up.
- **The famous [[summaries/62-hq-83894-serial-403|Guy Hottel memo]] (22 March 1950)** — frequently advertised as the most-viewed page in the FBI Vault — is **not** in Section 1; based on date it likely lives in Section 5 or as a separate serial in the same file series. The 1947-era inundation of pranks documented here is the institutional memory the Bureau drew on when filing the 1950 Hottel memo three years later.
