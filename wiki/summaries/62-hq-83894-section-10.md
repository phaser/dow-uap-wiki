## Key Points

Section 10 closes the main HQ file series, capturing the **late-1960s contactee culture** at peak, the **earliest documented FBI engagement with the Men-in-Black folklore**, the **post-Blue-Book transitional period** following Project Blue Book's December 1969 termination, and a **June 1977 Carter White House inquiry** that signals the executive-branch interest that eventually produced AARO-era frameworks. Across 11 years the FBI's posture is unwavering: "this Bureau is strictly an investigative agency... and as such, neither makes evaluations nor draws conclusions" — the line Hoover used in 1966 is repeated near-verbatim by Kelley in 1973.

### 1966 — AFSCA, Frank Edwards, the Condon study commissioning

- **31 August 1966, Goffstown NH** — Mrs. Levi J. (Florence C.) Dow, 63-yo NICAP member, writes Hoover about an [[entities/afsca-amalgamated-flying-saucer-clubs-of-america|AFSCA]] mailing she suspects of communist backing. The enclosed *Flying Saucers International* No. 24 (July 1966) included a New Age screed by "Master Kalen-Li Retan, head of the planet Korendor, about 400 light years distant", allegedly received via short-wave radio by Bob Renaud 1961-1966. Hoover's standard polite-deferral reply 6 September 1966.
- **Full speaker manifest** of the **AFSCA 3rd National Convention (8-10 July 1966, Centennial Coliseum, Reno NV)** preserved in file: Wayne S. Aho, Carl A. Anderson, Orfeo Angelucci, Michael "X" Barton, Wesley Bateman, Marianne Francis, Dr. Daniel W. Fry, Calvin C. Girvin, Gabriel Green (AFSCA editor, HQ 2004 N. Hoover St., LA 90027), Helen Green (asst. ed.), Dr. George King (Aetherius Society), Hanno Mayberry, Howard Menger, Laura Mundo, Mel Noel, Sidney Padrick, Chief Standing Horse, Dr. Frank E. Stranges, Mollie Thompson, Hope Troxell, George W. Van Tassel, Hal Wilcox.
- **9 October 1966, Atlanta GA** — anonymous citizen accuses the Air Force of "lying" about UFOs, citing "the Michigan signs" (the Hillsdale / Dexter MI swamp-gas episode), and warns of an Air Force credibility cliff "as did Nazi Germany in the 1930's".
- **19 October 1966 Bureau memo**, citing **Bureau Bulletins No. 57 (10/1/47) and No. 38 (3/25/49) titled "FLYING DISCS"**, encloses Frank Edwards' new book *Flying Saucers — Serious Business* (Lyle Stuart, Inc., 1966; Bantam paperback) and the *San Francisco Examiner & Chronicle* "This World" supplement of 16 October 1966 ("Armed Forces — Focus on UFO"). Documents the **commissioning of the Condon Committee**: "the Air Force, which is charged with the responsibility of investigating UFOs, has commissioned the University of Colorado [Physics Professor EDWARD U.] CONDON to conduct an in-depth study of flying saucers for the next 18 months, at an estimated cost of $300,000." Records Rep. **Gerald R. Ford**'s push for a congressional UFO investigation. Edwards' book is summarized at length: three basic UFO shapes (zeppelin up to 300 ft, disk a few-feet-to-100-ft, and "egg-shaped objects, which according to the author are the ones most recently sighted"); silent flight with bursts of light from underside scorching the ground; "wreckage of crashed saucers… reportedly recovered on at least three occasions" — including the Canadian-government-attributed material described as "a matrix of magnesium orthosilicate which contained thousands of 15-micron metal spheres throughout, and showing evidence of micro-meteorites on its surface."
- **21 October 1966** — free-lance writer **Moss** (4811 Illinois Ave NW, Washington DC) calls SA Thomas B. Coll asking the Bureau to identify the real name of "**Mel Noel**", an alleged former Air Force officer pseudonym to appear on Moss's TV production. Bureau declines.
- **November 1966, J.A. Hennessey letter from London** (10 Manson Place, South Kensington SW7) — NICAP / AIAA member writes Hoover requesting a copy of the Bureau's earlier 1965 reply (his copy destroyed in a fire). Hoover replies 21 December 1966 enclosing a fresh copy. **Internal note appended to the case file references the [[entities/socorro-zamora-1964|Socorro / Zamora]] sighting**: "Bufiles reflect that an unidentified flying object was sighted at Socorro, New Mexico, on April 24, 1964. Captain Holder, United States Air Force, along with SA D. Arthur Byrnes, Jr., interviewed Lonnie Zamora of the Socorro Police Department who had observed the phenomena. SA Byrnes typed such a statement from Zamora which was later included in a memo for dissemination. The Albuquerque Office did not conduct any investigation other than to keep the Bureau advised and maintain liaison with the Air Force." This is one of the earliest internal Bureau acknowledgements that the Albuquerque Office produced a Zamora witness statement — useful provenance for the Socorro record.
- **December 1966, Princeton WV** — Chief of Police Joe Thorn writes Hoover; reply 21 December 1966.
- **December 1966, Bradford PA** — schoolgirl Judith Goodman (12 Williams St) asks for "any and all information" for her science-fair project including "how many planes we have lost chasing U.F.O.s". Hoover replies 27 December 1966 deferring to USAF OSI.

### 1967 — Two extraordinary direct-witness reports (Collins, Peyerl), Sanderson UAO book, the IGAP/Adamski enclosure

- **18 January 1967, Chesapeake VA** — **[[entities/james-collins-chesapeake-1967|James Collins]] case** (212 Sharon Drive, tel. 547-4648). At 4:10 a.m., Collins reported observing a **large oblong-shaped object that "alighted in the street"** in front of him as he was walking home from his TV repair shop (Oak Grove TV, 6424 Great Bridge Road). He believed he was **"taken into this craft"** — described as made of a "glass like substance and being transparent" — and **manned by several "undersized creatures similar to members of the human race, probably not more than 4 feet tall, … wearing regular trouser pants and T-Shirts"**. Collins reports he was transported "for an undetermined distance and returned to his point of take-off approximately one hour later." He could **not account for the time between 8:00 p.m. and 4:00 a.m.** Spoke "in a coherent manner although he appeared to be under certain emotional strain"; denied drinking. Furnished to night supervisor SA A. R. Ware, Special Investigative Division. Bureau indices clean. Forwarded "for your information" — no follow-up. **Significance**: this is a structurally complete abduction-narrative report (sky-craft / small humanoid pilots / interior / missing time) that **predates the May 1968 *Look Magazine* publication of the Hill case and the popularization of "alien abduction" as a category** by 18 months — and it sits in the FBI's own files, untouched.
- **April 1967, Miami FL** — **[[entities/paul-peyerl-1967|Paul L. Peyerl]] case** (1430 SW 76th St, Miami; b. 3 May 1924, Austria; naturalized 1958; mechanic at Eastern Airlines Miami). On 26 April 1967 walked into the Miami FBI office reporting that **as a Luftwaffe test pilot in November 1944, assigned to a "top secret project in the Black Forest of Austria", he photographed a saucer-shaped craft "about twenty-one feet in diameter, radio controlled, and mounted several jet engines around the exterior portion of the craft. He further described the exterior portion as revolving around the dome in the center which remained stationary."** Photographed the craft in flight at 7,000 m (20,000 ft) and parked in a hanger "at the risk of my life". Designer **"(First Name Unknown) KUEHR, a German engineer"** allegedly apprehended in Vienna by the Gestapo late 1943 / early 1944. **Fuel/engine specs** copied from a hangar board: "Fuel mixture of N₂H₄O in Methyl Alcohol (CH₃OH) rather than 'oxygen-holding' mixture of hydrogen peroxide H₂O₂ in water. 7m, 1.3m high two rocket motors; smooth flow, rotary drive over 2,000 meters per second." Peyerl claimed the craft "was responsible for the downing of at least one American B-26 airplane." He was shot down by the British 14 March 1945, held in London then Brussels until 1946 release; arrived US 7 January 1952. **Earlier Miami visits**: 24 July 1959 (volunteered to return to Austria as US intelligence agent), 4 July 1961 (incoherent story about being asked to kill his mother). Miami SAC submitted the 1967 visit "to the Bureau for consideration of transmittal to the U. S. Air Force". **Significance**: a substantively detailed first-person Nazi-saucer / wartime-prototype claim from a named witness with verifiable wartime biography — extraordinarily rare in primary sources, even if the engineering claims are physically suspect.
- **25 July 1967, Newark NJ** — **[[entities/ivan-t-sanderson|Ivan T. Sanderson]]** (Oxford-educated naturalist, *Gary Moore Show* television guest) submits chapters 13 and 14 of his forthcoming book on **Unidentified Aerial Objects (UAO)** to the Bureau. The chapters allege impersonation of FBI Agents and military personnel by "men in black" types. Bureau finds no impersonation-statute violation; "no further action."
- **9 October 1967, Dallas TX** — anonymous young white female refuses to give her name to SA **Earl O. Cullum**. She claims a being from another planet "assumed earthly form" and met her in July 1967, departed 21 August 1967, now sends her messages. Operational claims attributed to her ET source: anti-missile missile fired at a UFO over Africa 22 May 1962 (UFO protected by force field); UFO detected 22,000 miles from Earth by radar c. 6 August 1967; UFO over Antarctica 20 August 1967; UFO over the **DEW Line** "in the past week" — shot down — outer-space beings trying to recover it; ET beings "shot down the Russian Cosmonaut" and "destroyed a moon explorer vehicle". She would meet Air Force officials only at the Dallas FBI Office; reachable only via FBI Agent's phone number. Forwarded to OSI Tinker AFB.
- **August 1967, IGAP/George Adamski enclosure** — Theodore Gray Hullett (561 37th Ave, San Francisco 94121), self-described reporter for **"ufo contact"** the IGAP (International Get Acquainted Program) journal, sends Hoover a copy. Hoover replies 24 August 1967 with a perfunctory thank-you. **The journal is preserved in the file** and is itself a primary-source artifact: it contains long testimonial material from **[[entities/george-adamski|George Adamski]]** associates **[[entities/madeleine-rodeffer|Madeleine Rodeffer]]** and **[[entities/fred-steckling|Fred Steckling]]** (with wife Ingrid), edited by Ronald Caswell (Harlow, Essex, England) and Major H. C. Petersen (Maaloev, Denmark). Embedded primary documents reproduced in this enclosure include:
  - **Letter from Lt. Col. George P. Freeman, Jr. (Chief, Civil Branch, Community Relations Division, Office of Information, Department of the Air Force, Washington 20330) to Fred Steckling, 17 March 1967** — invites Steckling to show his UFO film at the Pentagon. Quotation: "If you would get in touch with me at the Pentagon, we will arrange a showing for any Monday which is convenient for you."
  - **Steckling's account of his 20 March 1967 Pentagon visit** (Madeleine Rodeffer attended; "several high commission officers" reviewed his 7 September 1966 Mannheim-Frankfurt train footage three times); Col. Freeman's recorded reaction: "This is the best film I've seen yet" / "I have never seen anything like this before." Freeman directed Steckling to **inform the [[entities/condon-committee|University of Colorado UFO investigation committee]]**.
  - **Letter from Paul D. Lowman, Jr. (Geochemistry Laboratory, Laboratory for Theoretical Studies, NASA Goddard Space Flight Center, Greenbelt MD), File 17232, dated 20 February 1967, to Steckling** — invites Steckling and Madeleine Rodeffer to bring their films to **NASA Goddard Building A.I.** for review by the Photographic Branch. Steckling's account: 22 NASA officials viewed both films at 10:30 a.m. on 27 February 1967.
  - **El Paso Times article, 5 March 1967** — **[[entities/white-sands-march-1967|White Sands Missile Range mass UFO sighting]]**: at least 29 WSMR personnel eyewitnessed "silvery discs" between 10:30 and 11:00 a.m. on Thursday [2 March 1967]; objects estimated at 20,000–25,000 ft moving "approximately 2,000 miles per hour"; reportedly tracked on radar. Investigation taken from local control by Pentagon order; Maj. **James Stephens** at Holloman AFB designated UFO officer; Capt. Frank J. Morris (Holloman Information Officer) confirmed reports would be sent to Wright-Patterson AFB then Pentagon. National news suppression: per Terry Clark (KALG Alamogordo radio) the El Paso Times was the only paper to publish.
  - **Adamski / Soestdijk Palace audience with Queen Juliana of the Netherlands, 18 May 1959** (briefly recapitulated in the IGAP "Open Letter" addressed to "Her Majesty, Queen Juliana of the Netherlands, Soestdijk Palace, Soestdijk, Holland", June 1967, asking for a renewed audience for Adamski's surviving co-workers).
  - **"Quiet contacts"** narrative around Wilbert B. Smith (Canadian DOT engineer) and the channeled "Boys from Topside" — represented in IGAP as evidence of the contactee community's claim of a parallel, undisclosed government-engagement track.
- **Anonymous Texas letter, post-27 April 1967**, partially redacted under `(b)(7)(D)`, alleging FBI / state-government mind-control via "lie detector tests… absolutely manipulated", subliminal "hearing by sound or word, sight … blocks of intensity" — the "ESP / mind-control" tributary of mid-1960s UFO conspiracism, distinct from but adjacent to the contactee mainstream.

### 1969 — Pan Am 707 crash theory, the first MIB letter, "former CIA" walk-in

- **19 March 1969, Mount Holly NJ** — **[[entities/edward-stewart-jr|Edward A. Stewart, Jr.]]** (152 Mount Holly Ave) writes Hoover. Stewart claims that on **7 December 1963 at Moorestown NJ** he observed an unusual object; ~24 hours later, on **8 December 1963 near Elkton MD**, **[[entities/pan-am-707-elkton-1963|Pan American Boeing 707-121 N709PA]]** with 73 passengers and 8 crew exploded mid-air and crashed (CAB report File 1-0015 attributed cause to lightning strike, March 1965 release). Stewart's theory: the object he saw "was laying air mines" and the aircraft "was destroyed by contact with one of these mines." He cites pp. 8 lines 20-25 of the CAB report describing **"metal splatters on the leading edge of the horizontal stabilizer… formed from two different aluminum alloys… [that] could not be identified since the chemical composition of the deposits did not conform to any alloy used in the airplane."** Cross-referenced with a 18 July 1964 sighting Stewart sent to **Harvard College Observatory** (Richard B. Southworth replied 28 July 1964 attributing the second sighting to a meteor / bolide). Stewart's overall theory escalates: UFOs are **"cleverly designed missiles camouflaged to appear as a Meteor or as a Flying Saucer"** sent over both Americas by Russia, evading US radar by exploiting the "scoff at flying saucers" reaction. Letter copied to: **President Nixon, J. Edgar Hoover, Sen. Case (NJ), Sen. Charles H. Percy (IL), Melvin Laird (Secretary of Defense)**. The Pan Am 707 → Russian-mine theory is unique in this corpus.
- **30 April 1969, Belleville NJ** — Lon M. Cerame (77 Gless Ave), "Eastern Representative — National Investigations Committee on Unidentified Flying Objects" (a small NICAP-adjacent group, distinct from NICAP itself), inquiry. Standard deferral.
- **14 May 1969, Del City OK** — **[[entities/larry-stephens-mib-letter-1969|Larry Stephens]]** (Rt. 12 Box 206, 73115) writes Hoover asking about "rumors over the grapevine and in print that suggest **men with oriental features wearing dark clothes go around terrorizing people who have had close-up views of UFO's**. It is also rumored these creatures have impersonated armed forces officers and FBI investigators. They are supposed to ride around in black or dark automobiles that either have old licence tags or none at all. Several are reported to have attempted to run down witnesses of UFO sightings, made disturbing, almost macabre phone calls, silenced several investigators who were supposed to have learned some dark secret about extraterrestrial craft or mission plans and opened mail, tapped telephones and even taken pictures of several homes where UFO witnesses lived." This is the **earliest documented FBI inquiry on the [[concepts/men-in-black|Men-in-Black]] folklore** in this corpus, and one of the earliest known anywhere in primary records — predating the broad popularization of the trope but coinciding with Sanderson's UAO book and Albert Bender / Gray Barker contactee literature. Hoover's reply 21 May 1969 (via Helen W. Gandy): "the FBI has no information it can send to you regarding the rumors you have heard." Forwarded to OSI USAF.
- **16 May 1969, Compton CA** — **Forrest E. Wood** (4267 E. Compton Boulevard; tel. 635-6372). Telephoned the Bureau at 12:50 a.m. and spoke with SA Stephen F. McInerney, Special Investigative Division. Claimed to be a **"former CIA Agent"** and "wanted to report to the Director that recently he has been seeing flying objects." Tone "appeared he was intoxicated"; Wood "advised that he was recently discharged as a patient from the psycho ward of a Veteran's hospital." Bureau indices file 66-3182-8679 reflects an earlier 1 May 1969 rambling call from a possibly-identical individual. Liaison Section's **SA Sam Papich** caused a complete CIA-indices search for Wood — **negative results**.
- **8 October 1969, Ajax Ontario, Canada** — Paul Brassington, Stephen Smith (13), Gary Hargreaves (12) — UFO Astronomer Club (94 Dreyer Drive). Wrote Hoover asking about a magazine *UFOS A Pictorial* picture allegedly showing "two FBI Agents... leading a person from outer space down the street", first published in a German newspaper. Hoover's 13 October 1969 reply: "the photograph you mentioned does not represent employees of this Bureau."

### 1972 — Pentagon-leak threat, schoolgirl letter

- **15 February 1972, Westmoreland City PA** — Mary Beth Martine (Box 463 Oak St, 15692), one-line "your opinion on U.F.O.'s". Hoover replies 23 February 1972; this is one of his last UFO replies — Hoover died 2 May 1972.
- **20 June 1972, anonymous letter to The Pentagon** (received and routed to FBI). Author claims that **"in approximately seven months or January, 1972 [sic — date inconsistency in the original; likely should read 'January 1973'], certain copies of top-secret documents shall be sent to the New York Times as well as two other newspapers."** The documents will purportedly demonstrate Pentagon UFO involvement: "It will show that not only the U.S. Air Force was involved in UFO research but the other military branches as well." Cites **"General Wolfe... when he was head of the Army's UFO support program in the Pentagon during the Eisenhower years"**. The author explicitly compares the planned leak to the **Pentagon Papers** ("we feel that the secret UFO investigations are parallel in nature to the Times-Pentagon Vietnam controversy"). No follow-up; no such leak materialized publicly.

### 1973 — Ozanne / Stigliano case, post-Hoover continuity under Kelley

- **9 March 1973, Waco TX** — **Sgt. Paul Stigliano**, USAF Recruiting Office (Professional Building, Waco) reports to SA Richard T. Jesinger that a man identifying himself as **"Dave T. Ozanne"** had contacted the Waco Tribune Newspaper night supervisor inquiring about local UFO sightings. Ozanne was **wearing a USAF uniform with a chaplain's badge** and carried a **folder marked "Top Secret" containing pictures of various military installations**. Address on his card: **3911 Bowser Apt 209, Dallas TX**, phone 526-5908. "Did not act in any strange manner, and did not attempt to obtain anything other than information from the newspaper." Bureau forwarded to military.
- **23 October 1973, La Habra CA** — Ron Praide (130 East Francis Avenue, 90631) handwritten letter (heavily OCR-noisy) about UFOs being seen on the Atlantic and Pacific coasts and a reference to Eisenhower. Reply from **Director Clarence M. Kelley** in the same boilerplate language Hoover used: not within FBI jurisdiction.

### 1974 — FOI request from a UFO researcher; Milwaukee object recovery

- **31 March 1974, Arlington VA** — **[[entities/larry-w-bryant-1963-1974|Larry W. Bryant]]** (Apt 2, 2904 South 13th Road, 22204) writes Director Kelley referencing his own 17 September 1963 FBI interview **at Ft. Eustis VA by SA John S. Castles** (Newport News VA contingent), invoking Nixon's recent privacy / civil-liberties statements to request all FBI files on his UFO-research activities. Bufile 62-115530 ("FOI-REPLIES"). Kelley replies 15 April 1974 furnishing only "copies of prior correspondence with you and a record of your September 17, 1963, interview." Note: the 1963 interview "was initiated because of voluminous correspondence which Bryant had sent to Federal and military as well as law enforcement agencies." Bryant cc'd the **ACLU Washington DC office**.
- **22 August 1974, Milwaukee WI** — at 12:07 a.m., **Major Horn at the National Military Command Center (NMCC)** called FBI Security Patrol Clerk Charles F. Halty (Intelligence Division) asking about a report that "an unidentified object which fell from the sky at Milwaukee, Wisconsin, had been recovered by local police and turned over to the Milwaukee Office of the FBI." Night Duty Supervisor Harry E. Morris, Jr., contacted SPC Koller (Milwaukee FBI Office), who confirmed: at ~5:55 p.m. on 21 August 1974, **Captain Wills of the Milwaukee Police Department** had recovered an object — **"about 13 × 8 × 5 inches, metallic in substance and color, jagged on one side and had an 'internal heat source.'"** Police notified the local military. UPI carried the story morning of 22 August 1974. NMCC was advised to contact Milwaukee PD direct. **No further FBI action**; the object's eventual disposition is not in this file.

### 1977 — Project Blue Book Fact Sheet; Carter White House inquiry

- **14 January 1977**, Lt. Col. **H. A. McClanahan** (Chief, Civil Branch, Community Relations Division, Office of Information, Department of the Air Force) sends FBI's Mr. Malmfeldt a **UFO Fact Sheet**:
  - Project Blue Book terminated 17 December 1969 by the Secretary of the Air Force on the basis of the **Condon Committee report** ("Scientific Study of Unidentified Flying Objects", 3 vol., 1,465 p., 68 plates, NTIS AD 680:975 / 976 / 977), the National Academy of Sciences review (NTIS AD 688:541), past USAF studies, and post-1948 USAF investigation experience.
  - Three Blue Book conclusions: (1) no UFO ever indicated a threat to national security; (2) no evidence that "unidentified" sightings represented "technological developments or principles beyond the range of present-day scientific knowledge"; (3) no evidence that "unidentified" sightings were extraterrestrial vehicles.
  - **Total UFO sightings 1947–1969: 12,618** across 23 years. Per-year totals (extracted from the Fact Sheet table): 122, 156, 186, 210, 169, 1,501 (1952 — peak year, 303 unidentified), 509, 487, 545, 670, 1,006, 627, 390, 557, 591, 474, 399, 562, 887, 1,112, 937, 375, 146.
  - All Blue Book documentation transferred to the **Modern Military Branch, National Archives and Records Service**, 8th & Pennsylvania Ave NW, Washington DC 20408.
  - Donald W. Moore, Jr. (Asst. Director, External Affairs Division) acknowledged receipt 24 January 1977.
- **14 June 1977** — **Stanley Schneider** (Assistant to the Director, **Office of Science and Technology Policy, Executive Office of the President**) telephones J. Cochran (FBI). Schneider's question: "what the Bureau's procedure is for handling information received from the public… relating to sightings of UFOs", and "whether or not there is any coordination within the Executive Branch relating to information concerning this subject." Schneider explicitly attributes the inquiry to **Jody Powell** (Carter's Press Secretary). The 14 June memo records Cochran's response: "there appears to be no conceivable jurisdiction for us to conduct any inquiries upon receipt of information relating to a UFO sighting and, in the absence of some investigative jurisdiction… that information would be referred to the Department of the Air Force." On 15 June 1977 the FBI sent Schneider a copy of the **FBI Law Enforcement Bulletin, February 1975, page 16**, which contained an article on the handling of UFO sightings.

## Open Questions

- The **James Collins (Chesapeake VA, January 1967)** abduction-pattern report sits in the FBI's own files but does not appear to have surfaced in the popular abduction-canon literature. Was this case ever followed up by USAF OSI or any civilian researcher?
- The **Paul Peyerl (Miami, April 1967)** Nazi-saucer photographs: did the Bureau forward the materials and Xerox negatives to USAF OSI as recommended? The KUEHR engineering claims (rotary-drive 2,000 m/s tip-speed, twin H₂O₂/CH₃OH rocket-motor saucer-platform) are physically problematic — but the witness-biographical details (Luftwaffe service, March 1945 capture by British, naturalization 1958) are checkable.
- The **June 1972 anonymous Pentagon-leak letter** names "General Wolfe" as Eisenhower-era head of an Army UFO support program. "General Wolfe" does not match a widely-known Army Pentagon UFO figure. Either a misremembered name, a wartime-only colonel, or an unrecognized lead worth tracing.
- The **August 1974 Milwaukee object** ("13 × 8 × 5 inches, metallic, internal heat source"): no follow-up in this file. NMCC's interest is itself notable — the Pentagon's command-and-control center inquiring within hours of a local police object recovery suggests pre-existing attention rather than ad-hoc curiosity. Does the Milwaukee FBI office's separate file (62-MK series) contain the object's eventual disposition?
- The **June 1977 Carter White House inquiry** triggered by Jody Powell — was there a written response beyond the FBI Law Enforcement Bulletin reprint? OSTP records of this period would be the natural cross-reference.
