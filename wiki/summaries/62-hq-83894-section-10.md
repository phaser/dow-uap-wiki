## Key Points

- Section 10 closes the main HQ file series, capturing the **late-1960s contactee culture** explosion and the FBI's continuing posture of polite non-involvement.
- **Mrs. Levi J. (Florence C.) Dow letter, Goffstown NH (31 August 1966)** — 63-year-old NICAP member writes Hoover about a UFO-themed mailing she received from "AFSCA" (Amalgamated Flying Saucer Clubs of America, 2004 N. Hoover St, Los Angeles). She suspects it of communist backing; specifically the article in *Flying Saucers International* (Issue No. 24, July 1966) titled "BRIGHT WORLD IN HANDS OF SAUCER MOVEMENT" by **"Master Kalen-Li Retan, head of the planet Korendor**, about 400 light years distant," allegedly received via short-wave radio by **Bob Renaud** in 1961-1966.
  - The article is a New Age screed against the "Military-Industrial complex," names the JFK assassination as retaliation for opposing it, claims the Vietnam War is being fought to prevent depression, advocates "Universal Economics" (a money-less system).
  - Hoover replies (6 September 1966) declining to evaluate the publication: "this Bureau is strictly an investigative agency... and, as such, neither makes evaluations nor draws conclusions." Bufiles contain no record of correspondent or AFSCA.
- **AFSCA / Gabriel Green details (from the *Flying Saucers International* Issue No. 24 enclosed):**
  - Editor Gabriel Green, Asst. Ed. Helen Green; HQ 2004 N. Hoover St., Los Angeles 90027; phone 662-4404.
  - 3rd National Convention scheduled for 8-10 July 1966, Centennial Coliseum, Reno NV.
  - Speaker roster includes **Wayne S. Aho, Carl A. Anderson, Orfeo Angelucci, Michael "X" Barton, Wesley Bateman, Marianne Francis, Dr. Daniel W. Fry, Calvin C. Girvin, Gabriel Green, Dr. George King (Aetherius Society), Hanno Mayberry, Howard Menger, Laura Mundo, Mel Noel, Sidney Padrick, Chief Standing Horse, Dr. Frank E. Stranges, Mollie Thompson, Hope Troxell, George W. Van Tassel, Hal Wilcox.**
  - Issue obituaries: Dr. Raymond Bernard ("Hollow Earth"), Della Larson, Wilbur Miller, Maria Elliott. Notable mentions of **Mel Noel** (former AF Lt. fighter pilot lecturer); **Sid Padrick** (Watsonville CA, "two hours aboard a space craft"); Bob Renaud's Korendor radio contacts in *UFOI* issues #18-22.
- **Dallas FBI office, anonymous female informant (9 October 1967)** — a young white female refused to give her name to SA Earl O. Cullum at the Dallas FBI office. She claimed:
  - In July 1967 she met "a being from another planet who had assumed earthly form"; he gave her information then was "picked up and departed from the earth on August 21, 1967"; she now receives messages from non-earthly sources.
  - Specific operational claims attributed to her ET source:
    - An anti-missile missile was fired at a UFO over Africa on 22 May 1962 — UFO protected by "force field"
    - A UFO was detected 22,000 miles from Earth by radar on ~6 August 1967
    - A UFO was detected over Antarctica 20 August 1967
    - A UFO was detected over the "Dewline" in the past week and was shot down; outer-space beings are trying to recover it
    - Beings from outer space "shot down the Russian Cosmonaut" and "destroyed a moon explorer vehicle"
  - She would meet Air Force officials only at the Dallas FBI Office; reachable only via an FBI Agent's phone number. Forwarded to OSI Tinker AFB; no further FBI action.
- **AFSCA Issue No. 24 contains substantive UFO press-clipping section** (May 1966 wave): Massachusetts Highway Patrol pursuit (March 8 1966); Wanaque NJ UFO over reservoir (June 1966); Athens GA disc; Ann Arbor "swamp gas" sightings; Buffalo NY; Westchester NY (April 19 1966); Memphis TN (April 1966); Dexter MI (March 25 1966); Goldsboro NC; Hillsdale College sightings; London ON Canada; Ravenna OH (April 17 1966); West Sand Lake NY; etc. — collected as raw press for IFSB-style movement.

## Notable Incidents / Cases

- **September 1966, Goffstown NH** — Florence C. Dow citizen letter / Hoover reply: documents the FBI's policy of staying entirely out of evaluating UFO clubs.
- **8-10 July 1966, Reno NV** — AFSCA 3rd National Convention; full speaker manifest of the contactee scene preserved in file.
- **9 October 1967, Dallas TX** — anonymous "ET messenger" walks into FBI Dallas with detailed operational claims (Africa anti-missile, Antarctica radar, DEW Line shootdown).
- The 1966 spring "press clippings" wave preserved in the AFSCA enclosure documents (in aggregate) a major cluster of reported sightings across the eastern US.

## Open Questions

- The October 1967 Dallas anonymous-female "ET messenger" makes very specific claims — was any of her information later confirmed via AF channels? The forwarded LHM gives no follow-up.
- Section 10 effectively closes the HQ file. After 1967 the FBI evidently stops accumulating in 62-HQ-83894; do later 1970s+ matters live in successor files?
- The FBI's posture across this section — "thank you for writing, here is some literature about the Bureau, your matter has been referred to the Air Force" — became the Bureau's enduring public stance, surviving through the dissolution of Project Blue Book in 1969.
