## Key Points

- This section is the FBI's working binder during the period 30 July 1947 – early September 1947, when **Bureau Bulletin No. 42 (30 July 1947)** instructed all field offices to interview every flying-disc complainant and forward results, in coordination with Army Air Forces A-2.
- Joint FBI / A-2 investigation activity is heavy: SAC San Francisco (Hamilton Field) is the principal liaison with Lt. Col. Springer, A-2 Fourth Air Force; reports from Portland, Phoenix, Newark, and Detroit are routed through it.
- **Hackensack, NJ disc sighting (3 August 1947, ~7:30-7:45 pm)** — Charles Casella, Jr. (287 Euclid Avenue) and Pvt. William A. Truex (Fort Dix, 9th Infantry Division), watching a ham antenna on Simons Avenue, observe a round black/silver-blue object roughly 30-40 inches in diameter moving fast and steady from south to north; Joyce McFarland (Truex's girlfriend) corroborates partially. SA Arthur F. Williams of Newark Division interviews all three plus Mrs. Winfield S. Booth (a porch-witness across the street who saw nothing).
- **Lane / Dow Chemical "puff of fire" incident (Midland, MI, 9-10 July 1947)** — Raymond Edward Lane (Dow employee since 1922) and his wife Laura Lane report seeing a bushel-basket-sized "ball of white" burning a foot off the ground; collect fused sand. Dow Physics Laboratory examines material — ordinary sand emitting ammonia gas, with low radioactivity in some grayish portions. Lane has prior burglary conviction (1924, Midland Co.) and access to luminous radioactive paint; Bureau lab interest debated.
- **Richard Shaver lead (Lily Lake, IL)** — an unsigned telegram to Maj. Paul Gaynor at AAF HQ (5 July 1947, NYC) recommends contacting Richard F. Shaver as having "important information concerning the origin" of the flying disks. Shaver was the pulp-fiction author behind the "Shaver Mystery" published in Amazing Stories. The War Department asks the FBI to investigate Shaver. The Chicago Field Division is tasked.
- **Mrs. Mary "Madeline Gwynne Merchant" letter, Santa Fe NM (13 July 1947)** — eccentric correspondent claims direct contacts with Col. Herbert C. Gee (Communications Officer, Los Alamos), Maj. Sidney Newburger (Security, Los Alamos), Willard Zimmer (Military Intelligence, Santa Fe), Gen. S. R. Brennault (asst. to Gen. Nathan F. Twining, AAF Materiel Command), Col. Sumwalt (Kirtland Field), and Gen. Patrick Hurley; passes the material via Walter Winchell. She insists she has been "reporting directly to Dr. Norris Bradbury, Scientific Head of Los Alamos" that this aerial "disc" is real. Bureau treats her as unreliable but keeps the letter on file.
- **Guam sighting, Harmon Field (14 August 1947, 1040 hrs)** — three enlisted men of the 147th Airways and Air Communications Service Squadron observe two crescent-shaped objects flying twice the speed of fighter aircraft on a zig-zag westerly course at ~1200 ft; a third identical object emerges from clouds seconds later.
- **Mrs. J. M. Mixon, Estill, SC (14 August 1947)** — sends FBI Lab a sample of "part of a flying saucer"; analysis (T. F. Baughman / R. T. Harbo, FBI Lab) identifies it as **powdered soap stones**. Material returned via SAC Cincinnati.
- **Robert Little / Chicago Herald-American (8 August 1947)** — Mrs. Robert Little, 6327 Harper Ave Chicago, reports disc sailing above Lake Michigan off Jackson Park Beach at 3:30 am; her father-in-law Joseph Reiner says she had also seen a disc more than a month earlier.
- **William Albert Rhodes, Phoenix AZ (Phoenix file 62-213, 7 July 1947 sighting; investigated August 1947)** — engineer/inventor at Panoramic Research Laboratory, 4333 N 14th St, photographed a circular metal aircraft (estimated 20-30 ft diameter, ~100 mph, low-pitched P-80-like sound) making spirals over Phoenix at 5 pm. Two photographs (designated Exhibits I and II) survive. **The negatives were turned over to George Fugate, Jr. of A-2 Fourth Air Force on 30 August 1947**, with Rhodes consenting only after being told by SA J. Bailey Brower that he would likely never get them back. The Rhodes photos are among the most-cited 1947 saucer images.
- **Sept 17, 1947 Vermont sighting** — A. Courtney Parker, Vermont State Dept of Education superintendent at South Royalton, watching honeybees on Rix Ledges north of Lebanon NH, reports observing two objects (a "tennis-ball" white and a "saucer" trailing it at constant inclination) at 3:40 pm.

## Notable Incidents / Cases

- **3 Aug 1947, Hackensack NJ** — Casella/Truex/McFarland sighting (object ~3 ft diameter, oval-on-top point-on-bottom).
- **9-10 Jul 1947, Midland MI** — Lane "burning ball" / fused-sand incident at Dow Chemical company field.
- **7 Jul 1947, Phoenix AZ** — William Rhodes photographs (Exhibits I and II) — first widely circulated US saucer photographs; negatives surrendered to Army A-2.
- **14 Aug 1947, Guam** — three crescent-shaped objects sighted by 147th AACS Sqdn enlisted men over Harmon Field.
- **17 Sep 1947, near Lebanon NH** — A. C. Parker observes paired ball-and-saucer objects.
