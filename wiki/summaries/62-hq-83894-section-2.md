## Key Points

Section 2 is the **FBI's working binder** during the period when **Bureau Bulletin No. 42 (30 July 1947)** instructed all field offices to investigate flying-disc reports under AAF cooperation. The single most consequential preserved artifact is the **complete Kenneth Arnold first-person statement and the 4th AF CIC interview record** — material that is foundational to the entire 1947-wave canon and which sits inside the Bureau's HQ file in primary form.

### The Kenneth Arnold packet (Brown CIC interview, 16 July 1947)

- The 4th AF A-2 enclosure forwarded by Lt Col **Donald L. Springer** (Hamilton Field) to FBI San Francisco includes the **interview by SA Frank M. Brown, CIC 4th AF**, dated 16 July 1947. Brown's signature on the cover page survives — Brown died **15 days later** in the Kelso WA B-25 crash documented in Section 1, alongside Capt. William L. Davidson. The Brown report is therefore among the very last AAF Counter-Intelligence Corps work-product Brown produced before his death.
- **Brown's verbatim "Agent's Notes" on Arnold**: "Mr. Arnold is a man of 32 years of age, being married and the father of two children. He is well thought of in the community in which he lives... It is the personal opinion of the interviewer that Mr. Arnold actually saw what he stated that he saw. It is difficult to believe that a man of Mr. Arnold's character and apparent integrity would state that he saw objects and write up a report to the extent that he did if he did not see them. To go further, if Mr. Arnold can write a report of the character that he did while not having seen the objects that he claimed he saw, it is the opinion of the interviewer that Mr. Arnold is in the wrong business, that he should be writing Buck Rogers fiction." Brown explicitly checks Arnold's distance / speed / shape claims against an aeronautical map: "all statements made by Mr. Arnold in regard to the distances involved, speed of the objects, course of the objects and size of the objects, could very possibly be facts."
- **Arnold's reciprocal disposition**, also recorded by Brown: "Mr. Arnold is very outspoken and somewhat bitter in his opinions of the leaders of the U.S. Army Air Forces and the Federal Bureau of Investigation for not having made an investigation of this matter sooner." Arnold added: "if I, at any time in the future, saw anything in the sky, to quote Mr. Arnold directly, 'if I saw a ten story building flying through the air I would never say a word about it', due to the fact that he has been ridiculed by the press to such an extent that he is practically a moron in the eyes of the majority of the population of the United States."
- **Arnold's full first-person statement of the 24 June 1947 sighting** is in the file, signed:
  - 24 June 1947 (Tuesday). Took off from Chehalis WA airport ~1400, looking for a missing marine transport "supposedly went down near or around the southwest side of Mt. Rainier in the state of Washington and to date has never been found."
  - Climbed to ~9,500 ft over Mineral WA. Made a 360° turn over the city of Mineral; trimmed out toward Yakima.
  - DC-4 to his left and rear, ~15 mi distance, ~14,000 ft.
  - First sighted "**a chain of nine peculiar looking aircraft flying from north to south at approximately 9,500 feet elevation and going, seemingly, in a definite direction of about 170 degrees.**"
  - Used his **eight-day clock sweep second hand** as a precise stopwatch: "the first object of this formation passed the southern edge of Mt. Rainier" at one minute to 3 PM; the last unit "passed the southern most high snow-covered crest of Mt. Adams" 1 minute 42 seconds later. Distance Mt. Rainier → Mt. Adams ≈ ~50 miles, giving the canonical ~1,700-mph estimate.
  - "**They flew like many times I have observed geese to fly in a rather diagonal chain-like line as if they were linked together.**"
  - Estimated chain length: 5 miles (length of an intermediate ridge that the formation spanned simultaneously).
  - Object form: "they were just a black thin line and when they flipped was the only time I could get a judgment as to their size... when the sun reflected from one or two or three of these units, they appeared to be completely round."
  - **Drawing dimensions Arnold furnished**: "they seemed longer than wide, their thickness was about 1/20th of their width", color "mirror bright", "they did not appear to me to whirl or spin but seemed in fixed position".
  - Arnold consulted his **Yakima friend Al Baxter** ("listened patiently and was very courteous but in a joking way didn't believe me") and was given more credit by **pilots at Pendleton OR** including **Sonny Robinson, former Army Air Forces pilot, now operating dusting operations at Pendleton**: "What you observed, I am convinced, is some type of jet or rocket propelled ship that is in the process of being tested by our government or even it could possibly be by some foreign government."
  - Arnold's plane: a Callair (license 333487; airframe 33355), purchased January 1947 for high-altitude / short-rough-field work in his fire-control supply business.
  - Arnold's autobiographical sketch (preserved in full): born **March 29, 1915, Subelas Minnesota**; family homesteaded at Scobey Montana; grade and high school at Minot ND; Eagle Scout; entered **U.S. Olympic fancy-diving trials in 1932**; Red Cross Life Saving Examiner 1932-34; University of Minnesota under coach Bernie Bierman; in 1938 went to **Red Comet Inc. of Littleton CO** (automatic fire-fighting apparatus); 1940 founded his own **Great Western Fire Control Supply**, covering five western states from a Boise base.
- **David N. Johnson interview, 12 July 1947** — Aviation Editor, *Idaho Daily Statesman*, Boise. Brown also interviewed Johnson, an experienced pilot (2,800 hours; B-29 first-pilot, Twentieth USAAF, Tinian Island during the war) who knew Arnold and had been assigned by the Statesman editor to fly the paper's airplane and "exhaust all efforts to prove or disprove the probability of flying disks having been seen in the northwest area."

This packet — Brown's report, Arnold's first-person statement, Johnson's corroborating interview, and SAC San Francisco Kimball's recommendation that the **FBI Butte Office contact Arnold to explain "our lack of jurisdiction in such matters"** — is the FBI's foundational primary-source dossier on the originating modern UFO sighting.

### William Albert Rhodes Phoenix interview, 30 August 1947

The Phoenix office's full interview record (Phoenix file 62-213) preserves details substantially beyond what the existing summary captured:

- **William Albert Rhodes**, 4333 N 14th Street, Phoenix AZ; date of birth 12-29-1916 (30 yo at sighting); 6'3", 155 lbs; blue eyes, brown hair; Phoenix Union HS; married, one son.
- **Panoramic Research Laboratory and Hobby Shop** owner.
- Sighting on **7 July 1947 ~5 PM** during/after a Phoenix afternoon storm with clouds at ~5,000 ft. Walking from home to hobby shop; heard a noise "resembling a P-80 aircraft" coming from a westerly direction.
- Object NE of his position: **20-30 ft diameter, ~100 mph, spiraling in 1/2 to 3/4 mi radius**.
- Used a **120 Brownie Box Camera** retrieved from his shop. Photographed twice: Exhibit I as object completed first circle, Exhibit II as it banked right and disappeared into clouds.
- At final point ~1,000-2,000 ft altitude.
- Rhodes's described comparison: "**closely resembling a picture which appeared on the front cover of the May, 1947, issue of *Mechanics Illustrated Magazine***" — the only difference being "the ship appeared to be flying backward." This is a striking pre-figuration: a flying-disc image was on a popular-science magazine's cover *six weeks before* the Arnold sighting was publicized.
- Rhodes's career: Naval Ordnance Laboratory Washington DC during early WWII (left early 1942); then **Falcon Field near Phoenix** "leased by the British Government for training purposes" — Rhodes "instructed in instrument training, aircraft identification and gunnery practice." Hobbies: science, physics, radio, electronics, astronomy. Built a model of a radio-controlled airplane.
- The negotiation over the negatives is documented in operational detail:
  - **George Fugate Jr.** of A-2 4th Air Force called at the Phoenix office on **29 August 1947** with credentials and orders from Hamilton Field directing him to interview Rhodes. ASAC **Heber M. Clegg** (Phoenix) confirmed by phone with **SAC Harry M. Kimball** (San Francisco) that the FBI was to assist.
  - **SA J. Bailey Brower** of Phoenix accompanied Fugate. **At Fugate's request, Brower was introduced to Rhodes only as "a representative of the United States government" rather than as FBI** — Brower notes: "he felt it was a peculiar procedure, but it was not his business."
  - At interview's end Fugate asked for Rhodes's negatives. Rhodes asked whether they would be returned. Fugate "made no reply." After leaving, Brower asked Fugate directly; Fugate said he "doubted very much that they would be returned." Brower then advised Fugate that **before he, Brower, would accept these negatives, Rhodes must be advised of Mr. Fugate's identity AND that the negatives would not be returned**. On the morning of **30 August 1947**, when Rhodes called at the Phoenix FBI office to deliver the negatives, "they were accepted only after he was advised that they were being given to Mr. Fugate, a representative of the Army Air Force Intelligence, United States Army, and that there was little, if any, chance of his getting the negatives back. Mr. Rhodes turned the negatives over to this office with the full understanding that they were being given to the Army and that he would not get them back."
  - **Phoenix office did NOT receive the Bureau teletype** instructing that no joint investigation should be conducted **until after the matter had been handled** — a procedural breach noted in the Phoenix SAC report.

This is the cleanest documentary record in the wiki of how a key 1947-wave photographic primary source moved from civilian custody to AAF Intelligence custody, with the deliberate non-disclosure of FBI involvement.

### "Radio Ham" coded newspaper-clipping decoded by FBI Lab

A novel item the existing summary missed: **Mrs. A. G. Sarbanis, 66 Notre Dame Avenue, Hicksville (Long Island) NY** sent the Director on **10 July 1947** a copy of a coded message that had been published as a "letter to the editor" feature in *Newsday* (Nassau / Suffolk County). Mrs. Sarbanis decoded it; the FBI Laboratory checked her decoding and confirmed it as substantively correct. The Lab's preserved decoding:

> **"TIRED OF HUMAN [WAR?]. I AWAIT ATOMIC WAR DISRUPTING ORDER... I [ALSO?] SENT FLYING DIS[CS]... [WILL?] SET UP WORLD ORDER UNDER MARTIANS [?] [THIS YEAR]"**

NY Office (SA William A. Johnson) interviewed Mrs. Sarbanis 12 August 1947, then City Editor **Jack Altschul** of *Newsday* (Hempstead NY). Altschul: the original letter to the editor was signed only "**Radio Ham**", he had no way of identifying the writer, and his opinion was "the code had been made up by some local 'screwball' who had been affected by all of the newspaper publicity that had been given at that time to the story about flying discs." The FBI Lab subsequently advised that the FCC maintained amateur-radio records by geographical breakdown and "could possibly get the names of all amateurs in the Bellmore, Long Island, New York Section" — but no further action was taken. *A 1947-era purported Martian-origin claim, decoded by the FBI Lab and preserved in primary form*.

### Twin Falls ID multi-formation event (Aug 19, 1947) — full Banister teletype

The same case covered in [[summaries/army-incident-summaries-1-100|Army Incident 64]] reached the FBI as well via a **Butte SAC R. M. Banister teletype**:

- Witnesses Mr. and Mrs. H. H. Hedstrom (3rd Avenue North, Twin Falls), Mrs. Schultz, then Det. **Richard Frazier**, M. E. **Roundtree**, Richard **Scott** (Twin Falls PD).
- Sequence: 1 → triangle of 10 → triangle of 3 → another triangular formation → "large group of objects estimated at between thirty five and fifty" → groups of 3, 5, 7 returning SW.
- Banister's editorial line (preserved verbatim): "**It is believed continued appearance of such objects without official explanation may result in hysteria or panic Twin Falls, Idaho**."
- **AAF response (FBI URGENT teletype to SAC Butte, 25 August 1947)**: "Extensive inquiries at Army Air Forces reveal that no research or experiments were being conducted by AAF in the vicinity of Twin Falls, Idaho on or about August nineteen." Lt Col **G. D. Garrett Jr.** (Air Forces Intelligence) personally followed up with the Army area; both denied research.

### Hatfield / Ellison Myrtle Creek Oregon (6 August 1947)

A pilot-witness case the existing summary did not surface:

- **Ray Hatfield** (Tri-City Airport operator, Myrtle Creek OR; former Lt JG **U.S. Naval Air Corps Atlantic submarine patrol**, ~3.5 years flying duty) and student **Noble Ellison** observed a silver spherical aluminum-sheeting object east of Myrtle Creek during two consecutive take-offs at 400 ft altitude. Hatfield's flight-computer estimate: **~1,000 mph** as the object climbed and disappeared. On second take-off, both Hatfield and Ellison saw the object in approximately the same position. Hatfield estimated the sphere at 30 ft diameter; Ellison at 50 ft. Ellison: "silver ball or balloon". Hatfield "is a reputable citizen in Douglas County and is reported to be a qualified pilot."

### A. Courtney Parker letter, 17 September 1947

The full text of Mr. **A. Courtney Parker**, **Superintendent of Schools (State of Vermont, Department of Education, Orange-Windsor District)**, South Royalton VT, is preserved. Parker — observing honey-bees from a bee box on **Rix Ledges, north of Lebanon NH**, at 3:40 PM — saw a tennis-ball-sized white object "as white as cotton batting" approach rapidly and noiselessly from the east, followed closely by a saucer-shaped object "that maintained a constant angle of inclination to the ball and followed it at a constant distance." The objects subtended ~5° angle to his eye and were visible for over a minute before he lost them in the glare of the sun. Parker's analogy (with his **six-inch telescope** at home in mind): "If I had seen this in the night sky with my six inch telescope I would have thought that the planets Venus and Saturn had suddenly gotten together and were traveling..."

### Confirmed prank — Saybrook IL "flying disc"

**Mrs. June Anderson** of Saybrook IL reported finding a flying disc in her front yard at 6 AM on **26 July 1947**. SAC Springfield investigation: the disc was "an old wooden platter, which has assembled on it a silver plate, a spark plug, a timer, and some old brass tubing" — "apparently the concoction of some of the juveniles in the area." Six photographs of the disc were enclosed in the field-office report. SAC's note: "the stability of June Anderson is questionable." Director Hoover's reply 5 September 1947 authorized destruction once Army was offered the chance to take it.

### "Soviet espionage agents under instructions to find out about flying discs" (August 18, 1947)

A published article (the SAC's reference is to "the Washington line") claimed Soviet espionage agents in the United States were under instructions to investigate flying discs, attributing the source to "a Federal investigative agency." Liaison representatives at **Military Intelligence**, **AAF Headquarters Intelligence**, **Office of Naval Intelligence**, and the **CIG (precursor to CIA)** all "denied any knowledge of flying discs, as well as any knowledge of the basis for this article." The article is referenced but not preserved; its origin remains unidentified.

### Mrs. Madeline Gwynne Merchant — full chain via Walter Winchell

The existing summary had Mrs. Merchant; this section preserves the **full handoff chain**: Mrs. Merchant's letter (claiming direct contacts with Col. Herbert C. Gee, Maj. Sidney Newburger, Willard Zimmer, Gen. S. R. Brennault, Col. Sumwalt, Gen. Please excuse haste — Trying to make airmail west."* Winchell's slip is preserved in the file behind a routing slip dated **8 September 1947**.

### Mrs. Mary Mixon "powdered soap stones" sample (Estill SC)

**FBI Lab final disposition** preserved: SAC Cincinnati was instructed to return the material to Mrs. Mixon along with a letter advising it was identified as "powdered soap stones." **R. T. Harbo** and **T. F. Baughman** of the Lab are the named analysts.

## Open Questions

- **The Brown CIC interview report dated 16 July 1947 is signed by SA Frank M. Brown** — Brown died 1 August 1947 in the Kelso B-25 crash. His final-product ~2 weeks before death now sits in the FBI's primary file alongside Rhodes Phoenix material. Whether Brown wrote any other CIC reports between 16 July 1947 and 1 August 1947 (and where they live in the historical record) is a natural follow-up.
- **The "Radio Ham" coded message** — published in *Newsday*, decoded by the FBI Lab as a "Martians will set up world order this year" claim, signed only "Radio Ham" — was never traced. The Lab's offer to do an FCC geographic amateur-radio cross-walk for the Bellmore Long Island section was declined. Whether the same coded-message tradition recurs in other 1947-wave files is unknown.
- The **Rhodes negatives**: Brower's chain-of-custody documentation establishes that Rhodes surrendered them on 30 August 1947 with no expectation of return. Whether the negatives subsequently reached Wright-Patterson / SIGN / GRUDGE archives is not established in this section.
- The **Phoenix office "did not receive the Bureau teletype" instructing no joint investigation until after the Rhodes interview was already done** — a procedural breach. Was a similar pattern repeated elsewhere on Bureau Bulletin No. 42 implementation?
- The **August 18, 1947 "Soviet espionage agents under instructions" article** — Bureau Liaison Section interviewed MI / AAF Intel / ONI / CIG and all denied knowledge. Whose article was this? Did it ever surface again?
