## Key Points

- This section is the FBI HQ's working repository for the **Maury Island affair** — the story of a "flying disc" that allegedly dropped slag-like material onto Harold Dahl's harbor patrol boat near Maury Island in Puget Sound on 21 June 1947, killing his dog and damaging the vessel. The episode became a foundational narrative for early UFOlogy because it intersected with [[entities/kenneth-arnold]]'s 24 June 1947 sighting and the deaths of two Army Air Forces investigators.
- **Air Rescue Service Mission #39 Final Report (4 August 1947)**: at 0450 PST 1 Aug 1947, a B-25 (#1316) departed McChord Field at 0212 PST for Hamilton Field, California, and crashed in flames near Kelso, Washington (about 13-20 miles east of Kelso, in the vicinity of Goble Creek; coordinates 46°09'N 122°43'W). Sgt. E. L. Taff and T/Sgt. W. D. Mathews bailed out and survived; the pilot Capt. William L. Davidson and co-pilot **Lt. Frank M. Brown** (CIC Sub-Detachment Commander, A-2 Fourth Air Force) died in the crash. Cause: fire in the left engine. Capt. Forsberg secured the wreckage; Top Secret material was reportedly aboard.
- **CIC-R1 Counter-Intelligence Report by Lt. Col. Donald L. Springer (28 August 1947, classified Confidential)** lays out the Air Defense Command investigation. Sequence:
  - 31 July 1947: Kenneth Arnold telephones Lt. Brown from Idaho and says he has "very vital information" on the flying discs. Brown and Davidson fly to McChord, change into civvies, and meet Arnold, Capt. E. J. Smith (United Air Lines), Harold Dahl, and Fred Crisman in the Winthrop Hotel, Tacoma, the night of 31 July.
  - The journalist who funded Arnold's trip is **Ray Palmer** (R. A. Palmer of the Venture Press, 305 Studio Bldg, 1718 Sherman Avenue, Evanston IL — editor of *Amazing Stories* / *Fate*). Palmer paid Arnold $200. The FBI checks the Chicago indices, Chicago police, and Credit Bureau on Palmer / Venture Press — all negative.
  - Dahl claimed a fleet of six donut-shaped craft, ~100 ft across with 25-ft center holes, dropped tons of slag onto his boat off Maury Island on 21 June 1947, killing his dog. Crisman, owner of the boat, claimed an additional sighting at the sand pit. Both gave Brown samples.
  - Lt. Brown's CIC report concludes there is **no evidence** the dropped material was unusual; samples (per CIC) appear consistent with ordinary metal-foundry slag, and the boat shows no recent damage consistent with "tons" of debris striking it. Crisman is identified as a 406th AAF reserve captain (Serial 0758951) with a checkered employment record. Indices reflect Harold Dahl had prior military uniform-impersonation and government-property theft charges.
  - Springer's recommendations: **no further AAF investigation**, and consideration to revoke Crisman's Air Reserve commission as "an undesirable and unreliable officer."
- Several letters from **Ray Palmer to Kenneth Arnold** (26 June 1947 and 22 July 1947) are pasted in: Palmer commissions Arnold to write his Mt. Rainier sighting story for "Venture Press" at 2¢ per word; Palmer offers to pay Arnold's expenses to investigate the Maury Island affair in Tacoma (Dahl, Crisman, "twenty tons" of slag).
- Other Aug 1947 sightings: Bruce Armstrong (Boeing Aircraft Co.) and M. A. Nichols, both reported saucers in Seattle; SAC Seattle reports Armstrong's was "a piece of unburnt paper" seen by the Boeing Field control tower, and Nichols's was a weather balloon.
- **Philadelphia, 6 August 1947, 10:45 pm** — bluish-white flaming object at >1000 ft, NE-to-SW course over Philadelphia, 2-second smoke trail; observed by multiple reliable witnesses including a former Army Air Corps pilot (Sgt). The Philadelphia Inquirer covered it 7 August.
- **F. M. Johnson, Portland OR (24 June 1947)** — prospector in Mt. Adams district independently reports seeing the same Arnold-type formation of saucers ("about 30 ft in diameter tapering to a point... an object in the tail end like a big hand of a clock shifting from side to side like a big magnet"). Springer requests Portland FBI interview him.
- **Pacific Northwest air defense radar**: per AAF Capt. Tom Brown PRO, no radar in CONUS or AAF Communications Service has detected any flying disc as of late July 1947.
- Section 3 also contains a **Bureau Bulletin No. 57, Series 1947 (1 October 1947)** reference (later quoted in Section 4) by which the FBI formally **discontinued its flying disc investigations** and turned the matter over to the Army Air Forces. This is the bureaucratic pivot point of the file series.

## Notable Incidents / Cases

- **21 June 1947, Maury Island, Tacoma WA** — Harold Dahl boat-crew slag-drop incident; Fred Crisman sand-pit sighting; later concluded a hoax / contrived story by Lt. Brown's CIC investigation.
- **31 July 1947, Tacoma WA (Winthrop Hotel)** — Kenneth Arnold + Capt. E. J. Smith + Lt. Frank Brown + Capt. William Davidson interview Dahl and Crisman; Ray Palmer of Venture Press is funding Arnold.
- **1 August 1947, near Kelso WA, ~0236 PST** — B-25 #1316 crash en route McChord → Hamilton; **Capt. William L. Davidson** (pilot) and **Lt. Frank M. Brown** (CIC, A-2 Fourth Air Force) killed; T/Sgt. Mathews and Sgt. Taff parachute and survive. Top Secret material was on board (stated to include the Maury Island slag samples).
- **6 August 1947, Philadelphia PA** — bluish-white flaming object, observed by multiple reliable witnesses including a former AAC pilot.

## Open Questions

- The Bureau retains the Crisman/Dahl story but the materials Lt. Brown had collected were destroyed in the Kelso crash (per CIC report). Were duplicate samples ever recovered? — not in the sampled pages.
- Section 3 references a "Mr. Brady, Resident Agent, FBI Tacoma" continuing follow-up after CIC stepped out. What did Brady ultimately conclude?
- Why was the FBI moved to discontinue flying-disc investigations by 1 October 1947 (Bureau Bulletin No. 57)? Section 3 only documents the run-up; Section 4 carries the bulletin's text.
