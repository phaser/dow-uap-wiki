## Key Points

- Section 4 documents the FBI's transition: **Bureau Bulletin No. 57, Series 1947 (1 October 1947)** discontinues active FBI investigation of flying discs; field offices are told to refer future reports directly to the Air Force. Yet citizen letters and field-office referrals continue to pile up at HQ.
- **Portland OR sighting (11 September 1947, 5:21-5:27 pm)** — Officer Robert D. Adair (4325 SE Madison) and partner Officer J. R. Caldwell, both in District 18, observe a silver elliptical object high over the city. Chief of Police Leon V. Jenkins and his driver Officer H. S. Raney, in District 14, also report a round silver object at 10,000 ft turning east-to-south, "egg-shaped" as it flew. Officers Robert W. Shaylor and Donald W. Cowling (also District 14) sweep the sky and see nothing. *The Oregonian* covers the story 12 September 1947.
- **Bethel, Alaska (July 1947)** — Pilot Jack Peck and co-pilot Daly of Al Jones Flying Company, on a DC-3 approach to Bethel Airport ~10 pm in summer twilight, observe a flying-wing-shaped object of approximately C-54 size at 1000 ft, heading toward Nome at ~300 mph. Anchorage SAC vouches for both pilots' reliability.
- **Lonnie Edward Noack incident (Houston/Los Angeles, late 1948)** — complainant submits 8mm movie film and "parts of supposed flying disc" to FBI Lab; identified as a tow target and toy kite. FBI Lab declines further examination; items returned 25 January 1949.
- **William Presley Oak Ridge photographs (July 1947, evaluated 1948-1949)** — photographs of an "unidentified object that appeared to be flying over Oak Ridge" submitted to AMC Wright-Patterson; the Technical Intelligence Division concludes the spherical "object" is a developer drop on the negative emulsion. Evaluation transmitted by **Col. C. D. Gasser**, AMC Engineering Field Officer at the Nuclear Energy for the Propulsion of Aircraft (NEPA) project, Oak Ridge, to G. J. Rathman of AEC Security on 10 February 1949. Knoxville SAC forwards to FBI HQ.
- **Col. C. D. Gasser confidential briefing to FBI (24 January 1949)** — Gasser, AMC's principal scientist on the NEPA project, tells the Bureau that:
  - Air Force Intelligence regards "flying discs" as **man-made missiles, not natural phenomena**.
  - Russian flying-disc-type aircraft were known to U.S. agents "as early as four years ago" (i.e., circa 1945).
  - CIA reports from southern Europe / southern Asia indicate Soviets are testing a radical aircraft / guided missile capable of long-range out-and-return flight over the sea — far ahead of U.S. capability.
  - Reports show discs typically "approach the United States from a northerly direction and have been reported as returning in a northerly direction" — consistent with flights over the North Pole from the Urals.
  - Cites a Czechoslovakia mid-air collision report between an unidentified missile and a Czech transport (no parts recovered) as the "only known crash" of a disc.
  - Believes the only fuel that could explain the discs' range is **atomic energy**.
- **Kirtland AFB OSI message (31 January 1949, "OSI-1-96")** — references previous reports on "unknown aerial phenomena, file number 48"; details a sighting at 22:55Z on 30 January 1949 by ~30 people of an identical object reported in El Paso, Albuquerque, Alamogordo, Roswell, Socorro, etc.; AEC, AFSWP, 4th Army are "perturbed by implications of phenomena."
- **SAC San Antonio letter to Hoover (31 January 1949), "Protection of Vital Installations / Bureau File 65-58300"** — at recent G-2/ONI/OSI/FBI Weekly Intelligence Conferences, G-2 Fourth Army discusses "Unidentified Aircraft" / "Flying Discs" / "Flying Saucers" / "Balls of Fire," classified Top Secret.
  - Reports Eastern Airlines Montgomery AL (July 1948) sighting (Captain & co-pilot saw a wingless rocket-ship "with windows" pass through a thunderhead at ~2700 mph — the famous **Chiles-Whitted incident**).
  - Confirms a December 1948 wave of "green fireballs" near Los Alamos, NM (5, 6, 7, 8, 11, 13, 14, 20, 28 Dec 1948 and 6 Jan 1949) sighted by OSI agents, military and airline pilots, AEC Security, and citizens.
  - **Dr. Lincoln La Paz**, "meteoricist of some note" (Director, Institute of Meteoritics, University of New Mexico), is in charge of the observations. Spectrum analysis suggests the green color may be a **copper compound** known to be used in rocket experiments. Estimated speeds: 3 to 12 miles per second (~27,000 mph), East-West paths suggesting "approach from the Northern Quadrant" — consistent with a Soviet trajectory if originating in the USSR. Course tangent to Earth's surface at 6-10 miles altitude. Object size compared to ¼ moon diameter, basketball with baseball-sized trailing lights.
  - "Some nine scientific reasons" rule out meteorites.
- **SAC San Antonio follow-up (22 March 1949)** — confirms G-2 Fourth Army has now formally renamed the topic "**Unconventional Aircraft**" with investigations under the name **"Project Grudge"** and primary responsibility transferred to USAF AMC T-2 (Wright-Patterson). Describes a Dr. La Paz "Starvation Peak incident": initial bright (constant-intensity) light at 5200 Å (yellow-green), essentially horizontal path, ~2 sec duration, no noise.
- **SAC letter (March 1949 draft Conference action item)** — Mr. Fletcher (Domestic Intelligence Division) recommends the Executive Conference approve sending field offices new questionnaire instructions to gather flying-disc information for the Air Force, despite the FBI not actively investigating.

## Notable Incidents / Cases

- **11 Sept 1947, Portland OR** — Police multi-witness silver-object sighting (Officers Adair, Caldwell, Jenkins, Raney).
- **July 1947, Bethel AK** — Peck/Daly DC-3 flying-wing sighting at ~1000 ft.
- **July 1948, Montgomery AL (Chiles-Whitted)** — Eastern Airlines DC-3 sighting of wingless "rocket ship with windows" — referenced in San Antonio letter.
- **5 Dec 1948 – 6 Jan 1949, Los Alamos / Sandia / New Mexico** — green fireball wave (multiple sightings); Dr. Lincoln La Paz in charge of analysis. Concluded NOT to be ordinary meteoritic phenomena.
- **30 January 1949, ~22:55Z** — multi-state (El Paso / Albuquerque / Alamogordo / Roswell / Socorro) sighting of single trajectory object reported by ~30 (estimated 100+) witnesses; Kirtland OSI investigates.

## Open Questions

- Does the Czechoslovakia transport / unidentified-missile mid-air collision report (cited by Gasser) survive in any other declassified file? It is not corroborated elsewhere in the open record.
- What did Project Grudge ultimately conclude about the green-fireball wave, beyond ruling out meteorites? Section 4 documents only the early observation phase.
- How does La Paz's "copper compound" spectrum analysis square with the eventual debunking of the green fireballs as a natural (or unidentified-natural) phenomenon? Section 4 leaves the question open.
