## Key Points

### FBI Institutional Posture & Policy Transitions

- **ADC Mitchel Field letter (3 September 1947, by command of Lt. Gen. Stratemeyer, signed by Col. R. H. Smith)**: established FBI/AAF cooperation — ADC Air Forces would interview responsible observers; **FBI would investigate incidents of "discs" found on the ground**. FBI enlisted to "relieve the numbered Air Forces of the task of tracking down all the many instances which turned out to be **ash can covers, toilet seats and whatnot**."
- **SAC San Francisco's objection (19 September 1947)**: wrote Director FBI objecting to the "scurrilous wordage" calling FBI investigations "ash can covers, toilet seats and whatnot" as "**insulting to the Bureau**." Recommended discussing with AAF and protesting the arrangement.
- **Ladd memo (25 September 1947)**: Bureau was asked to assist AAF to rule out Communist-caused "mass hysteria." Results "failed to reveal any indication of subversive individuals being involved." Recommended Bureau "**protest vigorously**" and **discontinue all activity in this field**.
- **Hoover's blind memorandum to field**: "reliable and confidential source" advised discs are man-made missiles; USSR has been engaged in experimentation on unknown type of flying disc for ~4 years. Air Force furnished "Unconventional Aircraft" memo (classified "restricted").
- **Bureau Bulletin No. 57, Series 1947 (1 October 1947)**: FBI formally **discontinued its flying disc investigations** and referred all reports to Air Forces.
- **SAC San Francisco Kimball letter (12 February 1948)**: ADC letter (4 Feb 1948, by command of Stratemeyer) appeared contrary to Bulletin No. 57; SAC SF asked whether Bulletin 57 still in effect.
- **ADC 4 February 1948 letter**: numbered air forces responsible for prompt investigation; "futile expenditure of military funds and manpower must be avoided." CIC personnel may be utilized; hoax determination procedure (no further effort if "evident that reported incident is a hoax or the fabrication of a publicity seeking individual").
- **Hoover's SAC letter #38 (25 March 1949)**: "Unconventional Aircraft" memo to field offices. "Reliable and confidential source" advised discs are man-made missiles; USSR 4+ years of experimentation.
- **SAC San Antonio (26 April 1949)**: no Winchell interview; data from "a colonel in the United States Air Materiel Command, who obtained his information from persons actively engaged in the investigation."
- **SAC San Antonio (4 April 1949)**: G-2 Fourth Army asked Bureau to verify/substantiate Walter Winchell's statement that "flying discs seen in this country definitely emanated from Russia."

### G-2 Fourth Army / Project Grudge

- **G-2 Fourth Army formal naming**: "Unidentified Aircraft" / "Unidentified Aerial Phenomena" renamed "**Unconventional Aircraft**"; investigations given name "**Project Grudge**."
- **G-2 Fourth Army Feb 16, 1949 Los Alamos conference**: classified "secret"; green fireball investigation transferred to **USAF AMC T-2** (Wright-Patterson).
- **G-2 Fourth Army Feb 14, 1949 higher military authorities**: advised phenomena would ultimately have a **natural explanation**.
- **G-2 Fourth Army Nov 1, 1948**: Air Force advised sightings occur periodically; another period of sightings **imminent**.
- **Kirtland AFB OSI message (31 January 1949, "OSI-1-96")**: references previous reports on "unknown aerial phenomena, file number 48"; details a sighting at **22:55Z on 30 January 1949** by **~30 people** of an **identical object** reported in **El Paso, Albuquerque, Alamogordo, Roswell, Socorro** — multi-state single trajectory. AEC, AFSWP, 4th Army "perturbed by implications of phenomena."

### Green Fireball Wave (Dec 1948 – Jan 1949)

- **Dr. Lincoln La Paz** (Director, Institute of Meteoritics, University of New Mexico) in charge of observations.
- **"Starvation Peak incident"** — La Paz personally observed:
  - Initial bright light (no period of intensity increase), constant intensity during duration
  - **Yellow-green color about 5200 Angstroms**
  - Essentially horizontal path
  - Trajectory traversed at constant angular velocity
  - Duration about **two seconds**
  - **No accompanying noise**
- **Since December 5, 1948**: more than **ten incidents analogous** to the Starvation Peak description and **twenty others with minor deviations**.
- **Geographic confinement**: sightings confined to the **Los Alamos / Las Vegas / West Texas triangle**.
- **Spectrum analysis**: green color may be a **copper compound** known to be used in rocket experiments.
- **Estimated speeds**: 3 to 12 miles per second (~27,000 mph).
- **East-West paths** suggesting "approach from the Northern Quadrant" — consistent with Soviet trajectory from USSR.
- **Course tangent to Earth's surface at 6-10 miles altitude.**
- **Object size**: compared to ¼ moon diameter, basketball with baseball-sized trailing lights.
- **"Some nine scientific reasons" rule out meteorites.**
- **Camp Hood / Killeen, Texas flares (March 6, 7, 8, 17, 31, 1949)**: first lights near Camp Hood. March 31, 1949: **Lt. Frederick Davis** (12th Armored Infantry Battalion, alert force Abel) observed **reddish-white basketball-sized object with fire trail** at ~6,000 ft, traveling parallel to ground, passed directly over him at rapid rate, **10-15 seconds in view**, then suddenly disappeared high in sky. **No sound or odor**. Telephone static/electrical interference reported when Davis tried to call headquarters.
- **Killeen flares at 1:45 a.m., March 7, 1949** (~3 miles from Killeen Base); first flare ~½ mile north of Killeen Base near Camp Hood at 1:30 a.m., March 6.

### Chiles-Whitted Eastern Airlines DC-3 Sighting (24 July 1948)

- **Capt. C. S. Chiles** (Atlanta) and **Co-Pilot John B. Whitted** (Hapeville), Eastern Air Lines, near Montgomery, Alabama, ~0200 on July 25, 1948 (night of July 24).
- Saw: **gigantic wingless aircraft, black against night sky**, streaking at **5,000 ft altitude** with **fiery comet's tail 25-50 feet in length**.
- **100-foot fuselage** about **four times the circumference of a B-29's**.
- **Two rows of brilliantly lighted square windows**.
- "Veritable Flying Dutchman of the Skies. **Nary a living soul was seen aboard!**"
- Object banked and rolled and "breathed fire" over Atlanta.
- **Multiple corroborating witnesses**: Mrs. Atkinson (Brookhaven, "flying floor lamp"), Mrs. A. C. Mivby (Carey Park, "great ball of fire with great long purple tail"), Richard P. Sellers and wife (downtown, "reddish-white ball of fire about size of cantaloupe with tail on it"), Noah Atkinson ("bright light like top part of floor lamp — fiery-red lamp bo it trailed off in flame-spurting"), Mrs. Atkinson kept secret 3 weeks.
- **Atlanta Journal** (July 25-27, 1948) published full coverage with photos.
- **Madeline Gwynne Merchant** (Wichita Falls, TX) sent letter to Atlanta Journal requesting tear sheets (5 August 1948): "interest prompted by interest in the defense of our country." Claims she has **worked independently on the problem of aerial missiles** with data going directly to **Maj. Sidney Newburger of Los Alamos** and **General Thomas T. Handy**, Fourth U.S. Army Commander.

### Col. C. D. Gasser Confidential Briefing to FBI (24 January 1949)

- **Col. C. D. Gasser**, Resident Engineer, Air Materiel Command, **Nuclear Energy for the Propulsion of Aircraft (NEPA) Research, Oak Ridge, Tennessee**.
- Air Force Intelligence regards "flying discs" as **man-made missiles, not natural phenomena**.
- **Russian flying-disc-type aircraft known to U.S. agents "as early as four years ago"** (circa 1945).
- **CIA reports from southern Europe / southern Asia**: Soviets testing radical aircraft / guided missile capable of **long-range out-and-return flight over the sea** — far ahead of U.S. capability.
- Discs typically "**approach the United States from a northerly direction and have been reported as returning in a northerly direction**" — consistent with flights over the **North Pole from the Urals**.
- **Czechoslovakia mid-air collision report**: unidentified missile collided with Czech transport aircraft (no parts recovered) — "**only known crash**" of a disc.
- **Only fuel that could explain discs' range: atomic energy.**
- **Peculiar fact**: discs approach from north and return from north.

### Parrott Merced CA Sighting (4 April 1949)

- **Major William H. Parrott** (Active USAF Reserve, pilot since August 1941, ~2,200 hours flying time, considerable night flying in WW2), at ~2220 near Merced, CA.
- Heard **clicking noise of considerable intensity** for ~10 seconds, then observed object **blanking out stars** at ~40° from horizon, easterly direction.
- **Solid mass, 4-5 feet in diameter**, dull surfaced light-colored metal. **Reflections of ground lights** on only two occasions — under surface curved.
- Moving slowly west/northwest. **35 seconds in vision**. Heard ~10 seconds before locating.
- **90-degree left turn** with continuous clicking sound (became louder on new heading of south/southwest). Clicking stopped at greatest intensity directly overhead.
- **Clicking sound**: "might be compared to beaters of a home mixer that were not properly meshed." No whistle or roar. No lights or flame except reflections. No exhaust trail.
- **Dog reacted**: stopped romping, directed attention to object's flight area (without spoken word).

### Wade Harrison Fort Smith AR Sighting (16 April 1949)

- **Wade H. Harrison** (52, Special Delivery messenger, Fort Smith Post Office), at intersection of North Eleventh St and Grand Ave, Fort Smith, AR, ~1725 on 16 April.
- Observed **brilliant object** moving southeastern direction, ~2 miles high, **very bright, reflected light like a mirror**. Watched 3-4 miles before losing sight behind tree line. **No noise** (but heavy traffic at intersection).
- **Army officer (major or lieutenant colonel)** also saw it day before (15 April) near Oklahoma City, traveling toward Fort Smith. Officer's wife also saw it.

### Merced / Castle AFB Area

- **Lt. Col. Stanley Jacobs**, Intelligence Officer, Castle AFB, Merced, contacted local newspaper about "flying disk" reports.
- **George Bremer**, OSI Detachment Commander, Castle AFB, planning to clear with headquarters and probably conduct investigation.

### Ripley "Japanese Saucer" / Winchell Telegram

- **Robert Ripley** sent Western Union telegram to **Walter Winchell**: claimed to have "the only authentic Japanese flying saucer ever recovered in this country."
- Telegram notation: "**To J. Edgar Hoover - True?**"
- **Col. William E. Carpenter, OSI-USAF**: confirmed through excellent sources in Air Force — **no authentic information available concerning the phenomenon of the flying saucers**. Checked through trusted individuals not "usual channels" to avoid "stock answer." **No flying saucers of any kind recovered in United States.**
- **Winchell broadcast (3 April 1949)**: stated "flying discs seen in this country definitely emanated from Russia." G-2 Fourth Army asked Bureau to verify.

### Kaye Lochrie / Ray Palmer "Soul World" Theory

- **Kaye Lochrie** (900 N. Harding Ave, Chicago) visited Hoover's office (20 May 1949).
- **Ray Palmer** (Managing Editor, Jeff Davis Publishing Company, Chicago) theory: when people die, their souls enter an invisible space on Earth — a "soul world" with good and bad people involved in warfare. **Flying discs are explosives being used in the warfare between good and bad souls.**
- Lochrie's stated purpose: **get her own name cleared** from FBI espionage investigation. **Files check: no record of any investigation of this individual.** Bureau agent impressed her as "**somewhat mentally unbalanced**."

### Scranton / Lake Ariel, PA Fire (Woody Shaffer House)

- **Woody Shaffer** (Lake Ariel, ~1 mile S of South Canaan, near Scranton, PA) house burned ~6 months before May 1949.
- **Large object struck roof** during night storm, ignited house — burned to ground in **less than an hour**.
- **Fire continued to burn twelve hours after water first applied.**
- **Frederic Marquardt** (former AEC employee, H. A. Kuljan Company, Philadelphia) received ash sample from Shaffer, submitted to Philadelphia Fire Marshal for analysis.
- **Fire Marshal's chemical analysis by Dr. Edward Burke, City Chemist**: ash contained **Magnesium, Aluminum, Iron, and Calcium in form of silicates, sulfur and carbonates**.
- Marquardt's opinion: ash might be part of a **military flare or "flying saucer"**. Loud noise probably from **vacuum created as missile struck roof**. **One bushel of ash remained at Shaffer's premises.**

### Virginia / Tennessee Cigar-Streak

- Multiple witnesses in **Bristol, VA / Roanoke / Kingsport TN / Christiansburg / Pulaski** saw **long cigar-shaped object** with **red glow** and **fire/smoke at tail** — ~7:15 PM.
- **Radford, VA**: "long black thing flying across the sky" from direction of Pulaski, "great deal like a rounded stick of wood which was burning."
- **William C. Flanik** (chief aircraft communicator, CAA): checked with Atlanta Air Route traffic control — no knowledge of jet aircraft in vicinity. Speculated objects were "jet-propelled plane or objects were received flying at an altitude of 30,000 feet."

### Alexandria, Louisiana "Flying Saucer Seers Convention"

- YMBC member suggested promoting a **convention of flying saucer seers** (Alexandria known as convention city).
- Received **large number of requests** from "flying saucer seers" nationwide.
- **Air Force from Barksdale Field** inquired about plans, offered to furnish observer if convention held.

### Dayton Journal-Herald / Mantell Pursuit

- **Dayton Journal-Herald** (April 27) article: "The closest any airman came to the mystery discs was on Jan. 7, 1948, when one was sighted over Fort Knox, Ky."
- **Capt. Thomas F. Mantell** able to get close. Radio transcript:
  - "I'm closing in to take a good look,"
  - "It looks metallic and of tremendous size. It's going up now as fast as I am. That's 360 miles an hour. I'm going up after it. At 20,000 feet, if I'm no closer, I'll abandon chase."
- Plane crashed, **Mantell killed**.
- Air Force evaluation teams: "**We can't prove or disprove the existence of some of the remaining evidence.**" Possibility that "they were missiles fired of unconventional design."
- **30% weather balloons, 30% conventional explanation, 40% unexplained.**

### Dr. Cromwell / Joe Lippincott "Sausers from Spain"

- **Dr. H. E. Cromwell** (local practicing dentist, Methodist Church, Protestant Mens Club member) reported that **Joe Lippincott** (Director, Eastern Area, Red Cross) told him at Red Cross dinner (6 Nov 1947, Port Allegany, PA) that "**the saucers reported as seen in various parts of the country were actually from Spain**." Lippincott said this had been "ascertained by the Government in Washington, but that it was not being made known."

### Lonnie Edward Noack Helendale CA Object

- Complainant located object in desert near Helendale, CA (150 mi NE of Pasadena, ~20 mi from Lone Pine).
- **Henry T. Rice** (manufacturer and erstwhile financial backer) positively identified portions as **model tow target or kite**. FBI Lab declined further examination; items returned 25 January 1949.

### Flyin' Saucer Toy / PIPCO

- **Southern California Plastic Co.** (Glendale, CA) advertisement in Pacific Plastics (August 1948): "**Flyin' Saucer**" cellulose acetate flying toy.
- **Fred Morrison** (ex-Army Air Force) and **Major Warren Fractions** (ex-Army Air Force, China-Burma-India / Italy theatres) developed the product.
- **PIPCO product**, San Luis Obispo, CA. Promoted at Daytona Beach resort. Football coach at Hemet CA High School used for player conditioning.

### Oak Ridge Photographs (William Presley)

- **William Presley** photographs of "unidentified object that appeared to be flying over Oak Ridge" submitted to AMC Wright-Patterson.
- **Technical Intelligence Division** concluded spherical "object" was a **developer drop on negative emulsion**.
- Evaluation transmitted by **Col. C. D. Gasser** (AMC Engineering Field Officer, NEPA project, Oak Ridge) to **G. J. Rathman** of AEC Security (10 February 1949).

### Miscellaneous

- **Peter Cameron Jones** (Los Angeles) letter to Winchell about flying saucer leaving ground within his view — theorized it was a land craft from another planet. **LA Office couldn't locate Jones at 164 West 37th Street, Los Angeles 7** — present owner and former manager had no knowledge of any such person. "May have been a prank."
- **Hoover's standard dismissive replies** to Mrs. Milo Ag Durand (Elizabethtown, NY), Mrs. McCaul (Brooklyn), Varion Beuscher (Menomonee Falls, WI) — "thoughts prompting your communication are indeed appreciated."
- **Post Office Inspector Bahr** (Chicago) requested Bureau record on **C.W. Stevens** (theorized Ziff-Davis Publishing concealing information related to national security). Bureau response: insufficient identification for search.
- **Utah Ogden / Logan / Mantua sky observation**: guard at Army Supply Depot, **Lt. Ron Hatfield** (Logan PD), **L.N. Jeppson** (Mantua) — explosion in air, puffs of smoke, falling silver-colored object, "flash of fire."
- **Portland Police multi-witness (11 September 1947)**: Officers **Adair** (District 18) and **Caldwell** (District 18) observed bright-silver elliptical object at ~5,000 ft, ~150 mph, wide turn over city. **Chief of Police Leon V. Jenkins** and driver **Officer H. S. Raney** (District 14) observed round silver object at 10,000 ft, turned from NE to S, "**egg-shaped**" as it flew. Officers **Shaylor** and **Cowling** (District 14) scanned entire sky — saw nothing. *The Oregonian* covered story 12 September.

Anchorage SAC vouches for both pilots' reliability.
- **A. Courtney Parker (South Royalton, VT)** preserved-in-full 17 September 1947 letter on his Rix Ledges paired-objects sighting.
- **Merced, CA Castle AFB** — Lt. Col. Stanley Jacobs, Intelligence Officer, contacted newspaper about "flying disk" reports. George Bremer, OSI Detachment Commander, planning investigation.
- **Mrs. Frank Gardner** (Monte, CA) letter — Hoover forwarded to USAF.
- **New Orleans SAC (July 21, 1949)**: Alexandria, LA citizens reportedly seen flying discs; YMBC member suggested promoting "convention of flying saucer seers."

## Notable Incidents / Cases

- **24 July 1948, Montgomery AL (Chiles-Whitted)** — Eastern Airlines DC-3 sighting of wingless "rocket ship with windows" (100-ft fuselage, two rows of brilliantly lighted square windows, 25-50 ft fiery comet tail, 5,000 ft altitude). Multiple corroborating witnesses.
- **5 Dec 1948 – 6 Jan 1949, Los Alamos / Sandia / New Mexico** — green fireball wave (multiple sightings); Dr. Lincoln La Paz in charge of analysis. Starvation Peak incident: yellow-green 5200 Angstroms, horizontal path, 2 sec duration, no noise.
- **30 January 1949, ~22:55Z** — multi-state (El Paso / Albuquerque / Alamogordo / Roswell / Socorro) sighting of single trajectory object reported by ~30 (estimated 100+) witnesses; Kirtland OSI investigates. AEC, AFSWP, 4th Army "perturbed by implications."
- **6-31 March 1949, Camp Hood / Killeen, Texas** — multiple lights; March 31: Lt. Frederick Davis observed reddish-white basketball-sized object with fire trail at 6,000 ft, 10-15 sec, no sound/odor, telephone static reported.
- **April 1949, Merced CA** — Major William H. Parrott (USAF Reserve pilot, 2,200 hours) observed solid mass, 4-5 ft diameter, dull surfaced light-colored metal, "clicking" sound (like home mixer beaters), 90-degree left turn. Dog reacted.
- **April 1949, Fort Smith AR** — Wade Harrison (Special Delivery messenger) observed brilliant mirror-like object; Army officer also saw it near Oklahoma City day before.
- **July 1948, Oklahoma City** — Byron B. Savage (RCA Field Engineer, private pilot) sees flat disc at 10,000-18,000 ft, size of six B-29s, 3x jet speed; "probably atomic."
- **January 1949, Danforth IL** — "flying saucer instrument" found on farm → old radio loudspeaker parts. Hoax.
- **1949, 4AF McCoy specimen analysis** — plaster of paris + Baldwin 1910 speaker + bakelite coils + Polymet capacitor. All hoax.
- **August 1947, Guam (Harmon Field)** — three enlisted men, two crescent-shaped objects at 2x fighter speed.
- **Urie Snake River Canyon (13 August 1947)** — sky-blue object at 75 ft with fiery glow; trees "spun around as if in a vacuum."
- **Palmer's August 5, 1947 letter** — post-crash letter urging Arnold to continue; "nearly 40 years" of disk knowledge.
- **Anonymous caller transcript** — blow-by-blow descriptions of Winthrop Hotel room 502; "gets back to New Jersey."
- **FBI discontinuation chain** — Springer's "ash can covers" letter → SAC SF objection → Bureau Bulletin No. 57.
- **Warden Henry / McHill Ellensburg-Seattle (5 May)** — silver object disintegrating into persistent "pillar of gas."
- **Philadelphia Aug 6 multi-witness** — Snyder (ex-B-24 pilot), Kelly (retired police), Naddle, Fine — all corroborating bluish-white flaming object.
- **Ripley "Japanese saucer" → Winchell → Hoover** — OSI Carpenter confirmed no saucers of any kind recovered in US.
- **Kaye Lochrie / Ray Palmer "soul world" theory** — discs = explosives from soul warfare. Lochrie "somewhat mentally unbalanced."

## Open Questions

- The **Czechoslovakia transport / unidentified-missile mid-air collision** report (cited by Gasser) — does it survive in any other declassified file? Not corroborated elsewhere in the open record.
- What did **Project Grudge** ultimately conclude about the green-fireball wave, beyond ruling out meteorites? Section 4 documents only the early observation phase.
- How does La Paz's "**copper compound**" spectrum analysis square with the eventual debunking of the green fireballs? Section 4 leaves the question open.
- The **Kirtland OSI multi-state sighting (30 January 1949)** — what was the object? AEC and AFSWP "perturbed by implications" but no conclusion documented.
- **Gasser's "only known crash" of a disc** (Czechoslovakia) — what intelligence source provided this? Could it be a misidentification of a conventional incident?
- The **anonymous "reliable and confidential source"** advising that discs are man-made missiles and USSR has 4+ years of experimentation — who is this source? What is the provenance of the information?
- **Ripley's "authentic Japanese flying saucer"** claim — did Ripley actually possess something he believed was a saucer? What happened to the object?
- **Ray Palmer's "nearly forty years" of disk knowledge** and "ample proof" — what did Palmer claim to possess? His August 5, 1947 letter hints at deeper knowledge but reveals nothing specific.
- The **G-2 Fourth Army Nov 1, 1948** statement that "another period of sightings was then imminent" — was this a prediction or based on intelligence? The subsequent green fireball wave (Dec 1948) seems to confirm it.
