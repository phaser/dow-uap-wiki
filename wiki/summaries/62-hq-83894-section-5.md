## Key Points

- This section reflects the FBI's hands-off posture: agents now mostly route incoming flying-saucer letters to OSI / Air Force without investigation, but Hoover's office continues to receive personal correspondence and forward it.
- **San Antonio TX (15 July 1949)** — G-2 Fourth Army notes a Galveston *News* article (10 July 1949) advertising a "flying saucer convention" planned by the Young Men's Business Club of Alexandria, LA, for nationwide saucer-witnesses to "compare notes."
- **Walter Winchell / Ernest Cuneo correspondence (12 July 1949)** — Cuneo (NYC, longtime Winchell aide) phones Asst. Director D. M. Ladd over a letter Winchell received from a Peter Cameron Jones (16 W. 37th Street, Los Angeles), claiming a 1947 saucer sighting in the LA mountains. Hoover authorizes a discreet check; the LA SAC reports the apartment-court owner has no record of Jones; investigation negative.
- **Peter Cameron Jones letter (forwarded by Winchell, 12 July 1949)**: Jones recounted hiking in mountains out of LA in August 1947 and observing about half a block away "a large silver metal object shaped like a child's top about the size of a balloon"; gained the impression of "life within the object"; when he waved, the object took off in a second, knocking him to the ground.
- **U.S. News & World Report article (April 7, 1950)** — pasted into the file: "FACT: THESE DESIGNS, AND LATER ONES ARE REAL." Argues from "an authoritative engineer" that flying saucers are real disc-shaped jet aircraft engineered on principles developed during WWII (specifically the Navy's Chance-Vought Project — the Vought V-173 / XF5U "Flying Flapjack"). The article frames saucers as a U.S. Navy guided-missile / VTOL development program, citing the Chicago and Southern Air Lines pilots (Arkansas, March 1950 — Chiles-Whitted lineage), the Eastern Airlines DC-3 sighting (Georgia, July 1948), Fort Knox KY observations chased by F-51s, and White Sands radar speeds "well over the speed of sound."
- **Miguel Angel Garcia Macias letter (Veracruz, Mexico, 19 March 1950)** — sent to "the President of the Commission of Scientific Investigation of the United States of North America"; received via SAC New York 7 April 1950. Macias describes himself as a pianist / composer / "ideographic inventor" and proposes that flying saucers are stratospheric atomic-powered aerostats. Hoover forwards the letter on 17 May 1950 to the OSI / Air Force as "of interest" but Bureau takes no further action. (Macias's substantive submission later becomes the basis for serial 220 of this same file series.)
- **Laura McCloskey, Geneva IL (4 April 1950)** — citizen UFO data forwarded by Hoover to the Secretary of the Air Force.
- **Buffalo NY teletype (8 April 1950, 12:52 pm)** — flying saucer "complaint" from Elmira NY PD: a corrugated-cardboard box, 40 inches diameter, painted aluminum, with a Crosley radio tube wired to it and a trucker's red flare attached, was found in a field near Elmira NY airport. Resident Agent at Elmira identifies it as a hoax (the box is too fragile to have flown). Press inquiries handled, matter referred to Air Force OSI Captain Ralph C. Williams.
- **Philadelphia disc (26 September 1950, ~late evening)** — two Philadelphia police officers in a scout car on Vare Boulevard near 26th Street observe a parachute-like, lavender-colored, six-foot-circumference object slowly descending; lands in a field without bending the weeds, gives off a purplish "dewy" mist that disintegrates over ~25 minutes when touched. Reported in *Philadelphia press* as "Flying Saucer Just Dissolves." SAC Cornelius reports to FBI HQ; FBI refers to OSI Major Watts. (This is the famous "angel hair" / Vare Boulevard residue case.)
- **William Rhodes (Phoenix, 18 April 1950)** — re-emerges in the file: Rhodes wants the negatives of his July 1947 photographs back, having been contacted by *True Magazine*. SAC Phoenix refers him to OSI Fourth Air Force, San Francisco — where the negatives were turned over to Mr. Fugate on 30 August 1947 (cf. Section 2).
- The pattern of Section 5 is one of containment: Hoover routinely sends "thank you" form letters to citizens and forwards correspondence to OSI; the FBI's involvement is almost entirely administrative.

## Notable Incidents / Cases

- **August 1947 (LA mountains)** — Peter Cameron Jones "silver top-shaped object knocked me down" claim, surfaced via Walter Winchell July 1949.
- **April 7, 1950, Vernon NJ / *U.S. News & World Report*** — public flying-saucer-as-Navy-VTOL theory advanced.
- **8 April 1950, Elmira NY** — cardboard / radio-tube / road-flare hoax UFO landing.
- **26 September 1950, Philadelphia PA** — Vare Blvd / 26th St "purplish lavender mist disc" residue case observed by two PPD officers.

## Open Questions

- Section 5 does NOT contain (in the sampled spans) the famous Guy Hottel memo of 22 March 1950 — that document, while time-correlated with this section, may be filed in Section 6 or as a separate sub-file. Worth a targeted search.
- The Vare Boulevard / Philadelphia "purplish mist" case is forwarded to OSI but the substance is never analyzed by the FBI Lab in this section. Did Air Force ever determine what the "lavender soap-suds" residue was?
- The U.S. News & World Report April 7 1950 article advances a confident "flying saucers are Navy VTOLs" theory — was this disinformation, an honest engineering interpretation, or genuine policy leak? Bureau's filing of it without comment suggests they took it seriously enough to retain.
