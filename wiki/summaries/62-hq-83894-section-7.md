## Key Points

- Section 7 captures the **summer-1952 wave** — the year of the Washington National flap, of greatly intensified saucer reporting nationwide — but at HQ level, FBI is mostly doing acknowledgement letters routed to OSI.
- **Savannah River Plant AEC sighting (8 August 1952, ~9:30 pm)** — two E. I. du Pont employees observe a blue light with orange fringe, "shaped like a saucer," fly over the 400 area at high speed in a NE direction. Reported to FBI HQ via teletype 9 August 1952.
- **Hoover form-letter routing**: numerous citizen letters acknowledged and forwarded to "the Director of Special Investigations, Inspector General, Department of the Air Force" — including:
  - W. H. Jennings of Pontiac MI (5 Aug 1952)
  - Mrs. Ora A. Tygrett of New Palestine IN (31 July 1952)
  - Benedicto Romero of New York City (31 July 1952)
  - Mrs. Fred Haufe of Fairmont WV (1 Aug 1952)
  - "Robert D. Wolf" of Franklin IN, January 1953 (see below)
- **German-language anonymous letter received by Cincinnati Enquirer (August 1952)**, translated by FBI Cryptanalysis-Translation Section: claims a "V-weapon flying saucer" was tested in 1944, now in series production; round disc 48-50 m diameter with 15-30 outer-rim circular nozzles around a central plexiglass sphere holding atom-bomb / instrument payload, range 30-35,000 km, "in Russian hands"; signed "H. SCH." and citing **constructor Riedel** (Klaus Riedel was a real-life German rocket engineer who worked on the V-2). FBI Domestic Intelligence Division reviews; the letter is the prototype "Nazi-saucer" claim that recurs throughout 1950s UFOlogy.
- **Robert D. Wolf, Director of Civil Defense for Johnson County IN (Franklin, 20 January 1953)** — writes Hoover saying he has joined the **International Flying Saucer Bureau (IFSB)** founded by Albert K. Bender of Bridgeport CT, and asks if the IFSB has been "cleared" or is "subversive." Encloses October 1952 letter from Bender appointing Franklin IN representatives, plus issue No. 5 (Jan 1953) of *Space Review* (the IFSB house organ).
- **Albert K. Bender / IFSB / *Space Review* contents (Jan 1953)** — pasted into the file. International officers: Bender (President & Editor), Max Krengel (VP & Treasurer), Allan C. Rievman (Secretary). British branch in Bristol. Sample contents: Mayagüez PR Oct 3 1952 sighting; Norway / Sweden Oct 13 1952 (Norwegian govt says "saucer-shaped device" landed); Korea West Front Oct 29 1952 (US troops see half-dozen "spark-throwing cartwheels"); Gaillac, France Oct 29 1952 mass sighting (a "giant flying cigar" surrounded by flying saucers, dropping "angel hair"); Oloron, France Oct 17 1952. Has section "Theory No. 1-10" by IFSB members offering ET hypotheses; Reverend S. L. Daw (DC representative) reports having "personally photographed" flying saucers six times and one landing at Charleston WV with "two small men in red" emerging — claims this was a Collier's-style rocket test. Of historical note: Bender will famously dissolve the IFSB in 1953 after his "three men in black" experience, the foundational moment for the [[concepts/men-in-black]] mythos.
- **Hoover reply to Wolf (27 January 1953)** — the FBI is "strictly a fact-finding agency" and cannot evaluate organizations; literature returned. Internal note: "No references can be located in Bufiles on the I.F.S.B.; *Space Review* or Albert K. Bender."
- **Truman Bethurum / George Hunt Williamson interest (note 1954 carries over to Section 8)** — Section 7 closes the 1953 IFSB / contactee thread; Section 8 picks up the early-1954 contactee surge.
