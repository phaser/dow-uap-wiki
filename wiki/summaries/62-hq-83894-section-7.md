## Key Points

- Section 7 captures the **summer-1952 wave** — the year of the Washington National flap, of greatly intensified saucer reporting nationwide — but at HQ level, the FBI is mostly doing acknowledgement letters routed to OSI.
- **Savannah River Plant AEC sighting (8 August 1952, ~9:30 pm)** — two E. I. du Pont employees observe a blue light with orange fringe, "shaped like a saucer," fly over the 400 area at high speed in a NE direction. Reported to FBI HQ via teletype 9 August 1952.
- **German-language anonymous "V-weapon flying saucer" letter (August 1952, Cincinnati Enquirer)** — received by the Cincinnati Enquirer, translated by FBI Cryptanalysis-Translation Section. Claims a "V-weapon flying saucer" was tested in 1944, now in series production; round disc 48–50 m diameter with 15–50 outer-rim circular nozzles around a central plexiglass sphere holding atom-bomb / instrument payload, range 30–35,000 km, "in Russian hands"; signed "H. SCH." and citing **constructor Riedel** (Klaus Riedel was a real-life German rocket engineer who worked on the V-2). FBI Domestic Intelligence Division reviews; the letter is the prototype "Nazi-saucer" claim that recurs throughout 1950s UFOlogy.
- **Borderland Sciences Research Associates (BSRA) / Meade Layne / Max Freedom Long (August 1952, San Diego)** — FBI SAC San Diego writes to Hoover about BSRA members Francis Ohm's claims: BSRA predicted the Tehachapi earthquake; predicted a Pacific tidal wave that would wash away Japanese islands, Hawaii, and the U.S. West Coast; predicted a new Pacific island; and claimed flying saucers are "factual and actual," that they've been "in conversation with the men operating the flying saucers," and that the saucers are on a "peaceful mission" but have "disintegrators" that will "disintegrate these planes completely in no time flat" if the U.S. continues to fire on them. Ohm operates a large insurance business in San Diego and is described as "a perfectly sane, sound individual." Hoover forwards to Air Force OSI.
- **Wm. Hoffmeyer Jackson MN sighting (August 1952)** — farmer reports steady white light circling farm, then shredded tin-foil sample found in pastures in a round pile with clover "burned" underneath in a triangle. Hoover forwards to Air Force.
- **John R. Riley / George J. Stock Paterson NJ photograph (August 31, 1952)** — civilian photographer Riley and witness Stock allegedly observed and photographed an unidentified aerial object. Newark division conducts credit and criminal checks (negative), but no investigation of the photos themselves was made. Results forwarded to OSI New York City.
- **F. Eekhout The Hague letter (May 5 / October 6, 1952)** — Dutch citizen Eekhout visited the FBI office in Washington on May 5, 1952, to discuss "plans for a workable flying disc." Five months later, he writes Hoover from The Hague asking whether the silence agreement is still valid, noting the subject has "become active in Europe." Hoover replies (Oct 10, 1952) that the matter was not within FBI jurisdiction, was forwarded to the Air Force for evaluation, and further contact would come from the interested government agency.
- **Harvel F. Reece letter (October 21, 1952)** — forwarded to Air Force OSI.
- **Albert K. Bender / IFSB / *Space Review* (January 1953)** — Robert D. Wolf, Director of Civil Defense for Johnson County IN, writes Hoover saying he has joined the **International Flying Saucer Bureau (IFSB)** founded by Albert K. Bender of Bridgeport CT, and asks if the IFSB has been "cleared" or is "subversive." Encloses Bender's Franklin IN appointment letter and issue No. 5 of *Space Review*. IFSB international officers: Bender (President & Editor), Max Krengel (VP & Treasurer), Allan C. Rievman (Secretary). British branch in Bristol. Contents: Mayagüez PR Oct 3 1952; Norway/Sweden Oct 13 1952 (Norwegian govt says "saucer-shaped device" landed); Korea West Front Oct 29 1952 (US troops see half-dozen "spark-throwing cartwheels"); Gaillac, France Oct 29 1952 mass sighting (giant flying cigar surrounded by saucers dropping "angel hair"); Oloron, France Oct 17 1952. Reverend S. L. Daw (DC representative) claims having "personally photographed" flying saucers six times and one landing at Charleston WV with "two small men in red" emerging — claims this was a Collier's-style rocket test. Of historical note: Bender will dissolve the IFSB in 1953 after his "three men in black" experience, the foundational moment for the Men-in-Black mythos.
- **Hoover reply to Wolf (27 January 1953)** — the FBI is "strictly a fact-finding agency" and cannot evaluate organizations; literature returned. Internal note: "No references can be located in Bureau files on the I.F.S.B.; *Space Review* or Albert K. Bender."
- **Daniel Lang / New Yorker article (September 6, 1952)** — FBI counter-intelligence investigation of Lang's article which contained "inaccurate information regarding FBI investigations." Lang had gathered material ~2 years ago from newspaper sources. Hoover's office verifies with OSI, Air Technical Intelligence Center (Captain Ruppelt), and Dept of Defense (Mr. Albert Chop) that the FBI has never been called upon to furnish reports on flying saucers. Chop gave Lang "some routine items of interest" but instructed him not to contact ATIC. At no time was the FBI mentioned. Chop offers to discreetly investigate Lang's source but is declined.
- **Air Force official "flying saucer" FAQ (pasted into file)** — comprehensive Air Force position document: 1500 reports investigated; ~20% unexplained; 75% of reports related to known causes; difficulty in evaluating due to insufficient basic data; radar sightings explained by temperature inversion; "No orders have been issued by the Air Defense Command to its fighter units to fire on unidentified aerial phenomena"; future plans include diffraction grating cameras and Schmidt telescopes; reasserts that saucers are "not a secret weapon, missile or aircraft, developed by the United States."
- **Daniel Lang / New Yorker article (September 6, 1952)** — full article pasted. Covers Project Saucer history (1948–1949), Major Boggs interviews, Chiles-Whitted Eastern Airlines case, Kenneth Arnold sighting, Fargo ND light pursuit by Lt. Gorman (F-51), Dr. Hynek's analysis, Dr. Fitts' mass-suggestibility theory, Langmuir's paper-in-thunderstorm conclusion, Project Saucer's expansion to all Air Force installations' intelligence officers, and the Washington National flap (July 20 and July 26, 1952) with radar pips and interceptor dispatches.
- **Truman Bethurum / George Hunt Williamson interest (note 1954 carries over to Section 8)** — Section 7 closes the 1953 IFSB / contactee thread; Section 8 picks up the early-1954 contactee surge.
- **Montana white object (September 19, 1952)** — news releases indicate FBI was investigating a strange white object streaking across Montana sky for ~100 miles. Butte Office confirms report received but "no investigation was conducted."
- **Desvergers West Palm Beach cap burn (August 1952)** — scoutmaster claims MBB object hovered over his head, shot "red blob" causing loss of consciousness. FBI lab examines his cap: burns present but one minute-burned area likely from hot ember; singeing not uniform (wouldn't be if single flash from directly overhead); fold under insignia shows no singeing (suggesting cap wasn't being worn). FBI recommends advising Air Force Colonel Free of findings.
- **Hoover form-letter routing**: numerous citizen letters acknowledged and forwarded to OSI — including W. H. Jennings (Pontiac MI, Aug 5, 1952), Mrs. Ora A. Tygrett (New Palestine IN, Jul 31, 1952), Benedicto Romero (NYC, Jul 31, 1952), Mrs. Fred Haufe (Fairmont WV, Aug 1, 1952), Edmond J. Kane (Mauston WI, Aug 1, 1952), Mr. Elwell (Roadstown NJ, Sep 6, 1952), C. Lhoiskot (Koshkonong MO, Aug 14, 1952), John E. Lang (Los Angeles, Aug 12, 1952).
- **Bureau Bulletin enforcement (August 18, 1952)** — internal memo from Brannigan to all Supervisors on night duty: field offices are not observing existing instructions per Bureau Bulletin #57 (Oct 1, 1947) and SAC Letter #38 (Mar 25, 1949) to refer flying-disc complaints to OSI locally. New SAC Letter issued directing all field offices to promptly furnish flying-disc details to OSI locally. Captain William Deegan, OSI 4th Air Force Base, Bowling Field, requests immediate telephonic notification of any flying-disc sightings.

## Open Questions

- Did Cincinnati FBI ever attempt to identify "H. SCH." — the German V-weapon-saucer letter writer? Section 7 does not show it.
- Did the Bureau have any reaction to Bender's eventual September 1953 dissolution of IFSB and his "Three Men" claim that became the seed of the Men in Black mythology? That story should appear in late-1953 or 1954 material if filed at HQ.
- The BSRA / Layne / Long connection is notable: Meade Layne was associated with the Borderland Sciences Research Foundation (3615 Pine Place, San Diego), the same address referenced in the "Memorandum of Importance" etheric-origin treatise from section 1. Worth cross-referencing.
- Captain Ruppelt's dismissal of Lang's FBI-saucer-investigation claims is definitive — but was Lang's article factually wrong about everything, or just wrong about the FBI's role?
