## Key Points

- Section 8 is dominated by the FBI's reluctant brushes with the **contactee movement** — the new (post-1952) genre of self-described flying-saucer experiencers like **Truman Bethurum**, **George Hunt Williamson**, and **Daniel Fry**, plus the unique case of **Mrs. Frances Swan**.
- **Truman Bethurum / George Hunt Williamson, Cincinnati OH, June 1954** — Thomas Eickhoff (3721 Tappan Avenue, operator of Tom's Beauty Salon) walks into FBI Cincinnati on 7 June 1954 reporting that:
  - Henry Maday (364 W. Lewiston Ave, Ferndale MI) had advertised in the *Cincinnati Enquirer* a paid lecture by Truman Bethurum at Taft Auditorium ($2/ticket) on "the real flying saucer story."
  - Bethurum (Redondo Beach CA, age 55, former truck driver) claimed in the *Daily Breeze* (31 December 1953) that he had been aboard flying saucers eleven times and met "a ravishing woman commandant" of a saucer crew in the Nevada desert.
  - Williamson (associated with *Valor* magazine, Soulcraft Press, Noblesville IN, run by **William Dudley Pelley** — onetime fascist Silver Legion founder) describes himself as an archeologist and writer.
  - Eickhoff visited Lt. Col. John O'Mara at Wright-Patterson AFB on 6 June 1954 and was told by O'Mara that the Air Force has no flying saucers, and that **Major Donald Keyhoe**, retired USMC, "is a fraud" — a statement Eickhoff later relayed to Keyhoe himself, who "was going to Washington with his attorneys" to take it up with the Air Force.
  - Eickhoff intends to support a Bethurum lecture in Cincinnati and report to Federal authorities; planned a 7 June 1954 dinner at the home of **L. H. Stringfield** (7017 Britton Ave Cincinnati 27), with Williamson, Manspeaker, Bethurum, Ralph Zimmermann, and Eickhoff. Stringfield is a young UFO researcher who would later author *Situation Red: The UFO Siege!* and the "crash retrieval" Status Reports.
  - Eickhoff's July 2, 1954 follow-up: no progress on the Cincinnati meeting; Bethurum returned to California but may return in fall 1954. Eickhoff had been requested by Keyhoe to furnish in writing the "Keyhoe is a fraud" comment.
- **L. H. Stringfield / CRIFO / Newsletter (September 1954)** — Jack Gunderman (layout artist, DuBois Company, Cincinnati) forwards Stringfield's *Newsletter* (Civilian Research, Interplanetary Flying Objects, P.O. Box 55, Cincinnati) to FBI. Stringfield claims Newsletter has world-wide circulation of ~11,000 copies at $2/year. Key findings from Stringfield's Newsletter:
  - **EXCLUSIVE: Private talk with Lt. Col. John O'Mara, Deputy Commander, Intelligence, Wright-Patterson (9/21/54)** — O'Mara tells Stringfield: "Flying saucers 'do exist'" and "past contradictions were unfortunate." O'Mara categorizes saucers into three breakdowns: (1) controlled saucer from outer space, (2) secret American saucer-like device, (3) unexplained natural phenomena. O'Mara says "Definitely not!" when asked if all saucers seen by Americans were American devices. O'Mara says Air Force plans to cooperate with the public and a statement is forthcoming from Defense Dept.
  - **Saucers Harass Earth** — international censorship: Chilean Naval Mission in DC retracts Capt. Augusto Orrego's 1948 Antarctic saucer photos claim, saying Orrego stated he "had not seen any UFO's over the Antarctica in 1948."
  - **Detroit Flying Saucer Club** — open letter to the President (22 September 1954): "government with far greater facilities... is perfectly aware of such phenomena, but has adopted a policy of silence and secrecy." Requests audience regarding "coming of Space ships to this earth." Calls for "honest and forthright acknowledgment."
- **Mrs. Frances Swan / Adm. Herbert B. Knowles case (29 July 1954)** — John Hutson (Security Officer, Bureau of Aeronautics, US Navy) and Cdr. L. T. McQuiston interview the FBI WFO. Retired Rear Admiral Herbert B. Knowles of South Berwick ME — Mrs. Swan's neighbor — wrote to Adm. Espey, Director of Naval Intelligence, on 1 June 1954 enclosing telepathic messages received by Swan from "outer space"; she would write down messages "for four or five hours at a time without getting tired." ONI declined to act in absence of evidence of conversations with foreign powers; Bureau of Aeronautics also declines.
  - Swan claims to have contact with two space ships designated **M-4** and **L-11**, described as 150 miles wide, 200 miles long, 100 miles deep. Mother ships 150–200 feet long, ~5,000 total.
  - Commander of M-4 is **"Affa"** from planet Uranus; Commander of L-11 is **"Ponnar"** from planet Hatann.
  - Contact purpose: protecting Earth from destruction caused by atomic bomb, hydrogen bomb, and wars — which "disrupt the magnetic field of force which surrounds the earth." Also protecting the whole universe because if "fault lines" break, it would affect the entire universe. "Affa" and "Ponnar" are "presently working the area of the Pacific Ocean repairing fault lines."
  - Swan receives messages on a schedule: 6:00 AM, 12 noon, 6:00 PM daily. Gets a buzzing sound in her left ear when "on the line" — complains it is "very annoying and painful." Messages have been coming since May 27, 1954, at all times of night, causing sleep loss.
  - Husband Guy Swan and daughter Dawlyn also heard buzzing but cannot receive transmissions.
  - Swan says people from "outer space" can use her eyes and ears to see and hear via a "mechanical device" — all conversations are recorded. When asked a question, she began writing the answer before she had time to relay it.
  - Swan says she and Smith (Canadian physicist) could contact "outer space" on any frequency if she's informed first. Smith planned to try August 1, 1954 on high frequency. Swan advised the ship would come within 100 miles of Ottawa to ensure contact.
  - Admiral Knowles wanted to know if physical contact could be made. Swan said the people in "outer space" wanted to know if they could provide protection, and if physical contact were to be made, they would appear in a force of 5,000 "bells" or "flying saucers" over many nations during the latter part of August 1954, close enough to be seen by man and to see people on the street.
  - Knowles wrote to Senator Margaret Chase Smith (Maine), who forwarded to Secretary of Defense and copies to Army, Navy, Air Force. Knowles also wrote to the President.
  - Hutson visited Knowles at South Berwick, Maine (July 24–26, 1954), sat with Canadian physicist Wilber B. Smith (at Knowles' residence with his family, unofficial), observed Swan writing messages. Described Swan as "very simple minded person, religious, a student of the Bible, and one who has studied spiritualism and other thought control systems."
  - Later (not in this section): ONI engages Cdr. McQuiston / Capt. Sidney Newhouse in 1959 testing.
- **Carl R. Keyser sighting (Milford OH, 23 July 1954, 14:40)** — Civil Defense Col. J. M. Smith reports to FBI Cincinnati that Keyser and his wife observed a silver circular/spherical object 45° SE of their residence on Route 1 Milford. Referred to OSI Wright-Patterson.
- **Cincinnati paint incident (September 11, 1954)** — Residents in Cincinnati had similar experiences. Albert Allgeyer came home to find bright yellow trim on his two-story house stained to "washed-out brown, almost black color." Stains wouldn't rub off without taking the hard top coat. Looked like blow-torch damage; in some places stains appeared in dirty brown rivulets. Neighbor Howard May received similar treatment. Another house across the street also affected. Kettering Lab at University of Cincinnati took air samples and paint specimens. Dr. Hughes explained mystery was caused by chemical reaction of hydrogen sulphide which could attack lead in certain paints. Lab averred chemical probably emanated from Millcreek Valley near Allgeyer's home. Proctor & Gamble doctor said chemical not injurious to people, but Allgeyer complained of extreme swelling of both hands and eyelids five days after incident — described as some kind of allergy.
- **Peter Bartkus / Theodore McColm moon sighting (Rockford IL, Sept 5, 1954)** — amateur astronomers watching moon through 6" Cassegranian reflector telescope (100" focal length, 150 and 200 power oculars). Between 10:35 and 11:15 PM, Bartkus observed spherical object ascending from northern section of Mare Humboldtianum area. Not glowing or brilliant, seemed more like dull reflected light of a planet. Size estimated at diameter of craters Pitiscus or Vlaco. At 11:15, had traveled more than 29° 30" of arc in about 40 minutes. Object was ascending while moon was descending at 1081 mph — "object must have been a powered body because... ascending and not following a true orbit." McColm through calculus estimated object to be 12,500 ft. in diameter. Editor's note: "transcends any notion that 'saucer' vehicles are confined to earth's atmosphere."
- **Rome, Italy cigar-shaped object (September 18, 1954)** — AP/INS: startled Romans saw strange cigar-shaped object streaking over capital. Radar operators picked up on instruments. Ciampino Airfield technicians described "half-cigar" plummeting toward earth for 1000+ ft., then rising at tremendous speed before vanishing. Eyewitness Michael Chinigo: strange sound like thunder with staccato effect, series of explosions. Object stopped or appeared to at ~6000 ft., then shot upward leaving exhaust trail of milky white smoke. Defense Dept described it as "clipped cone" with smaller surface on bottom, or two semi-circular disks. Object's ability to "park" in mid-air for several minutes. Rome radar station registered it for 39 minutes.
- **Charles A. Yost / OC Research Laboratory letter (St. Clair Shores MI, 14 November 1953)** — letter to "Mr. Small" (typesetter for Nome Nugget in Alaska) found by Dr. Kennidy of Nome and forwarded by Major William H. Greenhalgh, Jr., 5004th Air Intelligence Service Squadron, to OSI Alaskan Air Command, then to FBI HQ. Yost says his lab's research is being kept secret, has a "connection with the flying saucer" but is "secondary" to research on **electric fields**, references work by a "research foundation in Western United States" not connected to him. Letter filed with no further action by FBI.
- **Detroit Flying Saucer Club open letter to the President (22 September 1954)** — preserved in full. "We believe that these phenomena are of a magnitude and importance such as to warrant the utmost openness and cooperation between government and citizenry." "The present policy of silence and secrecy is a reflection on the intelligence and loyalty of the American citizen." "Other countries have already acknowledged these phenomena and have publicly appointed governmental commissions to investigate, collect information and report to the people." Calls for "honest and forthright acknowledgment."
- **Hoover forwarding to Air Force (August 9, 1954)** — Hoover forwards the Frances Swan material to OSI, Army G-2, and ONI.
- **George Adamski reference**: Eickhoff mentions Adamski's *Flying Saucers Have Landed* (Werner-Lowery / British Book Center) in context of Bethurum credibility.
- **Donald Keyhoe reference**: *Flying Saucers from Outer Space* (Henry Holt / Harper's Publishers) cited as source-text Eickhoff was reading; O'Mara's "Keyhoe is a fraud" comment becomes the most quotable line in the section.

## Open Questions

- Did Donald Keyhoe in fact pursue a defamation action against the Air Force / Lt. Col. O'Mara over the "Keyhoe is a fraud" comment? Not documented further in this section.
- The Frances Swan case becomes a curious episode: ONI eventually does engage Cdr. McQuiston / Capt. Sidney Newhouse in 1959 testing — but the late-1950s follow-up isn't in this 1954-focused section.
- L. H. Stringfield's role in the 7 June 1954 Cincinnati gathering positions him at the start of his UFO career — does the Bureau ever revisit him in later sections?
- The Cincinnati paint incident (hydrogen sulphide from Millcreek Valley) has parallels to the "pitted windshields" and "metallic pellets" phenomena referenced by Meade Layne in BSRA's Round Robin. Worth cross-referencing with the Section 6 material.
- The Detroit Flying Saucer Club's open letter to the President is a significant artifact of citizen-organized UFO advocacy — who signed it and what happened to it?
