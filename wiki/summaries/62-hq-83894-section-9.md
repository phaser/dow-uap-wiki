## Key Points

- The post-Sputnik wave (October 1957 onward) renewed UFO reporting; Section 9 is the FBI HQ binder for that period.
- **R. R. Roach memorandum to A. H. Belmont (12 November 1957), "Unidentified Flying Objects (Flying Saucers)"** — key Bureau-level summary:
  - Since the Russians released *Sputnik*, "there has been a great increase in the number of flying saucers and other unidentified flying objects."
  - **10 October 1957, Vernal UT** — Air Force flooded with calls about an object "looked like the moon" trailing "blue flame that pulsed like a heart" — investigated, no object located.
  - "An object had landed in Nebraska with six people aboard, the persons had talked to a Nebraska farmer and then sped off into space" — referring to **Reinhold Schmidt's "Kearney NE" claim** of November 1957 (cf. sub_a press clippings).
  - "A fiery object was seen flashing across the southern skies from Albany, GA, to Miami, FL"; a Coast Guard cutter sighted "a huge object flying over the Gulf of Mexico"; persons in the Southwestern states while driving cars saw UFOs that caused car engines to stop — referring to **Levelland, TX (2-3 November 1957)** vehicle interference cases.
  - Bureau Liaison rep met with AF Intelligence: "for all practical purposes" all cases resolved as "nonauthentic or there was no basis for the sightings"; AF reports forwarded to **Air Technical and Intelligence Center, Wright-Patterson AFB** for evaluation.
- **Havana Cuba Legal Attaché report (20 November 1957)** — *Diario de la Marina* (12 Nov 1957) reports Jose Maria Nieto and Carmelo Guzman observed a hat-shaped flying disc hovering over Matahambre Mines, Pinar del Rio, on 11 Nov 1957; silent, faster than ordinary aircraft; departed seaward.
- **Robert T. Stone / Inter Continental Aerial Research Foundation (ICARF), Independence MO, December 1957** — Mr. B. B. Watson (City Editor, *Hannibal Courier-Post*) receives an unsolicited press-release from R. T. Stone, "Director, Missouri Filter Center, ICARF," 10717 E. 27th Terrace Independence MO. ICARF aims to set up "UFO Observer Posts" worldwide and gather sighting data from photo-equipped citizens. SAC St. Louis advises Bureau, refers Kansas City to investigate. No FBI investigation conducted; KC indices check only.
- **J. F. Wackerbarth memo (5 October 1959)** — confirms past 12 months no flying-saucer complaints have been referred from FBI to OSI (compared with reporting volume of earlier years).
- **Joseph Perry photographs (Grand Blanc MI, March 1960)** — Mr. Joseph Perry, 3079 Edwards Street, professional photographer for thirty years; photographed the moon on 21 February 1960 with 35 mm Retina Kodak using Super Ansco color film through home-made 2840-power telescope (set 350-400× during the saucer shot); a fluorescent-glowing oval object appeared in the developed slide. Reported to SA Louis Woiwade, 25th OSI District. *Detroit Times* article enclosed. Perry surfaces again in **sub_a** with the famous "saucer-photo returned to me by Pentagon — and they did something to it!" claim of May 1962.
- **John Hewes / "Research Interplanetary Intelligence Investigation Analysis" / "Flying U.F.O.U.F.O. Objects" — Oklahoma City OK (24 August 1959)** — letter to Hoover requesting a 350-word article for their planned September publication; on humorous letterhead. No reply substantive.

## Notable Incidents / Cases

- **10 October 1957, Vernal UT** — moon-like object with pulsing blue flame; Air Force investigates, no recovery.
- **2-3 November 1957, Levelland TX & Southwest** — automobile-engine stoppage UFO wave (referenced in Roach memo).
- **November 1957, Kearney NE** — Reinhold Schmidt "space ship in field with six occupants" claim (also documented in sub_a).
- **11 November 1957, Pinar del Rio, Cuba** — hat-shaped silent disc over Matahambre Mines.
- **21 February 1960, Grand Blanc MI** — Joseph Perry's "moon photograph" UFO image.
