## Key Points

### Post-Sputnik wave (1957-1958)

- **R. R. Roach memorandum to A. H. Belmont (12 November 1957), "Unidentified Flying Objects (Flying Saucers)"** — key Bureau-level summary:
  - Since the Russians released *Sputnik*, "there has been a great increase in the number of flying saucers and other unidentified flying objects."
  - **10 October 1957, Vernal UT** — Air Force flooded with calls about an object "looked like the moon" trailing "blue flame that pulsed like a heart" — investigated, no object located.
  - "An object had landed in Nebraska with six people aboard, the persons had talked to a Nebraska farmer and then sped off into space" — referring to **Reinhold Schmidt's "Kearney NE" claim** of November 1957 (cf. sub_a press clippings).
  - "A fiery object was seen flashing across the southern skies from Albany, GA, to Miami, FL"; a Coast Guard cutter sighted "a huge object flying over the Gulf of Mexico"; persons in the Southwestern states while driving cars saw UFOs that caused car engines to stop — referring to **Levelland, TX (2-3 November 1957)** vehicle interference cases.
  - Bureau Liaison rep met with AF Intelligence: "for all practical purposes" all cases resolved as "nonauthentic or there was no basis for the sightings"; AF reports forwarded to **Air Technical and Intelligence Center, Wright-Patterson AFB** for evaluation.

- **Havana Cuba Legal Attaché report (20 November 1957)** — *Diario de la Marina* (12 Nov 1957) reports Jose Maria Nieto and Carmelo Guzman observed a hat-shaped flying disc hovering over Matahambre Mines, Pinar del Rio, on 11 Nov 1957; silent, faster than ordinary aircraft; departed seaward.

### ICARF — civilian UFO filter-center network

- **Robert T. Stone / Inter Continental Aerial Research Foundation (ICARF), Independence MO, December 1957** — Mr. B. B. Watson (City Editor, *Hannibal Courier-Post*) receives an unsolicited press-release from R. T. Stone, "Director, Missouri Filter Center, ICARF," 10717 E. 27th Terrace Independence MO. ICARF aims to set up "UFO Observer Posts" worldwide (United States, Canada, Europe, Alaska, Australia, Japan, England, Africa, South America) and gather sighting data from photo-equipped citizens. SAC St. Louis advises Bureau, refers Kansas City to investigate. Kansas City indices check: **no information identifiable with ROBERT T. STONE or ICARF**; the 1957 Independence, Missouri Directory lists only **TERRELL O. STONE**, wife IVA, ice cream maker at Velvet Freeze, at the same address. No further investigation; KC designated Office of Origin.

### Delmaine H. Oveson / Torrington CT — fictitious "Electronics Service Unit 4"

- **Delmaine H. Oveson, Torrington CT (5 December 1957)** — Oveson, identifying himself as "Director of Operations, Electronics Service Unit 4, Roseau, Minnesota," wrote to the Air Force Office of Special Investigations asking about a UFO sighting near Torrington CT on 5 Dec 1957. The Air Force Ground Observer Corps Coordinator in Connecticut said neither he nor Oveson had any knowledge of the author. **U.S. Army and Air Force Postal Service confirmed no military units at Roseau, Minnesota.** The case was referred to the FBI for action. Hoover forwarded to the Inspector General.

### Donald E. Keyhoe / NICAP inquiry (September 1958)

- **Donald E. Keyhoe, Director of NICAP, letter to Hoover (22 September 1958)** — Keyhoe requested particulars of the Bureau's participation concerning unidentified flying objects. He described NICAP as "A privately supported fact finding civilian committee serving the public interests" and listed "prominent individuals" among Board members and Special Advisers. Keyhoe is a retired Marine.
- **FBI file notes on Keyhoe (26 September 1958)** — The Bureau's internal memo is notably dismissive: Keyhoe has been "known to the Bureau since 1935 and was, and may still be, a free-lance writer." In 1948 he was described by Mr. Nichols as "**a flamboyant writer and we have found from previous experience that much of his material is irresponsible.**" In 1951 the Director concurred: "We should not get involved with him." As an example of past unreliability, the memo cited a January 1941 *Cosmopolitan* article co-written by Keyhoe and John J. Daly entitled "Hitler's Plan to Seize the United States Merchant Marine" — which indicated the Bureau had possession of documents concerning such a plan by Hitler, an indication that was "completely false."
- **Hoover's draft reply to Keyhoe (26 September 1958)** — Standard Bureau position: does not investigate UFO sightings; does not issue instructions to witnesses not to talk; does not make character investigations of witnesses; does not have releasable information on UFOs (but "this does not imply that this Bureau has information concerning unidentified flying objects which cannot be released"); considers contact with extraterrestrial vehicles a matter for the Department of the Air Force, not the FBI. Interview request "not now necessary."

### The "Bender Affair" / C. Harold Marck, Jr. (December 1958)

- **C. Harold Marck, Jr., Scottsdale AZ (7 December 1958)** — Marck wrote to the FBI asking about the "Bender Affair." He claimed Albert Bender formed the **International Flying Saucer Bureau** in Bridgeport, Connecticut, in 1952 to investigate the flying saucer mystery. In 1953 Bender allegedly stated he "knew what the saucers are." Then "**three men in black suits**" silenced Bender to the extent that even today Bender will not discuss the matter of his "hush-up" with anyone. Marck claimed he has learned that "the secret of the saucers lies the 'dreadful underground menace' that is threatening the world" and that he has important information about this menace.
- **FBI field interview of Marck (19 December 1958)** — SAs George Hollingsworth and William M. Drew interviewed Marck at his Scottsdale residence. Marck referred agents to Gray Barker's book "They Knew Too Much About Flying Saucers" (University Books Publisher, April 1956). Marck also corresponded with various harbor masters and the U.S. Navy relative to the "dreadful underground menace which relates to the disappearance of crew and passengers of various ships found floundering in the open seas out of control." The interview report concludes: "From the admonishment given him by his mother for writing letters to such agencies as the FBI, it would appear that **CLAUDE HAROLD MARCK is a 'chronic', living in a dream world of flying saucers and stories of the sea.**" The Bureau's subsequent attempt to locate Marck (who had moved to Denver) was unsuccessful.
- **Bureau's file on Bender** — The only identifiable data on Bender in Bureau files: a letter he wrote on 10/26/52 on the letterhead of the International Flying Saucer Bureau to a Mr. L. H. Frahm, Franklin, Indiana, suggesting how to organize a chapter of the Saucer Bureau. Also in files: a copy of the January 1953 magazine "Space Review" put out by the Saucer Bureau, containing articles and squibs on worldwide saucer sightings. **No security significance.**

### Aerial Phenomena Research Group (APRG) / Robert Gribble

- **APRG, Seattle WA (1957-1959)** — The Bureau investigated APRG at 5108 Findlay Street, Seattle, after Lt. Col. Oliver R. Hertel of Fort McClellan, Alabama (April 1959) claimed the address belonged to the chauffeur for the Russian Consul in Seattle. The **Seattle Office found Robert Gribble**, a regularly employed **fireman for the City of Seattle**, who said his hobby is acquiring information regarding unidentified flying objects. APRG has a national and international membership of about 350 members, charging $5.00/year for a periodic publication and incidental expenses. Gribble stated the organization is a non-profit hobby. **The Seattle Office confirmed there is no Russian Consulate or other establishment of the U.S.S.R. in the City of Seattle.** Hertel's recollection was "completely erroneous."

### James Maney / "The Saucerian Bulletin" (November-December 1958)

- **James Maney, 13-year-old, Oklahoma City OK (November 1958)** — Maney wrote to Hoover as "Acting Deputy Director" of a local Oklahoma City group called the "Interplanetary Intelligence of Unidentified Flying Objects," interested in "the serious investigation of UFO." He asked whether FBI agents had discouraged certain saucer investigators (particularly Mr. Bender of Bridgeport, CT). **Hoover replied in person** (letter dated 2 December 1958): information in FBI files is confidential; UFO investigation is not within the Bureau's jurisdiction; referred him to OSI. The Bureau's background check found Maney's files negative except for one reference to a "Jimney" at the same address who collected FBI letterhead stationery.

### Hayden Hewes / "Research Interplanetary Intelligence" (August 1959)

- **Hayden Hewes, Oklahoma City OK (24 August 1959)** — Hewes, Associate Director of "Interplanetary Intelligence of Unidentified Flying Objects," requested a 350-word article from Hoover for their planned September publication on a humorous letterhead. **Hoover replied (8 September 1959):** "Although I would like to be able to help you, in view of the many similar requests and the pressure of my official duties, I am unable to comply with your wishes." No record in Bureau files on Hewes or his organization.

### Joseph Perry Grand Blanc MI saucer photo (February-March 1960)

- **Joseph Perry, Grand Blanc MI (21 February 1960 / March 1960)** — Perry, proprietor of Perry's Pizza Grill at 3079 Edwards Street, Grand Blanc, Michigan, is a professional photographer for thirty years with a hobby of taking photographs of the moon through a homemade telescope (2,840× power, set at 350-400× for the saucer shot). At about 1:00 AM on 21 February 1960, he photographed the full moon and on developing found **what appeared to be a flying object silhouetted against the moon** — a flat oval shape with a "fluorescent glow" and a "blue-green radiation trail." Perry showed the slide to restaurant patrons who agreed it looked like a saucer. He contacted the FBI Flint Resident Agency on 4 March 1960. The FBI forwarded two slides to the **Office of Special Investigations, Selfridge AFB, Michigan**.
- **Detroit Times article (9 March 1960)** — Full article with photo of Perry and his telescope, datelined Grand Blanc, Michigan. Headline: "Pizza Pie—Or Flying Saucer?" Article notes Perry received at least 50 letters and calls; NICAP wanted the picture made public; Perry has had offers to buy the slide but says "It's not for sale."
- **Perry letter to President Eisenhower (21 March 1960)** — Perry wrote directly to President Eisenhower because he "can't find out any information from anyone." He quoted NICAP's letter saying "we consider it unlikely that you will ever see your picture returned." Perry asked for the assurance that his colored transparency would be returned to him "within a short time, as it would be of great interest in the pursuit of my hobby."
- **Wilton B. Persons / White House referral (26 March 1960)** — The White House referred Perry's letter to the Department of Justice, which referred it to the FBI.
- **Bureau follow-up (April 1960)** — SAC Detroit advised that Perry's letter to the President had been acknowledged; photographs were furnished to OSI Selfridge AFB; no investigative action was being taken. Perry was personally contacted by SAs Raymond E. Moomey and Martin A. McNerney on 12 April 1960 at his home.

### NICAP follow-up (April 1960)

- **Richard Hall, Secretary of NICAP, letter to FBI (21 March 1960)** — Referenced the Detroit Times article and requested confirmation of whether the Bureau had changed its UFO policy, whether it gathered evidence for the Air Force, and whether the Perry photograph had been analyzed at FBI laboratories. Bureau's March 25, 1960 reply: no change in policy; does not gather evidence for the Air Force; the Perry photograph was not examined by the FBI Laboratory.

### J. F. Wackerbarth memo (5 October 1959)

- Confirms past 12 months no flying-saucer complaints have been referred from FBI to OSI (compared with the reporting volume of earlier years). This reflects the **decline in Bureau-level UFO activity** post-1957.

### Larry W. Bryant / Newport News VA (February 1959)

- **Larry W. Bryant, Newport News VA (1 February 1959)** — Bryant wrote to the "Director of Field Investigations, Unidentified Flying Objects" at the FBI asking: (1) How many UFO sighting reports involving alleged photographs or films of unconventional aerial objects has the FBI collected, evaluated, or analyzed since January 1, 1952? (2) Has the FBI ever been furnished with an analysis of maneuvers of UFOs? (3) Has any FBI representative ever given testimony before Congressional committees on this subject? **Hoover replied (9 February 1959):** FBI files are confidential; UFO investigation is not within the Bureau's jurisdiction; referred his letter to the Director of Special Investigations, Air Force. Bureau files negative on Bryant.

## Open Questions

- Section 9 references the Levelland TX vehicle-stoppage cases only obliquely via the Roach memo. Did the Bureau ever look at Texas state-trooper reports directly?
- The "huge object flying over the Gulf of Mexico" sighted by a Coast Guard cutter — what cutter, what date, what data? Not detailed in sampled pages.
- ICARF's later trajectory after 1957? The FBI found no trace of Robert T. Stone; the directory listing showed only Terrell O. Stone, an ice cream maker. Whether ICARF continued operations is unknown.
- The "Bender Affair" — the FBI found no evidence of any government involvement. The "three men in black suits" narrative was later popularized by Gray Barker's book "They Knew Too Much About Flying Saucers." The Bureau obtained a copy through the Chicago office.
- The Perry photographs — were they ever analyzed by OSI or Air Force? The Bureau's April 1960 memo says the slides were "furnished to the Office of Special Investigations, Department of the Air Force, Selfridge Air Force Base" for "whatever action was deemed appropriate." No follow-up on the Air Force's disposition is in this section.
