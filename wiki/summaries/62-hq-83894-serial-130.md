## Key Points

- Serial 130 is a Mitchel Field / Air Defense Command bundle of investigative summaries collected in summer 1947, forwarded to the FBI as part of joint AAF / FBI sharing.
- **Pan American Constellation sighting (4 August 1947, ~16:00 EDST, between Everett MA fan marker and Bedford radio beacon, ~10 miles NW of Boston)** — Capt. Alpheus O. Powell (Pan American Airways, ~4000 command pilot hours, Maxwell Field 41-C class) and navigator Walter L. White, en route Gander Newfoundland → La Guardia Field, observe a bright orange cylindrical object.
  - Aircraft at 8000 ft, 265 mph IAS, course 244° magnetic.
  - White (right seat) first sees orange object below right of plane; Powell banks left to look out side window — sees a P-40-fuselage-length, blunt-ended cylindrical object at ~7800 ft, ~1 mile away, 45° to the left.
  - Object had defined shape (no gaseous dissipation suggesting jet/rocket exhaust); estimated course 200°, speed 160 mph.
  - Object lost behind cloud after ~50 seconds. Pursuit not attempted.
  - White separately interviewed (he saw 30 seconds; describes "elliptical, sun glint"; estimated a half mile away, 1000 ft below).
  - Both pilots considered very reliable by Mitchel Field investigators; not given to "flights of fancy."
- **Birmingham AL "Mass Sighting" (5 July 1947 evening)** — Forwarded with the Powell file. Birmingham *Age-Herald* covered "Flying Saucers Reported From 39 States, But Seem to Skip Over the Equator." Reporter J. C. Crossland made a 15-second exposure photograph at 8:37 pm at his home (29th Street and Highland Avenue) which he claimed shows a saucer; the *Age-Herald* phones were "bedlam" with calls from Edgewood, Mountain Brook, Avondale, Southside, Fairfield, Pratt City, West End, Central Park, Bessemer. Reports varied: large/small objects, fast/suspended, sounds/silent, formations/individual.
  - Birmingham AAF base (Hq 14th Air Force, Orlando FL) declines to investigate, characterizing it as "an isolated case." First Indorsement to ADC concurs.
- **Capt. Smith** of Pan Am New York Operations vouches for Powell; multiple inter-pilot endorsements for both Powell and White.
- A separate "1st Indorsement" memo from Hq 14th AF Orlando (8 July 1947) on "Report on Local Flying Disc" attaches the Birmingham material to the Mitchel package.

## Notable Incidents / Cases

- **4 August 1947, NW of Boston MA** — Pan American Constellation captain (Powell) and navigator (White) sighting of orange cylindrical object.
- **5-6 July 1947, Birmingham AL** — mass municipal flying-saucer flap; Crossland 8:37 pm photograph; *Age-Herald* phone deluge.
