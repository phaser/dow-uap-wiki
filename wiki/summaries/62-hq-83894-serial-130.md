## Key Points

### Pan American Constellation sighting (4 August 1947)

- **Capt. Alpheus O. Powell** (Pan American Airways, ~4,000 command pilot hours, Maxwell Field 41-C class) and navigator **Walter L. White**, en route Gander Newfoundland → La Guardia Field, observed a bright orange cylindrical object between Everett MA fan marker and Bedford radio beacon (~10 miles NW of Boston).
- Aircraft at 8,000 ft, 265 mph IAS, course 244° magnetic. White (right seat) first sees orange object below right of plane; Powell banks left — sees a P-40-fuselage-length, blunt-ended cylindrical object at ~7,800 ft, ~1 mile away, 45° to the left.
- Object had defined shape (no gaseous dissipation suggesting jet/rocket exhaust); estimated course 200°, speed 160 mph. Lost behind cloud after ~50 seconds. Pursuit not attempted.
- White separately interviewed: saw 30 seconds; describes "elliptical, sun glint"; estimated a half mile away, 1,000 ft below.
- Both pilots rated **very reliable** by Mitchel Field investigators; not given to "flights of fancy." Capt. Smith of Pan Am New York Operations vouches for Powell; multiple inter-pilot endorsements for both Powell and White.
- **Related Report** (12 September 1947, Hq ADC): cross-references the Powell interview.

### Birmingham AL mass sighting (5-6 July 1947)

- **Birmingham *Age-Herald* mass municipal flap** — Reporter **J. C. Crossland** made a 15-second exposure photograph at 8:37 pm at his home (29th Street and Highland Avenue) which he claimed shows a saucer. The *Age-Herald* phones were "bedlam" with calls from Edgewood, Mountain Brook, Avondale, Southside, Fairfield, Pratt City, West End, Central Park, Bessemer.
- Reports varied: large/small objects, fast/suspended, sounds/silent, formations/individual.
- **Staff Sergeant Ira L. Livingston** (RA 14 153 972, Air Corps) sworn statement (6 July 1947): at 2045 hours, eating supper at 1354 Meadow Lane, Green Acres, Birmingham, next-door neighbor Herman M. Seokwel called to see "Flying Discs" outside. Objects appeared West of Birmingham traveling Southeast at ~2,000 ft above horizon at 45° angle, ~2 ft diameter, dim glow, 500-600 mph, traveling in definite arc, one appeared to come straight up. Livingston believed 7-10 objects, silent, composed of single light.
- **Birmingham AAF base (Hq 14th Air Force, Orlando FL)** declines to investigate, characterizing it as "an isolated case." First Indorsement to ADC concurs.

### Newfoundland / Harmon Field sightings (July 1947)

Serial 130 contains **five distinct Newfoundland reports** not previously surfaced:

- **10 July 1947, ~6 miles SSW of Harmon Field** — Disc-shaped object, same size as a C-54 as seen from 10,000 ft; translucent/silvery, left a **bluish-black trail ~15 miles long** (similar to afterglow of powerful landing light). **Mr. Robert E. Leidy took two (2) Kodachrome photographs of the trail** — film transmitted to Asst. Chief of Staff, Intelligence, Newfoundland Base Command. Witnesses: **John N. Merhman** (TWA mechanic), **John W. Woodruff** (PAA mechanic, Chief Mechanic, 7 years experience), **Robert Leidy** (PAA mechanic). Woodruff described the object as "cut a straight path right through the cloud" leaving trail "dark bluish in color." Called it "as straight as an arrow." Not a meteor: "too clean." Interrogation transcripts preserved.
- **11 July 1947, Codroy, Newfoundland** — **Flame-colored disc** with cone-shaped trail, barrel-head size, seen by **John Legge** (foreman of Buchans Mine, 40, former civilian Air Detection Corps member), **Wm. Evans**, and **Albert Samms** (12-year-old boy). Legge: "felt sure that the object he had seen was something new which he had never seen before." Samms' mother **Mrs. John Samms** (Post Mistress) corroborated after Albert ran in to tell her. A **Game Warden at South Branch** also reported a second-hand sighting agreeing with Codroy reports.
- **20 July 1947, SS *Burgeo* (enroute Sydney NS → Port aux Basques NF)** — Four to five **silvery-to-reddish flashes**, ~1 second apart, equidistant, like fireflies but larger. **Messrs. Maitland, Larkin, Douglas, Hamilton** (Hamilton, Metcalfe and Kansas City Bridge Companies) and **Captain Gullage** (master of the *Burgeo*) all observed. Captain Gullage said same thing seen 15 July at same location, but **faster and frequently changing course, changing course abruptly several times**.
- **23 July 1947, Harmon Field, Stephenville, Newfoundland** — **Intermittent reddish flashes** for ~3 minutes at ~10,000 ft, from South heading NNE (30°), high velocity. Observers said not a shooting star (no streak) and not an airplane (maneuvers too abrupt, no motor noise). **Miss Patricia Abbott** (Newfoundland Government Employee) and **Lt. Hammaker** (Navigator and Public Relations Officer).
- **Grand Falls, Newfoundland, 9 July 1947** — **Constable LeStrange** report: four round figures overhead at terrific speed, side by side, phosphorus glow, barrel-head size, rocking motion. Saw one additional object described as "fish flash across the sky." Wife, mother-in-law, and **John Jackman** (resident of St. John's) also observed.

### F. M. Johnson Portland OR letter (20 August 1947)

- **F. M. Johnson**, 106 No. West 1st Ave, Portland, OR, prospectors in Mt. Adams district, saw the **same flying objects** at about the same time Kenneth Arnold claimed his sighting (24 June 1947). Described: ~1,000 ft altitude, ~30 ft diameter, tapering sharply to a point at head end, oval shape, **bright top surface**, no noise, object in tail end looked like **big hand of a clock shifting side to side like a big magnet**. Last sight: objects standing on edge, banking in a cloud. 4th AF Lt. Col. Donald L. Springer forwarded to FBI: "Your attention is invited to the similarity of statement by Mr. Johnson and Mr. Arnold."

### Dick Rankin Bakersfield CA sighting (10 June 1947)

- **Richard "Dick" Rankin** (7,000 hours pilot, brother of late "Tex" Rankin, stunt pilot, US Forest Service mapper, auto court operator) saw **ten articles flying from South to North** at ~8,500 ft, ~350 mph, on 10 June 1947 from his front yard at 150 South 1st St, Bakersfield, CA. About 2:15 PM they returned on reverse course — **only seven in formation**. Resembled **navy's XF5U-1 Flying Flapjack** (thin, round, twin propellers, stubby tail). The navy and manufacturer had announced **only one XF5U-1 was built and it never left Connecticut**. The *Oregonian* (8 July 1947) ran a front-page article.

### Urie / Hawkins Snake River canyon (13 August 1947)

- **A. C. Urie** (Twin Falls County fish and game commissioner) and sons Keith (8) and Billy (10) saw **two circular objects** at ~75 ft above Snake River canyon floor, ~1,000 mph, at 1 PM Wednesday. Urie got a "close-up view" — **light sky blue with red tubular fiery glow at side of top**, ~20 ft long × 10 ft high × 10 ft wide, oblong, "inverted pie-plate or broad-brimmed straw hat compressed from two sides." Could see a **knot on the side** from which flames shot. No smoke or odor. "Rode up and down over the hills and hollows at a speed indicating some type of control faster than the reflexes of man. It is my opinion that it is guided by instruments and must be powered by atomic energy." Made just a **S-W-i-s-h** sound. Urie's sons got bottom and side views from ~45° angle. **L. W. Hawkins** (Twin Falls county commissioner) also confirmed seeing two circular objects reflecting light at Salmon dam same morning. Newspaper article with artist's conception (Vic Goertzen) preserved.

### Dan Nelson "Flying Saucer Mystery Solved" (30 July 1947)

- **Dan Nelson**, 53, attorney-at-law, 926 Perrine Building, Oklahoma City, interviewed by OCI Kalman D. Simon at Tinker Field. Nelson claimed to have **solved the Flying Saucer Mystery** through automobile experiments. His theory: saucers are **reflections from the sun shining on bright objects in or on the automobile**, reflected onto the driver's **ventilation wing (wing) window**. Movement of automobile + non-perfect mirror + passing objects = apparent great speed. Car vibration = apparent rotation. Any number of objects visible depending on direction and number of bright objects reflected. He notified War Department 19 July 1947; received answer 23 July referring to Air Forces. Released statement to *The Oklahoman* 29 July. Nelson's detailed multi-page treatise is preserved in the serial, explaining in detail how car door handles, rings, and electric lights produce disc-like reflections.

### John F. Cole South Brooksville ME (28 July 1947)

- **John F. Cole** wrote to **Dr. E. Tousey** (Micron Waves Section Head, Naval Research Laboratory) about objects seen **prior to the first reports of "flying saucers" in New England**. ~50° altitude, NW direction, 10-15 seconds, estimated 1,200 mi/hr. Loud roar, 10 light-colored objects in no regular formation, spread ~90° arc. Left-tangent showed two dark shapes that "may have been tail wings." Suggested naval demonstration from Eastport / Bar Harbor / Belfast.

### Lt. McGinty Grand Canyon (30 June 1947)

- **Lt. William G. McGinty, USN, P-80 student**, flying at 25,000 ft over Grand Canyon, Arizona, on 30 June 1947 at ~0710 MST, saw **two round objects going at "inconceivable speeds, straight down."** His reaction: turn away from the objects. One followed the other seconds apart. Objects ~8 ft diameter, light gray. Would have hit ground ~25 miles south of South Rim.

### Lt. B. B. Armstrong Lake Mead (4 July 1947)

- **1st Lt. B. B. Armstrong** (170th AAP Base Unit, 10th AAF, Brooks Field) saw **five or six white, circular objects** at ~10,000 ft at 4 o'clock, smooth and in close formation, ~36 inches diameter. Heading ~180°. **His brother, Captain Maleola O. Armstrong** (Instructor, Williams Field), relayed the report.

### M. Lenore Gorey Tarzana CA (6 July 1947)

- **M. Lenore Gorey** wrote to **Director of Military Intelligence, War Department** (via Dr. Hugh L. Dryden, Bureau of Standards) about two phenomena observed on Sunday afternoon, 6 July 1947, at a Tarzana bus stop. First: "Saucers" appeared out of nowhere, uniform size, regular intervals, turned corner from West, rolled along at great speed parallel to Ventura Boulevard. Second: **milky white rays in sunlight crisscrossing like searchlights**, converging toward a center with **spinning red fragments** (color of cornelian stone, sharp and irregular, "flying as if by a magnet"). She speculated the rays came from the saucers as they turned the corner.

### Air Training Command Williams Field / Brooks Field (July 1947)

- **Williams Field, Chandler, Arizona** (35 July 1947 memo): **Lt. B. B. Armstrong** report of five or six white circular objects at 10,000 ft near Lake Mead, Nevada.
- **Brooks Field, San Antonio, Texas** (15 July 1947 MOIC): Armstrong's report forwarded to Flying Division, Air Training Command.

### "Summary of Information" — ADC analytical conclusions

- A detailed analytical document (from detailed study of reports selected for their impression of veracity) formed several conclusions:
  - (a) Situation is "not all too much in some natural phenomenon or imagination. **Something is really flying around.**"
  - (b) Lack of topside inquiries vs. demanding inquiries from former events gives "more than ordinary weight to the possibility that this is a Project."
  - (c) Physical appearance conclusions: (1) metallic surface; (2) when trail observed, light-colored blue-brown haze similar to rocket engine exhaust, fuel may be throttled (liquid rocket); (3) circular/elliptical, flat bottom, slightly domed top, C-54/Constellation size; (4) two tabs at rear, symmetrical about time axis; (5) 3-9 in formation, speed always above 300 knots; (6) discs oscillate laterally while flying.

## Open Questions

- **Leidy's Kodachrome photographs** of the 10 July 1947 Harmon Field trail — were they ever analyzed? The serial says the film was "transmitted to the Asst. Chief of Staff, Intelligence, Newfoundland Base Command for processing" — no follow-up is preserved in this serial.
- **Crossland's Birmingham negative** — the 15-second exposure would, in principle, be analyzable for star background; no lab follow-up is documented in this serial.
- **The "Summary of Information" analytically concluded** that "Something is really flying around" and gave six physical characteristics — but who authored this summary? The document has no named author, only ADC routing.
- **M. Lenore Gorey's secondary phenomenon** — milky white rays converging on spinning red cornelian fragments: was this ever traced? The letter was sent to the Director of Military Intelligence via Dr. Dryden — no reply is in this serial.
- **Dan Nelson's claim** — forwarded to Air Forces as part of the serial, but no analysis of his "solution" is preserved.
