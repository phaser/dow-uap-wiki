## Key Points

- **R. Presley, age 24, 218 Illinois Avenue, Oak Ridge, TN** — photographed his family and house on one afternoon in July 1947, then used remaining film on the roll to take a picture of the mountain. When developed, the last shot showed an object he identified as a "flying saucer."
- **Knoxville News-Sentinel clipping** — photostatic copy of a newspaper article about the Presley photographs, preserved as an enclosure to the Bureau.
- **Internal Security classification** — the Knoxville file was filed under "Internal Security" rather than standard UFO investigation, suggesting the Bureau treated it as a potential security concern (given Oak Ridge's role as the Manhattan Project's uranium production site).
- **Quote from Presley**: "Now don't start trying to explain it off. Just go ahead and say what it looks like. Sure. Sure. You're right. It is a Flying Saucer."
- **No follow-up investigation** — the file contains only the photographs and newspaper clipping; no field office report or AAF referral is preserved in this serial.
