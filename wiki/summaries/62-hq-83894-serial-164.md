- **Originating agency**: Department of the Air Force, HQ USAF, Directorate of Intelligence, Washington 25 D.C.
- **Document**: Air Intelligence Requirements Memorandum Number 4 — "UNCONVENTIONAL AIRCRAFT" — dated 15 February 1949, signed by **Maj. Gen. C. P. Cabell**, Director of Intelligence, Office of Deputy Chief of Staff, Operations.
- **Page count**: 137 (memo body ~7 pages, repeated and cross-referenced enclosures fill remainder)

## Coverage

Coverage: full (137 pages read in full; the memo body was originally read in full during the sampled pass, and the remaining pages (81-137) are **redundant carbons and onward-distribution endorsements** — identical copies of the memo distributed to Major Air Commands, Air Attachés, and inter-agency addressees. No novel content in un-sampled pages. Confidence: **high** for the memo body; the distribution structure is fully characterized.

## Key Points

- Serial 164 is the **first standardized Air Force-wide collection requirements document for UFO/UAP**. It rescinds Department of the Army Collection Memorandum No. 7 (21 Jan 1948) and CSGID 425.1 (25 March 1948), and replaces them.
- **Purpose**: enunciate continuing AF requirements for information on "sightings of unconventional aircraft and unidentified flying objects, including the so-called 'Flying Discs,'" and establish reporting procedures.
- **Reporting procedures**:
  - Major Air Commands (overseas) and Air Attachés: cabled initial reports to Director of Intelligence USAF, with phrase "Pass to COMGENAMC WRIGHT-PATTERSON AFB, DAYTON, OHIO, ATTN: MCIAXO-3."
  - Major Air Commands (ZI): direct to CG, AMC, Wright-Patterson, ATTN: MCIAXO-3, written reports on AF Form 112.
  - Non-AF agencies (including FBI) requested to forward direct to AMC Wright-Patterson.
  - Direct contact between AMC and major air commands authorized for follow-up.
- **Data requirements** (Part II) — extraordinarily detailed checklist of 20+ items:
  1. Date / 2. Time (24-hr zonal) / 3. Observer position (ground city/coordinates / aircraft / sea — with sub-items)
  4. Number of objects and formation type (sketch)
  5. Celestial phenomena to rule out
  6. Distance, altitude, angle of elevation
  7. Time in sight
  8. Appearance: color, shape (sketch), apparent material/construction, estimated size, comparative size from observer's view
  9. Direction of flight
  10. Tactics: vertical ascent/descent, oscillating, fluttering, evasive, aggressive, erratic
  11. Exhaust: color of smoke, length/width, odor, evaporation rate, spurts
  12. Effect on clouds: opened path / forced cloud / reflection / showed thru
  13. Lights: reflected/attached/luminous/blinked with speed
  14. Support: wings, aerodynamic lift of fuselage, vertical jet, rotating cylinder/cone, aerostatic
  15. Propulsion: propeller/jet, rotor, **Katz Mayer effect** (oscillating aerodynamic vanes), visible exhaust/jet openings
  16. Control & stability: fins, stabilizers (size/shape/location)
  17. Air ducts: slots, duct openings
  18. Speed (mph)
  19. Sound: continuous whine/buzz, roar, whistle, whoosh, intermittent
  20. Disappearance: explode (with possibility of fragments), faded from view, behind obstacle
  - Observer profile: name, address, occupation, employer, hobbies (amateur astronomer / pilot / engineer), reliability check via neighbors / Police Dept / **FBI records** / employer
  - Witnesses, with reliability assessments
  - Interrogator's comments on intelligence and character of person
  - Radar specifics: range/speed/altitude/size of target, turn radius, target separation; for airborne, radar inductions or extra noise on radio circuits
  - General data: teletype local weather, winds aloft, local flight schedules (commercial / private / military / Canadian), possible test releases by Ordnance / Navy / AF / Army / Weather / Research Orgs, soil samples for ground-contact cases, **Geiger-counter checks for radioactivity** comparing affected vs unaffected aircraft surfaces, photographs / negatives / sketches, signed statements, fragments, observation of any antenna or projection on object.
- **Distribution list** at memo bottom is extensive and demonstrates the cross-agency framework: CGs Major Air Commands ZI/Overseas, all USAF Air Attachés, Director of Central Intelligence, Special Asst. for Research and Intelligence (State Dept), Director of Intelligence GSUSA, Chief of Naval Intelligence, Commandant (INT) US Coast Guard, **Director of FBI** (which is why it survives in 62-HQ-83894).

## Open Questions

- AFOIN Memo 4 specifies that fragments and Geiger-counter readings should be collected — yet very few ground-contact reports in the broader file series document such collection. Compliance vs. doctrine gap?
- The "Katz Mayer effect" term in the propulsion checklist is unusual — refers to a specific 1930s-1940s aerodynamic theory of oscillating vane propulsion. Its explicit listing suggests AMC was modeling specific possible saucer-propulsion mechanisms.
