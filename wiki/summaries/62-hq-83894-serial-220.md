## Source Metadata

- **Originating agency**: Letter from a private citizen in Mexico, forwarded to FBI HQ via SAC New York; also appears as cross-reference in Section 5.
- **Document**: Letter from **Miguel Angel Garcia Macias** (Pianist, Composer, "Discoverer and Ideographic Inventor"), No. 324 Pino Suárez Avenue, Veracruz, Veracruz, Mexico, dated 19 March 1950. Addressed to "the President of the Commission of Scientific Investigation of the United States of North America, New York."
- **Page count**: 15
- **Classification**: never classified; received by FBI 7 April 1950.
- **File size**: 8 MB.
- **Raw path**: `raw/65_hs1-834228961_62-hq-83894_serial_220.pdf`

## Coverage

Coverage: full (15 pages, including Spanish original, English translation by Mrs. Sophia Saliba, and the photographic / sketched enclosures).

## Key Points

- The letter is a self-described "ideographic" submission to the (non-existent) US Commission of Scientific Investigation, claiming the writer has invented numerous devices that were stolen and patented in the United States.
- **Inventions claimed by Macias**:
  - "Gote-Graduns" (graduated dropper)
  - "Automatic Shovels" for dump trucks
  - "Rooms for Measuring Optics" (vision examination)
  - "The Gradual Centimetric Music of the Future" (Pianometric / Phonometric / Centimetric / Discophonometric)
  - "Ferrotactometro / Ferrocemactometro" — railway/automobile collision avoidance from a distance
  - "Exquinocis" — graduated time/distance for clocks
  - Stability-of-surface theory for transatlantic shipping safety
  - "Stratospheric Aerostats" — combining global stability of a balloon with semi-global stability of a parachute, powered by atomic/nuclear stratospheric skyrockets, "velocity Greater than that of Light or Sound."
- **Key claim about flying saucers**: Macias asserts the United States already possesses "stratospheric aerostats" (i.e., flying saucers), powered by atomic force, and submits his theoretical-physical sketches "to avoid Atomic Explosion on the Earth." The letter is essentially a sales pitch / invention disclosure.
- **Enclosure** (received and pasted in): a copy of *El Universal* Mexico City (16 March 1950, No. 12,096) carrying the headline "Las Primeras Fotografías de un 'Plato Volador', Logradas en Durango a Nueve mil Pies de Altura" — "The First Photographs of a 'Flying Saucer', Obtained in Durango at 9000 Feet of Altitude," credited to **Germán Horacio Robles, Jr.**, student at the National School of Engineering. Robles describes the apparatus as a "double truncated cone joined at its bases" propelled by jet propulsion, photographed against the skies of Durango.
- **Macias's hand-drawn sketches** (titled "Wings and Propellers" / "Capto-Aereo-Fuerza"): four drawings of his proposed disc with rotating air-capture/expulsion blades, a 3-level vertical structure (1st level air entry, 2nd level oxygen chamber, 3rd level vacuum), with "Atomic Force" and "Global-Conic and Semi-Global Stability" annotations.
- **FBI handling**: Hoover, on **17 May 1950**, forwards the letter to the Director, Office of Special Investigations, Inspector General, Department of the Air Force, "in view of the fact that a translation reflects the correspondent's interest in flying discs." No reply by Bureau to Macias.
- This case is interesting historically because it shows the FBI being used in 1950 as an inadvertent clearinghouse for Latin American "saucer claims," and because the *El Universal* Robles photographs of Durango are one of the earliest Mexican UFO photographic claims in the historical record.
