## Key Points

- This serial is the dust jacket of **Gray Barker's *They Knew Too Much About Flying Saucers*** (University Books, Inc., 404 Fourth Avenue, New York 16, NY, 1956; jacket designer Gropper Associates, Inc.; price $3.50).
- The book is the foundational work of the **Men in Black mythology**: Barker chronicles "what happened to certain researchers and investigators who found out where the saucers come from."
- Front-flap blurb (preserved on the scan):
  - "One by one, the leading figures among flying-saucer researchers who have challenged the government denial that saucers come from outer space have had their mouths shut. The men in dark suits got to them ... They are still alive, still living among us, but they no longer publish saucer material and they will not talk about saucers or why they no longer will speak of them. They have been silenced."
  - "Three men in dark suits have visited them and warned them what they were exposing." Barker calls the visitors "the Three Men."
  - "Whether the saucers have indeed visited men from outer space, or whether the events recorded in this book have anything to do with anything atomic ... we do not know. They are simply real, told by an otherwise prosaic and successful Clarksburg, West Virginia film booking and buying agent."
- Back-flap biography of Barker:
  - Six-foot, "handsome, with a warm infectious laugh"; West Virginia farming country accent.
  - Has been head of an English department in a Maryland school system, audio-visual education consultant for a large school supplier, theatre manager and motion picture projection technician.
  - Publishes *The Saucerian* magazine.
  - Mentions the *Clarksburg News* publisher having permitted Barker to use a letter in the book — context for the famous **Albert K. Bender** material that drove the book.
- Although only the dust jacket is in this serial, the FBI's retention of the cover/jacket of a popular UFO book in the file series is itself notable: the Bureau was tracking the saucer-publishing industry circa 1956 and indexing key titles by reference name.

## Notable Incidents / Cases

- The book chronicles **Albert K. Bender's "three men" experience (Bridgeport CT, September 1953)** — Bender's IFSB (cf. Section 7) was abruptly dissolved after a visit by three men in dark suits; this episode codified the **Men in Black** as a recurring trope in saucer literature.
