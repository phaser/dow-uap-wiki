## Key Points

- Serial 438 documents the **Lonnie Zamora / Socorro New Mexico landing case** of **24 April 1964** — one of the most credible single-witness UFO landing reports in the historical record, also investigated extensively by Project Blue Book (Hynek, Quintanilla) and personally observed by FBI SA D. Arthur Byrnes Jr. within hours.
- **Sequence (per FBI LHM 8 May 1964):**
  - Special Agent Byrnes was at the Socorro NM State Police office on other business late afternoon 24 April 1964.
  - At ~5:45-5:50 pm, **Nep Lopez** (radio operator, Socorro County Sheriff's Office) came down the hall to the State Police office and told Sgt. **M. S. Chavez** (NM State Police) that **Officer Lonnie Zamora** had radioed about an "unknown object which 'landed and has taken off,'" about one mile southwest of Socorro.
  - Byrnes finished his work at ~6:00 pm and proceeded to the site, where Officer Zamora, Socorro County Undersheriff **Jim Luckie**, Sgt. M. S. Chavez, and Officer **Ted Jordan** (NM State Police) were already assembled.
  - Byrnes vouches for Zamora's character: "known intimately for approximately five years, is well regarded as a sober, industrious, and conscientious officer and not given to fantasy. Officer Zamora was noted to be perfectly sober and somewhat agitated over his experience."
- **Physical trace evidence (observed by SA Byrnes):**
  - **Four indentations** in the rough ground at the site, each approximately **16 inches by 6 inches rectangular**, depth ~2 inches; each made "by an object going into the earth at an angle from a center line"; earth pushed to the far side of each.
  - **Three burned patches of clumps of grass** inside the four depressions; one burned area outside; surrounding clumps of grass appeared not to be disturbed.
  - **Three circular marks**, each ~4 inches in diameter, smooth, depth ~1/8 inch — "as if a jar lid had gently been pushed into the sand."
  - No other persons observed in the area. No other connected objects. No houses or inhabited dwellings in sight of the area.
- **Zamora's narrative** (recounted to FBI 24-25 April 1964): While in Socorro Police Car #2 (1964 Pontiac white) starting at ~5:45 pm, he started chasing a car (suspected speeder, possibly Floyd Reynolds' 17-year-old son Vivian, in a new black Chevrolet) due south from the courthouse. Near George Morillo residence on Old Rodeo Street (extension of Park Street south), about half a mile south of Spring Street, the chased car was about three blocks ahead going toward the rodeo grounds — and that is where the LHM excerpt cuts off in the read pages.
- **Zamora's office contact info preserved** in the FBI record: residence 606 Reservoir Street Socorro, home phone 835-1134, office phone 835-0941, on the 2:00-10:00 pm shift.
- This LHM is the FBI's contemporary, on-scene record. It is consistent with — and was a foundational data source for — Project Blue Book's later investigation. It captures the indentations and burned clumps without any speculation about origin or interpretation.

## Notable Incidents / Cases

- **24 April 1964, ~5:45 pm, ~1 mile SW of Socorro, NM** — **Lonnie Zamora / Socorro Landing.** Sole-witness landing report by veteran NM police officer of an egg/oval craft with two humanoid figures, with subsequent take-off; physical trace evidence (4 rectangular impressions, burned grass, 3 small circular marks) photographed and documented by FBI SA D. Arthur Byrnes Jr. within ~2 hours.
