## Coverage

Coverage: sampled (pages 1-20 and 60-80 of 124). Sub A is by nature a scrapbook; quality and content vary widely page to page.

## Key Points

- This sub-file collects public-domain newspaper clippings about UFOs that the Bureau retained as background context.
- **Capt. Peter Killian, American Airlines DC-3 sighting (24-25 February 1959)** — Capt. Killian (Syosset NY, 15 years senior pilot) and co-pilot John Dee (Nyack NY) on a Newark NJ → Detroit non-stop flight at 8500 ft between Philipsburg and Bradford PA at 8:45 pm see three "bright whitish lights" / saucers paralleling the plane for **45 minutes**. 35 passengers also observed. *AP wire, 25 Feb 1959.* Killian: "I have never seen anything like it before."
- **William J. Nash and William H. Fortenberry — Pan American DC-4 sighting (Norfolk VA, 14 July 1952)** — pilot and first officer of a Pan American DC-4 see **eight glowing orange-red disc-shaped objects** ~100 ft diameter, 10-15 ft thick, in formation below the plane; objects make a 150° sharp turn west, then a 90° banked climb, joined by two more for total of ten. Nash: "We feel ... they must be from some extraterrestrial source." Santos Cellanes (Acting PAA Operations Manager) backs the pilots. Cited as one of the most-quoted commercial-aviation UFO observations of the 1952 wave.
- **Dr. Carl Jung statement (Alamogordo NM, 29 July 1958)** — Swiss psychologist, having studied UFOs since 1944, releases report through L. J. Lorenzen of the **Aerial Phenomena Research Organization (APRO)** UFO Filter Center at Holloman AFB. Key quote: *"I can only say for certain these things are not a mere rumor; something has been seen ... A purely psychological explanation is ruled out ... if the extra-terrestrial origin should be confirmed this would prove the existence of an intelligent inter-planetary relationship."* — referenced widely in subsequent UFOlogy. The clipping notes the AF maintains UFOs do not exist as such.
- **Reinhold Schmidt "Kearney NE space ship" claim (5 November 1957)** — grain dealer in Kearney NE; claims to have visited a space ship in a field with six occupants, conversed amicably. Air Force Continental Air Defense Command investigators questioned him; Schmidt's car oil "found to be ordinary." Local merchants joked: "Space Ships Tuned Up", "space ship specials." *Omaha World-Herald* clipping retained.
- **Joseph Perry, Grand Blanc MI (March-May 1962 follow-up)** — *Detroit Free Press* article by Charles Manos: Perry, the "talented pizza man," took color slides of the moon in February 1962 with a homemade telescope (350-400 power), captured a saucer-like object silhouetted against the moon. Sent the slide to the Pentagon, who returned it weeks later — Perry says the slide "AIN'T what it used to be ... it's not the same"; he believes Pentagon "did something to it." UFO group offered to buy the rights; a national magazine offered to buy the picture. Joseph Perry first appeared in [[summaries/62-hq-83894-section-9]] for his February 1960 photo.
- **L. J. Lorenzen, APRO Filter Center, Holloman AFB AZ → NM (10-12 August 1958)** — APRO Director sends teletype: nine persons reported "stars" clustered like a hand for more than an hour near Alamogordo NM, then the stars "moved apart and disappeared." Air Force, airports and U.S. Weather Bureau confirm no Air Force / civilian / weather phenomena explain it. Times: 9:45 pm – 11:30 pm.
- **Dr. Urner Liddel / Skyhook explanation (Look Magazine, 12 February 1951 reprint clipping)**: AP wire reports Navy ONR's Liddel says flying saucers are "real but they're sun-lit cosmic-ray studies" — referring to the **Skyhook plastic balloons** (100 ft diameter, up to 19 miles altitude, 200 mph drift). Skyhook bottoms light up with the sun's lateral rays at dusk producing the saucer appearance. Liddel concludes: "There is not a single reliable report of an observation which is not attributable to the cosmic balloons." Quotes Mantell (7 Jan 1948 KY death) as a Skyhook chase victim.
- **O'Hare AFB / Chicago saucer reports (3 July 1952)** — Public Information officers report 4-8 UFO reports in Chicago vicinity that week; jet patrols on routine 24-hour alert (no special "saucer alert" ordered); some reports traced to an orphanage picnic that released 5,000 toy balloons.
- **John Baldwin / Hanford Atomic Plant sighting (Richland WA, 5 July 1952)** — four Florida pilots flying with three WWII veterans in a private plane near 9000 ft over the Hanford AEC plant report a "perfectly round disc, white in color, almost transparent with small vapor trails like the tentacles of an octopus." The disc backs away, changes shape to flat, gains speed, and disappears.
- **Wide variety of additional clippings** in the not-fully-read pages: 1962 saucer photos in *Detroit Times*, AP wire stories, *Washington Post and Times Herald*, *N.Y. Herald Tribune*, *N.Y. Journal-American*, *N.Y. Times*, *N.Y. Daily News*, *Wall Street Journal* — the FBI clipping clerk used a standard checkmark form (e.g., "WD-22") to log which papers each clipping came from.

## Open Questions

- The Sub A material is curated by the Bureau but without commentary — what filtering principle was used? It seems to favor "credible witness / aviation pilot" stories and to include Skyhook-explanation rebuttals.
- Joseph Perry's "Pentagon altered my saucer slide" claim: was this ever investigated? FBI does not appear to have followed up substantively.
- Carl Jung's APRO statement is widely cited in UFOlogy but rarely traced to its primary source — the FBI clipping (29 July 1958, *Washington Post* lineage) is one of the best contemporary captures.
