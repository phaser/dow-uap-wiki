## Key Points

### Nash-Fortenberry Pan American DC-4 sighting (14 July 1952)

- **William J. Nash** (pilot) and **William H. Fortenberry** (first officer) of Pan American DC-4, Norfolk VA → Miami FL, see **eight glowing orange-red disc-shaped objects** ~100 ft diameter, 10-15 ft thick, in formation below the plane. Objects make a 150° sharp turn west, then a 90° banked climb, joined by two more for total of ten. Nash: "We feel ... they must be from some extraterrestrial source." **Santos Cellanes** (Acting PAA Operations Manager) backs the pilots. Cited as one of the most-quoted commercial-aviation UFO observations of the 1952 wave.

### Capt. Peter Killian American Airlines DC-3 sighting (24-25 February 1959)

- **Capt. Peter Killian** (Syosset NY, 15 years senior pilot) and co-pilot **John Dee** (Nyack NY) on a Newark NJ → Detroit non-stop flight at 8,500 ft between Philipsburg and Bradford PA at 8:45 pm see **three "bright whitish lights" / saucers** paralleling the plane for **45 minutes**. 35 passengers also observed. AP wire, 25 Feb 1959. Killian: "I have never seen anything like it before."

### Dr. Carl Jung statement (29 July 1958)

- Swiss psychologist, having studied UFOs since 1944, releases report through **L. J. Lorenzen** of the **Aerial Phenomena Research Organization (APRO)** UFO Filter Center at Holloman AFB. Key quote: *"I can only say for certain these things are not a mere rumor; something has been seen ... A purely psychological explanation is ruled out ... if the extra-terrestrial origin should be confirmed this would prove the existence of an intelligent inter-planetary relationship."* — referenced widely in subsequent UFOlogy. The clipping notes the AF maintains UFOs do not exist as such.

### Reinhold Schmidt "Kearney NE space ship" claim (5 November 1957)

- **R. O. Schmidt**, ~50, grain dealer of Bakersfield CA (now living in Kearney NE), claims to have visited a space ship in a field with **six occupants** (two men and two women working on wires/instruments, one spoke German, one spoke excellent English), conversed amicably. Air Force Continental Air Defense Command investigators questioned him; Schmidt's car oil "found to be ordinary" (resembled commercial auto oil from a partially-empty can found near the landing site). Schmidt's car was **paralyzed** — he "couldn't move" when the occupants approached. He said the ship's occupants **searched him for weapons**, then invited him inside. The ship had **fans at each end**, no sound. When repairs completed, he was asked to leave but told he'd be unable to start his car until the machine disappeared. He said the machine "blended into the sky — like it changed color or disappeared into the air." Air Force Wright-Patt official told Dayton Journal-Herald: "no evidence in the past 10 years that flying saucers are real"; 5,700 reports investigated 1947-1957; not a single landing impression, footprint, saucer, or "little green man" found. Local merchants joked: "Space Ships Tuned Up," "space ship specials." **State Penitentiary records showed a man of the same name served a term for embezzlement from Scotts Bluff County in the 1930's.** Sheriff Warrick was "convinced [he] saw nothing."

### Joseph Perry Grand Blanc MI follow-up (March-May 1962)

- **Detroit Free Press** article by Charles Manos: Perry, the "talented pizza man," took color slides of the moon in February 1962 with a homemade telescope (350-400 power), captured a saucer-like object silhouetted against the moon. Sent the slide to the Pentagon, who returned it weeks later — Perry says the slide **"AIN'T what it used to be ... it's not the same"**; he believes Pentagon "did something to it." UFO group offered to buy the rights; a national magazine offered to buy the picture. Perry first appeared in [[summaries/62-hq-83894-section-9]] for his February 1960 photo.

### L. J. Lorenzen APRO Filter Center Alamogordo NM mass cluster (10-12 August 1958)

- APRO Director sends teletype: nine persons reported "stars" clustered **like a hand** for more than an hour near Alamogordo NM, then the stars "moved apart and disappeared." Air Force, airports and U.S. Weather Bureau confirm no Air Force / civilian / weather phenomena explain it. Times: 9:45 pm – 11:30 pm.

### Dr. Urner Liddel / Skyhook explanation (Look Magazine, 12 February 1951 reprint clipping)

- AP wire reports Navy ONR's Liddel says flying saucers are "real but they're sun-lit cosmic-ray studies" — referring to the **Skyhook plastic balloons** (100 ft diameter, up to 19 miles altitude, 200 mph drift). Skyhook bottoms light up with the sun's lateral rays at dusk producing the saucer appearance. Liddel concludes: "There is not a single reliable report of an observation which is not attributable to the cosmic balloons." Quotes Mantell (7 Jan 1948 KY death) as a Skyhook chase victim.

### O'Hare AFB / Chicago saucer reports (3 July 1952)

- Public Information officers report 4-8 UFO reports in Chicago vicinity that week; jet patrols on routine 24-hour alert (no special "saucer alert" ordered); some reports traced to an orphanage picnic that released 5,000 toy balloons.

### John Baldwin / Hanford Atomic Plant sighting (Richland WA, 5 July 1952)

- Four Florida pilots flying with three WWII veterans in a private plane near 9,000 ft over the **Hanford AEC plant** report a "perfectly round disc, white in color, almost transparent with small vapor trails like the tentacles of an octopus." The disc backs away, changes shape to flat, gains speed, and disappears.

### Quantico Marine Base red lights (22 nights, December 1953)

- **Mysterious red lights** over Quantico Marine Base, 22 times in six nights. **Pfc. Norman Viets** (18, Greenville PA) first sighting at 9:05 pm Dec 30 at Tank Park. Light ~1.5 ft diameter, 40-15 mph, followed tree line, went straight down then straight up. **Sergt. Francis R. Salinder** also saw it. At least **30 other Marines including half a dozen officers** saw it. On one occasion, sentries reported seeing **three lights at once**. Lights dropped straight down, flew straight up, stood still. **Maj. D. D. Pomerleau** (base provost marshal) admitted the lights had characteristics he never expected on an airliner. Base information officer Maj. A. B. Ferguson offered "airplane navigation light" explanation (new blinking red lights installed on airline tails six months prior). Viets: "That was no airplane. I first thought it might be a weather balloon, but it wasn't. Either way, you could have seen the shape." When he saw it come up, he "grabbed a butcher knife and headed for the tank shed to help out his troops."

### Keyhoe / Utah film controversy (January 1954)

- **Maj. Donald E. Keyhoe** (ret.) claims in his book "Flying Saucers from Outer Space" that the so-called "Utah film" proves saucers are interplanetary. **Albert M. Chop** (former Air Force civilian expert on saucer project, now with Douglas Aircraft) published a letter on the book jacket stating the Air Force is "aware of Maj. Keyhoe's conclusion that the flying saucers are from another planet." Air Force challenged Chop's claim and said he was "merely expressing his personal opinion." Keyhoe claims the final analysis proved "the saucer formation could not be explained as any known aircraft or other conventional objects." Air Force Brig. Gen. Sory Smith: "We do not know enough about it to deny that flying saucers exist. Conversely, we have no proof that they do exist." Keyhoe: "If any official ... says that it did not rule out birds, known aircraft or conventional objects as the cause of those objects, I will call him a liar to his face."

### Canada saucer observatory announcement (November 1952)

- **Wilbert B. Smith** (scientist, federal transport department) announces establishment of a **Canadian Flying Saucer Observatory** at Shirley Bay, Ottawa River, 10 miles west of Ottawa. "There is a very high degree of probability that flying saucers are real objects, and a 60 per cent probability that they are alien vehicles." Project under transport department and defense research board; includes theoretical physicist and gravitational studies specialist. Equipment: ionospheric reactor, electronic sound measurement devices, gamma ray detector, gravimeter. Jet planes may be sent from air force field near Ottawa.

### Dr. Donald H. Menzel Harvard astronomer saucer explanation (1952)

- Harvard astrophysics professor Menzel argues saucers are **fancy mirages** — refraction of lights displaced through miles of cold/warm air layers. Produces similar phenomena in laboratory. Claims Air Force radar scopes in Washington have been described as "spotting the saucers at the same time pilots and ground observers were seeing them with the naked eye" — but radar failed to pick up impulses that CAA added to flying saucer reports.

### Bowling Green OH pilot sighting (date in clipping)

- A private plane pilot saw "an object brighter than the sun for about five seconds" at 2,000 ft, 2 miles west of Bowling Green OH, traveling at ~80 mph. "Looked like a ball of fire," brilliant white like molten steel, then turned blue and took off straight west within five seconds. Faster than any jet. About a foot in diameter.

### Santa Fe NM White Sands missile testing (date in clipping)

- **Col. M. Hendricks**, Commandant of White Sands Proving Grounds, says research "may soon demonstrate" on tentatively — possible that the Department of Defense may explain some things sighted in southwestern skies as "flying saucers." But "there certainly won't be anything like a flying saucer" — "merely streaks of light" from particles of outer space entering atmosphere, heated to incandescence by air resistance.

### Herbert Long Kutztown PA saucer sketch (date in clipping)

- **Herbert Long**, 29, insurance salesman of Kutztown PA, contends he saw a saucer parked on a road 30 ft from his car. Too frightened to approach. Sketch preserved.

### Walter Elliott Anacortes WA saucer photograph (date in clipping)

- **Walter Elliott** of Anacortes photographed an unknown object in his view finder at 1/100th second. Object moves swiftly through sky, seeming part of cloud formation.

### G. Tilghman Richards South Kensington Science Museum (July 1950)

- Senior research assistant and official lecturer at South Kensington Science Museum, London, studied all available flying saucer reports and photographs. Concludes they are **photographs of disc-type aircraft**. Explains annular monoplane design history: Charles H. Zimmerman (1934-35) built disc-wing airplane + helicopter; U.S. Navy granted license to Chance Vought (1937); development went secret. Performance: speeds from 0 to 500 mph, hover, ascend, descend with little forward motion.

### England "flying saucer" over Devon (1950)

- Multiple witnesses from Woolacombe, Exeter, Cullompton, Sidmouth Junction, Paignton (60 miles apart) saw objects at ~4 pm: **no noise, trail of fire from back**. Descriptions substantially in agreement. Mr. J. Violet-Stewart (70-year-old Woolacombe pensioner): "two large circular objects traveling close together in formation, blue, travelling close together." Members of crew of a liberty boat: "two large circular objects travelling south in a horizontal position looking something like large white flames." Mr. Arthur Bearne (55, estate agent, Paignton): saw "circular objects at an incalculable speed leaving a trail of fire" late on Monday night.

### England "flying saucer" over Chard Somerset (December 1950)

- **Sunday Dispatch** reports: 500+ spectators at soccer cup match at Chard, Somerset, saw strange white phenomenon dart across sky at ~3:45 pm. Within split second, seemed to disperse on horizon. Mr. Arthur H. Jenkins (postmaster of Chard): "like a diamond flash; its shape was like a big pear-drop, with the thin end tapering behind. Suddenly it 'arrived' into nothingness." Spectators at rugby match, football match at Perry Street, North Petherton all reported similar. Described as "snake-shape thing which streaked through the sky at a terrific rate." Mr. T. Hollinghurst: "appeared suddenly and went away suddenly, leaving a creamy smoke. There was no noise and there were no planes about."

### Air Force UFO fact sheet (date in clipping — pre-1953)

- Air Force Office of Public Information summary: program established 1947; peak 1,700 reports in 1952, dropped to 429 in 1953; first nine months 1954: 254 sightings. **Over 80% explainable as known objects** (balloons, aircraft, astronomical bodies, atmospheric reflections, birds). Radar explanations: temperature inversion reflections, ionized clouds, birds (flock of ducks in one case). Visual explanations: jet aircraft, weather balloons, 90-foot research balloons, meteors/planets, reflections from objects (mirror analogy), brilliant flashing lights (airline tail lights). **Photographs "worthless as evidence"** — "Innumerable objects, from ashtrays to wash basins, have been photographed while sailing through the air." Videon cameras distributed to military installations. **No evidence received which would tend to indicate that the United States is being observed by machines from outer space or a foreign government.**

### G. Klein "Nazi saucer" story (date in clipping)

- **Georg Klein**, former secret weapons expert in German War Production Ministry, now engineer in Switzerland, claims flying saucers are **top secret weapons of the USA and Russia**, continuation of wartime experiments. Prototypes built in Germany during war (saw one reach 40,000 ft in three minutes over Prague in 1945). Russians captured scale model and top technicians at Breslau. **Walter Schriever** (V-weapon inventor) heads U.S. saucer development in a "clever" deal with the United States.

### Bowling Green OH pilot sighting (date in clipping)

- Private plane pilot saw object "brighter than the sun for about five seconds" at 2,000 ft, two miles west of Bowling Green OH. "Looked like a ball of fire," brilliant white like molten steel, then turned blue and took off straight west within five seconds.

## Open Questions

- The Sub A material is curated by the Bureau but without commentary — what filtering principle was used? It seems to favor "credible witness / aviation pilot" stories and to include Skyhook-explanation rebuttals.
- Joseph Perry's "Pentagon altered my saucer slide" claim: was this ever investigated? FBI does not appear to have followed up substantively.
- Carl Jung's APRO statement is widely cited in UFOlogy but rarely traced to its primary source — the FBI clipping (29 July 1958, *Washington Post* lineage) is one of the best contemporary captures.
- The Quantico red-light case (22 nights, 30+ Marines) — was this ever formally investigated by OSI or the Air Force? The clipping is preserved but no follow-up is in this serial.
- The Keyhoe / Chop / Utah film controversy (January 1954) — Keyhoe claims the final analysis proved saucer formation "could not be explained as any known aircraft." Air Force Brig. Gen. Sory Smith says "neither affirms nor denies." The actual Utah film analysis is not preserved in this serial.
