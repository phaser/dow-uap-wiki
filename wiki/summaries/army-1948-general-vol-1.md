## Key Points

- The folder is a routing / cover-letter compilation — almost all pages are HQ USAF "AFOIR-CO-5" indorsements forwarding incoming "Flying Disc" reports to AMC at Wright-Patterson, the agency designated by Air Force policy to "collect, collate, evaluate, and distribute" all such atmospheric sightings (per AFOIR-CO-5 disposition forms dated 25–27 February 1948 quoting AFOIR Letter / ADC Ltr 45-5, 25 March 1948).
- The standardized incident-report template (Location, Time, Weather, Witness names/occupations/addresses, Photographs/Sketches, and a 10-item object checklist: Number / Shape / Size / Color / Speed / Heading / Maneuverability / Altitude / Sound / Exhaust trail) is documented in this folder — this is the template that produced the [[summaries/army-incident-summaries-1-100]] series.
- Project SIGN is named explicitly on multiple correspondence reference forms ("Project SIGN", "Clippings Service for Project SIGN", 13 February 1948; 15 February 1948).
- Air Materiel Command (Col. H. M. McCoy, Chief T-2) had proposed in early 1948 to maintain interceptor aircraft on continuous alert at all CONUS Air Force installations to chase and gun-camera-photograph "flying discs"; the Director of Plans & Operations (Maj. Gen. S. E. Anderson) recommended against the proposal as infeasible (3 March 1948), and HQ USAF concurred (9 March 1948), citing inadequate radar coverage, manpower cost, and the civilian origin of most reports.
- The folder contains evidence of cross-pollination with German-aerodynamics intelligence: a T-2 report titled "German Flying Wings Designed by Horten Brothers" (Document P-SU/411-IA-1) is referenced; a 1947 U.S. military attaché report from Moscow alleging that 1,800 Soviet aircraft were derived from the Horten VIII jet flying-wing design is also referenced. Collection Memorandum #7 (21 January 1948) directed European Command G-2 to interrogate the Horten brothers, who had been located.
- Reporting policy required reports be sent direct to "Commanding General, Air Materiel Command, Wright-Patterson Air Force Base, Dayton, Ohio, Attn: MCI" (later MCIAXO-3 / TSDIN). Department of the Army installations were instructed to use the same channel.

## Notable Incidents / Cases

- **Hobson, Ohio — night of (date partially OCR-illegible, May 1948)**: Round, phosphorescent disc, ~9 in apparent diameter (from ground), great speed, heading 90°, altitude estimated 6–8 miles, phosphorescent trail. Witnesses: Ben Rupe (NYC RR car inspector), Earl Koush, Bob White (NYC yard clerk), C.K. Hite (NYC patrolman). Reported via FBI Special Agent B.K. Brown (Cleveland, OH, 25 May 1948), forwarded by HQ Eleventh Air Force (Harrisburg, PA), Lt. Col. Garrett.
- **Bakersfield, California — 5–8 March 1948**: Two separate observations of two objects falling earthward with smoke and debris (resembling burning aircraft) southwest of Bakersfield toward Buena Vista Lake. Witnesses: Mr. Les Buchner (5 March 1948, 1610–1655 hrs); Mr. Denio, Pacific General Electric employee (8 March 1948). Sergeant A.M. Larsen (Sheriff's Office, Bakersfield) phoned Fourth Air Force HQ at Hamilton Field on 9 March 1948. No wreckage recovered despite searches by aircraft and rescue units. Lt. Col. Donald L. Springer (AC of S, A-2, Fourth Air Force) signed.
- **Houston, Texas — 19 December 1947 ("Flying Disks" report)**: 175th AAFBU at Ellington Field summary forwarded by Tenth Air Force, Brooks Field (Maj. Raymond D. Stephens, Acting AC of S, A-2). Document is a routing slip, content not in this folder.
- **Tyndall AFB, Panama City, Florida — 12 April 1948**: "Report of Information on 'Flying Disc'" forwarded through Air University (Maxwell AFB), Major M.E.J. Price signing. Content forwarded to AMC.
- **Canada — 2 February 1948**: Military Air Attaché (Canada) report "closes the incident reported by Johnson and Harrison." Underlying incident not described in this folder.
