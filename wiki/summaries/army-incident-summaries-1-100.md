## Source Metadata

- **Originating agency**: U.S. Army Air Forces / U.S. Air Force, Air Materiel Command (AMC), Wright-Patterson AFB — the case-by-case "Incident Summary" series compiled under Project SIGN (later passed to Project GRUDGE / Project BLUE BOOK). Cases originate at AAF/USAF installations across CONUS plus a small number of FBI / civilian / police / commercial-airline reports.
- **Series / identifier**: NARA RG 38, container 143685, Box 7, "Incident Summaries 1-100." Authority block on cover sheet: "NND 917033."
- **Date range of records**: spans **24 June 1947 (Kenneth Arnold) through January 1948**, with case numbering **not strictly chronological** — some early-1948 incidents (Mantell, 7 January 1948) appear in the 30s while much earlier events (28-30 June 1947) appear in the 80s-90s. Numbering reflects order of arrival at AMC, not date-of-event.
- **Page count**: 209 pages (PDF). Typical case = 1 cover Check-List page + 1–4 narrative / witness-statement pages, with sub-letters for multi-witness incidents (33a-g, 48a-d, 60a-b, 61a, etc.).
- **Classification markings observed**: "RESTRICTED" / "CONFIDENTIAL" stamps on most pages.
- **File size**: 187 MB (OCR'd, replaces the 247 MB original in place; PDF gitignored locally — exceeds GitHub's 100 MB per-file limit).
- **Raw path**: `raw/38_143685_box7_incident_summaries_1-100.pdf` (gitignored locally); text extract: `raw/ocr/38_143685_box7_incident_summaries_1-100.txt`.

## Coverage

Coverage: full (209 pages OCR'd via `ocrmypdf`; previously-sampled summary upgraded — original sample was pages 1-20 and 100-115). ## Key Points

This is the **operational evidence base of Project SIGN** — every case filed on the standardized 26-field Check-List documented in [[summaries/army-1948-general-vol-1]]. The file's most important content groupings:

1. **The 24 June 1947 Kenneth Arnold report and the 4–8 July 1947 wave** at Muroc AAF and Portland OR (Incidents 1–10).
2. **The 7 January 1948 Mantell incident in full**: Incidents 33 / 33a-g preserve the verbatim Godman Field tower radio-conversation reconstruction from multiple witnesses, with [[entities/mantell-incident|Capt. Thomas F. Mantell]]'s last transmissions captured.
3. **The first wave of overseas-theater pilot encounters**: Pacific (Pan Am Capt. Griffin / 2nd Officer Polhems Midway-Oahu, Inc 59), Alaska (Capt. Peck / Co-pilot Daly NW of Bethel, Inc 58), North Atlantic (SS *Burgeo* witnesses off Newfoundland, Inc 57).
4. **A police multi-witness mass-formation case at Twin Falls ID** (Inc 64) with multiple witnesses and three police officers (Det. Frazier, Rountree, Scott).
5. **Senior-officer scientific witnesses**: Lt Col F. L. Walker Jr., GSC (Scientific Branch, Research Group, R&DD-WDGS) at Silver Springs OH (Inc 66) — a Pentagon-level scientific witness ruling out comet hypothesis.
6. **Retroactively-filed June 1947 events** (cases 78-91) — the Bureau ingested civilian reports of pre-Arnold-publicity sightings as the wave matured.

### Mantell incident, 7 January 1948, Godman AFB / Fort Knox KY (Incidents 33–33g)

The file preserves the complete witness array for the Mantell case, with verbatim radio conversation. Witnesses and their entries:

- **Inc 33 — T/Sgt Quinton A. Blackwell, Chief Operator, Godman Control Tower** (USAF Detachment 733-5 AF Base Unit, 103D AACS Sq). At ~1520, "Sgt Cook from the CO's office notified the observer that according to Ft Knox Military Police & Ky Town State Police, a large circular object about 250 to 300 ft in diameter was over Mansville." Object first sighted by Blackwell at ~1300 over Madisonville, then over Irvington, then over south Cordtonette, then Owensboro.
- **Inc 33a — 1st Lt Paul I. Orner, Detachment Commander Godman Field**. Object "white, like parachute - round" with "some red light around the lower part", "appeared stationary", "half speed of P-51".
- **Inc 33b — PFC Stanley Oliver, Control Tower Godman**. Object "like ice-cream cone topped with red — white with red above", cone-shaped, southwest of Godman, over 15,000 ft.
- **Inc 33c — Capt. J. F. Duesler Jr., USAF, Godman Control Tower**. Object teardrop-then-fluid round, silver bright, no tail. Visible 1420 to 1600 CST (over an hour and a half). With Lt Col E. Garrison Wood. Wood's note: "while it appeared about 1/10 the size of a full moon, if the object were a great distance away, as compared to the diminishing size of the P-51's flying toward it, it would seem that it was at least several hundred feet in diameter."
- **Inc 33d — Capt. Cary W. Carter, Operations Officer Godman**. Object round / cone-shaped, very high, white, 360 mph per flight leader, 210° from Godman, visible through cirrus.
- **Inc 33e — Col. Guy F. Hix, Commanding Officer Godman**. Object white round, 25,000 ft estimated, 180 mph, 215° south to west, "remained stationary for approx 1-1/2 hours", visible 5 minutes from his sighting, "seemed at times to have a red border at the top and at the bottom", disappeared behind a cloud.
- **Inc 33f — Capt. Thomas F. Mantell, Flight Leader NG 869** (Kentucky Air National Guard, F-51 Mustang). The radio conversation is preserved verbatim across multiple witness reports:
  - 1115: "Object traveling at 180 MPH. Directly ahead of and above me now and moving at about 1/2 my speed."
  - "It appears metallic of tremendous size."
  - "Object going up and forward as fast as he was — approx 360 MPH."
  - "Going to 20,000 ft and if no closer will abandon chase."
  - Last message: "I'm trying to close in for better look."
  - Mantell crashed shortly thereafter. **File note: "Apparently, Mantell blacked out at 20,000 ft."**
- **Inc 33g — anonymous witness at Madisonville KY, ~1310** — observed the object **through a Finch telescope**.
- **NG 800** (one of the other P-51s) reported "it appeared like the reflection of sunlight on an airplane canopy" and resumed search to 33,000 ft, 100 miles south, but did not sight anything.
- **Inc 34 — Lt. C. W. Thomas (USAF Pilot, Flight AF 99 Dayton-to-Washington)** — at 3,000 ft, observed "large bright light off to the west", amber, "looked like a large star or planet", ~15 miles distant — same evening, suggesting Venus visible from cross-country flight. Used by AMC investigators as part of the Venus / comet hypothesis check.
- **File note**: "Later, an astronomer was contacted who attempted to account for this phenomena as either Venus or a comet. (?)" The Venus hypothesis arose the same night within AMC's analytical chain. The investigator's parenthetical "(?)" is preserved.

This is the **most comprehensive Mantell witness corpus** in any single source in the wiki, capturing tower personnel, pursuing-aircraft pilot, multiple field-grade USAF officers, and a Madisonville civilian-with-telescope witness.

### Major overseas-theater pilot encounters

- **Incident 57, 20 July 1947 0015Z, off Newfoundland** — SS *Burgeo* enroute Sydney NS → Port aux Basques. Witnesses Maitland, Larkin, Douglas, Hamilton (Hamilton, Metcalfe and Kansas City Bridge Co. employees working at Harmon Field). 4–5 silvery-to-red flashes "approximately 1 second apart and equidistant", traveling NNE faster than tracer bullets.
- **Incident 58, 4 August 1947 evening, NW of Bethel Alaska** — **Capt. Jack Peck and Co-pilot Vince Daly (Al Jones Flying Service)** in a DC aircraft sighted and FOLLOWED a flying saucer at sunset. Object as large or larger than a C-54, **black, "silhouetted against a brilliant evening sky"**, 500–1,000 ft altitude, ~520 mph (3× C-54 speed), saucer-shape "smooth-surfaced and streamlined and resembled a C-54 without motors (from the rear) and was without wings or any visible means of propulsion whatever." Pulled up to 1,200 ft to avoid possible collision; object crossed their path at right angles, then they swung in behind at 170 mph but "the thing was out of sight in four minutes." **Official-in-Charge of Peck's airline: "the pilot is not the imaginative type."** First substantive Alaska theater commercial-pilot pursuit in the SIGN corpus.
- **Incident 59, 12 September 1947, Pacific between Midway Island and Oahu (beyond Necker Island, 23°34'N 164°42'W)** — **Capt. Stanley S. Griffin and 2nd Officer Edward B. Polhems (Pan American Airways)** in eastbound flight from Midway, magnetic course 98 at 9,000 ft / 205 knots. At 0558 GCT, intense white light 60° off starboard bow, 5 miles distance, course 350° true. Light grew in intensity to ~1 mile off starboard, then **"abruptly veered to course 109° and split into two parts assuming reddish hue"**. Twin lights ~wing-length apart "burned out 30 miles ahead." Both observers ruled out meteor: "for the manner in which it held altitude. At no time did it appear to get above or below 9,500 ft." Estimated speed: **1,000 knots / ~1,160 mph**. **Pan Am pilot witness, Pacific theater, ~1 month before pre-Arnold ghost-rocket-style activity**.

### Twin Falls ID police multi-witness mass-formation event (Incident 64, 19 August 1947 21:30)

A particularly substantial multi-witness case the existing summary missed:

- Initial witnesses: **Mr. Hedstrom (Executive, Twin Falls Housing Authority, 322 3rd Ave North), Mrs. Hedstrom, neighbor Mrs. Shultz**.
- Sequence of formations observed:
  1. Initial single object NE at terrific speed.
  2. Triangle formation of 10 similar objects, NE direction; 3 broke off NE, the rest closed ranks NE.
  3. 5–6 objects in triangle formation, same direction.
  4. Large group of **35–50 objects** in triangular formation, same direction.
  5. Reverse course: groups of 3, 5, 7 returning SW at ~5-minute intervals.
- Police corroboration: Detective **Richard A. Frazier**, **M. E. Rountree**, **Richard Scott** (Twin Falls PD) — observed a group of about 12 objects in formation over the city in SW direction.
- Witness rule-outs: "the objects flew on a level line and did not appear to drop" (not meteors); "appeared lighted from the inside" (not birds).

### Senior scientific-officer witness — Inc 66

- **Incident 66, 10 August 1947, near Silver Springs OH, ~21:00** — **Lt Col F. L. Walker Jr., General Staff Corps, Scientific Branch, Research Group, R&DD WDGS** observed a low-flying bright orange glow with white tracer-streak trailing behind, 70° arc in 3-4 seconds, "absolutely horizontal", "simply vanished in mid-air." Walker "states he never observed a comet which acted in this manner and was of the opinion that he had sighted a flying saucer." **Pentagon-level scientific officer witness — substantively significant for the SIGN dossier given Walker's institutional standing.**

### Other substantive cases sampled

- **Incident 63, 29 July 1947 12:05, 25 mi NE of Helena, Montana** — **R. J. Madden** (Division Plant Engineer, Pacific Telephone & Telegraph Co.) and party. Polished-nickel circular object 3 ft × 3-4 inches thick, hovering, rising/descending vertically 50-100 ft, then "swooping NE at tremendous speed", **"melted into thin air"**.
- **Incident 67, 14 August 1947 16:00, 5 mi SW of Placerville CA** — **Mr. Ralph A. Switzer (Insurance Executive, McGuire & Wallis) + wife**. Polished-chromium rectangular object 5 ft × 12-14 inches at 500-1,000 ft altitude, ~750 ft from observer, in a "very shallow dive", **engulfed in a 10-ft-diameter puff of dark grey smoke** and disappeared completely; no falling particles observed.
- **Incidents 60 / 60a / 60b, early September 1947, Cordroy Newfoundland** — three witnesses including **Albert Samms (12-year-old)**, Legge, Evans. Object red-and-yellow circle "size of a large dinner plate" with yellowish tail, traveling much closer than a shooting star. "His mother, Mrs. Samms, is the Post Mistress of the town."
- **Incidents 61 / 61a, 8 September 1947 22:30, Logan UT** — Mr. & Mrs. Richard Anderson + Mr. & Mrs. Norman Hall. Group of approximately a dozen white illuminated objects "size of pigeons" but flying north faster than birds, 2,000-3,000 ft altitude.

### Pre-Arnold retroactive cases (Incidents 78-91)

The file ingested as the wave matured several reports of sightings that pre-dated the 24 June 1947 Arnold publicity. These appear in the case-number 78-91 range with dates in late June 1947 (Inc 78 — 30 June 1947, Inc 90 — 29 June 1947, Inc 91 — 28 June 1947). They are useful as evidence that observations were not solely a post-Arnold media artifact but were already happening (and largely unreported) in the days before.

## Open Questions

- The **Madisonville Finch-telescope witness (Inc 33g)** is unusual — telescopic observation of the Mantell object during the event is rarely cited in the canonical Mantell literature. Identifying this witness from the OCR'd text requires a closer pass at adjacent pages.
- The "Mantell **blacked out at 20,000 ft**" file note is preserved verbatim — but the file does not establish whether this was a contemporaneous AMC determination or a retrospective reconstruction. It pre-empts the Skyhook-balloon hypothesis (which became dominant later) by attributing the crash to oxygen deprivation rather than to a high-altitude craft pursuit.
- The **Pan Am Capt. Griffin / Polhems Pacific case (Inc 59)** is a substantial commercial-airline pilot pursuit at ~1,000 knots that does not appear in the better-known early-program-era pilot-encounter canon. Worth cross-referencing with Pan Am 1947 incident records and the wider Pacific-aircrew sighting tradition.
- The **Twin Falls ID multi-formation event (Inc 64)** with 35-50 objects in triangular formation, witnessed by police officers and an executive of the local Housing Authority, is one of the largest single-event formation reports in the corpus — and would extend the [[summaries/army-incident-summaries-101-172|Rapid City SD diamond formation]] (Aug 1948) and [[summaries/army-incident-summaries-173-233|off Grays Harbor WA]] (Oct 1948) precedent line back to August 1947.
- The pre-Arnold retroactive case-numbering (Incs 78-91 with late-June 1947 dates) confirms the case-numbers reflect order of arrival at AMC, not date-of-event. A focused page-by-page read could surface what civilian observers reported in the **24-30 June 1947 window before the Arnold publicity broke** — primary-source data on whether the wave existed independent of the media narrative.
- The **NND 917033 authority block** on the cover sheet — what is the parent NARA finding-aid number? External cross-walk with NARA RG-38 / RG-341 catalogs would close this loop.
