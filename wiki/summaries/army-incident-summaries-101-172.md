## Key Points

The volume captures the **mid-1948 maturation of the Project SIGN incident-summary practice** — the original 26-line CHECK-LIST is in continuous use across these cases (the 25-line redesign begins later, in vol. 173-233). The case load shows three distinct strands:

1. **CONUS sighting waves** preserved with multi-witness cross-correlation: the **Bakersfield CA March 1948 "burning aircraft" wave** (Incidents 106-109), the **Ashley / Delaware OH 8 April 1948 cylinder cluster** (Incidents 112-112c), and the **Selfridge Field MI 25-28 May 1948 disc formations** (Incidents 134-135).
2. **Scandinavian / European "ghost rocket" continuity** — the volume records December 1947 and February 1948 sightings from **Norway / Denmark / Sweden** with "green tails" "from the direction of Peenemünde" (Incidents 132, 133), suggesting the early-postwar [[concepts/ghost-rockets|ghost-rocket]] phenomenon was still being reported a year-plus after the famous 1946 Swedish wave.
3. **Pacific theater single-pilot encounters** — Incident 111 (1 April 1948, Philippines, 1st Lt Robert L. Meyers, 67 FS / 18 FG) is the volume's first overseas pilot pursuit.

The volume also documents **systematic FBI cross-referrals**: the Baltimore "foreign-aircraft-sound" anonymous-housewife report (Incident 110, March 1948) traveled FBI Baltimore → Director of Intelligence Second Army → AMC. These referrals foreshadow the post-Hottel relationship between the FBI and Air Force OSI on UAP correspondence.

### Notable case summaries

- **Incident 101 / 102 (Feb 1948, Norcatur KS / Green River UT–Limon CO area)** — covered in the prior summary; preserved.
- **Incident 103, 18 February 1948, near Green River UT** — Capt. Howard H. Berodt + Capt. Maurice T. Ritenour (718th Bomb Sq, 28th Bomb Gp VH, Weaver AFB, SD), in B-29s observed a **multi-colored ball of fire and dense cloud of smoke** at ~100 mi distance, ~20,000 ft altitude, "very high speed", southeast of Limon CO. Cross-references Incident 102.
- **Incident 105, 8 March 1948, Belmont NC** — Mr. A. C. Morrison (Superintendent, Meter Dept., Duke Power Co., Charlotte NC; engineer of 30 years standing, witness rated highly reliable) saw a **round metallic-appearing object** at ~600 mph easterly above scattered fleecy clouds, 10 seconds. Note: "He does not claim he saw the 'flying disc' — merely … an object which they could not identify with any natural phenomenon or known type of aircraft." A textbook conservative-engineer witness.
- **Incidents 106-109, 5-9 March 1948, Bakersfield CA** — Bakersfield burning-aircraft wave. **Mr. George L. (Les) Buchner**, **Mr. H. B. Nix**, **Mr. Denio** (Pacific General Electric Co. employee), **Mrs. Callie R. Mason + Dr. J. E. Johnson** (Haberfelde Building, 5th floor) — all reported a falling-aircraft-like object on fire with red-and-black smoke trailing, sometimes with a small dark object hanging from a parachute drifting eastward into the hills. Searches by the **Sheriff's Office (Sgt. A. M. Larsen)**, USAF aircraft, and rescue units found nothing. Investigation by **Lt Col Donald L. Springer** (Asst. Chief of Staff A-2, Hamilton Field, 4th Air Force). Forwarded to Air Defense Command Mitchel Field, FBI San Francisco, G-2 Sixth Army, DIO San Francisco / 12th Naval District. **A discrete multi-witness March 1948 wave that resembles fall-of-debris but was never resolved.**
- **Incident 110, 23 March 1948, Hamilton (Baltimore) MD** — anonymous housewife, "active in American Legion activities", FBI Baltimore call. Reports unusual aircraft-sound 3:00-5:00 a.m. on multiple nights for past 6-8 weeks: "the type of airplane motor drone which is generally heard from American planes" — instead "more like that of a foreign type airplane". Discussed with neighbor (also ex-G.I. household) who confirmed similar sound. **Significance**: a citizen "foreign-aircraft" anxiety report formally entering the SIGN file via FBI → Second Army → AMC. The cold-war framing (foreign-aircraft motor-drone) is distinct from the "flying disc" framing of most other cases in the volume.
- **Incident 111, 1 April 1948, Philippines** — **1st Lt Robert L. Meyers, 67 FS / 18 FG, USAF**, lead of a 4-ship flight, observed two silver objects ~30 ft × 20 ft "flying-wing type" at 200 mph, 3 mi distance. Object made 90° left turn, level acceleration, disappeared in 5 seconds. Meyers was the only witness — wing-mate maneuvers prevented their own observation, and his radio was out. Officer character-vetted as "reliable non-excitable individual ... quite positive in his statements."
- **Incidents 112 / 112a / 112b / 112c, 8 April 1948 1430, Ashley & Delaware OH** — **multi-witness cylinder sighting** with six independent observers across two towns ~10 mi apart:
  - Miss Winifred Paines (Asst. Bank Cashier) + Mrs. Bertha Slack (housekeeper), Ashley OH — "oblong, long and narrow, like silver streak"
  - Mrs. Selah Stephens (newspaper reporter), Ashley OH — **"opalescent, like mother of pearl"**, "long cylindrical body like a stick of firewood"
  - Mrs. James B. Stephenson (housewife), Delaware OH — "Vertical, larger at bottom and narrow at top", "cylindrical, with vapor around the top"
  - **Rev. James B. Stephenson (Pastor)**, Delaware OH — "white", "vertical cylinder larger at bottom narrow at top, with streamers or streaks of vapor attached"
  - Mrs. John Bergen, Delaware OH (further details OCR-degraded)
  Object slow-moving SW, no sound, no exhaust, observed for 5+ minutes. The Delaware airport was alerted but did not detect arrival. **Significance**: a multi-town 1948 daylight cylinder case with a pastor and a newspaper reporter among the witnesses, structurally consistent across observers — predates better-known cylinder cases of the era.
- **Incident 131, 20 June 1948, Belleville IL (Scott AFB)** — **Lt Col Wm T. Hull, Pilot**, AC of S Operations and Training. White-light "approx round" object below 6,000 ft, ~500 mph, **zigzag course** over Scott AFB at night. Senior officer pilot witness at a Strategic Air Command base.
- **Incident 132, 12 December 1947, Oslo Norway** — daughter of **Military Attaché Col. Kai E. Rasmussen, GSC**. Luminous white object with **greenish-yellow tail** performing an "arc of a circle, curvature very great", 2-3 seconds. Note: "Office employee reports unusual no. of falling stars night of 13 Dec. Univ Observatory has no record of incident."
- **Incident 133, 20-26 February 1948, Norway / Denmark / Sweden** — generalized pattern report. Objects appearing **usually around 2130 hours**, **tree-top to 20,000 ft altitude**, **1-2 miles per second**, **"come from the direction of Peenemünde"**, **green tails**. Investigator note: "The trend of these objects to appear at 2130 hours might be significant." This is a remarkable late-period [[concepts/ghost-rockets|ghost rocket]] series report — over a year and a half after the famous 1946 Swedish wave.
- **Incident 134, 25 May 1948 1500 EDT, 6 mi east of Monroe MI (en route Selfridge Field → MacDill)** — **1st Lt Kokolonis, Corps of Engineers, Selfridge Field**. **Five silvery-gold disc-shaped objects** (3 at first, 2 later), 300-400 ft size (compared to lake-freighter scale of ~500 ft), 8,000 ft altitude, 500+ mph, in **stepped-up line-astern formation**, "abrupt turns to right". Sketch preserved.
- **Incident 135, 28 May 1948 1500 EDT, 6 mi east of Monroe MI** — **M/Sgt Ernest Davis, Jr.** (form notes "(colored)" — racial annotation typical of period documents). Single round shiny-brass-colored object ~4 ft diameter at 8,000 ft, 100+ mph southwest. Cross-references Incident 134; "Discrepancy as to position of 2nd object in group as sighted by Lt Kokolonis." This is a **3-day-apart same-area pair**.
- **Incident "135" (mid-August 1948 entry, Rapid City AFB / Weaver SD)** — separate from Incident 135 above, internal renumbering apparent. **Maj Elmer H. Hamer (Intelligence Officer, 28th Bombardment Group)**. **Approximately 12 yellowish-white elliptical objects** in **diamond-shaped formation**, 100+ ft length, 500+ mph, 10,000 → 6,000 ft altitude, "tight formation", disappeared SW heading. **One of the largest formation reports in the SIGN catalog.**
- **Incident 136 / 136a, 30 June 1948 ~2140, Knoxville TN / Oak Ridge TN area** — Mrs. Whitehouse (wife of Maj. Harvey Whitehouse, CO ASU 3319 Tennessee ORC instructors) + **Mr. Tryus W. Setliff (Oakridge TN)**. Orange ball of fire with bluish trailing streamer, ~3 seconds visible, ~1,000 mph at 30,000-50,000 ft, westerly direction. **Significance**: an [[concepts/atomic-energy-site-overflight|atomic-energy site]] adjacency case (Oak Ridge witness) tied to an apparent meteor / fireball — but the stated speed is 4× typical meteor and the visible-streamer-emitting-sparks duration is much longer than typical meteors.
- **Incidents 137 / 137a, 7 July 1948 ~2114-2125, Chapel Hill NC** — University of North Carolina mathematics instructor and **Capt. H. W. Daniels, USAR Infantry Reserve, Box 188 Chapel Hill**. Three cigar-shaped objects, **"blunt front tapering to a point in the rear"**, "shaped like a cigar", ENE direction, jet-sound, ~1 minute observed. Daylight three-cigar formation in academic/military witness pool.
- **Incident 138, 8 July 1948 ~0920, Columbus OH** — **Mrs. Wilda Pittock** (2579 Avalon Ave., housewife). Observed through M3 6×30 binoculars: 3-1/2 inches apparent length, gray/silver, cigar/torpedo shape, ~3,000 ft altitude. Observation through binoculars makes this an unusually well-instrumented civilian sighting for the period.

### Investigative pattern notes

- **Witness vetting**: continued throughout. Engineer/professional witnesses (Mr. Morrison, Duke Power); reverends (Rev. Stephenson, Delaware OH); senior military officers (Lt Col Hull at Scott; Maj Hamer at Rapid City); reserve officers (Capt. Daniels, Chapel Hill). Each character-evaluated by the field investigator.
- **Multi-witness cross-correlation**: the Bakersfield wave (106-109), the Ashley/Delaware cylinder cluster (112-112c), and the Selfridge field paired sightings (134-135) all show the SIGN practice of recording multiple independent observers to the same event and noting agreement / discrepancy.
- **Foreign-territory cases**: the Oslo / Scandinavia cases (132-133) and the Philippines case (111) precede the volume 173-233 non-CONUS expansion. The Scandinavian "Peenemünde-direction" framing is striking in 1948 — Peenemünde was in the Soviet zone of Germany at this date.
- **First SIGN reference to airborne radar contact** in this volume: not yet present; the radar contacts begin in vol. 173-233 (e.g., Incident 183 F-61, Incident 198 Wakkanai). This volume is dominated by visual observations.

## Open Questions

- **The Bakersfield March 1948 wave (Incidents 106-109)** with multi-source "burning aircraft / parachute" descriptions but no debris or wreckage found is structurally similar to a high-altitude balloon failure or a lost weather/test flight — but Lt Col Springer's investigation reportedly found no matching test or weather flight. Was this resolved later within Project SIGN's records?
- **The Scandinavian Peenemünde-direction green-tailed objects (Incident 133)** continuing into early 1948 are anomalous given that by then the V-weapons era was fully ended and Peenemünde was in Soviet hands. Were these truly Soviet test launches, residual German weapon systems, meteoric activity, or a misattributed origin? Connecting this case file with the [[summaries/german-armament-equipment-1944-1945|SHAEF foo-fighter dossier]] would be valuable.
- **The 6-witness Ashley/Delaware OH cylinder case (Incident 112)** has unusually consistent multi-observer descriptions including a pastor and a journalist — but does not appear in the standard Project SIGN narrative summaries (Mantell, Chiles-Whitted, Gorman, etc.). Was it considered for inclusion in the *Estimate of the Situation* package and downgraded?
- **The Rapid City "12 elliptical objects in tight diamond formation"** is one of the corpus's largest formation counts — only [[summaries/army-incident-summaries-173-233|Incident 199]] (10-20 translucent egg-shaped objects off Grays Harbor WA, Oct 1948) is comparable. A focused synthesis on early-1948 large-formation reports would be productive.
- Cases not enumerated above (113-130 except 110-112c, 139-172) likely include additional substantial events; the volume's mid-section was not exhaustively read. A second-pass index would surface them.
