## Key Points

- **The form has been redesigned vs. the 1–100 volume**. The new "INCIDENT SUMMARY" template is a 25-line form (re-titled, fields re-ordered): Date and Time of Observation / Where Sighted / Observer's Position / Name and Address of Observer / Occupation and/or Hobbies / Attention Attracted by / Number of Object(s) / Size of Object(s) / Color / Nature of Luminosity / Shape / Estimated Distance from Observer / Estimated Speed / Time in Sight / Tactics / Sound / Direction of Flight / Apparent Construction / Effect on Clouds / Exhaust Trail Color / Manner of Disappearance / Weather Conditions / Peculiarities Noted / Summary of Incident. This redesign reflects evolved investigator priorities (luminosity, witness occupation/hobbies, peculiarities — fields not on the original 1947 checklist) and likely corresponds to the operational transition into Project GRUDGE (Feb 1949).
- Investigators continue the practice of **comparing multiple witnesses to the same event and noting discrepancies in their reports** (Incident 175: Angier vs. Fairchild contradict each other on shape, motion, and duration; Incident 176: Col. Bakins vs. Mr. Bentham give "direct contradiction" descriptions of the same Castro Ranch object).
- Witness-vetting language continues: "Mr. Williams seemed a reliable level-headed person. He appeared conscientious and desired no publicity"; "Mr. Angier ... is an experienced pilot, comes from a middle-class family, is a little above average in intelligence and is a good conversationalist"; "Subsequent investigation of this incident reveals that according to neighbors Mrs. McDaniel possesses a vivid imagination and that the daughter was inclined to take after the mother."
- The case file shows continued reliance on **pilot witnesses** (Mr. B.B. Williams, "considerable flying experience"; A. Ruble Angier, "pilot since 1928"; Maj. Robert C. Drum, USAF rated pilot) — and explicit vetting of their visual and color perception.

## Notable Incidents / Cases

- **Incident 174 — 23 (?) September 1948, 0540, Ascension Parish, Louisiana (Township 10 South, Range 4 East), ~50 mi from New Orleans**. Witness: B.B. Williams (President, United Land Co., 219 Dryades St., New Orleans, RA-0515; student pilot). Single object resembling an "ice cream cone" or funnel with the larger end forward, white-hot at the front fading to dark red toward the rear, ~size of a passenger airliner, ~300 mph, 1500–2000 ft alt, ~3/4 mi away, soundless, no trail, no metal/structure visible — "encased in flame." Observer note: "I think it was a man-made object, as evidenced distinctly by the outline and functional appearance."
- **Incident 175 — 23 September 1948, 0945, Santa Fe / Los Alamos, New Mexico**. Witness: A. Ruble Angier (Sign Manufacturing Co. employee, 2620 P—— St., Texas; pilot since 1928). Perfectly white circular flat object ~100–150 ft diameter at estimated 30 000 ft altitude, stationary or near-stationary for 15–20 minutes while observed near Los Alamos radio tower; companion Mr. John C. Fairchild (Building Superintendent, mechanical/woodworking hobbies) saw a different object — a bright silvery oblong "egg-shaped" body at 700–800 mph with a sunflower-shape exhaust pattern. Investigator marks the discrepancy. **Significant location: Los Alamos** (atomic-weapons facility).
- **Incident 176 — 23 September 1948, ~1200, Castro's Ranch, 4 mi due east of San Pablo, CA / 4.1 mi south of Pinole, CA**. Witnesses: Col. Horace S. Eakins, U.S. Army (Ret.), 1024 Esther Drive, Walnut Creek, CA (retired veterinarian / California State Dept. of Agriculture meat inspector) AND Mr. Sylvester Bentham, 2655 Dam Road (retired butcher, 70 yrs old). Eakins describes an **amoeba-shaped translucent gray-canvas-textured object the size of a 4-engine bomber** flying east above an Army bomber, with a dark spherical center and "3 appendages forward and 2 aft" undulating like flaps. Bentham, looking at the same object, sees a **rectangular buff/gray "vegetable-crate-shaped" object** with vertical ribs at faster-than-jet speed, NW heading. Investigator: "Mr. Bentham's description as to the shape, direction and overall appearance of this object is in direct contradiction with statements made by Col Horace Eakins."
- **Incident 177 — 5 September 1948, 15:00–15:30, Kentwood, Louisiana**. Witness: Mrs. McDaniel and daughter. Object not seen — only a sound at "very high altitude" at "terrific" speed. Investigator note: neighbors say Mrs. McDaniel possesses a "vivid imagination," daughter "inclined to take after the mother," neighbors heard nothing. Case effectively dismissed.
- **Incident 178 — 15 October 1948 (form says "18 October 1948" in title, narrative says 15 October), 1705–1715 EST, Honolulu, T.H., 158° / 21°5′N**. Witness: Major Robert C. Drum, USAF (rated pilot, 2737 Pacific Heights Rd., Honolulu) plus wife and ~14-year-old daughter. Bright silvery 10–15 ft circular/elliptical object at 10 000–14 000 ft moving NE between two cloud layers, observed intermittently for ~10 minutes (longest single observation: 10 seconds). One B-26, one B-17, and one PBM were flying in the vicinity at the time. Drum compared the object to "a silver dollar slightly turned from a flat position." All three family members independently described the same shape/motion. **First sampled non-CONUS case** (Hawaii).

## Open Questions

- The form-redesign between Incident 100 and Incident 174 marks an institutional inflection — when exactly did Project SIGN drop the original 26-field Check-List in favor of the 25-field Incident Summary template? Cases 101 (Feb 1948) still use the old form per [[summaries/army-incident-summaries-101-172]]. The new form likely came in mid-to-late 1948.
- The **Los Alamos overflight (Incident 175)** is one of a documented cluster of UFO sightings over atomic-energy facilities in late 1948 (Green Fireballs, etc.). Worth cross-referencing once a Green Fireballs entity exists.
- Was Major Robert C. Drum's wife / 14-year-old daughter ever interviewed independently? The narrative says "Both repeated precisely the same observations" but does not preserve their statements.
- Cases 179–233 not sampled. The volume's range likely runs into 1949 — potential overlap with the Project GRUDGE transition (December 1948 / February 1949).
- "B SL S 02576" authority block on cover — what is this NARA finding-aid number?
