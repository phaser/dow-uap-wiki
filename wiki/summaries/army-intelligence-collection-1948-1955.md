## Key Points

- The file contains routing/cover sheets (AFOIN-4 record-of-correspondence forms) and a small set of USAFE TT-1524 intelligence items addressed to **General Charles P. Cabell** (Director of Intelligence, USAF) at Pentagon room 4B-854.
- Three substantive USAFE TT-1524 items, all dated **4 November 1948**:
  1. **TT-1524 Item 2** (USAFE 2, "To Gen Cabell") — Forwarding "one complete set of all reports prepared by special intelligence organization of the European Command from its conception to present date." Notes that the reports will "lend weight to arguments for allocating Air Force funds to this organization." Cautions that "most of them were forwarded as received," i.e., raw uncorroborated.
  2. **TT-1524 Item 10** (USAFE 10, "From OI OB"): an unidentified-aircraft sighting report.
     - **5 September 1948, 1402Z** (Operation Daggar context).
     - Three crews of the **307th Bomb Group** sighted an unidentified aircraft off the **west coast of Holland (51°55'N / 03°55'E)** at **30,000 ft**.
     - Object first cruising at "normal jet speed, heading 120 degrees"; soon began leaving smoke trails and condensation trails accompanied by sudden acceleration and then climb.
     - Generally agreed to be "a single jet propelled A/C employing probably rocket assists with tremendous reserve power, more than normal cruising speed for jets of the 1947 variety. Never within identification range; its course did not indicate its purpose."
     - Evaluation: "B-2" (intelligence reliability rating: B = "usually reliable" source, 2 = "probably true").
  3. **TT-1524 Item 14** (USAFE 14, "From OI OB") — the major item:
     - "For some time we have been concerned by the recurring reports on flying saucers. They periodically continue to crop up; during the last week, one was observed hovering over **Neubiberg Air Base for about thirty minutes**."
     - "They have been reported by so many sources and from such a variety of places that we are convinced that they cannot be disregarded and must be explained on some basis which is perhaps slightly beyond the scope of our present [intelligence]."
     - **Swedish Air Intelligence Service consultation**: Officers of the Directorate visited the Swedes; Swedish answer was that "some reliable and fully technically qualified people have reached the conclusion that 'these phenomena are obviously the result of a high technical skill which cannot be credited to any presently known culture on earth.' They are therefore assuming that these objects originate from some previously unknown or unidentified technology, possibly outside the earth."
     - **Lake-crash report**: A Swedish technical expert observed an object "near his home on the edge of a lake. The object crashed or landed in the lake and he carefully noted its azimuth from his point of observation. Swedish intelligence was sufficiently confident in his observation that a naval salvage team was sent to the lake. Operations were underway during the visit of USAF officers. Divers had discovered a previously uncharted crater on the floor of the lake. No further information is available, but we have been promised knowledge of the results. In their opinion, the observation was reliable, and they believe that the depression on the floor of the lake, which did not appear on current Hydrographic charts, was in fact caused by a flying saucer."
     - Closing: "Although accepting this theory of the origin of these objects poses a whole new group of questions and puts much of our thinking in a changed light, we are inclined not to discredit entirely this somewhat spectacular theory, meantime keeping an open mind on the subject. What are your reactions?"
- The cover sheet at the front of the file shows AFOIN coordination routes (OIP-PP, OIP-FL, OIR-CO, OIR-DD, OIR-AA, OIR-CM, OIR-RC, OAI-AF, OAI-SV, OCI-RR, OCI-CI, etc.) — i.e., the document was widely circulated within USAF Intelligence.

## Notable Incidents / Cases

- **Operation Daggar Unknown Aircraft, 5 September 1948** — 51°55'N / 03°55'E (off west Holland), 30,000 ft, 1402Z; observed by three crews of the 307th Bomb Group; a single jet aircraft showing performance "more than normal cruising speed for jets of the 1947 variety." Intelligence rating B-2.
- **Neubiberg Air Base "Hovering Saucer," ca. late October 1948** — flying saucer reported hovering over the U.S. air base at Neubiberg, Germany, "for about thirty minutes."
- **Swedish "Lake Crash" Report, 1948** — Swedish technical expert observes an object crash into a lake; Swedish naval salvage divers find a previously uncharted crater on the lake floor matching the reported impact location. Outcome of further investigation not in this file.
