## Source Metadata

- **Originating agency**: USAF Flight Service / Flight Service Centers (Lowry FSC, McChord FSC, Olmsted FSC, Maxwell FSC, etc.) reporting under **Flight Service Regulation 200-4 (2 November 1948), Subject: "Unidentified Flying Objects"** — the regulation that mandated all USAF Flight Service Centers report UFO sightings directly to AMC at Wright-Patterson AFB. Reports forwarded to: CG Air Materiel Command, Wright-Patterson AFB, Attn: MCIAXO-3 (or MCIAS); cc to Chief of Staff USAF (Director of Intelligence), Commander MATS, Chief of Flight Service.

## Key Points

- This is **the operational regulation-200-4 file** — the standardized USAF sighting-report channel post-Mantell that funneled all CONUS UFO reports through regional Flight Service Centers (Lowry, McChord, Olmsted, Maxwell, etc.) to AMC at Wright-Patterson. The reports are by-the-book and follow a much more rigorous template than the earlier Project SIGN incident summaries (e.g., explicit fields for "Was any radio antenna to be observed," "Manner of disappearance: Explode / Faded from view / Disappeared behind obstacle," and Air Force / Navy / AAF / weather-unit / research-organization activity at the time).
- This is **chronologically the Project GRUDGE era** (GRUDGE replaced SIGN in February 1949). The reporting infrastructure is mature: dedicated regulation, standardized form, multiple channels.
- The reports are **dominated by post-Mantell flightline witnesses** — controllers, tower operators, ARTC controllers, AACS air/ground operators — rather than the largely civilian/police witnesses of 1947. This represents a professionalization of UFO observation.
- Standard close-out for each report: weather data at the time, winds-aloft data, local flight schedules of commercial / private / military aircraft, and Ordnance / Navy / Air Force / Army / Weather / Research / AAA activity in the area, plus a "no opinion / unknown" reliability rating on the observer.

## Notable Incidents / Cases

- **Spokane, Washington — 26 July 1949, 2020 PST**. Witness: Bill Miller (civilian commercial pilot, 411 Carahan, Spokane). Eight (8) **dish-shaped objects, each "larger than a B-29,"** in formation east-to-west, ~14 000 ft altitude, "much faster than a B-29," sound similar to a formation of jet aircraft, accompanying a B-29 (AF-7742) that was at 13 500 ft. The B-29 pilot reported nothing unusual. Reported via Spokane Radio → Seattle ARTC → McChord FSC.
- **Medford, Oregon — 8 August 1949, 1120P–1250P**. Multiple AACS Air/Ground operators of Medford Airways (Cpl. E.H. Conger, S/Sgt. Arthur Paulson, Cpl. Frank Bishop) and CAA Tower operators (G.E. Milligan, Jack Edmonds, P.O. Box 632, Medford). East and southeast of Medford. (Detail of object truncated by sample boundary.)
- **Greenville, South Carolina — 15 August 1949, 1930E**. Witness: Mr. Harper (employee, Greenville Municipal Airport, Lansing Avenue) and one unidentified witness. Round, fairly large, gray object moving NE at undetermined constant speed/altitude, no sound, no trail. **Operations section of the 316th Troop Carrier Wing investigated and determined the object was a Geological Weather Service balloon released in Tennessee.** This is a documented "explained" case in the 1949 file.
- **Seattle, Washington (over the city) — 22 August 1949, 1845P**. Three USAF NCO witnesses: Sgt. Jack Faulkner, S/Sgt T.D. Mullen, Sgt. Roger H. Studeman — all controllers of the **143rd National Guard Aircraft Control and Warning Squadron** (6736 Ellis Avenue, Seattle 8, WA). Single circular object ~75–100 ft diameter, 10–15 ft thick, shiny aluminum, north-to-south, ~500–600 mph, ~10 000 ft, jet-like sound, no exhaust trail. Reported through Boeing Field CAA tower → McChord FSC.
- **Northwest of Seattle-Tacoma Airport (~30 mi WNW) — 22 August 1949, 1920P**. ARTC Controllers Ben Frieman and Howard Watson at Seattle ARTCC. F-84-shaped, F-84-sized, F-84-colored object — "displayed visual characteristics of a jet fighter" — south-to-north, 4 000–5 000 ft, no sound, no exhaust trail. McChord verified no jet flight plans were active in the area; all base inquiries returned negative.
- **In-flight, AF 7110, Bedford MA → Griffiss AFB Rome NY — ~22 September 1949** (Olmsted FSC report). Pilot: Lt. Col. Phillip J. Kuhl, Air Materiel Command, AF Procurement Field Office, Bedford, MA, accompanied by Major Earl Goodrich (co-pilot). Cylindrical silvery/aluminum object ~30 mi off the right rear at 4 o'clock, descending from 20 000 ft into overcast at 7 000 ft, with **flame more than twice the length of the object**, appearing rocket- or jet-propelled, at high speed. Coordinates of sighted object ~43°40′N 74°55′W (Adirondacks region of upstate New York). Winds aloft: 280° / 40 kts.
- **Kansas City, Kansas / Olathe, Kansas — 6 January 1950, night**. Witnesses: James F. Grey (6200 Hadley St., Raytown, MO; pilot, employed Bendix Aviation Kansas City) and Robert Van De Vyvere (5532 Raytown Rd., MO; Bendix Aviation). Two spherical brilliant-white objects emanating orange/red flashes, sized like old-fashioned street lights at two-block range, moved from Kansas City to Olathe, hovered motionless 10–15 minutes over Olathe, then departed SW at high speed at 7 000–8 000 ft altitude, no sound, no exhaust. Reported via Fairfax AFB Operations → Lowry FSC. Signed Lt. Col. Robert M. (surname OCR-corrupt).
