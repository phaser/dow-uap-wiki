## Key Points

### U-2/UFO Connection — Specific Findings from the OCR Text

- **Section "U-2s, UFOs, and Operation BLUE BOOK" (pp. 72–73)** — This is the definitive CIA internal account of how the U-2 program generated UFO reports. The mechanism was specific and deliberate:

  - **Altitude differential**: In the mid-1950s, commercial airliners flew at 10,000–20,000 ft and military aircraft (B-47, B-57) below 40,000 ft. U-2s operated above 60,000 ft. "At this time, no one believed manned flight was possible above 60,000 feet, so no one expected to see an object so high in the sky."

  - **Twilight-reflection mechanism**: "Such reports were most prevalent in the early evening hours from pilots of airliners flying from east to west. When the sun dropped below the horizon of an airliner flying at 20,000 feet, the plane was in darkness. But, if a U-2 was airborne in the vicinity of the airliner at the same time, its horizon from an altitude of 60,000 feet was considerably more distant, and, being so high in the sky, its silver wings would catch and reflect the rays of the sun and appear to the airliner pilot, 40,000 feet below, to be fiery objects."

  - **Daylight glints**: "Even during daylight hours, the silver bodies of the high-flying U-2s could catch the sun and cause reflections or glints that could be seen at lower altitudes and even on the ground."

  - **Reporting channels**: Airline pilots reported sightings to air-traffic controllers; both pilots and ground observers wrote letters to Wright Air Development Command in Dayton. This led directly to the establishment of "the Air Force's Operation BLUE BOOK. Based at Wright-Patterson."

### The Quantitative Claim — Specific Number and Quote

- **The document states (p. 73)**: *"U-2 and later OXCART flights accounted for more than one-half of all UFO reports during the late 1950s and most of the 1960s!"* This is footnoted as "Information supplied by James Cunningham to Donald E. Welzenbach" (one of the co-authors interviewing a project staff insider).

  This resolves the previous Open Question about whether the "half" claim was a CIA public-affairs gloss — it is documented directly in the classified internal history, sourced to a named project insider.

### BLUE BOOK — CIA Liaison Process

- **Exact mechanism confirmed**: *"BLUE BOOK investigators regularly called on the Agency's Project Staff in Washington to check reported UFO sightings against U-2 flight logs. This enabled the investigators to eliminate the majority of the UFO reports, although they could not reveal to the letter writers the true cause of the UFO sightings."*

  This establishes that Blue Book personnel did know (at an institutional level) that U-2 flights were the source of many reports — but operated under a security constraint that prevented them from disclosing that explanation to the public or to witnesses.

### The U-2 Cover Story — "Weather Research"

- The U-2's cover story, approved by President Eisenhower in 1956, was that the aircraft conducted **high-altitude weather research** under the **National Advisory Committee on Aeronautics (NACA)**, later **NASA** (after 1958). NACA issued a press release on 7 May 1956 to this effect.
- U-2 detachments were publicly designated **1st Weather Reconnaissance Squadron, Provisional (WRSP-1)**. Detachment A deployed to the UK on 29 April 1956.
- James Killian and Edwin Land proposed a more forthright cover ("guard against surprise attack") but Richard Bissell's weather-research cover prevailed. This cover story remained in place through the 1 May 1960 shootdown — and its fragility was fatally exposed when Khrushchev revealed that Francis Gary Powers was alive, completely demolishing the U.S. position.
- Operation MUDLARK was the ad-hoc project established on 1 May 1960 to analyze the shootdown and coordinate cover-story responses.

### Project GENETRIX — Balloon Predecessor and Political Foreshadowing

- Before the U-2 became operational, the Air Force conducted **Project GENETRIX** (late 1955 – February 1956): 516 camera-carrying balloons launched over the Soviet Bloc from Western European bases. Only 34 yielded useful photography. The balloons used a **"weather research"** cover story too (International Geophysical Year).
- Diplomatic protests from Eastern Europe and the Soviet Union were severe. President Eisenhower ordered the project halted on 7 February 1956, concluding that GENETRIX "gave more legitimate grounds for irritation than could be matched by the good obtained from them."
- GENETRIX nearly killed the U-2 program before it started — CIA officials worried the president might sour on all overflights. DDCI Cabell wrote to Air Force Chief of Staff Twining warning against further balloon operations.
- An unexpected benefit: the 91 cm steel rigging bar on GENETRIX balloons coincidentally matched the wavelength of the Soviet TOKEN S-band radar, resonating when struck by radar pulses and enabling NATO to map previously unknown radar installations.

### Specific U-2 Overflight Dates and Locations

From Appendix D and Chapter 3 (U-2 overflights of the Soviet Union, 4 July 1956 – 1 May 1960):

- **4 July 1956 (Mission 2013)**: First U-2 overflight of USSR. Pilot: Hervey Stockman. Route: Wiesbaden → East Germany, Poland, Minsk → Leningrad → Baltic coast → return. Soviet radar tracked the flight but could not intercept.
- **5 July 1956 (Mission 2014)**: Second overflight. Pilot: Carmine Vito. Route: Moscow region. Soviet MiG fighters scrambled but could not reach 72,000 ft.
- **5 August 1957**: First photograph of Tyuratam (Baykonyr) missile test range from a U-2.
- **28 August 1957**: Photographed Tyuratam again; also photographed Saryshagan ABM test site.
- **1 May 1960 (Mission 4154)**: Francis Gary Powers shot down by SA-2 missile near Sverdlovsk at 70,500 ft. Target: Tyuratam, Kyshtym, Sverdlovsk. The shootdown ended all U-2 overflights of the Soviet Union.

### Groom Lake / Area 51 Origin Details

- Discovered 12 April 1955 by Bissell, Ritland, Kelly Johnson, and Tony LeVier flying in a Beechcraft. The old airstrip was compacted earth that had turned to ankle-deep dust; LeVier landed on the lakebed instead, narrowly avoiding a crash that would have killed the U-2 program's entire leadership.
- Kelly Johnson called it "Paradise Ranch" (shortened to "The Ranch") to make it sound attractive to workers. The base was also known as "Watertown Strip."
- AEC Chairman Admiral Lewis Strauss added the area to the Nevada Test Site's real estate holdings at Bissell's request, with presidential approval. The designation "Area 51" comes from its map grid number.
- The base became operational by July 1955. A regularly scheduled MATS C-54 shuttle (dubbed "Bissell's Narrow-Gauge Airline") flew personnel from Burbank. This shuttle crashed on 17 November 1955, killing 14 including CIA Security Officer William H. Marr — the single greatest loss of life in the entire U-2 program.

### Other Notable Details

- **Pilot selection**: Eisenhower originally demanded non-U.S. citizen pilots. The CIA recruited [REDACTED nationality] fliers who proved unable to handle the U-2. The program shifted to "sheep-dipped" Air Force SAC reserve pilots — they resigned commission to become civilian CIA employees, with a guarantee of reinstatement.
- **Suicide pills**: Six liquid potassium cyanide ampules ("L-pills") were made available to U-2 pilots before missions. Some took them; most did not.
- **P-31 engine shortage**: First U-2s used the inferior P-37 engine, causing chronic flameouts in the 57,000–65,000 ft "badlands" altitude band. The preferred P-31 (13,000 lbs thrust) wasn't available in quantity until spring 1956.
- **British involvement**: Four RAF pilots flew U-2 missions from Detachment B (Adana, Turkey). After the 1960 shootdown, they were secretly evacuated. RAF pilots continued at Detachment G (Edwards AFB) until 1974 but never again flew operational overflights.
- **President Eisenhower's position**: "I want this whole thing to be a civilian operation. If uniformed personnel of the armed services of the United States fly over Russia, it is an act of war — legally — and I don't want any part of it."

## Notable Incidents / Cases

The document is a program history, not an incident report, but it provides the definitive explanation for a vast category of 1950s–1960s UFO reports:

- **Twilight "fiery object" UFO reports from airline pilots** — caused by U-2 silver wings reflecting sunlight against a dark sky 40,000 ft below the U-2's altitude.
- **Daylight glinting-object reports from ground observers** — caused by U-2 fuselage/wings catching the sun.
- **The 1960 "weather plane" cover story collapse** — after Powers was shot down, NASA released a statement on 3 May 1960 claiming a "high-altitude weather plane" was missing over Turkey and "the pilot reported over the emergency frequency that he was experiencing oxygen difficulties." Khrushchev released the truth in stages, with the final revelation on 7 May that Powers was alive and had confessed. This "completely demolished the US cover story."
