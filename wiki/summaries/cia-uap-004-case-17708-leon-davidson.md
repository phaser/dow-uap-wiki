## Key Points

- **Memo from Chief, Chicago Office to Chief, Contact Division / Support Branch**, dated January 9, 1958, regarding closing of Case 17708.
- **Subject: Dr. Leon Davidson** — a researcher who had been in contact with the CIA regarding a "spice message" (likely "space message" — OCR degraded) and its transmitter.
- **CIA response**: Told Davidson that records on the transmitter had been destroyed by the originating agency. Davidson accepted this but noted he had been told the same before.
- **Davidson's activities**: He had a second article prepared for publication concerning "Air Force handling of space sightings" (likely UFO sightings) and the Pentagon security review process. He believed the article was "mild enough to pass" the Pentagon security review.
- **Davidson's persistence**: Davidson indicated he might "take a crack at a later date" at the matter of the "particular message" — suggesting he was not fully satisfied.
- **Agency coordination problems**: The memo notes "there have been many cooks in the kitchen on this dish" — multiple agencies involved. The "noncommittal and evasive answer" given to Davidson was "the only one possible to avoid crossing up previous statements of our own, and other involved agencies, to this man."
- **Fairness concern**: The memo writer acknowledged the answer was "hardly fair to Davidson" and "not likely to be fully accepted by him."
- **Background telephone conversation**: Revealed that Davidson may not have known he was dealing with CIA — contacts with "Walker" and "Smikich" (names uncertain due to OCR). Efforts had been made to conceal CIA identity from him.
