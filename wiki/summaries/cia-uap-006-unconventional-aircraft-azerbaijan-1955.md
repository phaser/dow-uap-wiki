## Key Points

- **Source**: US national, 41 years old, vice president of publicity and advertising for a large US corporation. Phi Beta Kappa, political science background, no technical training or aviation experience. Visiting USSR as a tourist at the invitation of a senior Soviet official.
- **Date/Time**: October 4, 1955, approximately 19:10 local time (2 hours 40 minutes after 16:30 departure from Baku)
- **Location**: Between Baku and Tiflis (Tbilisi), Azerbaijan SSR, between 50-65 miles south of Baku. Railroad line near the Caspian Sea coast. One source estimated airfield distance at ~5 miles, then revised to ~2 miles.
- **Sighting**: Three US nationals on a train observed a triangular object on the ground at a large airfield, illuminated by a huge searchlight. Object size comparable to a U.S. jet fighter, "squat shape," equilateral triangle form with three lights — one on each point (presumably two wing lights and a tail light).
- **Launch sequence**: Object was "ejected from its launching site" — not a conventional take-off but "more like a missile ejection." Made "not less than three and not more than seven fast spirals in the air," then climbed "extremely fast at about a 45 degree angle." Searchlight followed it all the way to high altitude.
- **Second launch**: Companion in adjacent compartment reported witnessing a second launching in rapid succession just before the others arrived at the window.
- **MVD intervention**: A steward entered and pulled down the blinds. When the witness protested, the steward pointed toward the rear of the car and shook his head — indicating the MVD (Soviet security) agent who had boarded at departure had ordered the blinds drawn.
- **Travel anomaly**: The witness party had wished to fly between Baku and Tiflis but were told by Intourist in Baku there were no flights — Intourist in Tiflis later expressed surprise they hadn't flown, stating several flights operated daily.
- **Second page**: Stamped as page 2 of report; appears to be distribution/routing information and continuation of limited dissemination notice.
