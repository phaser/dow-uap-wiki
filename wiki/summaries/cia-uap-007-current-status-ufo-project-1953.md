## Key Points

- **P&E Division assumed responsibility for the OSI UFO project** effective May 27, 1953, per a previous memorandum. The project's scope was confined to "maintaining awareness" of other agencies' UAP activities and maintenance of files.
- **USAF Project Blue Book status (December 1953)**: Air Force continues interest but with "apparently decreasing emphasis." Directorate of Intelligence, Hq. USAF interest is "confined to a cursory cognizance" of ATIC's project (Bluebook No. 10073).
- **ATIC staff reduction**: The project carried by one officer (Captain Charles A. Hardin), one airman (A/1C Max G. Futch), and a secretary — operating as the "Aerial Phenomena Section" of the Electronics Branch, Technical Analysis Division.
- **No more field investigations**: ATIC personnel "no longer conduct field investigations of UFOB sightings." These are requested from USAF intelligence officers (primarily Air Defense Command and Airways and Air Communications Service) nearer to the sightings. ATIC confines activities to receiving/checking reports, requesting additional field investigation, checking against meteorological/astronomical/aircraft/balloon data, and recording findings.
- **Unsolved rate**: For about the past year, "approximately ten percent of the reported sightings have been tagged as unsolved."
- **Transfer to Air Defense Command**: ATIC is "in the process of transferring project Bluebook to Hq., Air Defense Command." According to Lt. Col. Harry Johnston, Chief of Electronics Branch, the reason was that ADC had been doing most of the field investigation work.
