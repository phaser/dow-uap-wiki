## Key Points

- **Source**: Naturalized US citizen of [REDACTED] extraction. Corresponds regularly with two nieces living in Hungary.
- **Information source**: Letter received from niece in "West [REDACTED]" (likely Budapest or western Hungary) in November 1955.
- **Content of niece's letter (translated)**: "The so-called flying saucers (rockets) [sic] for several weeks kept the people in a nervous state. These very fast speeding heavenly bodies — scientific groups very busy. I am sure you heard already from the press. 12,000 km per hour was estimated on these flyers."
- **Additional material**: Included with the letter was a sketch indicating the formation and suspected course of the objects — depicted as traveling from the direction of Budapest toward Moscow.
- **First mention**: The source notes this was the first time his niece mentioned such objects in her letters.
- **Physical evidence**: A photostatic copy of the original letter (in Hungarian) and its English translation was available on loan from the CIA Library. The excerpt quoted in paragraph 1 was marked in brackets in the original letter.
