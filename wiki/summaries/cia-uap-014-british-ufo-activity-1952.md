## Key Points

- **Author**: H. Marshall Chadwell, Assistant Director, Scientific Intelligence, CIA.
- **Source**: A recently arrived messenger from Britain (unnamed), who spoke with Dr. R.V. Jones, head of British scientific intelligence.
- **British standing committee on flying saucers**: Created about 16 months prior (~August 1951). Presumably now under Dr. Jones "through inheritance." RAF are "action people."
- **British conclusion**: The group has concluded that "the observations are not enemy aircraft and that none have been over Britain."
- **Yorkshire incident**: About 10-12 days before the memo (~December 6-8, 1952), during an RAF air show demonstration at a Yorkshire field, a **"perfect flying saucer"** was seen by high RAF officials and RAF pilots. Multiple people saw it, resulting in press articles. This was "disturbing to Jones" because "the creation of the correction of public opinion is a part of his responsibilities."
- **Swedish incident reference**: Jones reminded the contact about "the Swedish incident (presumably he refers to the one in 1946)" — referencing the [[concepts/ghost-rockets]] wave.
- **Chladni reference**: Jones mentioned a paper on the origin of meteorites published in the French Academy of Sciences between 1760 and 1780 by Chladni — a historical parallel of a phenomenon once dismissed that proved to be real (meteorites were dismissed by the French Academy until Chladni's work).
- **Tremonton film interest**: The British messenger told Jones of CIA interest at the time and mentioned the motion picture film taken by the Navy non-commissioned officer (Tremonton, Utah incident). Jones may write asking for a copy.
- **Page 2 — Additional CIA threat assessment text** (possibly from a different document appended): Lists three potential dangers of UFO sightings:
  1. Difficulty and delay in positive identification could weaken early warning systems in event of hostilities.
  2. Possibility of mass hysteria purposefully induced by an enemy at a critical time by faked reports.
  3. Possibility that emergency communications systems might be seriously overloaded at a critical time.
- **Proposed action**: Communications overload to be addressed through Joint Communications and Electronics Committee. Mass hysteria issue to be brought to Federal Civil Defense Administration and Interdepartmental Committee on Internal Security.
