## Key Points

- **Geographic scope**: Sightings across four Himalayan regions during February-March 1968: Ladakh (Indian Kashmir), Northeast Nepal, North Sikkim, and Western Bhutan — all border regions with China (Tibet).
- **Document structure**: Detailed table with columns for date, local time, area observed, direction of flight, and particulars of the object. Reports come from ground observers in remote Himalayan outposts.
- **Specific incidents**:

  | Date/Time | Location | Direction | Description |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | February 19, 1968, 02:00 | Walungchung Gola, Nepal | NE to SW | Fast-moving object, long and thin, red and green lights, bright as daylight. Thunder sound heard ~30 seconds after. Object giving light from tail, visible across entire area. |
  | February 19, 1968, 02:00 | Lachung, Lachen, Thangu, etc., Sikkim | SE to NW | Same object reported. Direction change over Chholhamu and Green Lake to NE-SW — coincides with Nepal report. |
  | February 21, 1968, 21:30 | Thimpu, Bhutan | Circular path | Object following circular path, left a trail of smoke. Direction from Tibet likely (NE to SW). |
  | February 25, 1968 | Kului region, NW Nepal | North to South | **Blazing object, flashing intermittently, accompanied by thunder sound**. Disintegrated over Kului. |
  | February 25, 1968 (probable same event) | Baltichaur (~5 miles NE of Pokhara?) | — | **A huge metallic disc-shaped object with a 5-foot base and 4 feet in height was recovered from a crater at Baltichaur.** Portions of the "missile object" also found at [REDACTED]. |
  | March 4, 1968, 13:00 | Chang La, Fukche, Koyul, Ladakh | East to West | White light, simultaneously two blasting sounds. Reddish light followed by white smoke. |

  | March 25, 1968, 21:50 | Fukche, Koyul, Demchok, Ladakh | East to West | A bluish-colored object moving at high speed without noise. Bright enough to brighten the area. |

- **Physical evidence recovered**: At Baltichaur, Nepal — a "huge metallic disc-shaped object with a 5-foot base and 4 feet in height" in a crater, with portions found at other locations. This is a [[concepts/physical-trace-case]] of major significance.
- **Multiple detonations**: Several reports mention "blasting sounds" and "thunder sounds" associated with the objects. The February 25 disintegrating object was accompanied by thunder.
- **Extended trans-Himalayan path**: The reports trace objects moving along a NE-to-SW axis from the Tibetan border across multiple mountain ranges, with consistent timing suggesting single objects seen from multiple vantage points.
- **Transmission note**: The document was retrieved on March 21, 1974 at 4:21 PM from the FTN database at Wright-Patterson AFB and forwarded to CIA. The LINDA system returned 152 documents matching the search criteria.

## Notable Incidents / Cases

- **February 25, 1968, Baltichaur, Nepal** — Metallic disc-shaped object (5-foot base, 4 feet tall) recovered from crater. Debris found at multiple locations. **Physical trace case with recovered hardware.**
- **February 19, 1968, Sikkim-Nepal corridor** — Fast-moving long/thin object with red and green lights, daylight brightness, thunder 30 seconds after passage. Seen across multiple locations.
- **February 25, 1968, Kului region, Nepal** — Blazing object disintegrating in flight, accompanied by thunder.
