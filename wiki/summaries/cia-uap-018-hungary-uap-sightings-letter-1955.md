## Key Points

- **This document is a more complete version of CIA-UAP-013** — same source, same report number, but includes the full translated letter text.
- **Source** (same as 013): Naturalized US citizen of [REDACTED] extraction, corresponding with two nieces in Hungary.
- **Summary of intelligence content** (page 1): In November 1955, a letter from the niece in Budapest contained the following: "The so-called flying saucers (rockets) [sic] for several weeks kept the people in a nervous state. These very fast speeding heavenly bodies — scientific groups very busy. I am sure you heard already from the press. 12,000 km per hour was estimated on these flyers." Includes sketch showing formation and suspected course from **Budapest toward Moscow**.
- **Full letter text** (page 2, English translation):

  > Dear Uncle,
  > 
  > I don't know whether the letter I sent before will arrive. I wish you all a very Merry Christmas and a happy New Year. Here, the children are preparing for Christmas. The shops are full of toys and Christmas trees. The streets are crowded with window shoppers.
  > 
  > The three of us are spending the holidays in Pest [Budapest]. The children will have a vacation from 20 December through 17 January. I took a day off on All Souls' Day. [REDACTED] you write so little. How are yours? Has the family grown? Are you all well?
  > 
  > [REDACTED] is having a lot of trouble with her back. [REDACTED] has been sent off for a week to do some work in the province, he won't return till the evening of the 24th.
  > 
  > A famous German circus is on tour here now with many animals and artists. They say it is very good. We are planning to see it with the children.
  > 
  > **Everyone has been excited by the so-called saucers for the past few weeks. These fast-rushing heavenly phenomena have been and still are keeping scores of scientists busy — [REDACTED] was surely seen them or read about them; these amazing flyers moved at a speed of 12,000 kilometers per hour.**
  > 
  > We wish you all good health and send our regards and affection to mother and the children. Much love and many prayers...

- **Context of the saucer mention**: The UAP reference appears within an otherwise ordinary family letter about Christmas preparations, children's vacations, health, a German circus visiting Budapest. It reads as a casual mention of something that was widely known and discussed among the public.
- **Repeated mention**: "Everyone has been excited by the so-called saucers for the past few weeks" — suggests sustained, multi-week wave of sightings, not a single event.
