## Key Points

- **CIA assessment of the Sary Shagan weapons testing range** in the USSR — a former Soviet ABM (Anti-Ballistic Missile) test site.
- **Source**: Former Soviet citizen who worked at the range. Report provides limited information on facilities, work areas, security fencing, regional headquarters (WHQ 03080), and warhead checkout unit (V … 03142).
- **System-75 (SA-2) warheads**: Site 4 checked warheads for the System-75 (SA-2) surface-to-air missile.
- **System-300 / Aldan (ABM-1/GALOSH)**: Site 4 also checked warheads for a missile known as System-300 and/or Aldan. Source did not know if Aldan was another name for System-300 or two similar missiles. Identified in available reference material as the **ABM-1/GALOSH**.
- **Warhead specifications**: System-300/Aldan warhead — ~2 meters long, 80-100 cm diameter, ~400 kg, contained unrecalled number of black cassettes (45 × 12 cm) housing unknown number of metal balls (shariki). SA-2 warheads had ~100 grey cassettes (30 × 7 cm), each holding 200-300 metal balls (~1.5 cm diameter). All work described as "experimental" (opytnaya).
- **Rumored laser research**: Experiments involving laser weapons reportedly conducted at an unknown location at the range, involving "power-beam antennas" (no further details).
- **UAP sighting by source**: In **late summer 1973**, while watching a Canada vs. USSR sports competition on TV, the source stepped outside and observed an **unidentified sharp (bright) green circular object or mass** in the sky.
  - Object situated west of the site at ~70-degree angle of sighting
  - Altitude undeterminable
  - No clouds that evening; source believed green mass would have been above cloud level
  - **Within 10-15 seconds, the green circle widened and several green concentric circles formed around the mass**
  - Within minutes, coloring disappeared
  - **No sound** associated with the phenomenon
  - Source could not estimate diameter; provided no further details
  - No resultant rumors at the facility
