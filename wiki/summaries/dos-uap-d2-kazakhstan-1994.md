## Key Points

- **January 27, 1994**: Tajik Air chief pilot **Ed Rhodes** (American citizen — AmCit) and his two American pilot colleagues encountered a UFO while flying a **Boeing 747SP** at **41,000 feet over Kazakhstan**, latitude 45°N longitude 55°E.
- Pilots reported the encounter to Embassy Dushanbe on January 29, 1994.
- Object first appeared as a "bright light of enormous intensity" approaching from over the eastern horizon at "great rate of speed and at a much higher altitude" than the 747.
- Crew watched the object for **~40 minutes** as it maneuvered in **circles, corkscrews, and 90-degree turns** "at rapid rates of speed and under very high G's."
- Captain Rhodes took several photos with a pocket Olympus camera; promised to send copies to Embassy and the Tajikistan Desk (Lowry Taylor) "if they come out."
- After the maneuvers, the object adopted a horizontal high-speed course and disappeared over the horizon.
- **Light signature**: described as having a "bow wave" — "resembling a high-speed photo of a bullet in flight, in which a very small object gives off a much larger trailing wave of heat/light."
- ~45 minutes after initial sighting, as sun was rising, the aircraft flew under the **contrails the object had left behind**. Rhodes estimated contrail altitude at ~**100,000 feet** — noting "too little air/moisture at that extreme altitude to enable creation of contrails by the propulsion mechanisms of ordinary aircraft." The contrail paths reflected the object's maneuvers (circles, corkscrews).
- Asked whether the object might have been a meteor skipping off the atmosphere, Rhodes and crew were "adamant" — they had seen "thousands of falling stars and other space junk" during their PanAm careers and "this... was nothing like a meteor."
- Rhodes' opinion (echoed by crew): the object was "extraterrestrial and under intelligent control."
- Embassy comment (para 4): "We have no opinion and report the above for what it may be worth."

## Open Questions

- Were Rhodes' Olympus photographs ever received by Embassy or State? Cable is non-committal ("if they come out") and no follow-up cable in this batch confirms.
- Were the two other American pilot colleagues' names recorded in any follow-up document?
- The contrail altitude estimate (~100,000 ft) matches U-2/SR-71 operational ceilings — but those aircraft do not perform circles/corkscrews/90-degree turns and were not deployed in that area in 1994. Identification remains open.
