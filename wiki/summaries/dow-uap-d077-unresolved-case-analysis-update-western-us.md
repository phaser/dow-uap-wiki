## Key Points

This is AARO's formal analysis memorandum evaluating a reported UAP incident near a sensitive national security site in the western United States. **As of June 2026, the case remains unresolved.**

- Six federal law enforcement special agents, in teams of two, reported observing "orbs launching other orbs" near a sensitive national security site in the western United States at approximately dusk over two days in October 2023.
- A luminous orange "mother orb" appeared for 1-2 seconds, released clusters of 2-4 smaller red "orbs," then disappeared. The cycle repeated multiple times over several hours.
- Red orbs exhibited varied kinematic profiles: coordinated horizontal motion, apparent altitude changes, and in at least one instance, a red orb remained stationary above a ridgeline for several hours.
- All phenomena described as "silent." No video, photographic imagery, or technical data was collected by the agents.
- AARO cross-correlated agent accounts against commercial and military flight logs, radar data, spatial estimates, and ADS-B data.

### Hypothesis Evaluation

1. **Misidentified Military Aircraft Exhaust** (Ruled Out): Aircraft altitudes too high; inconsistent with silence, loitering, and "launching" behavior.
2. **Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAV)** (Unlikely): Multi-hour loitering exceeds typical military and commercial UAV battery limits.
3. **Military Aircraft Dispensing Flares** (Partially Plausible): ~60% of reported activity plausibly attributable to military flares (historical flights confirmed in area with IR countermeasure flares). However ~40% of observations occurred when no known aircraft were active in observers' line-of-sight. Stationary multi-hour orb incompatible with any known flare.
4. **U.S. Capability Deconfliction / Blue Force** (Plausible, inconclusive): Some characteristics align with U.S. military technologies, but records inconclusive on whether present at time/place. No single Blue Force capability fully accounts for all reported characteristics.
5. **Foreign Intelligence Activity / Red Force** (Highly Unlikely): Kinematic profiles diverge significantly from known adversary systems.
6. **Phenomenological & Environmental Factors** (Unlikely): Weather conditions inconsistent with ball lightning or sprites. Clear skies, seasonable temperatures, typical light pollution.
7. **Unrecognized Technology** (Pending): Provisional exclusion-based hypothesis for ~40% of phenomena. Based solely on narrative testimony and elimination of competing hypotheses; unsubstantiated by technical data or physical evidence.

### Analytic Factors

- Agents did not collect video or photographic data
- AARO notes intrinsic biological/perceptual limitations for optical estimation of unreferenced phenomena in low-light conditions
- Mitigating factors: close alignment with other regional incidents, agents' professional familiarity with military systems, high consistency across multiple viewing angles
- Cannot determine if reported silence is inherent characteristic or acoustic attenuation
- Ambiguity on whether single or multiple "mother orbs" were present

### Next Steps

AARO intends to integrate scientific modeling from partner agencies, expertise from scientific/academic institutions, and multi-domain multispectral data exploitation. Plans to broaden relationships for Blue/Red Force deconfliction and optimize data collection protocols.
