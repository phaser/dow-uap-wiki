## Key Points

This is a 1949 U.S. Army Plans and Operations (P&O) internal study evaluating the "**flying saucer phenomenon**." The document captures routing slips and memoranda showing how Army Intelligence Division (ID) was tasked to evaluate flying saucer reports.

### Document Flow

1. **22 March 1949**: Executive Branch, P&O routing slip. Study prepared at P&O's request to determine whether various flying saucer reports "stemmed from natural phenomena or if the origin could be traced definitively to the activities of a foreign power." ID directed to keep P&O informed of future developments.
2. **5 April 1949**: P&O 350.05 filing form — "Evaluation Study of Phenomenon (Flying Saucers)." Submitted by Executive Branch, P&O. Routing to D/ID (Director of Intelligence Division).
3. **5 April 1949**: Memorandum for Record from Lt Col Peisinger, P&O. States ID submitted the flying saucer study, which "indicated that of all cases investigated there was no foreign nation implication in these flying saucers." Mr. Stites (ID) indicated ID would take action at P&O request.
4. **20 April 1949**: Memorandum for Record from Maj J.S. Byrne, P&O. Notes that a number of CONUS radio broadcasts in February 1949 on "flying saucers" prompted P&O to request ID on 24 February 1949 to evaluate the phenomena. On 7 March 1949, ID forwarded their evaluation. After **Walter Winchell's 3 April 1949 broadcast** about flying saucers, D/P&O sent Comment #1 to ID requesting verification of Winchell's statements. ID's Comment #2 constitutes the ID answer.

### Key Finding

The ID evaluation concluded there was **no foreign nation implication** in the flying saucer phenomena investigated — a conclusion parallel to the contemporaneous [[entities/project-sign]] assessment and the later [[concepts/robertson-panel]] finding of no direct national security threat.
