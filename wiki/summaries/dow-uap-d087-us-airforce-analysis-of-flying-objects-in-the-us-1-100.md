## Key Points

This is the **standardized 26-field CHECK-LIST incident catalog of Project SIGN**, the Air Force's first formal UFO investigation program. Each incident is recorded on a standardized form covering: date, time, location, observer name/occupation, number of objects, distance, altitude, speed, direction, tactics, sound, size, color, shape, odor, apparent construction, exhaust trails, weather, effect on clouds, sketches/photos, manner of disappearance, and remarks.

### Early Incidents (Pages 1-40, June-August 1947)

**Incidents 1-10 (June-July 1947)**: Cover the [[entities/kenneth-arnold]] sighting (24 June 1947) and the 4-8 July 1947 wave including:
- Multiple sightings at [[entities/muroc-aaf]], California
- Witnesses including test pilots at Muroc reporting dome-like or disc-like objects
- Portland, Oregon reports — multiple observers, objects described as disc-shaped with oscillating/fluttering motion

**Incident ~14**: Witnessed by a General at Muroc AAF, observing objects at 7,500-10,000 ft performing maneuvers including "light circle" — third object performing tight circle while others flew horizontal. No exhaust, no sound. Color: "silvery."

**Incident ~26**: Test pilot Maj. J.C. Wise at Muroc AAF, 7 July 1947 (~1010 hours). Observed from XP-84 aircraft on the ground — object 10,000-15,000 ft, 200-225 MPH heading west. Yellowish-white color, 5-10 ft diameter. Initially thought a weather balloon.

**Incidents ~29-35**: Portland, OR wave (4 July 1947) — multiple disc-like objects witnessed by patrolmen, described as "shiny aluminum." Objects at 40,000 ft, moving at 90-degree angles without slowing. Police corroboration. Up to 8 objects observed.

### Mid-Series Incidents (Pages ~44-93)

**Incident #52 (28 June 1947)**: 30 miles NW of Lake Meade, Nevada. Lt Eric D. Armstrong, 70th Army Air Forces, Perry Division, observed 5 or 6 white circular objects flying in a "very close formation" at ~10,000 ft heading on a course of 285 degrees. Speed: 215 MPH indicated. Objects described as not being jets or conventional aircraft since no exhaust was visible. Lt Armstrong was "very positive" in his statement and "not the impressionable type." Objects were "definitely circular in shape" and maintained close formation. White color, no exhaust trails, no sound.

**Incident #53 (28 June 1947)**: Near coast/Norfolk, VA area. Pilot of Acapulco (airliner) reported a single object at 6,000 ft, pink-white color, speed ~175 MPH. Object described as similar to a fighter in maneuvering, with left-to-right and right-to-left movements. Object maintained 6,000 ft altitude, described as pink-white and unlike any conventional aircraft.

**Incident #55 (~July 1947)**: Near Honolulu/Hickam Field, Hawaii. Mrs. Patricia Arnott [OCR partial] and Lt. Bearrer [OCR partial] observed a reddish light initially thought to be a shooting star but it left no streak. Object reappeared with intermittent flashes for three minutes, maneuvers "too abrupt" for a plane. No motor sound heard.

**Incident #62 (1 September 1947)**: Logan, Utah. Mr. & Mrs. Fred Thomel and 5 other people observed 5 groups, each containing 35 to 50 objects (approximately 175-250 total), yellowish-white color, circling the city at "several thousand feet" at a "high rate of speed." Due to distance and cloud conditions, size undetermined. Related to Incidents 61 and 61a.

**Twin Falls, Idaho (Fall 1947)**: Mrs. Redestrom and two witnesses observed multiple waves of objects traveling in triangular formation at "terrific speed" NE, then returning SW. Successive waves: 3 objects, then 3 more, then 5-6, then 35-50 in triangle formation — all observed over approximately 20-25 minutes. Witnesses ruled out meteors because: (1) objects flew on level line, not dropping; (2) observed in definite formation; (3) lighted from underside; (4) changed course from NE to SW deflection. Witnesses ruled out birds because objects were "lighted from the inside" and could not be reflections of city lights since objects maintained formation and changed course.

**Philadelphia, PA (~6 August 1947)**: Jordan Sayer, former pilot in Army Air Corps, and neighbors observed an object emitting blue-white vapor. Traveling NE to SW at estimated 300-600 MPH. Left vapor/smoke trail lasting ~2 seconds. High-pitched whining sound accompanied the sighting. Object appeared to be a projectile-like light.

**Placerville/Sacramento, CA area (Fall 1947)**: Mr. Ray A. Meyer and wife observed an object 4-6 ft in length, 10-12 inches wide, of a metal color with very bright reflection like "highly polished chromium." Traveled at "terrific speed," left a white smoke trail. Object appeared rocket-like with slightly curved top surface. First seen at 1030 position, crossed to 1200 position emitting puffs of dark smoke.

**Cascade Mountains, Washington/Oregon (1947)**: A prospector "appeared to be very reliable" saw 4 discs traveling in a southeasterly direction. Observed for 45-60 seconds through a telescope. Estimated ~30 ft in diameter, no noise. At approximately 1,000 ft distance. Discs did not fly in any particular formation.

**Scientific Branch Sighting (undated, 1947)**: An employee of the Scientific Branch, Research & Development Board observed a "low flying object" appear over the top of a barn. It moved north to south across a 70° arc in 3-4 seconds at an absolutely horizontal path, then "simply vanished in mid-air." Bright orange glow with long straight narrow white streak. Witness "never observed a comet which acted in this manner and was of the opinion that he had sighted a 'flying saucer'."

### Late-Series Incidents (December 1947 – January 1948)

**Incidents #94-97 (30 December 1947)**: Coordinated observations of an exploding object across Nevada, California, and Oregon:
- **Incident #94**: 30 Dec 1947, 1925 PST. Lt Col V.V. Jones (pilot) at Suisun AAF, CA. Single object observed for 2 seconds at 13,000 ft elevation. Object appeared to rise vertically. Speed not determined.
- **Incident #96**: 30 Dec 1947, 1925 PST. Motorist on U.S. Highway 40, 30 miles west of Lovelock, Nevada (approx. 39°53'N, 118°53'W). Object observed at 300 ft above ground, exploded at 1,200-1,300 ft altitude. Fairfield Control Tower relayed from Hamilton Field Flight Control at approx. 2300 EST. Connected with Incidents 94, 95, and 97.
- **Incident #97**: 30 Dec 1947, 1925 PST. United Airlines Flight 191 pilot on airways between Medford, Oregon and Mt. Shasta, California. Single object observed. Saw flash north of position — presumably an explosion. Fairfield Control Tower obtained report from Oakland Airport Flight Control (CAA) at ~2315 PST.

**Incident #98 (2 November 1947)**: Houston, Texas. Mr. & Mrs. Elmer L. Brimberry (employee of Immigration Service, Dept. of Justice). Just before dawn, observed a single object appearing to fall in a field adjacent to their residence. Round, fire-colored, 26-30 inches in diameter with a tail approximately 20 ft in length. Spinning. Mr. Brimberry described it as "almost round, maybe a little oval or saucer-shaped and giving off a bright light." Object suddenly "went out" just before reaching the ground. Search of 18-acre field found nothing.

**Incident #99 (3 January 1948)**: Vaasa, Finland. Helsinki Press report. A "brightly shining object" with a long tail was observed moving west to east for 30 seconds. U.S. Legation Helsinki forwarded report.

**Incident #100 (5 January 1948)**: Pietarsaari, Finland. Source: Military Attache. Shining object observed for 10 seconds moving north to south. Eyewitnesses stated "flames were ejected and grey streaks left in the sky."

### Structural / Compilation Observations

- Cases include sub-lettered variants (e.g., a, b, c) for multi-witness incidents
- Check-lists include a "Remarks" field for narrative elaboration; some include supplements
- Not all numbered incidents in the sequence are present — Incident #1 and #1a marked "cancelled"
- OCR quality is consistently poor across the document; many fields read as "N/S" (not stated)

### Statistical Patterns

- **Geographic spread**: Heavy concentration in Western U.S. (California, Oregon, Washington, Nevada, Utah, Idaho) with clusters around military installations (Muroc AAF, Fairfield-Suisan, Hamilton Field). Emerging reports from Texas, Ohio, and Pennsylvania later in the series. Intercepts from Finland (Helsinki Press reports, U.S. Legation channels).
- **Temporal clustering**: Intense 1947 summer wave (June-July), continuing at lower density through autumn, with a notable cluster 30 December 1947 (Incidents 94-97). Finnish reports (99-100) arrive January 1948.
- **Witness caliber**: Significant proportion are military personnel (Maj. Wise, Lt Col Jones, Lt Armstrong, Gen. ~Muroc, AF pilots) and credible professionals (Dept. of Justice employee, Army Air Corps veteran, prospector, scientists).
- **Object typology**: Discs and circular objects dominate early cases; rocket-like projectiles with smoke trails appear more frequently in late 1947 cases; fireballs/spinning objects also represented.
