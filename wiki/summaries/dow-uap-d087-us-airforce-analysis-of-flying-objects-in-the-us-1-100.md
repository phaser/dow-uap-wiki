## Key Points

This is the **standardized 26-field CHECK-LIST incident catalog of Project SIGN**, the Air Force's first formal UFO investigation program. Each incident is recorded on a standardized form covering: date, time, location, observer name/occupation, number of objects, distance, altitude, speed, direction, tactics, sound, size, color, shape, odor, apparent construction, exhaust trails, weather, effect on clouds, sketches/photos, manner of disappearance, and remarks.

### Representative Incidents from Sample (Pages 1-40)

**Incidents 1-10 (June-July 1947)**: Cover the [[entities/kenneth-arnold]] sighting (24 June 1947) and the 4-8 July 1947 wave including:
- Multiple sightings at [[entities/muroc-aaf]], California
- Witnesses including test pilots at Muroc reporting dome-like or disc-like objects
- Portland, Oregon reports — multiple observers, objects described as disc-shaped with oscillating/fluttering motion

**Incident ~14**: Witnessed by a General at Muroc AAF, observing objects at 7,500-10,000 ft performing maneuvers including "light circle" — third object performing tight circle while others flew horizontal. No exhaust, no sound. Color: "silvery."

**Incident ~20**: Portland, Oregon — objects described as discs that moved against prevailing wind, ruling out weather balloons. Witnesses stated objects "performed too violently to be balloons."

**Incident ~21**: Muroc AAF — three objects observed from ground. Altitude 10,000 ft. Speed "terrific." Direction: south. Shape: disc. Color: metallic.

**Incident ~26**: Test pilot Maj. J.C. Wise at Muroc AAF, 7 July 1947 (~1010 hours). Observed from XP-84 aircraft on the ground — object 10,000-15,000 ft, moving at 200-225 MPH heading west. Yellowish-white color, 5-10 ft diameter. Initially thought a weather balloon.

**Incident ~29**: Sightings at Portland, OR — three disc-like objects witnessed by patrolmen, described as "shiny aluminum" and performing maneuvers.

**Incident ~30 (p. 30+)**: Multiple objects observed over Portland/Puget Sound area (4 July 1947). Descriptions of objects moving at 90-degree angles without slowing. Reports from police and civilians. Mention of the [[entities/maury-island-affair]] connection period.

**Incident ~33**: Six-person observation at Portland, OR on 4 July 1947. Discoid objects at 40,000 ft, capable of rapid altitude changes.

**Incident 35 (p. 31)**: Portland area — 8 objects described; police patrol corroboration.

### Structure Observations

- Cover sheet (p. 1) has "Authority: NND 917033" and header mentioning "Incident Summaries of Unidentified Flying Objects" with forwarding instruction
- Cases include sub-lettered variants (e.g., a, b, c) for multi-witness incidents
- Check-lists include a "Remarks" field for narrative elaboration
- Some cases include attached supplements (witness statements, sketches)
- Not all numbered incidents in the sequence are present — some pages are blank or cross-reference-cancelled (e.g., Incident 1 and 1a marked "cancelled" on p. 4)
