## Key Points

- Mission flown by **50 ATKS** (50th Attack Squadron, 432 AEW — MQ-9 Reaper at Creech AFB).
- Domain AIR; MAJCOM ACC; **COCOM USEUCOM** (not CENTCOM); supporting 603 AOC; OP HUMMER SICKLE; killbox 41EMD (Eastern Mediterranean).
- Take-off 1355Z 29MAY22 from **Sigonella Naval Air Station (LICZ), Italy**; on station 1551Z; off-station 0616Z; landed 1025Z 30MAY22 at LICZ; total mission **20h 30m**.
- Targeting pod: AN/DAS-4; additional avionics: **AH/SAR** (AirHandler + Synthetic Aperture Radar). FMV exploited by DGS-IN and DGS-4.
- 39 IMINT targets prosecuted, including extensive Russian Navy / SAA / Hezbollah / Latakia naval-monitoring sites along the Syrian and Lebanese coast. Targets include **Syrian Navy Ops Areas, OPIR Israel-Lebanon-Syria DSAs, Latakia Naval Mon DSAs, Bassel al-Assad Intl Airfield, Tartus Naval Base, Beirut Army Bks, Bamlkah SIGINT Facility**, etc.
- **Russian fleet sightings logged**: Slava-class CG (hull 055), Gorshkov-class FFG (hull 461), Udaloy-I DD (hulls 626 and 564) all transiting east; A-50U Mainstay D and 2x Il-38; 1x Il-76 active; 1x probable Su-27/35 landed and another took off.

## Notable Incidents / Cases

- **REACTION 1 — 29 May 2022, 2147Z, ~5 NM west of Syrian coast** — MQ-9 was approached by **1–3 Russian Aerospace Forces (RFAF) aircraft including 1x possible Su-30 Flanker** at FL190 vs. MQ-9 orbit at FL243. The Su-30 flew directly **under** the MQ-9's orbit for ~2 minutes, headed south for ~10 NM, then turned west. No mission impact, no visual acquisition by MQ-9. Source of ID: NTIG.
- **OBSERVATION 1 — 30 May 2022, 0011Z, Bassel al-Assad area, MGRS 36SYE6xx2xxx** — 1x probable **Su-27/35 observed landing** via FMV at the Russian-operated airfield in coastal Syria.
- **UAP 1 — 30 May 2022, 0117Z, MGRS 37SBV28xx [Syria interior]** — at FL240, friendly speed 142 KIAS, MQ-9 observed **1 UAP flying north to north-east**, followed it as long as possible. **DGS screener could not get a positive ID**. UAP signatures: none detected; UAP RF: none. Notable that this UAP report is verbatim identical phrasing to D12 — suggesting boilerplate-template reuse or the same screener.
