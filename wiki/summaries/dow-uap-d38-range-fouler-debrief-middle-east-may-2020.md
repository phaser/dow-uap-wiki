## Key Points

- "Range fouler" is U.S. military jargon for an unidentified object that **intrudes on a U.S. military training/test range or active mission area**. SPEAR routinely collects standardized debriefs from aircrew so the AARO/SPEAR cell can pattern-match across reports.
- Aircrew identifying info, squadron, BuNo (Bureau Number) and side number redacted for privacy ("SPEAR sanitizes all reports of identifying information").
- Mission profile: night ISR; date 14 May 2020 at 20:40Z. Approximate location: 28°31'4" N, 49°52'4" E (Persian Gulf — west of Bahrain, east of Saudi Arabia). Contact altitude **20,000 ft**, altitude not constant. Contact moving (direction blank).

## Notable Incidents / Cases

- **14 May 2020, 20:40Z, Persian Gulf (~28.5°N 49.9°E), 20,000 ft** — aircrew narrative: *"While performing an ISR tasking (ULTN/Black Hot/Lin), a **solid white object flew through the FOV**. There was a temporarily lose of the object but re-acquired shortly thereafter. The crew was able to follow the object as it appeared to make **erratic moments above the water**. During the follow, crew was able to obtain 4x zoom on the object but lost the object due to poor track placement. While following, the sensor operator was continuously manipulating the sensor to maintain eyes on the object. This is apparent by the waves of the water in the background being visible and not being visible."* Round, solid white, intermittent radar trackfile, erratic motion above water.
