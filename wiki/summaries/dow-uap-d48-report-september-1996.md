## Key Points

- Presents how the **DAMP (Damage Area Methodology Program?)** risk-analysis software models **Mode-5 failure responses** — uncommon space-booster failures that cause **significant deviation from the intended flight line**.
- An impact-density function with two shaping constants is fitted to simulated Mode-5 malfunctions for **Atlas IIAS, Delta-GEM, Titan IV, LLV1**, and other launch vehicles.
- **Appendix D** (~75+ pages) contains a **launch and performance history of Atlas, Delta, Titan, and Thor missile and space-vehicle launches from the Eastern and Western Ranges from the beginning of each program through August 1996**, with narratives of every failure / anomalous behavior. This is a comprehensive corpus of known U.S. range anomalies — directly relevant to disambiguating UAP reports near launch ranges.
- Mode-5 failures are the rarest class but the most relevant to off-axis "unidentified flying object" misidentification near launch corridors.

## Notable Incidents / Cases

- The narrative tables in Appendix D list every Atlas, Delta, Titan, Thor failure or anomalous behavior since program inception. Each entry includes vehicle configuration, success flag, flight phase of anomaly, and a Mode (0–5) classification. Selected anomalies sampled in the document include "various anomalous events occurred early in flight, the missile appeared to follow [...]" and entries describing third-stage performance leading to erroneous orbits.
- This document is included in the DoW UAP corpus likely as a **disambiguation / reference resource** — i.e., to allow analysts examining reported UAP near U.S. launch ranges to cross-check against known Mode-3/4/5 booster anomalies.
