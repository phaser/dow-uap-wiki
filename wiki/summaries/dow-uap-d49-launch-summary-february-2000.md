## Key Points

- Official registry of all major launch operations conducted from **Vandenberg Air Force Base, California** from the first launch (16 December 1958) through early 2000.
- Two tabular matrixes are included: **"Annual Launch Summary by Booster"** and **"Annual Launch Summary by Command"**. Plus a **Launch Facility Guide** and **Glossary**.
- Glossary includes Cold War / aerospace acronyms relevant to the UAP corpus, e.g., **ABRES** (Advanced Ballistic Reentry System), **ABRV** (Advanced Ballistic Reentry Vehicle), **AMARV** (Advanced Maneuverable Reentry Vehicle), **ANT** (Advanced Nose Tip), **AST** (Airborne Surveillance Testbed), **BMRS** (Ballistic Missile Reentry System), **BOMARC** (Boeing-Michigan Aero Research Center surface-to-air missile), **DMSP** (Defense Meteorological Satellite Program), and **SAMPEX** ("**Solar, Anomalous and Magnetospheric Particle Explorer**").
- Distribution list spans HQ USAF/XOFS, AFSPC/HO, USSPACECOM, NASA HQ, KSC, JPL, TRW, Boeing Defense & Space Group, ITT Federal Services, Aerospace Corporation, Orbital Sciences Corp.
- Vandenberg local time used for launch dates. East Coast (Cape Canaveral) launches **not** included.

## Notable Incidents / Cases

- Each launch entry in the chronology can be cross-referenced against UAP sightings near the Western Range (California / NW Pacific coast). Of particular UAP-relevance:
  - SAMPEX = "**Solar, Anomalous and Magnetospheric Particle Explorer**" — a satellite mission name that includes the word "Anomalous," reflecting space-physics terminology rather than a UAP study.
  - ABRES / ABRV / AMARV / ANT — reentry-vehicle test programs that produced highly visible / unusual phenomena over the WR (Western Range), often misreported as UFOs through the 1970s–80s.
- The document is included in the DoW UAP corpus likely as a **disambiguation / reference resource** to allow analysts to cross-check UAP sightings near the California / Western Range against known scheduled launch activity.
