## Key Points

- **CTG 67.1** (Commander Task Group 67.1, U.S. 6th Fleet maritime patrol task group) reported a **P-8A Poseidon** observation of an unidentified low-flying object **55 NM northwest of Latakia, Syria**, on 18 November 2016.
- Object was observed via the P-8A's **EO/IR sensor** for ~2 minutes, traveling at **~500 kts** on a **southeasterly heading**, **outbound from KCTG** (Russian **Kuznetsov Carrier Task Group**, "K" denoting Kuznetsov).
- Object's trajectory passed between the **Russian Navy auxiliary INGUL ARS (Russian fleet salvage tug)** and 1x unidentified vessel.
- P-8A position 26 NM south of object at initial detection (1610Z [sic — likely 1310Z, slide is timestamped 13/10Z]); object lost approximately 40 NM NW of Latakia at 1312Z.
- Mission Commander assessed the object as a **possible missile** in **sea-skim mode** outbound from KCTG. Although this was the first such observation by P-8A in the Eastern Mediterranean, it was assessed as **standard activity consistent with the assessed activity of the KCTG**.
- Weather: P-8 aircrew characterized visibility as clear, no range limitations. Mission commander characterized the interaction as **safe**.
- Video footage is referenced in the slide ("Video footage can be found at this link.").

## Notable Incidents / Cases

- **18 November 2016, 1310Z–1312Z, 55 NM NW of Latakia, Syria, Eastern Mediterranean** — P-8A Poseidon detected a possible sea-skimming missile launched from the Russian Kuznetsov CSG (KCTG), traveling SE at 500 kts. Object passed between INGUL ARS (Russian fleet tug) and an unidentified vessel; 2-minute track on EO/IR sensor.
- **Note**: this report describes a likely **conventional Russian missile** (probably a Kalibr / Kh-35 / 3M-54E launched from a Kuznetsov-CSG escort). It is included in the DoW UAP corpus because the initial classification of the object was uncertain — the term "unidentified low-flying object" is used in the slide title. Once correlated to KCTG activity, the assessment was "standard activity," not anomalous. This is therefore a **borderline UAP report later resolved to conventional ordnance**.
