## Notable Incidents / Cases

- **27 October 2020, 01:12:21Z, contact altitude 26,000 ft, mission DCA over CENTCOM AOR** — narrative: *"**KINGPIN directed ID of unknown contact.** [Aircraft callsign 1.4a] obtained **radar lock and target pod video** but unable to get closer than **16.9 NM TID** to get a better ID. The target pod showed **2 IR-significant contacts**. **One range fouler was circling around the other**, and in **1/30th of a second they were gone**. Tally achieved was **2x red blinking strobes** and **noise jamming was received**. Noise jamming was indicated by two chevrons. First detection of the range foulers were at B/E ZIM 248/17 [from bullseye ZIM, bearing 248°/range 17 NM]."*
- This is one of the highest-quality F-15E DCA UAP debriefs in the corpus: **two objects, one orbiting the other, then both disappearing in 1/30 of a second**, accompanied by **noise jamming** and **2x red blinking strobes** as the visual cue. JTAC controller "KINGPIN" matches the OIR DCA ESSA killbox controller pattern (see [[summaries/dow-uap-d19-mission-report-syria-february-21-2023]]).

## Open Questions

- "11 EFS" — a real designation could refer to the 11th Expeditionary Fighter Squadron; alternatively "11 EFS" might be hard-to-parse OCR for another unit number. Verify.
- "1/30s disappearance" — implies an angular displacement faster than the sensor's frame interval. Was this on slow-track FLIR or fast-track radar?
- "Noise jamming with two chevrons" — chevrons in the F-15E TEWS display indicate detected ECM emitter. Was this jamming originating from the UAPs themselves, or from a co-located adversary platform?
- "2x red blinking strobes" — pattern matches conventional aircraft anti-collision lights, but the kinematics (one orbiting the other; instantaneous disappearance) do not.
