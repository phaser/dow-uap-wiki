## Key Points

- Aircraft observed a "weapons quality 1" radar track of a UAP traveling **with the winds at 31,000 ft MSL** in the vicinity of grid cell 323's.
- Crew was able to perform a "next to shoot" cue on the radar track and visually confirmed the UAP using TFLIR (Targeting Forward-Looking Infrared).
- UAP described as "**looks like a balloon, similar to previously reported UAP from 48FW**" — explicit cross-reference to prior 48th Fighter Wing UAP observations.
- Object's apparent drift with prevailing wind is consistent with a passive lighter-than-air object profile rather than powered flight.

## Notable Incidents / Cases
