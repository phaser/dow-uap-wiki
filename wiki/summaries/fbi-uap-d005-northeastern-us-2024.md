## Key Points

- Witness [REDACTED] interviewed at FBI office, currently employed by [REDACTED] and works primarily at [REDACTED] facility
- **First UAP sighting**: November 2021, ~5:00 AM. While exercising at home, noticed lights outside back window. Initially thought someone was in the yard with a flashlight
- Upon going outside, observed a **singular light that eventually split into two or three lights**, forming a **triangular pattern with a light in the center**, lasting approximately 10 minutes
- While filming on smartphone, the witness accidentally took a **flash photo** — the lights **immediately disappeared**
- **March 2022 sighting**: At a different location; played video previously provided to FBI
- Sightings are typically at dusk or at night; the light observed appears to **morph and move laterally**
- Witness reached out via LinkedIn to [REDACTED], formerly of [REDACTED], who told them: "**people have been hurt by these things**" — warned to be careful
- That contact also shared a **1996 personal incident**: driving with an Army officer from Fort Bliss, TX to Fort Carson, CO through New Mexico; the vehicle they were in **shut down**, a large light shone on them for ~2 minutes, then the light went away and the vehicle's electronics turned back on
- Witness has lived at current property since 2021, previously lived ~5 miles away from 2006
- Has seen **hundreds of these lights** from the property; has numerous videos and photos
- Witness's [REDACTED] and others who visited the property have also seen the lights
- More activity observed since September 2024
- Sightings have included shorter bursts; **video corruption** is common when trying to record these sightings
- Witness used **ADSB data** to cross-reference sightings against known aircraft — ruled out many as aircraft, but some remain unexplained
- Acknowledged throughout interview that they never had an interest in UAPs and that "a lot of what [they] said may seem weird or crazy"
