## Key Points

- Reverend Barnes writes to J. Edgar Hoover to report an observation he believes "may be of great significance, or may be merely an observation of a scientific experiment"
- **Observation (May 1948)**: One afternoon, saw **four beams in the sky** passing from the **northwest to the southeast**, converging in the Cascade Mountains
- In those four narrow beams, **small clouds were forming**
- Where the beams met (apparently against the mountains), a **"great explosion effect"** was visible
- Beams were visible for at least **10 minutes or longer**
- The sight reminded him of a pre-war article about experiments in Europe using **radio beams to effect rainfall** — speculates it might have been from Science Digest
- Connects the observation to the **Columbia River flood of the previous summer** and unusual precipitation that winter, suggesting potential national security implications
- Mentions having one son in the **Air Corps** and another working with the **A.E.C. at Hanford**, which "causes me to pray for peace"
- Adds that "the explosion effect [rose to] a height of about **ten thousand feet**"
- **FBI response** (page 3, partially legible): Hoover's office acknowledged receipt of the letter and referred the information — character of the referral is partially illegible but appears to be a standard acknowledgment
- The response is dated **February 10, 1949** and marked "Communications Section" — "MAILED"
