## Key Points

### August 1952 — Photograph Investigation Referral from OSI
- Air Force Office of Special Investigations (OSI), 20th District, New York, transmitted copies of a restricted memorandum dated August 12, 1952 from the Chief, Technical Analysis Division, Air Technical Intelligence Center (ATIC), **Wright-Patterson AFB**, Ohio
- Transmitted **photographs allegedly of unidentified aerial objects** taken by **John H. Riley**, 28, 571 Main Street, Paterson, NJ
- Known witness: **George J. Stock**, 221 Brooks Ave., Passaic, NJ
- OSI requested FBI investigate: background of photographer and witness to determine reliability; circumstances under which photos were taken; locate other sources; and obtain any data pertinent to determining authenticity
- Also transmitted a letter from **Charles Gregg**, staff writer for "The Herald-News," Passaic, NJ, to ATIC dated August 2, 1952

### August 1957 — Clifton, NJ Sighting
- U.S. Air Force Reserve Captain [REDACTED] Schey, 24 Day Street, Clifton, NJ, called at 8:46 PM, 8/27/57
- Observed object due west of Clifton at 8:30 PM
- Object shape **resembling an underwater demolition bomb**
- Light in the shape of a **cross** was coming from within the UFO
- Using field glasses, saw **diamond-shaped holes** in the UFO from which the light emitted
- Agent George Weertz, OSI Duty Officer, notified

### November 1957 — Glen Ridge, NJ Sighting
- Mrs. [REDACTED] reported she and her husband observed a **long, cylindrical, dark silver object** with **no wings but fins on the rear**, moving toward the northeast
- No markings; flying low and slow
- Object returned from the direction it had gone at dusk; appeared to have some motor noise; flew southwest at higher altitude, same speed

### May 1958 — Glen Ridge, NJ Sighting
- Mrs. [REDACTED] observed a **brilliant white, cigar-shaped object with delta wings toward the tail**, flying south to north
- No noise

### February 1958 — Union, NJ Sighting
- Mrs. Mary Jane [REDACTED] observed a **bullet-shaped, silver object, no wings**, flying south at very high altitude with vapor trail — higher than commercial aircraft

### April 1959 — Multiple Caller Event
- Between 5:40 and 6:45 PM on 10/28/59, the Newark office received **seven calls** about a UFO
- Described as: very bright object with twinkling lights, surrounded by haze/cloud-like mist, vapor trail dissipating quickly
- Callers from various NJ locations: [REDACTED], Surf City, East Orange, Green Brook, Hamilton Township, Palisades Park, Upper Montclair
- An additional ~7 callers reported to SA Shahan
- OSI representative placed on list for follow-up 10/29/59

### January 1960 — Bloomfield, NJ Sighting
- Witnesses at First Presbyterian Church, Broad St., Bloomfield, heard what sounded like a roar or jet, went outside and saw a **bright stationary object** with hazy outline at ~5,000 feet east toward NYC; disappeared after 5 minutes
- Second witness in West Paterson saw a jet flying SE, heard explosion, saw object leave plane and move easterly; bright light emanated from object; after several minutes appeared to drop from sky toward NYC

### February 1961 — Westfield, NJ Sighting
- Witness observed round, bright object northwest of residence; saw same object Monday and Tuesday evenings; third night saw three similar objects
- Objects changed from round to lateral shape, "like a spectrum"
- Wife, daughter (school teacher), son, and neighbor all verified findings

### September 1961 — Lodi, NJ "Flying Saucers" Report
- Witness [REDACTED] Stockes, employed by a detective agency, reported seeing flying saucers over Lodi, NJ on 9/10/61
- Notified local police; two patrol officers arrived but "did nothing"
- Sent documents and affidavits to U.S. Air Force in Washington, D.C.

### February 1963 — USAF Public Information Division Letter
- Major [REDACTED], Deputy Chief, Operations Branch, Public Information Division, Office of the Secretary of the Air Force, wrote to J. Edgar Hoover
- Former DoD spokesman for **Project Blue Book** (Foreign Technology Division, Wright-Patterson AFB)
- Enclosed copy of "**Saucer News**" (Volume 10, Number 1, March 1963), published quarterly from Fort Lee, NJ; Editor: **James W. Moseley**
- Publication described as covering "non-believers, believers, and skeptics of the 'flying saucer' subject"

### March 1963 — "Saucer News" Article
- Included article about forthcoming book "**The Second Coming of Christ and Flying Saucers**" by **Richard Ogden**, P.O. Box 9005, Seattle, Washington
- Printer refused to publish the book after legal advice: "too harshly critical of the U.S. Government, particularly the FBI"; feared libel suits
- Ogden planned to self-publish mimeographed edition; would release all copies simultaneously to avoid confiscation
- Ogden wrote: "I risk personal ruin to get this thing out"

### 1964 — NICAP Inquiry
- Letter dated January 15, 1964 from a member of **NICAP** (National Investigations Committee on Aerial Phenomena), Matawan, NJ
- Sent to Arizona Department of Law and Public Safety, Division of State Police, Phoenix, AZ (copy in FBI file)
- Asked whether USAF compelled withholding UFO info from press; Arizona State Police policy on UFO reports; results of recent UFO investigations
