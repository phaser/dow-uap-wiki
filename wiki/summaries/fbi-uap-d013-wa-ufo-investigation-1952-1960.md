### July 1952 — Pasco, WA Newspaper Reports
- Local newspaper ("Columbia Basin News," Pasco, WA, July 29, 1952) reported a **"dish-like object" that appeared to have a tail all around it**, observed by [REDACTED]
- Newspaper reports from Seattle area collected by FBI under the NW 90291 file number

### January 1953 — Sand Point Naval Air Station Sighting
- Miss **Delma J. Segram**, Seattle, telephoned FBI at 10:10 AM, 1/ [REDACTED]/53
- Reported that Mrs. [REDACTED] Webster observed a **very large object "quite high"** directly above and somewhat west of Sand Point Naval Air Station
- Described as "**like a large parachute**" and **"so silver that she could hardly look at it"**
- After approximately two minutes, the object appeared to **take away to the North**
- Information relayed to OSI and Radio Station [REDACTED]

### February 1953 — Sand Point Sighting
- Mrs. [REDACTED] Ironsides, while crossing the University Bridge in Seattle at 1027 hours on 2/10/53, observed a **bright object in the sky high over Sand Point Naval Air Station, a little to the South**
- Sky was "beautifully clear" at the time; extreme brightness drew her attention
- Could furnish no description as to the shape of the object

### July 1953 — Detailed Telescopic Observation (Boeing Employee)
- Letter dated July 15, 1953 from [REDACTED], 1405 Haupt Ave., [REDACTED], Washington, to FBI
- Writer was a **design supervisor of the CJA 512 R design** (Boeing employee/contractor)
- On the afternoon of July 15, 1953, used a **30-power Bausch & Lomb telescope** to observe an object
- Description: object looked like **"a large sheet of brown paper at about 10,000 feet"**
- Had a **silver ring about blue in color** on the bottom showing the object (attached sketch); writer initially thought it was a **radar target balloon made of transparent plastic**
- Object was **hovering, then moved toward the east at incredible speed**, disappearing over the horizon in **about 10 seconds** — "the latter motion could only be observed with the naked eye due to the tremendous speed"
- Writer spent ~40 years as a self-taught telescope observer/target shooter; described as "a design supervisor" confident in observation
- Attached sketch showing the object as seen from the bottom
- Writer stated: "no short description can be adequate"

### June 1954 — Pasco, WA Half-Moon Objects
- At 11:35 PM, 6/6/54, **Gail Plock**, Pasco, WA, supervisor of a local Ground Observer Group, reported sighting **two half-moon shaped objects about six times as large as a half moon**
- One object **formed into a large fireball**; the other formed into **two fireballs**
- Original color of both objects was white
- Estimated altitude: **about 1,000 feet**
- Location slightly north of Pasco, grid coordinate AB 5517
- Observed for a period of **ten minutes**
- 25th Air Division intelligence officer not submitting written report but will follow up with letter

### June 1954 — Tacoma Flying Saucer Report
- Colonel Raynor, OSI McChord AFB, reported that an item in the Tacoma News Tribune indicated a **transit driver for the Tacoma bus company** had seen a flying saucer over Tacoma the previous night

### June 1954 — Radar/Visual Intercept Incident at Neah Bay
- **Between 19:30 and 19:45 hours on 6/[REDACTED]/54**: Radar station at **Neah Bay** picked up an unidentified "blip" or target
- Target was **38 to 40 miles SW of Neah Bay** at altitude **15,000 to 100 feet [sic]** estimated
- **F-86 interceptors** scrambled from McChord AFB; pilots sighted the objects
- Target separated into **two "blips"** — pilots saw two objects
- Pilots were being interrogated at McChord
- Radar track of approximately **8 to 12 minutes** before targets faded from scopes, presumably due to fast air speed
- Objects flew South, then turned East, then North

### June 1954 — Air Force Intelligence Summary
- Formal intelligence summary from 25th Air Division to Air Technical Intelligence Center, Wright-Patterson AFB, Ohio, re: **Unidentified Flying Objects**
- Dated June 24, 1954; referenced Special Agent Sullivan, FBI
- Detailed narrative of the June 1954 radar/visual sighting:
  - Unknown blip or target picked up on TIMOTHY radar scope in General [REDACTED] at 200 knots speed; estimated altitude 15 angels
  - Two F-86D interceptor aircraft ("Blue #1" and "Blue #2") on CAP at McChord were vectored to intercept
  - "Blue #2" obtained an airborn [sic] radar pickup at approximately 88 knots from blip; target separated into two blips at 0435
  - "Blue #2" attempted to close on 30-degree port heading; target increased speed from 75 to 300 knots and continued accelerating
  - Target then turned from 30 degrees to 60 degrees right; "Blue #2" lost radar contact at approximately 9,000 feet altitude
  - Ground radar showed blips or targets splitting and merging throughout the 8-12 minute engagement
  - Conclusion: "objects observed are tentatively evaluated as radar targets probably in preparation side AM general configuration and adheres to B-36 or B-52 type aircraft with maximum dash speed of Mach 1 or better"
  - ATIC stated it does not expect any further information concerning this incident
- Additional note: F-86D pilot observed "a tail at about 5 feet only a 1/2 foot wide" — object appeared oval with a tail, some substance to it

### About August 1954 — Kennewick, WA Police Sergeant Sighting
- **Sgt. Gordon Richardson**, Kennewick, Washington Police Department
- Advised that for several years he had been **very interested in flying saucers** and had read everything available
- Had information he considered "of utmost importance to the defense of the U.S."
- **About a year prior (circa August 1953?)**: Was on night shift at about 10 PM in the vicinity of 1st and Washington, Kennewick
- Observed a **large, shining, metal ball about 30 feet in diameter** "swoosh in" from the West at rather low altitude — only **200 or 300 feet in the air or less**
- Ball traveling at high speed; when it got about three blocks east of Washington Street, **it came to a complete stop**
- Ball had left a **tail of fire** until it stopped, then emitted no fire or smoke
- Described as **perfectly spherical** and made of **shiny metal**
- Appeared to have at least **two different kinds of motors**: when standing still, could hear a **"chug chug" of the engine sounding like a tractor engine**, apparently an internal combustion engine
- Ball stood still for several minutes, then **took off with speed so great it disappeared from sight in a matter of seconds** — obviously a different type of engine (like a jet) for flight
- No fire or smoke on leaving
- Richardson had never told anyone about this matter; had not discussed with other officers because they "never pay any attention to what he says"
- Prefaced remarks by stating he "realizes his information sounds like a 'nut complaint' but assures the writer this is not so"
- Information forwarded to Air Force / interested Defense Agency

### September 1956 — "Mark-Six Aircraft" Recovery
- Duty officer, U.S. Coast Guard called at 4:36 PM, 9/[REDACTED]/56
- Referenced previous report of **unidentified objects** — determined to be a **"Mark-Six Aircraft"** (likely a drone/target drone)
- Reports from **Willapa, Point Granville, and Neah Bay** — all destroyed by demolition squad except Neah Bay (scheduled for Monday)
- Coast Guard conducting search for additional objects along the coast

## Notable Incidents / Cases

- **July 1953 Boeing Employee Telescopic Sighting** — Skilled observer using 30-power telescope watches a silver-ringed, brown/disc-shaped object hover then accelerate to horizon-crossing speed in ~10 seconds. High-credibility single-witness report with technical description and sketch.
- **June 1954 Neah Bay Radar/Visual Intercept** — Radar tracks unidentified target at 15,000–20,000 feet off Washington coast; F-86 interceptors scrambled; target splits into two and accelerates beyond 300 knots; pilots visually confirm objects. ATIC evaluates as possible B-36/B-52 type aircraft but speed profile inconsistent.
- **Kennewick 30-Foot Metal Ball (circa 1953)** — Police sergeant observes a 30-foot metallic sphere at 200–300 feet altitude that stops midair, emits "tractor engine" sound while hovering, then accelerates out of sight in seconds. Extraordinary low-altitude close-range observation by law enforcement.
- **June 1954 Pasco Half-Moon Objects** — Ground Observer Corps supervisor reports two half-moon objects that transform into fireballs at ~1,000 feet altitude, observed for 10 minutes.
- **September 1956 "Mark-Six Aircraft"** — Coast Guard/USAF recovery of drone targets along Washington coast, initially reported as UFOs.

## Open Questions
