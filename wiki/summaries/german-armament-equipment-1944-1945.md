## Key Points

- This file is the **SHAEF investigative dossier on the "Foo-Fighter" phenomenon** reported by Allied night-fighter aircrew over the European Theater in late 1944 / early 1945. The "German Armament Equipment" file label reflects SHAEF's working hypothesis at the time that these were enemy weapons.
- **Origin of the SHAEF inquiry** (16 January 1945, HQ XII Tactical Air Command, Lt. Col. Leavitt Corning Jr., A/C of S A-2): forwards 415th Night Fighter Squadron's training/tactical information for December 1944 — quoted verbatim:
  > "We have encountered a phenomenon which we cannot explain; crews have been followed by lights that blink on and off changing colors etc. The lights come very close and fly formation with our planes. They are agitating and keep the crews on edge when they encounter them, mainly because they cannot explain them. It is requested further information be furnished on this subject, such as similar experiences of other night units."
- **First Tactical Air Force (Prov)** (20 January 1945, Maj. Boykin) — no instances reported elsewhere; requests color/intensity/size/duration/altitude/time data plus whether lights cross Allied lines, where mounted on enemy aircraft, etc.
- **415th Night Fighter Squadron 2nd W/Ind** (30 January 1945, Capt. R. B. Ringwald, Intelligence Officer) — provides verbatim sortie-report extracts. Twelve incidents excerpted:
  - **Night 14-15 December 1944** — vicinity Erstein (V-9381) at 1000 ft: large red light at 2000 ft going east at 18:40 hrs, ~200 mph.
  - **Night 16-17 December 1944** — 20 mi north of Breisach (W-0173) at 800 ft: "5 or 6 flashing red and green lights in 'T' shape"; first thought to be flak; reappeared closer behind the aircraft, followed turns port and starboard; closed to 8 o'clock at 1000 ft; held position several minutes then disappeared.
  - **Night 22-23 December 1944** — patrolling Sarrebourg-Strasbourg at Angels 10; 06:00 hrs: two lights coming up from the ground, leveled off at plane altitude, stayed on tail ~2 minutes. Lights described as "a large orange glow"; would peel off and turn away, fly level, then go out — "appeared to be under perfect control at all times." Vicinity Hagenau.
  - **Night 23-24 December 1944** — reddish flames at considerable distance, ~10,000 ft; later, ~5 mi NE of Point X, "glowing red object shooting straight up. Changed suddenly to plane view of a/c doing a wing over and going into a dive and disappearing."
  - **Night 26-27 December 1944** — 01:45 hrs: two yellow streaks of flame at 3000 ft off port; red balls of fire that stayed up ~10 sec, ~45 mi away; "felt what was thought to be prop wash; very distinct"; groups of lights making distinct lines "somewhat like arrows."
  - **Night 26-27 December 1944** — vector 090 near Y-7050: airborne white lights, "staggered evenly vertically," 1 to 4 visible at once, stationary at 10,000 ft.
  - **Night 26-27 December 1944** — vicinity of Worms: light at same altitude came within 100 ft; took evasive action but light continued for 5 minutes; then pulled up rapidly and out of sight.
  - **Night 27-28 December 1944** — Q-1378 vicinity: lights suspended in air moving slowly then disappearing; orange in color; singly and in pairs; observed 4-5 times.
  - **Night 27-28 December 1944** — 8 mi NE of Luneville at 19:10: three sets of three lights (red/white) on starboard and port, 1000-2000 ft to rear closing at Angels 10. Pulled up, lights went out; called Churchman GCI — negative reply.
  - **Night 30-31 December 1944** — group of lights flying 30-40 mi east of base at Angels 9-10.
  - **Night 1-2 January 1945** — "Saw foofighters North of Strasbourg and North of Saverne."
  - **Night 14-15 January 1945** — large orange glow ~5 ft diameter, vicinity Ingweiller, 6000 ft, 20:30 hrs.
  - **Night 29-30 January 1945** — 00:10: Foofighter half-way between Weissembourg and Landau; off starboard rear at Angels 2; amber lights, one 20-50 ft above the other, ~30 sec duration; 1000 ft away following; lights ~1 ft diameter; disappeared when Travel 34 turned into them.
- "**In every case where pilot called GCI Control and asked if there was a Bogey A/C in the area he received a negative answer.**" (Capt. Ringwald)
- Footnote in original: "Foofighters is the name given these phenomenon by combat crews of this Squadron."
- **SHAEF response / escalation**:
  - 11 Feb 1945, A/Cdre. Grierson (SHAEF A-2): forwarded to USSTAF (Col. Bradley, Chief Tech Intel Officer); recommended a USSTAF Air Technical Intelligence Officer visit the unit. Notes "pilots and crews are becoming slightly worried by them"; suggests "everything possible should be done to get to the root of the matter." Scientific Investigation Division (Mr. Robertson — likely Howard P. Robertson) also asked to consider.
  - 17 Feb 1945, A.D.I.(K), Air Ministry — replies that the phenomena "probably refers to 'Flak Bombe' mentioned in A.D.I.(K) Report No. 562/1944 paragraphs 128 and 129."
  - 8 March 1945, SHAEF Air to First Tactical Air Force (Prov) — quotes Air Ministry D.D./I.2 reply: "Bomber Command crews have for some time been reporting similar phenomena. A few of the alleged aircraft may have been Me-262s and for the rest, flak rockets are suggested as the most likely explanation. The whole affair is still something of a mystery and the evidence is very sketchy and varied so that no definite and satisfactory explanation can yet be given." Signed E. D. M. Hopkins, Group Captain.
  - 8 March 1945 (Air Ministry D.D./I.2 letter to W/Cdr Grierson) — same Hopkins letter, "BALLS OF FIRE - RED."
- **Photographic intelligence component**: 1 March 1945 SHAEF AIR/S.37153/A-2 — request for photographs from HQ 1st Tac Air Force; photos "of long cylindrical objects" claimed by 107th P.R. Squadron pilot proved unsuccessful (failed to develop / show object). HQ IX TAC (Quesada) confirms: "Photos of long cylindrical objects taken by 67th TAC/R Pilots 911030 vicinity F-5710 were unsuccessful. Full written report will follow." (Cable QQY).
- A March 1945 message describes one of those objects as a "long cylindrical object aluminum coloured ... with small ... the lower end ... the object was deflated ... a red ... resulted without ... cylinder ... did disintegrate" — text fragmentary in OCR but indicates an aluminum-colored cylinder, possibly inflatable, that "deflated" and "did not disintegrate." (Ambiguous between V-1 / Trippelwerke-related ordnance and an unknown object.)
- File also references **Trippelwerke (Molsheim)** German bomb/torpedo stocks logistics — suggesting SHAEF was simultaneously concerned about German exotic-weapon recovery, an adjacent topic that explains the file label.

## Notable Incidents / Cases

- **December 1944 - January 1945 Foo-Fighter Wave** — at least 12 separate incidents reported by 415th Night Fighter Squadron over the upper Rhine region (Strasbourg, Sarrebourg, Saverne, Luneville, Worms, Hagenau, Erstein, Breisach, Weissembourg/Landau, Ingweiller). Pattern: glowing red, orange, amber, white, or green lights — in singles, pairs, T-formations, "arrow" lines, or vertical stacks; at altitudes from 800 ft to ~10,000 ft; closing within 100-1000 ft of aircraft; following maneuvers; sometimes leveling off and tracking aircraft for several minutes; sometimes "shooting straight up"; sometimes appearing as a "wing-over" silhouette. GCI Control consistently reported no bogey aircraft.
- **107th P.R. Squadron / 67th TAC/R Photo Attempts (March 1945)** — pilots photographed "long cylindrical objects" near F-5710; photos failed to develop adequately.

## Open Questions

- Did the Air Ministry's Me-262 / flak-rocket explanation hold up to subsequent investigation? Modern history has not validated either explanation for the bulk of the cases (Me-262 was a jet fighter, not a glowing orb; flak rockets had no known capacity for sustained 5+ minute aircraft-pursuit).
- Mr. Robertson — likely **Howard P. Robertson**, who later chaired the 1953 CIA Robertson Panel on UFOs — appears to have first encountered the foo-fighter problem here at SHAEF Scientific Investigation Division. This is potentially the throughline from WWII foo-fighters to postwar UFO debunking; worth confirming via biography.
- The 107th PR / 67th TAC/R "long cylindrical object" photographs — did the "full written report" mentioned by Quesada survive elsewhere?
- The phrase "Trippelwerke 15" near a Trippelwerke Molsheim torpedo / bomb stock recovery message is an artifact of file co-location, not (apparently) a foo-fighter explanation.
