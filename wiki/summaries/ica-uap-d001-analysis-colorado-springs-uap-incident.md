## Key Points

- On **15 February 2022 at 0935 MST**, five U.S. Army service members at Fort Carson, Colorado, observed an airborne object approximately 6 miles to the west, over and slightly behind the **Cheyenne Mountain** silhouette.
- Object was observed for approximately **30–180 seconds**.
- Witnesses described the object as roughly the **size of a large jet**, resembling an **angular, non-symmetrical potato made of uneven panels**, completely stationary about **300–500 feet above Cheyenne Mountain**, while slowly changing shape.
- The object had a **slightly translucent, shimmering white appearance** with defined edges and a milky shimmer that reflected sunlight.
- Despite witnesses keeping their eyes on the object, it **suddenly disappeared**.
- Witnesses reported **clear, blue skies**; multiple weather reports (AFWA, Weather Underground) indicated **partly to mostly cloudy** conditions that morning.
- **AARO assessment**: The object was possibly **[[concepts/backscattering|backscattering]]** of sunlight reflecting off snow-covered ground (snow depth 6–12 inches on Cheyenne Mountain), illuminating low-level clouds. This would account for the object's visibility followed by its sudden disappearance if clouds or sun shifted slightly.
- AARO holds **low confidence** in this assessment due to uncertainty in each witness's field of view, exact snow cover, and exact elevation/amount of cloud cover.
- **No anomalous data or characteristics** were recorded or assessed; the event did not represent an **unknown adversarial capability**.
- No aircraft or balloons were noted active in or around Cheyenne Mountain during the observation period.
- At 0945 MST, the sun was at ~27.5° altitude in the southeast sky at azimuth ~125°, casting shadows toward the northwest.
- Analyst notes describe **altostratus clouds** (mid-level, 6,500–23,000 ft) and the **backscattering** phenomenon.
