## Key Points

- Part I covers the mission from an **operational standpoint**: countdown (crew insertion, communications, comfort, sounds, vibrations, visual, controls), powered flight (lift-off, staging, BECO, SECO), insertion, orbital flight (station-keeping, extravehicular activities), and retrofire/reentry/landing.

- Crew: **James A. McDivitt (Command Pilot)** and **Edward H. White II (Pilot)** — second-generation astronauts. The 4-day mission was the longest two-man flight to date.

- No "Visual Sightings" section in Part I — the visual sightings, systems operations, operational checks, and experiments are covered in Part II (see [[summaries/nasa-uap-d017-preliminary-gemini-4-crew-debriefing-part2-1965]]).

- The Preface notes the document was rough-edited due to urgent mission analysis needs; an official transcript was to be published later. Errors and omissions were acknowledged.

- The debriefing guide followed a structured format: countdown → powered flight → insertion → orbital flight → station-keeping → EVA → retrofire → reentry → landing.
