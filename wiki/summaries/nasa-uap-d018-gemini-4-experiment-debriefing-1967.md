## Key Points

- This is a composite document including: a NASA **routing slip** (April 1969), a **Mission Operation Report** for GT-4 (May 24, 1965), an **Additional Flight Activities** memorandum (June 1, 1965), and later experiment analysis materials.

- **GT-4 Additional Flight Activities** (June 1, 1965 memo): Three significant additions were made to the flight plan: (1) extravehicular activity with 25-foot umbilical, (2) extravehicular propulsion using a hand-held ZIP unit, and (3) rendezvous demonstration with the booster second stage (flashing lights installed for this test).

- **EVA suit details**: The new G4C suit replaced the G3C. New features: triple-lens overvisor (visual, thermal, impact, micrometeorite protection), Nomex restraint layer, redesigned ventilation fittings, integrated thermal/micrometeorite cover layer, improved gloves.

- **Rendezvous procedure**: Post-launch separation, two orbits flown within 1/4 mile of launch vehicle. Beginning of second orbit: cabin depressurized, Ed White would EVA with camera-equipped ZIP unit, photograph booster. After EVA, spacecraft would separate, then one orbit later initiate visual rendezvous with booster using elevation-angle measurements against star background.

- **D-1 Basic Object Photography**: Objective was to photograph objects in space (second stage, etc.) to evaluate ability to view, track, and maintain object-camera alignment.

- **Mission report (M-913-65-04)**: Details the full GT-4 mission: 87–161 NM initial orbit at 32.5° inclination, 62 revolutions, ~97 hr 50 min duration. Primary objective: demonstrate 4-day systems endurance. Secondary: OAMS retrofire backup, in-plane/out-of-plane maneuvers, 13 experiments.

- The document is largely a pre-flight planning document with post-flight routing — not a traditional crew debriefing transcript.
