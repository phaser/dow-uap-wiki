## Key Points

- Part I covers the **operational** aspects of the Gemini 5 mission: countdown, powered flight, insertion, orbital flight, retrofire, reentry, and landing/recovery.

- Crew: **L. Gordon Cooper Jr. (Command Pilot)** and **Charles "Pete" Conrad Jr. (Pilot)**. The 8-day mission launched August 21, 1965 and was the longest manned spaceflight at the time (surpassing Gemini 4's 4 days and Vostok 5's 5 days).

- The debriefing guide followed the standard format. No visual sightings section — those are in Part II (see [[summaries/nasa-uap-d020-gemini-5-technical-debriefing-part2-1965]]).

- The Preface notes the transcript was made from voice tape recordings and urgently needed by mission analysis personnel; a thorough editorial review was deferred. Errors were to be corrected in a later official transcript.

- Notable operational events mentioned in the content: Cooper noted communications volume was "still down on the little comm" and crew comfort was "real fine" with two suit fans running. The crew found visual observations of the horizon during countdown to be unimpeded.
