## Key Points

- Part II covers **systems operation** (platform, OAMS, RCS, ECS, communications, electrical, computer, crew station), **operational checks** (Apollo landmark identification, cabin lighting survey, SPADATS tracking, UHF antenna tests, thruster illumination, radar tests, ID1 evaluation), **visual sightings**, experiments, pre-mission planning, mission control, and training.

- **OAMS thruster failures**: By day 5, multiple thrusters degraded. The crew turned off the OAMS heater during a power-down — Conrad suspected this caused the thrusters to freeze. "We were continually floating around in these old silver balls of either hydrogen or oxygen." When clearing thrusters, "all kinds of garbage came by the spacecraft... great big balls of liquid." Later: "We'd never seen anything like that before and we'd been up there 5 days and seen all sorts of things."

- **Section 10.0 Visual Sightings**:
  - **Powered Flight**: At SECO — "a lot of debris... stuff all over everywhere... snow all over the whole area... all sorts of glittering pieces." A washer floated in front of the window at 17,000 mph; a bolt came off an hour later.
  - **Orbital Flight**: The crew did not see their own booster (too busy with the flight plan). Saw the REP (Radar Evaluation Pod) and REP blanket repeatedly — the blanket somehow got between the spacecraft and the REP. **Satellites**: Did not see any of the scheduled ones — "you've got to have the platform to see them." One time "Pete and I thought we saw something and we didn't have time to identify it. We were in drifting flight and we never could identify it. I don't even know if it was a satellite. So many things are going by when you're drifting that it's difficult to say."
  - **Geographical**: "Millions of geographical details" — streets, airports (El Paso streets individually visible in right lighting), bridges, launch pads at the Cape, cars (Cooper found exactly one), an airliner, contrails, individual houses in Tibet.
  - **Celestial**: Stars to 7th magnitude visible despite "filthy" windows — far better than Mercury windows. Saw clusters in Orion's leg "I've never seen before." Observed the moon under all kinds of conditions. Sun polarization tests: circular polarization observed, linear only at sunset.
  - **Zodiacal light**: "We saw a Zodiacal light. We saw—" clearly observed when night vision was right.
  - **Aurora-type phenomenon**: "It looked like an Aurora... a very bright green. It changed in color and was in the airglow layer. It actually changed the height of the airglow below. It brightened it above... below it, it seemed to cut it down and make it more definitive." Cooper: "I don't know what it was. It was apparently some kind of Aurora effect."
  - **Meteorites**: "We saw a lot of meteorites."
  - **Lightning / weather**: "Hundreds and hundreds of miles of tremendous big thunder storm lines... as many as 15 to 20 thunderstorms lighting up all at the same time." Dust storms in Arabia and Africa clearly visible. Prevailing wind tracks visible in desert sand for hundreds of miles.
  - **Reentry**: Not specifically covered in the sampled sections.

## Notable Incidents / Cases

- **Gemini 5 unidentified object during drifting flight** — Cooper and Conrad spotted something during drifting flight but couldn't identify it; not confirmed as a satellite. Low-confidence sighting; crew themselves were uncertain.
- **Aurora-type green phenomenon** — A bright green, color-shifting light in the airglow layer that altered the airglow's height and definition. Described as "some kind of Aurora effect" but bright green and sharply defined — distinct from the diffuse airglow.
- **Silver balls of liquid oxygen/hydrogen** — Recurring observation of vented cryogens floating by, demonstrating how easily known spacecraft effluents produce dramatic visual phenomena.

## Open Questions

- The aurora-type green phenomenon: Cooper said he didn't know what it was. Could it be a known type of auroral emission (green line 5577 Å oxygen) observed from above rather than below? The crew's position above the phenomenon is a novel observation geometry.
- The unidentified object during drifting flight: Was this ever correlated with known satellite passes for that time?
- The REP blanket getting between spacecraft and REP — how did this happen, and did it affect any observations?
