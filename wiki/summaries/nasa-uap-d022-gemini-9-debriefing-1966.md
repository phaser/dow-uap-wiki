## Key Points

- This is a **compilation document** containing: routing slips, J.R. Gill's memorandum to file about the experiments debriefing of Gemini IX, and experiment descriptions/results from multiple principal investigators.

- **J.R. Gill memo (June 16, 1966)**: "Gemini IX's performance on the OSSA experiments was impressive. The guiding performed for S001 and S001 was the best that has yet been done on a manned spacecraft."

- **Experiment results summary**:
  - **S001 (Zodiacal Light Photography)**: 40 usable pictures of the zodiacal light and Milky Way. Guiding was good even for 30-second exposures. On Tri-X film, "these are the best pictures yet of the zodiacal light surpassing the results achieved on Gemini V."
  - **S011 (Airglow Horizon Photography)**: About 40 usable pictures of the airglow with a split-field arrangement attached to a wide-angle Maurer lens. "Guiding was very good even though Cernan had to do the experiment from a very awkward position."
  - **S012 (Micrometeorite Collection)**: Hemenway presented enlargements up to 35,000×. Only a small portion of the exposed surface had been analyzed; 9 impacts were recorded. Investigation of possible "contamination between experiments" was underway before sending sections to guest investigators.
  - **Biological experiments**: Pre-flight swabs taken inside the spacecraft were dirty; post-flight, same areas were sterile. T-4 bacteriophages and penicillium specimens survived the spaceflight; two other specimens did not.
  - **S-5 / S-6 (Synoptic Terrain and Weather Photography)**: Not officially carried but done as "operational procedure." Approximately 300 color pictures taken with Hasselblad and Maurer cameras (first time Maurer was carried operationally). A strip over Peru revealed "features which do not appear on any existing map; for example, a glacial lake in the Andes." The Peruvian Embassy was informed — the lake could portend an avalanche hazard for downstream villages.
  - **AMU (Astronaut Maneuvering Unit)**: Unsuccessful. "It proved to be too much 'work' under the present arrangements. It takes two hands just to control the body position." Hand-held photography during EVA deemed "out of the question" at present.

Carpenter (MSC), and others. The full document likely contains detailed per-experiment results from multiple principal investigators.
