## Key Points

- The PDF is **not** the full Apollo 12 voice transcript — it is a curated selection of pages NASA flagged as containing crew remarks on unusual visual phenomena, released as part of the agency's UAP-disclosure document set.
- The selected passages center on the LM ("Intrepid") rendezvous and CSM rejoin period after lunar lift-off, plus subsequent transearth coast.
- Routine technical content dominates: lift-off time updates ("142:03:47"), state-vector uplinks, AGS (Abort Guidance System) anomalies, RCS hot-fire checks, P22 tracking PADs, battery-charge management.
- The CMP (Gordon, "Yankee Clipper") greets the returning lunar crew — "Roger. You got a couple of coal miners coming up to see you" — referring to Conrad and Bean dust-blackened from EVA.
- One AGS DEDA (Data Entry & Display Assembly) "all 8's flash" anomaly on Intrepid was diagnosed by ground controllers ("Fredo") as electromagnetic interference (EMI) seen during Bethpage spacecraft testing. **Not a UAP — an instrument artifact.**

## Notable Incidents / Cases

### Bean — Particles "escaping the Moon" (MET 05 19 27 25)

LMP Alan Bean, looking out the LM Alignment Optical Telescope (AOT) into "the dark quadrant" (quadrant 1, left), describes:

> "You can see these lights — particles of light, flashes of light just seem to come from — in this case, I'm looking in quadrant 1 which is the left one. It's coming from behind me, the left, and they're just sailing off in space. I was thinking they're dropping from my water boiler, but it looks like some of those things are escaping the Moon. They really haul out of here and just press off at the stars."

CapCom acknowledges with a single "Roger" — no follow-up. Most plausibly the LM's own water-boiler venting, but Bean himself notes some "really haul out of here" and "press off at the stars," which is inconsistent with nearby spacecraft outgassing.

### Conrad / Gordon — "Bits and pieces" floating with the spacecraft, tracking-light disagreement (MET 06 00 21 51)

After CSM rendezvous, Conrad reports the LM tracking light has burned out:

> "On the first nightside pass we had little bits and pieces floating along with us and we could tell that the tracking light was flashing on them. And we still have, I've presumed to think, bits and pieces floating along and nothing's flashing on them, so I'm pretty sure it burned out."

Houston's electrical telemetry however shows current flowing to the tracking light. Gordon (CMP) cannot see the light from the CSM. The crew continue to observe debris/particles co-moving with the spacecraft for an extended period — routine and consistent with paint flakes / urine-dump ice / SLA (Spacecraft-LM Adapter) panel debris.

## Open Questions

- Why did NASA include this particular Apollo 12 transcript fragment in the UAP-disclosure set? The "particles escaping the Moon" line and the tracking-light/co-moving-debris exchange are the only candidates. Both have prosaic explanations available, but neither was systematically resolved on-mission.
- No discrete UFO/UAP "incident" with shape, size, or trajectory data appears here. Disclosure value is contextual rather than evidentiary.
