## Key Points

- Despite living inside NASA's UAP document drop, this excerpt is a **scientific debrief on UV astronomy results** from Apollo 17, not a UAP report. It records Dr. Henry summarising five astronomical findings from the mission's far-UV instruments:
  1. Coma cluster — searched for redshifted Lyman-alpha radiation from ionised hydrogen as a possible candidate for the cluster's missing mass; **none detected**, lower limit set; *"a real mystery as to what is holding the thing together"* (early independent confirmation, in spaceflight context, of the dark-matter problem).
  2. UV background at the North/South Galactic Poles — high-galactic-latitude diffuse emission detected; spectrum looks like hot stars but no hot stars in the field; conservative interpretation = scattered light from galactic-plane stars off interstellar dust; "at least possible that this is extragalactic radiation" (i.e. an early measurement of the extragalactic UV background).
  3. Lyman-alpha hydrogen radiation in the heliosphere — Apollo data complementing Gary Thomas (University of Colorado) and Charles Bertaux's (OCR-rendered "Barthum") OGO-5 observations of interstellar hydrogen streaming through the solar system.
  4. Spectrum of the Earth observed from outside (only fragmentarily covered in the excerpt — text cuts off).
  5. Gamma-ray background context (cross-reference to Trombka's separate report).
- **No UAP / UFO content in this excerpt.** Why NASA included this in their UAP disclosure release is unclear — possibly because *any* mention of unexplained sky background ("a real mystery") was included for completeness, or because the file is bundled with the broader Apollo-17 debrief corpus.
