## Key Points

- An FBI Special Agent (name redacted) interviewed a witness ([redacted]) "in person" at a [redacted] U.S. location. Supervisor present.
- Witness role: had been the [redacted] / [redacted] for [redacted] years (likely a long-tenured technical/operations role at a federal facility).
- **September [redacted] 2023**: Witness was with [redacted] contractors working on a "special project" with [redacted]. **She had restricted the air space in [redacted] for the upcoming tests.** Specific date redacted but anchored mid-September 2023.
- 7:02 a.m.: contractors received the base's standard [redacted] brief, got into three vehicles, began driving to the test site.
- 7:15-7:30 a.m.: witness drove to a gate that "restricted access to part of the [redacted]." Gate fob first opened "just a little and then closed on three separate tries"; on the fourth attempt the gate "opened completely and stayed open" — abnormal behavior. No prior issues with the gate, none after.
- As witness drove through the gate she **looked up and saw a cigar-shaped object** with an extremely bright light SW of them, ~500-3000 ft above the nearest tree line (treeline ~1 mile SW of [redacted] position).
- Object was **almost hovering, slowly moving east to west**.
- Light: "intense diamond white light with what appeared to be a ring around the light," located on the eastern end of the object; light "pointing south east"; looking at it "was like looking into the sun."
- Object: **"metallic bronze in color"**; length of "two or three Blackhawk helicopters lined up nose to tail" (i.e., ~50-150 ft using Blackhawk ~50 ft rotor span / ~50-65 ft length); width approximately "one and a half Blackhawks"; partially obscured by light on its eastern end.
- **Completely silent**.
- Witness was initially annoyed because she had restricted ranges for tests and assumed an unauthorized aircraft/drone — "but then she realized the object was not an aircraft or drone."
- She inched her vehicle forward; she and [redacted] watched the object for 5-10 seconds and then it "just disappeared." Sky was clear, no clouds, no contrails. They searched but did not see it again.
- No vehicle engine interference noted; only one object observed; she "felt it left when it saw them."
- Driver in second vehicle ([redacted]) said he saw the object too.
- Witness noted she "would not have reported the object if she had seen it by herself"; co-workers "subsequently made fun of her" for the report.
- No photos or video were taken.
- Witness had "seen most of the aircraft and drones used by the US military during her fifteen years working at [redacted] and had never seen anything like the object she observed."
