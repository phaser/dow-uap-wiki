## Key Points

- Detailed first-person operational narrative of a multi-hour orb encounter and chase by federal personnel in 2025, with FLIR, NVG, and naked-eye corroboration.
- 1700: Two senior U.S. intelligence officials (WITNESS 1 and WITNESS 2), Federal Partners 1 & 2, and two state-partner pilots departed an Operations Center in [STATE PARTNER ORGANIZATION] helicopter (CALL SIGN 1) for a daytime aerial search west of [SITE CODE NAME] over [MOUNTAIN RANGE NAME]. Goal: investigate previous eyewitness reports of orbs/lights and "thuds as if something has fallen and hit the ground." Earlier that day a successful test of [redacted system] had been completed at the site.
- 1751: CALL SIGN 1 spotted a **large cavern entrance** at [coordinates] and conducted a short orbit.
- 2050: CALL SIGN 1 landed west of the mountains, dropped off WITNESS 2 with [Federal Partner 3] personnel; 2052 headed to the site to refuel.
- 2141: CALL SIGN 1 lifted off, headed to debris spotted by LP/OP ([Federal Partner 4] using FLIR + NVG) ~4 mi east of the site in the mountains. Orbited; NVG and naked-eye search; negative results.
- 2149: Departed; LP/OP reported [redacted] hits 4 mi from their location; CALL SIGN 1 moved to intercept.
- 2202: CALL SIGN 1 arrived; spotted a possible aircraft on horizon moving away west. LP/OP confirmed the sighting; reported an **orb under FLIR described as "super-hot,"** hovering at ground level before heading [redacted] from LP/OP. Orb headed E then S at high speed and **broke into two objects**.
- 2207: JOC (Joint Operations Center) reported five [MILITARY AIRCRAFT] training in the airspace.
- Orb gained elevation, **came within 10 feet of CALL SIGN 1**, then headed east. CALL SIGN 1 unable to match speed; broke off pursuit. Co-pilot (NVG) reported "something emerge from the two objects and travel in a different direction at high rate of speed."
- 2218: At [coordinates], pilots (NVG) and WITNESS 1 (naked eye) spotted **a swarm of lights, too many to count**, moving in all directions, generally west of [redacted] and headed south.
- 2227: In proximity of [SITE CODE NAME] heading south, **two large orange-colored oval orbs** with "white or yellow center" appeared close to CALL SIGN 1, west and above the rotor disk. Orbs flared up, remained stationary side-by-side; a third orb flared below them, then a fourth, then a fifth — total of 4-5 in a vertical stack; then flared down in reverse order until visual lost.
- 2228: Four or five similar orbs flared up west over the mountains **above the [MILITARY AIRCRAFT]**, in a horizontal formation; flared down in opposite order after 10-15 seconds.
- 2233: Similar formation east toward [NEARBY TOWN NAME]; same flare-up/flare-down pattern.
- 2241: Single orb flared west of [SITE CODE NAME] over the mountain.
- 2249: Near [SITE CODE NAME], **swarm of lights moving in all directions** west of [redacted] with **three distinct orbs in a triangle formation**.
- 2252: Five-six orbs IVO [LOCAL TOWN], horizontal formation, flare-up/flare-down sequence.
- 2257: **Four orbs flared up over the [MILITARY AIRCRAFT] as they descended to land** at [redacted].
- 2306: Returned to Operations Center; 2316 low on fuel; 2320 headed south, passed [FEDERAL PARTNER AIRCRAFT] heading north toward the site.
- **WITNESS 1 comments**: pilots indicated they were recording, but many sightings were above the helicopter, outside FLIR camera angle. Some sightings the witness could not see with naked eye but the pilots called out via NVG. The orbs "appeared to break off from CALL SIGN 1 and pursue the [MILITARY AIRCRAFT]."
