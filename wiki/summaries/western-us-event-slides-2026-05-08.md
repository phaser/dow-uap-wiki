## Key Points — Four Distinct UAP Sightings (Western U.S.)

### Slide 1 — "Orbs Launching Orbs" (dusk, two separate days)
- **Three teams of two federal law enforcement special agents each (USPER1 through USPER6)** independently described seeing **orange "orbs" emit/launch smaller red "orbs"** in groups of 2-4 (consensus: 3).
- Pattern observed at least **five times** over the two-day window. Each time:
  - Orange "mother" orb appears, launches red orbs, then disappears.
  - Orange orb visible only 1-2 seconds.
  - Red orbs typically move horizontally away from mother; sometimes one moves "up at an angle"; sometimes red orbs "swoop down" after launch.
- Multiple teams from varying locations and vantage points witnessed.
- Open question (slide-stated): unknown whether one or several orange "mother" orbs.

### Slide 2 — "Large, Fiery Orb" (dusk)
- Two federal LE agents (**USPER5 and USPER6**) witnessed a glowing orange orb at "approximately 500-600 meters" — *AARO measurements later assess ~1,050 m distance and 12-18 m diameter*.
- Orb perched **close to a rock pinnacle**, similar in size to "a small helicopter cockpit."
- USPER6: object "almost appeared to have a small spindle or something connecting it from underneath to the rock formation."
- No noticeable sound.
- Described as resembling "the Eye of Sauron from Lord of the Rings, except without the pupil, or maybe an orange Storm Electrify bowling ball."
- USPER6: object "seemed to be hovering with zero resistance or movement, or to be suspended..."
- Sighting lasted "about a minute."
- Slide includes an artist rendering — orange-red orb with a horizontal bright streak inside.

### Slide 3 — "Dark Kite" (pre-dawn hours)
- Two federal LE agents (**USPER5, USPER6**) observed what initially appeared to be a car traveling along a road in a restricted zone.
- "Car" had **two lights (one red, one white) about 2-3 ft off the ground**.
- As agents approached within a few hundred feet, the object "suddenly moved off the road over the desert without changing its orientation" — moved **laterally at ~15-20 mph**, no height change, "zero resistance."
- Object stopped ~100 m off the side of the road and turned off lights.
- USPER6: described the back as "a thin line." USPER5: "ill-defined, dark kite shape that had some rounded width to the sides." (In later AARO discussions, described as **triangular**.)
- USPER6 used NVG; saw object briefly, then lights off; under NVG saw "a very thin line" remaining.
- Object ~4 ft wide, positioned horizontally to ground.
- Object then began moving ~15-20 mph, **moved up in height while remaining a flat line**; lost after a few seconds.
- Slide shows recreated NVG drawing: red light + white light with horizontal line/bar.

### Slide 4 — "Transparent Kite" (pre-dawn, ~30 min after Dark Kite)
- USPER5, USPER6 plus colleague **USPER7** returned to the same general area in response to a report of another unauthorized object.
- Within a few hundred meters of the Dark Kite sighting:
  - **Kite-shaped object with similar lighting pattern**, ~6 m off the ground, canted at an angle from lower right to upper left, "floating slowly with the wind."
  - USPER5 (NVG) + USPER6 (bare eye) saw it; USPER7 did not.
  - In NVG: USPER5 "could vaguely see a bright star or two in the distance through the object, though somewhat more faint" — suggesting **partial transparency**.
  - USPER5 used a spotlight to relocate it: "at one point my beam went from shining far into the distance to stopping about 50 yards away on nothing in particular, it just was not projecting into the distance and then it was." Pointed light at the apparent obstruction; light then projected normally. **Could not reacquire**.
- Slide shows recreated drawing: triangular/kite outline with one filled red dot at upper left and three open white dots; note: "Outline was not definitive, but location of lights stayed in exact formation."
