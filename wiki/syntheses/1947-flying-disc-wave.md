## Scope

Cross-cutting analysis of the **1947 flying-disc wave** as it appears in the U.S. Army / Air Force, FBI, and Project SIGN records preserved in this corpus. Geographic scope: CONUS. Temporal scope: late June through December 1947, with the 4 July 1947 weekend as the densest cluster.

## Findings

### Volume and witness reliability are unprecedented

The Project SIGN Box 7 file (see [[summaries/army-incident-summaries-1-100]]) opens with **Incidents 1–4 at [[entities/muroc-aaf]]** on 7–8 July 1947 — and every witness across those four cases is a serving Army Air Forces officer or test pilot ([[entities/strapp-john-paul]], [[entities/mchenry-joseph-c]], [[entities/wise-j-g]], [[entities/shoop-richard-r]]). On its own, this case set neutralises the most common institutional skepticism — that UFO reports come from low-credibility witnesses. By the time AMC's Technical Intelligence Division stood up [[entities/project-sign]] in late 1947 / early 1948, the 1947 wave had already produced the witness corpus that anchored the program's case files for the next two years.

### Geographic and temporal clustering

- **4 July 1947 weekend** — Portland, Oregon police wave (Incidents 5, 5a, 6); near-simultaneous public sightings nationwide (in press, not all in this corpus).
- **7–8 July 1947** — Muroc AAF / Rogers Dry Lake (Incidents 1–4); Hamilton Field follow-on Incident 8 on 29 July 1947.
- **Autumn 1947** — Cape Mendocino blimp (1 Jul), Oakland "Saturn-like" object (13 Oct), Ticonderoga discs (12 Nov), Phantom Reef (24 Nov), Oswego, OR cluster (3 Sep, 12–15 round silver objects).

### Object descriptions converge

Across the sampled 1947 cases the typical descriptor is silver / aluminum / white **discs or spheres**, 5–25 ft estimated diameter (occasional 50-ft outliers like the Strapp object), 200–600 mph estimated speeds, altitudes 4 000–30 000 ft, **no exhaust trail, no audible sound**, sometimes performing tight circles or 90° instantaneous heading changes. The kinematic signature is striking: silent, fast, and capable of maneuver beyond known 1947 aircraft.

### Disposition mechanisms emerge

The 1947 corpus is also the source of the methodological practices that survive into the modern UAP record:

- The **26-field check-list** (see [[concepts/incident-checklist-template]])
- Explicit [[concepts/witness-reliability-evaluation]] (occupation, demeanour, prior reports)
- [[concepts/multi-witness-corroboration]] as primary disposition criterion
- Routing of fireball cases to a meteoritic-explanation specialist ([[entities/lapaz-lincoln]])

### The first ETH discussion

The **Markham letter** (in [[summaries/army-incident-summaries-1-100]]) treats the 1947 cluster as evidence consistent with non-terrestrial reconnaissance — the earliest documented serious entertainment of the [[concepts/extraterrestrial-hypothesis]] inside a U.S. Army intelligence file. By 1949 [[entities/project-grudge]] would tilt the institutional posture against ETH, but the 1948 record preserves the open-question phase.

## Open Threads

- The **Mantell incident** (7 Jan 1948) and the **Clinton County AAF cluster** the same evening (Incidents 30, 32, 33, 33a–33f, 48, 48a–48d) need a focused re-read of the 33-series pages — the Mantell-cluster pages were not in the sampled ranges of the 1-100 incident-summary file.
- The **Maury Island incident** (June 1947) is referenced in the Markham letter but no primary case file from the corpus has been located; it may be in unread pages of the 1-100 or 173-233 volumes.
- The **Roswell incident** (early July 1947, New Mexico) does *not* appear in the sampled portions of the corpus — neither in the Project SIGN incident summaries (which begin with Muroc on 7-8 July, *after* the Roswell debris-field reports of ~3-4 July) nor in the FBI 62-HQ-83894 sections sampled to date. This is itself notable: either the Roswell case sat in a separate file, was deliberately excluded from the standard incident-summary series, or appears in unread pages.
