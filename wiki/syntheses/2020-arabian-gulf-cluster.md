## Scope

Cross-cutting view of the unusually dense **2020 cluster** of UAP Mission Reports and range-fouler debriefs in the Arabian Gulf / Persian Gulf / Strait of Hormuz / Arabian Sea — collectively the [[entities/centcom]] AOR, dominated at the air-component level by [[entities/afcent]]. The cluster is one of the most distinctive geographic-temporal signals in the modern DoW UAP release set (`dow-uap-d<N>`).

## Findings

### Cluster shape

The 2020 CENTCOM-AOR cluster spans at minimum:

- **D3 / D4 / D5 / D6 / D7** — Arabian Gulf, 2020 (date redacted; typical 17:36Z timeframes)
- **D38** — Middle East, May 2020, range-fouler debrief
- **D44** — Arabian Sea, October 2020, range-fouler debrief
- **D56** — Arabian Sea, August 2020, range-fouler debrief
- **D60 / D61** — Persian Gulf, August 2020
- **D62** — Strait of Hormuz, September 2020
- **D63** — Strait of Hormuz, October 2020
- **D64** — Iran, November 2020
- **D65** — Persian Gulf, July 2020

That is **at minimum 14 distinct UAP events** in a single CENTCOM AOR over a single calendar year — a notable density, even allowing for selection bias in what AARO chose to release.

### The 482 ATKS Persian Gulf MQ-9 cluster

Within the 2020 CENTCOM cluster, **482 ATKS** — an MQ-9 Reaper attack squadron — appears as the originating unit on **six sorties** between July and November 2020, every one of which logged at least one UAP encounter and 1–5 Iranian Air Defense guard calls. This is the strongest single-unit / temporal cluster in the entire DoW UAP release set. NAVCENT was the supported COCOM-component; tasking flowed via [[entities/afcent]].

### Other observed patterns

- **Sphere-with-tail / probe morphology** (D25 diamond+tail, D27 sphere+pole, D74 "bouncy ball") — recurring shape across multiple AORs, suggesting a real form-factor signal rather than reporting noise.
- **Cold thermal signature** (D23, D44, D57) — counter-intuitively IR-*cold* objects, distinct from the more common white-hot signature class (D8, D19, AC-130 D28). See [[concepts/cold-thermal-signature]].
- **DCA / F-15E / F-16 high-altitude UAP formations** (D19, D20, D58) — 2020s Operation INHERENT RESOLVE Defensive Counter-Air (DCA) orbits over Syria recurrently encountering bright objects above flight level FL240–FL600+.
- **EMI / lost-link / RF-jamming co-occurrence** (D19 Turkish X-band jammer + UAPs, D58 noise jamming with disappearance, D62 27-minute lost-link prior to UAP) — a possible RF-environment correlation worth follow-up.
- **172 / "1172" ATKS Gulf of Aden cluster** (Sep + Oct 2020) — round IR-cold orbs with abrupt directional changes; two reports with nearly identical morphology.

### Filename-vs-content mismatches (data-quality caveat)

Several files in the dow-uap-d* set carry filenames that do not match their actual content. Confirmed mismatches: D14 ("Iraq" → actually Eastern Mediterranean); D20 ("Southern US 2020" → actually Syria F-16 DCA, March 2023); D27 ("UAE Oct 2023" → actually Arabian Sea June 2024); D28 ("East China Sea" → Iraq, Ayn al Asad); D42 ("Japan 2023" → actually CENTCOM, August 2020); D60 (PDF metadata says D36). Per ingest rules, the wiki preserves the as-released filenames (they index AARO's release decisions) but each affected summary flags the mismatch in its Open Questions. **Do not trust filenames for geographic / temporal clustering** — read the per-summary metadata.

### Common signature

Where the redacted text permits inference, the cluster shares:

- ISR / fighter platforms on routine missions encountering UAPs in their sensor field of view ([[concepts/uap]], [[concepts/range-fouler]]).
- Frequent **multiple-object** detection in tight time windows (D3: 4 UAPs across 27 seconds — see [[concepts/multiple-object-formation]]).
- Cloud / weather obscuration cited as a frequent cause for loss-of-track.
- Standard Misrep template, Service Tasked = U.S. Air Force or U.S. Navy depending on platform; SIPR-domain (`@mail.smil.mil`) email correspondence.

### What is being redacted, consistently

Across every 2020-cluster Misrep, the consistent redactions are: aircraft type, callsign, tail number, basing, and the entire Narrative block under (b)(1)1.4a. What survives is the **UAP block** (object count, propulsion, signature, capabilities) and the date / time / region / MAJCOM / COCOM headers. AARO has effectively chosen to release the *that-it-happened* skeleton of each event while withholding the *how-we-saw-it* sensor and platform details.

## Open Threads

- Is the 2020 density **a real spike** in UAP activity over the CENTCOM AOR (as some operational accounts suggest), or **a release-selection artifact** (AARO chose to declassify the 2020 batch first)? The corpus alone cannot discriminate.
- Are the 14+ events related (e.g., a single sustained UAP campaign) or independent? Unit-level / tail-level correlation would settle this — but those are exactly the fields under (b)(1)1.4a redaction.
- The Iranian / Strait of Hormuz overlap with the **3 January 2020 Soleimani strike** and elevated U.S.–Iran tension across mid-2020 invites the obvious question of whether some of these "UAP" detections are conventional Iranian / non-state drone activity later not-yet-attributed. The released Misreps explicitly check **"UAP Advanced Capabilities And/Or Materials = NO"** in the D3 case, consistent with a less-exotic interpretation.
