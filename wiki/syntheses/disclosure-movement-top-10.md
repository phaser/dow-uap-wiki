## Scope

A curated ranking of the ten most disclosure-movement-relevant primary-source documents in the wiki's corpus, considered against the contemporary (2017–2026) disclosure context: the 2017 NYT *Glowing Auras* article, the 2019 Senate briefings, the 2020 ODNI report, the AARO establishment under NDAA-2022, the 2023 Grusch testimony, the Schumer-Rounds NDAA-2024 disclosure provisions, and the 2026 Department of War / AARO public release that this corpus is built on.

"Disclosure-relevant" is operationalized here as: **primary-source weight × novelty / surprise × political or scientific load**. A document scores high if it is a verified primary record, contains material that the disclosure community has long been pointing at as significant, and engages the question of UAP institutional handling at a level above routine field-investigator paperwork.

The ranking is a judgment call. Confidence on this synthesis is set to `medium` because the underlying documents are verified to exist in the corpus, but the *ordering* reflects an editorial weighting that another reader could reasonably rearrange.

### Justifications (long form)

**1 — [[entities/hunter-policy-memo-1963|Hunter NASC memo, 18 July 1963]]** ([[summaries/59_214434_sp_16_1963-07-18]])
A National Aeronautics and Space Council *staff* memo, "Thoughts on the Space Alien Race Question", treats the alien-contact scenario as a policy-actionable concern at the executive-office level — not as a UFO investigation, but as a policy-planning paper about how an encounter would be handled. The most disclosure-relevant document in the corpus on the strength of *what* is being discussed *where*: the NASC sat directly under the Vice-President.

**2 — [[summaries/western-us-event-slides-2026-05-08|DoW Western US Event Slides, 8 May 2026]] + [[summaries/usper-statement-redacted|USPER Statement (redacted)]]**
The corpus's most recent items, postdating most published AARO material. Preserves the active-period morphology categories — [[concepts/orbs-launching-orbs]], "Fiery Orb", "Dark Kite", "Transparent Kite" — and a 2025 helicopter-platform engagement of an orb (see [[entities/western-us-helicopter-orb-2025]]). This is the live tip of the disclosure trail.

**3 — [[syntheses/2020-arabian-gulf-cluster|2020 Arabian Gulf / Persian Gulf cluster]]**
14+ DoW UAP mission-report and range-fouler-debrief documents from the [[entities/482-atks|482 ATKS]] MQ-9 zone in 2020 — Strait of Hormuz, Iran, Gulf of Aden, Persian Gulf. Heavy redactions on admin/asset metadata, but the GENTEXT/UAP narrative blocks survive. Single most documentary-rich modern military-platform corpus in the wiki, structurally analogous to (and partially overlapping with) the AATIP-era cases that have driven the post-2017 disclosure wave.

**4 — [[summaries/62-hq-83894-section-10|FBI HQ 62-HQ-83894 Section 10 (1966–1977)]]**
Earns the slot for *three* distinct disclosure-relevant artifacts surfaced by the OCR re-ingest:
- The **June 1977 Carter White House UFO inquiry**: Stanley Schneider (OSTP) called FBI for Jody Powell, asking whether there was any executive-branch coordination on UFO information. The FBI replied there was none.
- The **August 1974 Milwaukee object recovery**: Capt. Wills (Milwaukee PD) recovered a 13×8×5 inch metallic object with "internal heat source"; **NMCC (National Military Command Center) called the FBI within 6 hours** asking about it. The Pentagon's command-and-control center inquiring on a local police object recovery is itself the disclosure-relevant data point.
- The **Paul Peyerl walk-in (April 1967)**: Luftwaffe veteran walks into the Miami FBI office with claimed photographs of a 1944 Black-Forest test saucer and engineering specs. See [[entities/paul-peyerl-1967]]. Physical claims are problematic, but the documentary fact of the walk-in is striking.

(Bonus from the same file: the **James Collins Chesapeake VA abduction report** of 18 January 1967 — a structurally complete pre-Hill abduction narrative lodged with the FBI and never publicized. See [[entities/james-collins-chesapeake-1967]].)

**5 — [[entities/maury-island-affair|Maury Island Affair, June 1947]]** (covered in [[summaries/62-hq-83894-section-3]])
The canonical 1947-wave case: physical debris claim from Maury Island WA, the **Kelso B-25 crash 1 August 1947 that killed two AAF intelligence officers (Capts. Davidson and Brown) ferrying purported samples**, and accusations of FBI/AF coverup. Foundational to the disclosure canon's "they recovered something" narrative. The corpus preserves the FBI's investigation file.

**6 — [[summaries/nasa-uap-d4-apollo-11-technical-crew-debriefing-1969|Apollo 11 Technical Crew Debriefing, 1969]]**
Astronaut post-mission technical debrief mentioning a hollow-cylinder object Armstrong/Aldrin/Collins observed in cislunar space, plus Aldrin's discussion of unexplained flashes inside the spacecraft. This is the actual NASA technical debrief transcript, not third-hand summary — credentialed-witness astronaut primary record in a non-public-statement setting. Pairs with [[summaries/nasa-uap-d7-skylab-technical-crew-debriefing-1973|Skylab D7]] (Skylab 3 "red satellite" + SAA-correlated flashes).

**7 — [[entities/socorro-zamora-1964|Socorro / Lonnie Zamora, 24 April 1964]]** (FBI LHM in [[summaries/62-hq-83894-serial-438]])
The cleanest physical-evidence case in the wiki: landing trace, multi-agency on-scene investigation (Socorro PD + FBI SA D. Arthur Byrnes Jr. + Capt. Holder USAF), Project Blue Book disposition of "unidentified" — the rarest dispositional category in the program's history. The newly-surfaced [[summaries/62-hq-83894-section-10|Section 10]] internal Bureau note (December 1966) confirms the Albuquerque FBI office produced the original Zamora witness statement that fed downstream Bureau and AF dissemination.

**8 — [[entities/borman-gemini-7|Borman Gemini-7 "Bogey at 10 O'Clock High", December 1965]]** ([[summaries/255_t_763_r1b_transcripts]])
Frank Borman's verbatim radio transmission during Gemini 7, preserved in the State Department transcript collection. Astronaut UFO observation in flight, primary text not paraphrase.

**9 — [[summaries/62-hq-83894-sub-a|FBI HQ 62-HQ-83894 Sub A "scrapbook"]]**
A single Bureau scrapbook preserves primary materials for **Nash–Fortenberry (1952)**, **Killian (1954)**, **Jung correspondence**, and **Swan/Knowles** — four canonical 1950s pilot and civilian cases in one source. High signal density per page.

**10 — [[summaries/341-numerical-file-5-2500|USAIRA Prague IR 193-55 — 1955 Czechoslovakia disc]]**
US Air Attaché Prague intelligence report (IR 193-55) on a 1955 Eastern-bloc disc sighting. Cold-war classified-attaché primary source on UAP behind the Iron Curtain — the only such Eastern-bloc attaché-channel item in this corpus, narrow but unusually authoritative.

## Open Threads

### What this corpus does NOT contain

For honest contextualization with the disclosure movement, several canonical items the disclosure community routinely cites are **not in `raw/`**:

- **Roswell July 1947** — the wiki notes this gap; only referenced via [[entities/cometa-report|COMETA Report]] Appendix 5.
- **Tic-Tac / USS Nimitz 2004 incident** — the FLIR1 video, Cmdr. David Fravor's testimony, and SCU report are not in this corpus.
- **AATIP / AAWSAP DIRDs** (Hal Puthoff et al.) — not in this corpus.
- **Project Aquarius** DIA papers — not in this corpus.
- **Grusch / Mellon NHI / biologics / crash-retrieval** specific testimony — not in this corpus.
- The 1980s–early-2000s middle period is thin overall: corpus density is highest 1947–1970s and 2020–2026.

### What this corpus has that's *under-represented* in disclosure-movement discourse

- The **late-1940s SIGN incident-summary form** as a methodological artifact ([[summaries/army-1948-general-vol-1]] and the 1-100 / 101-172 / 173-233 trilogy). The form's evolution from 26-line CHECK-LIST to 25-line INCIDENT SUMMARY captures the program's analytical maturation in a way that public disclosure narratives rarely surface.
- The **1966–1977 FBI / White House transition** preserved in [[summaries/62-hq-83894-section-10]]. The Carter-WH inquiry alone is a primary record that anchors a missing piece of the institutional-handling history.
- The **Scandinavian "Peenemünde-direction" ghost-rocket continuity into 1948** ([[summaries/army-incident-summaries-101-172]] Inc 132–133). Connects the [[concepts/foo-fighter|WW2 foo-fighter]] / [[concepts/ghost-rockets|1946 ghost-rocket]] phenomena to the 1947+ flying-disc wave with primary-source bridges.
