## Scope

Cross-cutting analysis of how the [[entities/fbi]]'s institutional posture toward unidentified aerial phenomena has shifted across four phases visible in this corpus: (1) **1947–1949 Open Question** under [[entities/j-edgar-hoover]] following the 1947 wave; (2) **1949–1956 Active Monitoring** with field-office reports flowing through [[entities/62-hq-83894]]; (3) **1956–1969 Folkloric / Contactee Drift**, where the file accumulates [[concepts/contactee]] correspondence, [[concepts/men-in-black]] adjacency, and channeled-message material; (4) **1964 Operational High-Water Mark** with the Lonnie Zamora Socorro case and a full FBI on-scene LHM; and after a long quiet period, (5) **2023–2025 Operational Re-Engagement** with FBI FD-302s on a restricted-range UAP at a LiDAR drone test (paired serial-3 and serial-4 redacted documents).

## Findings

### 1947–1949: Open Question (Hoover era)

The Bureau opens [[entities/62-hq-83894]] within weeks of the 1947 wave. Field offices submit witness statements, slag samples (the [[entities/maury-island-affair]]), photograph negatives ([[entities/rhodes-phoenix-photographs]]), and inter-agency reports. The institutional posture is genuinely uncertain — Hoover's office routes to Cabell at AFOIN ([[entities/cabell-charles-p]]) for the "[[concepts/unconventional-aircraft]]" assessment but does not commit to a Bureau finding.

### 1949–1956: Active Monitoring

After AFOIN Memo No. 4 (1949) and the [[concepts/skyhook-balloon]] explanation for the [[entities/mantell-incident]], the Bureau settles into a steady-state monitoring posture: clip the press, interview repeat reporters, file correspondence with letter-writers to the President and Director.

### 1956–1969: Folkloric / Contactee Drift

The file accumulates [[entities/62-hq-83894]] Sub A, Serial 403, Serial 449, and Section 10 — material that is decreasingly operational and increasingly cultural. **Bender / Barker** Men-in-Black material (Serial 403), the **Renaud / Korendor** [[concepts/channeled-message]] correspondence (Serial 449 + Section 10), the **Carl Jung** APRO statement (Sub A), the contactee-author cohort (Adamski, Bethurum, Williamson, Van Tassel, Menger, Fry, Schmidt, Padrick) all enter the file. The Bureau's interest is closer to *anthropology of a sub-culture* than to *investigation of an aerial phenomenon*.

### 1964: Operational High-Water Mark

Against this drift, the **[[entities/socorro-zamora-1964]]** case stands out: an FBI on-scene Letterhead Memorandum (LHM) by SA D. Arthur Byrnes Jr., joint with Sgt. M. S. Chavez of Socorro PD and Air Force Project Blue Book. **Serial 438** of [[entities/62-hq-83894]] preserves this LHM. Of every event in the FBI HQ flying-disc file, Socorro is the one where the Bureau's investigative apparatus produces a tightly-corroborated physical-trace + multi-officer-witness record.

### 2023–2025: Operational Re-Engagement

Two paired FD-302s (`serial-3_redacted.pdf` and `serial-4-redacted_redacted.pdf`) document a **September 2023 cigar / linear UAP at a restricted U.S. range during a LiDAR drone test** — multiple LE-witnessed, redacted, but unmistakably operational. Together with the 2025 [[entities/western-us-helicopter-orb-2025]] event (USPER1–7), the Bureau's UAP record visibly re-activates after a long quiet period — concurrent with the [[entities/aaro]] / Congressional disclosure cycle.

## Open Threads

- The corpus contains essentially no FBI UAP material between **1969** and **2023** — the long gap is striking and may reflect (a) an actual institutional pull-back, (b) FOIA-release ordering rather than absence, or (c) Bureau redirection of UAP-relevant work into the AFOSI / DIA / ODNI streams that don't surface in FBI file numbers.
- The Guy Hottel memo of 22 March 1950 — the most-cited single document in the FBI UAP record outside this analysis — was *not* surfaced in the sampled spans of [[entities/62-hq-83894]] sections. Its status here is a known gap; a targeted full-section OCR pass on Sections 5–6 should resolve.
