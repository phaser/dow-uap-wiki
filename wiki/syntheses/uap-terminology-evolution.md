## Scope

Cross-cutting analysis of how the U.S. government's vocabulary for unidentified aerial phenomena has shifted across four eras represented in this corpus: WW2 (1944–1945), the 1947 wave through Project Blue Book (1947–1969), the post-Blue-Book lull (1969–2017), and the modern AARO era (2017–present).

## Findings

### "Foo fighter" (1944–1945)

Wartime Allied aircrew slang for unidentified luminous balls / spheres pacing aircraft over Europe and the Pacific. Connoted curiosity, *not* threat. Period investigators considered candidates including German secret weapons (Wasserfall, Henschel, V-weapons), St. Elmo's fire / electrical phenomena, ball lightning. Documented in the German armament equipment file of 1944–1945 (see [[summaries/german-armament-equipment-1944-1945]]) — Allied investigators reviewed captured German records for evidence of a foo-fighter weapons program. See [[concepts/foo-fighter]].

### "Flying disc" / "flying saucer" (1947–early 1950s)

Coined in press accounts of Kenneth Arnold's June 1947 Mt. Rainier sighting; rapidly adopted into U.S. military and FBI vocabulary as the dominant 1947–1955 descriptor. The shape descriptor (disc, saucer, oval, sphere) is foregrounded; the [[concepts/extraterrestrial-hypothesis]] is implicit but not yet doctrinally codified. See [[concepts/flying-disc]], [[entities/62-hq-83894]] (the FBI HQ "Flying Discs" file).

### "UFO" (1952–2017)

Project Blue Book's bureaucratic abbreviation for **U**nidentified **F**lying **O**bject — a deliberately neutral, shape-agnostic term that displaced "flying disc" / "flying saucer" because (a) reported shapes had diversified beyond discs (triangles, cigars, orbs, elongated cylinders), and (b) the older terms had acquired tabloid connotations that institutional investigators wanted to escape. The term dominated for ~65 years.

### "UAP" — Aerial → Anomalous (2017–present)

The modern term has two distinct phases:

- **2017–2022 — UAP = Unidentified *Aerial* Phenomena.** Adopted by the UAPTF (UAP Task Force) and the 2021 ODNI preliminary assessment, partly to escape "UFO"'s cultural baggage and partly to admit non-craft phenomena (lights, sensor returns) into formal reporting.
- **2022–present — UAP = Unidentified *Anomalous* Phenomena.** Broadened by [[entities/aaro]] to cover [[concepts/transmedium]] (water/space) cases and orbital phenomena. The shift from "Aerial" to "Anomalous" was a deliberate inclusion of non-aerial signatures that had been arriving from Navy / Marine sources (object enters/exits water without splash, etc.).

## Open Threads

- Did "flying disc" displace "foo fighter" in 1947 *because* the disc-shape press coverage drowned out the orb-shape WW2 vocabulary, or did the underlying phenomena change shape distribution between 1945 and 1947? The corpus does not contain enough mid-1946 records to discriminate.
- Project Blue Book's institutional posture (1952–1969) is under-represented in this corpus — the 1948 incident summaries are pre-Blue-Book, and the modern DoW UAP records are post-Blue-Book by 50 years. A targeted ingest of Blue Book closing-era records (1968–1969) would close this gap.
- The 2022 "Aerial → Anomalous" rename is documented here from the receiving side (Misreps tagged with the broadened scope) but not from the rule-making side; the AARO charter / DoD instruction that effected the change is not in this corpus.
